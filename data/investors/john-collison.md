## Background

John Collison was born on August 6, 1990, in Dromineer, County Tipperary, Ireland [^1]. His father was an electrical engineer who worked for Dell, and his mother was a microbiologist; both parents went on to start their own companies [^1][^2].

Collison attended Castletroy College for secondary school, where he received eight A1 and two A2 grades in the Irish Leaving Certificate examination in 2009 [^1][^2]. He enrolled at Harvard University in September 2009 to study physics but dropped out the following year to focus on Stripe [^1][^3].

Before Stripe, Collison and his brother Patrick co-founded Shuppa in 2007 in Limerick, Ireland, which later merged with Auctomatic, a Y Combinator-funded company that built tools for eBay sellers [^1][^2]. Auctomatic was acquired for $5 million in March 2008, when John was 17 years old [^1][^2].

In 2010, John and Patrick Collison co-founded Stripe, an online payments infrastructure company [^1]. Their first venture round garnered approximately $2 million from Sequoia Capital, Andreessen Horowitz, SV Angel, and notably, PayPal founders Elon Musk and Peter Thiel [^2]. Stripe processed over $1 trillion in total payment volume in 2023 and operates across 47+ countries [^4]. As of February 2025, Stripe was valued at $91.5 billion [^4].

Collison was named the youngest self-made billionaire in the world in 2016 at age 26 [^1][^3]. His net worth was estimated at $9.3 billion by the Bloomberg Billionaires Index as of 2025 [^1].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Collison says publicly about his approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Collison has not published an explicit investing thesis but has shared relevant views in interviews:

- **Infrastructure focus:** The Collison brothers consistently invest in companies building foundational technology that other businesses depend on, rather than chasing consumer apps or trends [^4].

- **On building products:** Collison has stated that when launching new products at Stripe, they "start them small and make them earn their way by responding to customer feedback" [^5].

- **On culture:** Collison has said: "Culture is what happens when the CEO isn't in the room" [^5].

- **On geographic challenges:** Collison has observed that "coming from Ireland, it's quite hard to do a startup because you're culturally so far away from what everyone else is doing, whereas in the Bay Area it's much easier — equivalent to an actor or actress moving to Hollywood" [^5].
