## Background

Amy Cheetham is a Partner at Costanoa Ventures, where she focuses on seed and Series A investments in B2B fintech and modern SaaS [^1]. She joined Costanoa in 2019 and was promoted to Partner in November 2021 [^2][^3].

Before joining Costanoa, Cheetham ran North American sales strategy and operations as Chief of Staff at Zuora, a public enterprise software company [^1][^3]. Prior to Zuora, she spent three years as a growth equity investor at Summit Partners, where she sourced the firm's investments in Podium, InfoArmor (acquired by Allstate), and onXmaps [^1][^3]. She began her career on Wall Street at JP Morgan, working as a technology investment banker and as a cash equities trader focused on biotechnology and financial services [^1][^3].

Cheetham holds a B.A. in Government from Connecticut College [^4]. She is an advocate for financial inclusion and diversity in venture capital, and works with AllRaise to run a VC careers job board aimed at broadening access to roles in the industry [^1][^3].

She was named to the Forbes Midas Brink List in 2023, recognizing her as one of the top ten emerging VCs [^1][^5]. She was also named to EVCA's Top 25 Emerging VCs [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Cheetham has publicly described her focus as seed and Series A stage B2B fintech companies, encompassing fintech infrastructure, payments, and application-layer tools [^1]. She has stated that she has expanded Costanoa's fintech practice into emerging markets, specifically Latin America and Africa [^1][^3].

In a 2025 LinkedIn interview, Cheetham stated that "the real alpha is going to be at the app layer" in AI for financial services, noting that "enterprises are leaning into AI for real efficiency gains, product and engineering orgs still set the agenda, and the whitespace in 'boring' industries is wider than most people assume" [^6]. She also stated that "the next breakout companies in AI for financial services won't come from the obvious places" [^6].

Cheetham has said she believes that "for-profit technology companies stand the best chance of solving many of the world's toughest problems and venture lets me be a part of that process" [^1].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 10 verified investments led or co-led by Cheetham at Costanoa, the following patterns emerge. Note: sample size is small and these are qualitative observations rather than statistically robust breakdowns.

**Sector focus:** The portfolio is overwhelmingly fintech. Of 10 verified investments, at least 9 are fintech or fintech-adjacent (90%). The portfolio spans payments infrastructure (Highnote, Malga, Tensec, Basis Theory), payroll/financial services for underserved markets (Cadana, Highline), identity verification/fraud prevention (Trully), solopreneur financial tools (Assis), and cross-border finance (Tensec). Only Kevala (healthcare workforce management) falls outside fintech.

**Geographic distribution:** A notable portion of the portfolio targets emerging markets, particularly Latin America and Africa. Malga (Brazil), Assis (Brazil), Trully (Mexico), Cadana (Africa/emerging markets), and Tensec (cross-border with offices in Mexico City and Sao Paulo) all have significant emerging market operations. This is a distinctive pattern not commonly seen among US-based seed investors.

**Stage distribution:** Primarily seed-stage investments, with follow-on participation at Series A and beyond. The Basis Theory Series B lead (2025) shows willingness to lead later rounds for portfolio companies.

**Founder profiles:** The portfolio shows a preference for founders with deep domain expertise in financial services and payments. Highnote was founded by former Braintree/PayPal executives [^7]. Tensec was founded by veterans from PayPal, Goldman Sachs, Visa, Mastercard, and Credit Karma [^8]. Basis Theory was co-founded by Ben Milne, previously founder of Dwolla [^9].

**Board involvement:** Cheetham sits on the boards of Tensec, Assis, Trully, Cadana, Malga, and Kevala, and serves as Board Observer at Highline — indicating a hands-on, high-engagement model [^1].

**Notable pattern not mentioned in stated thesis:** The strong concentration in Latin American fintech (3 of 10 companies based in Brazil or Mexico) is a distinctive specialization that goes beyond the general "emerging markets" framing in her stated thesis.
