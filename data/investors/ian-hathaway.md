## Background

Ian Hathaway is a Partner at the OpenAI Startup Fund, where he leads the $175 million venture fund affiliated with OpenAI [^1]. He joined the fund at its launch in 2021 and assumed formal control in April 2024 when Sam Altman transferred leadership of the fund to resolve a corporate governance concern [^2]. The transition was notable because Altman had served as the fund's named controller since inception, and former OpenAI board member Helen Toner stated that Altman had not disclosed his ownership of the fund to OpenAI's board [^2].

Before joining OpenAI, Hathaway was an investor at Haystack, a San Francisco-based seed-stage venture capital firm, from 2017 to 2021 [^1] [^3]. At Haystack, he supported early-stage companies including Instacart, DoorDash, HashiCorp, Wag, and Carta [^1].

Hathaway was named to Global Corporate Venturing's Powerlist in both 2024 and 2025, recognizing him as one of the top 100 corporate venturing professionals globally [^1] [^4].

In addition to leading the fund's investments, Hathaway oversees OpenAI's Converge accelerator program, launched in December 2022, which provides selected startups with a $1 million equity investment and early access to OpenAI models [^1] [^5].

## Stated Thesis

Hathaway and the OpenAI Startup Fund publicly describe their focus as backing "exceptional engineers, designers, researchers, and product builders using AI to solve critical problems in healthcare, law, education, energy, sciences, and enterprise" [^6]. The fund states its core belief is that "new, powerful AI systems will give rise to a transformative wave of startups" [^6].

VCSheet describes Hathaway's approach as seeking to back "outsider founders building AI-native companies outside traditional Silicon Valley hubs" [^7].

Hathaway has stated that cybersecurity is a key area of focus, noting: "AI is reshaping the cybersecurity threat landscape faster than most organizations can respond" [^8].

On AI in education, Hathaway stated regarding Speak: "Our 2022 investment in Speak was driven by a shared vision of revolutionizing language learning with AI" [^9].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 20 verified investments made through the OpenAI Startup Fund (see Portfolio below), Hathaway's actual investment pattern reveals a portfolio heavily concentrated in applied AI across regulated and high-impact verticals, with notable emphasis on healthcare, robotics, and developer tools.

**Sector breakdown** (20 verified investments):
- Healthcare/biotech: 4 of 20 (20%) -- Ambience Healthcare, Thrive AI Health, Chai Discovery, Valthos
- Education/language: 3 of 20 (15%) -- Speak, Class Companion, Heeyo
- Robotics/hardware AI: 3 of 20 (15%) -- 1X Technologies, Figure AI, Physical Intelligence
- Developer tools/infrastructure: 2 of 20 (10%) -- Anysphere (Cursor), EdgeDB
- Cybersecurity: 1 of 20 (5%) -- Adaptive Security
- Legal tech: 1 of 20 (5%) -- Harvey AI
- Fintech: 1 of 20 (5%) -- Kick
- Sales/marketing tech: 1 of 20 (5%) -- Unify
- Productivity/media: 2 of 20 (10%) -- Descript, Mem
- Autonomous driving: 1 of 20 (5%) -- Ghost Autonomy (shut down)
- Consumer: 1 of 20 (5%) -- Milo

**Stage distribution** (20 investments, by earliest fund participation):
- Seed/pre-seed: 12 of 20 (60%) -- Anysphere, Kick, Chai Discovery, Heeyo, Class Companion, Milo, Thrive AI Health, Ghost Autonomy, Physical Intelligence, Atomic Semi, Valthos, Unify (seed)
- Series A: 2 of 20 (10%) -- Harvey AI, 1X Technologies
- Series B: 4 of 20 (20%) -- Ambience Healthcare, Figure AI, Speak, Endex
- Series C: 2 of 20 (10%) -- Descript, EdgeDB

**Notable patterns:**
- **AI-native mandate**: Every single portfolio company builds on or integrates AI capabilities. This is a 100% AI-focused fund, which distinguishes it from most generalist VC firms.
- **Strategic ecosystem investing**: Many portfolio companies build directly on OpenAI's models, creating a symbiotic relationship between the fund's investments and OpenAI's platform adoption.
- **Converge pipeline**: Several investments (Descript, Harvey, Mem, Speak, Unify) originated through the Converge accelerator, which serves as a deal sourcing funnel [^10].
- **Multi-round conviction**: The fund frequently follows on -- Harvey AI received investment across multiple rounds from seed through Series D, Speak across Series B and C, and Adaptive across two tranches [^11] [^12] [^9].
- **Unicorn hit rate**: At least 5 portfolio companies have achieved unicorn status: Anysphere/Cursor ($29.3B as of November 2025), Harvey AI ($8B), Figure AI ($2.6B+), Ambience Healthcare ($1B+), and Speak ($1B) [^11] [^13] [^14] [^9].
- **Co-investor patterns**: Frequent co-investors include Andreessen Horowitz (Ambience Healthcare, Adaptive), Kleiner Perkins (Ambience Healthcare, Harvey), Thrive Capital (Chai Discovery, Unify), General Catalyst (Kick), and Lux Capital (Valthos) [^15] [^8] [^16] [^17].
- **Geographic concentration**: The vast majority of portfolio companies are US-based, with the notable exception of 1X Technologies (Norway) and Speak (significant operations in South Korea).

**Gaps between stated and actual thesis:**
- The stated thesis emphasizes "outsider founders" outside Silicon Valley, but the portfolio is overwhelmingly concentrated in San Francisco Bay Area and New York companies.
- Robotics represents 15% of the portfolio despite not being prominently featured in the stated thesis.
- Energy, mentioned in the stated thesis, has minimal representation in the verified portfolio.
