## Background

Arash Afrakhteh is a Partner at Pear VC, the Palo Alto-based pre-seed and seed fund founded by Pejman Nozad and Mar Hershenson [^1]. He joined Pear in February 2020 as one of the firm's first-ever visiting partners — a new role created after Pear closed its third fund at $160M in December 2019 — and subsequently became a full Partner [^2].

Afrakhteh immigrated to the United States from Iran in 1993 and began his career as a software engineer, working at Microsoft, Apple, Broadbase Software, and Vertical Networks, where he was principal architect of their network management software [^3]. In 2001, he co-founded Cariden Technologies, an IP/MPLS traffic engineering and network planning software company, without taking any venture capital or angel funding [^3]. Over eleven years he bootstrapped Cariden to dominant market share — approximately 85% of global tier-1 Internet Service Providers relied on the company's software — before Cisco acquired the company on December 17, 2012 for $141 million in cash and retention incentives [^3][^4]. Cariden's MATE product family addressed network capacity planning, traffic engineering, and SDN infrastructure, and Cisco cited its nLight technology for IP and optical convergence as the strategic rationale for the acquisition [^3].

After the Cisco acquisition, Afrakhteh spent time as an operator and angel investor before joining Pear. He holds a Computer Engineering degree from Sharif University of Technology and is based in Menlo Park, California [^1]. At Pear he focuses on enterprise and B2B startups, with a particular sub-focus on AI/ML, cloud infrastructure, data center infrastructure, data infrastructures, and SDx (software-defined everything) [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Afrakhteh publicly describes his focus as enterprise and B2B startups at the pre-seed, seed, and Series A stages, with a sub-focus on AI/ML, cloud infrastructure, data center and data infrastructures, and SDx [^1]. His investment philosophy centers on working closely with founders from day one: "he loves working with founders from day one, getting into the trenches, and collaborating on company building, from engineering and processes, to hiring and culture, and GTM and fundraising" [^1].

His most-cited guiding principle: "If you want to change the world for the better through your startup, the best place to start is your own team." [^1]

He has articulated a thesis on AI agents as a primary investment category, writing in August 2024 that AI agents represent "the closest approximation we have today to the vision of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)" and identifying seven archetypes of agents he believes will create substantial value: human-simulating, assistant, automation, general-purpose, vertical, embodied, and collaborative agents [^5].

On the pitfall he watches for in founders: drawing from his experience at Cariden, he has flagged the risk of "falling in love with your PhD thesis" — pursuing ultra-optimized academic solutions over market-driven product development [^3].

## Inferred Thesis

**Based on 8 verified investments where Afrakhteh's individual involvement is documented via board seats, named quotes, or direct attribution. Sample is limited; percentages are approximate and should not be treated as definitive.**

Of the 8 verified investments, the sector breakdown is:

- AI/ML and enterprise automation: Orby AI (enterprise workflow automation), Typedef (AI data infrastructure), DevAI (AI for enterprise IT), Advex AI (synthetic data for AI vision) — 4 of 8 (50%)
- Data and cloud infrastructure: Aklivity (streaming API management/Kafka), Mezmo/LogDNA (log management) — 2 of 8 (25%)
- Enterprise supply chain / logistics: Expedock (AI freight forwarding automation) — 1 of 8 (12%)
- AI enterprise (insurance underwriting): Federato — 1 of 8 (12%)

Stage distribution across documented investments is consistently pre-seed and seed: Expedock (PearX S20 pre-seed), Federato (PearX S20 pre-seed), Aklivity (PearX → $4.1M seed), Orby AI ($4.5M seed), Advex AI ($3.5M seed), DevAI ($6M seed), Typedef ($5.5M seed). Afrakhteh also participated in Orby AI's $30M Series A follow-on (2024), indicating he tracks portfolio companies through later rounds [^6].

Co-investor patterns: NEA (Orby AI seed and Series A), Wing VC (Orby AI), 468 Capital (Aklivity seed lead), Emergence Capital (DevAI seed lead), Construct Capital (Advex AI seed lead). These suggest Afrakhteh frequently co-invests with deep-tech and enterprise-focused funds rather than generalist mega-funds at the seed stage.

Technical founder preference: Every documented deal involves a technical founding team with domain depth — former Cisco/DevNet executives (DevAI), Google Cloud AI engineering leaders (Orby AI), AI manufacturing specialists (Advex AI), and database infrastructure engineers (Typedef). This pattern is consistent with his own identity as a technical founder.

Geographic concentration: All documented portfolio companies are US-based, primarily in the Bay Area and Palo Alto corridor, with Expedock operating as a global logistics company.

Notable gap between stated and inferred thesis: Afrakhteh's stated focus emphasizes "cloud infrastructure" and "SDx," legacy categories from his Cariden era. His actual investments since joining Pear are concentrated in AI/ML application layer (automation agents, synthetic data, AI infrastructure) rather than pure cloud or networking infrastructure. The portfolio has shifted materially toward the AI stack.
