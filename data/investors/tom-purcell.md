## Background

Thomas W. Purcell Jr. is the Co-Founder, Managing Partner, and Co-Portfolio Manager of Alua Capital Management LP, a global investment firm based in New York [^1][^2]. He co-founded Alua in November 2020 with Marco Tablada [^2].

Purcell began his investment career as a generalist at Tiger Management in 1998, the legendary hedge fund founded by Julian Robertson [^1]. He then spent over 14 years at Viking Global Investors, where he rose through the ranks to serve as Co-Chief Investment Officer, and also served on the firm's Management Committee and Executive Committee [^1]. After leaving Viking, he founded Lake Trail Capital, a single-family office [^1].

Purcell holds an MBA from Harvard Business School, where he graduated as a Baker Scholar (top 5% of class) [^1]. He earned a B.S. in Foreign Service from Georgetown University [^1]. In 2017, he gave a talk at Georgetown's Leaders of Global Finance Speaker Series discussing trends in the long/short hedge fund industry and the rise of passive investing [^3].

## Stated Thesis

Alua Capital describes its approach as "agnostic with respect to 'growth' or 'value' styles of investing," employing bottom-up fundamental analysis to search for "individual ideas that we believe can deliver exceptional returns over the medium to long term" [^2]. The firm uses a "low-to-no leverage, high conviction portfolio strategy" with a "medium to long-term investment horizon" [^2].

Alua invests globally across financial services, consumer, business services, industrials, technology, media, and telecommunications sectors [^2].

The firm emphasizes alignment of interests with limited partners: Purcell and Tablada have invested "the vast majority of their respective liquid net worths" in Alua, with internal capital comprising approximately one-third of total fund net asset value [^2].
