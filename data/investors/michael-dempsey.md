## Background

Michael Dempsey is the Managing Partner at Compound, a thesis-driven, research-centric investment firm based in New York that invests at the early stage in deeply technical and science-driven areas including AI/ML, robotics, biology and healthcare, crypto, energy, and materials science [^1][^2]. He was formally announced as Managing Partner in March 2022, having joined the firm in 2016 when it was being relaunched [^3][^4].

Dempsey was born in Los Angeles, grew up in Boston, and graduated from New York University in 2011 [^4]. After college he spent roughly 2.5 years at Crane Partners, a small multi-strategy hedge fund where he worked across cross-border private equity, public equities, derivatives, and venture capital, eventually leading the firm's early-stage tech investments [^4]. He was an early employee (among the first 15) at the predictive analytics startup CB Insights, where he helped build the data and research team and specialized in emerging technologies including drones, space, VR/AR, food tech, and international venture capital [^4][^1]. Before joining Compound he was on the investment team at Rothenberg Ventures, leading seed-stage Frontier Tech investments in space, robotics, drones, and autonomous vehicles [^4].

Compound has reportedly grown to "a little north of $200 million under management across several small funds" [^5]. The firm operates from 414 Broadway in New York and the team includes General Partner David Hirsch, Partners 0xSmac and Shelby Newsad, CFO Tara Eckert, and venture partners specializing in computational biology, AI, robotics, and crypto [^2].

## Stated Thesis

Compound publicly describes itself as "a Thesis-Driven, Research-Centric Investment Firm" focused on "Helping accelerate the futures that our founders believe in" [^2]. Dempsey has stated that the firm's edge is "understanding mismatches in time horizons between what's technologically possible and what other people think is possible" [^6].

In his own framing, Dempsey defines deep tech as "trying to solve a problem that a subset of people think is largely impossible, or rather largely impossible on the prescribed time horizon" [^6]. He has also said that Compound seeks "post-science project investing" — opportunities in emerging categories where there is at least some commercial viability — and on his personal site writes, "if it's a little more than a science project, I'd love to learn about it" [^4][^7].

He has publicly described an explicit power-law fund model: "We lose money 40% of the time is our model and if we're right on two out of 25 companies, we'll have very good returns" [^5]. On portfolio shape he has stated that the firm develops year-by-year theses on sector trajectories, "acknowledging 80% may prove wrong while aiming for highly accurate predictions in the remaining 20%" [^8].

## Inferred Thesis

This analysis is based on 13 verified investments where Dempsey or Compound participation is confirmed by a primary or contemporaneous press source. Compound's full portfolio listed on its website is materially larger than this verified subset, so percentages below should be read as directional rather than statistically definitive.

**Sector distribution (13 verified investments):** AI / ML / autonomous systems is the dominant sector — Wayve, Runway, Deepgram, Prime Intellect, Orbital Materials, and Fort Robotics together account for 6 of 13 (46%). Crypto/blockchain is a clear secondary theme — The Graph, Livepeer, Stacks (Blockstack), and Plural Energy account for 4 of 13 (31%). Healthcare / biotech accounts for 3 of 13 (23%) — Tia, Talkspace, and Bionaut Labs. Compound's own published portfolio page additionally lists ~22 healthcare/biology companies and ~13 crypto companies, suggesting these categories are larger in the full portfolio than the verified subset implies [^9].

**Stage distribution:** Of the 13 verified rounds, 7 are seed or pre-Series A (Wayve seed, Deepgram seed, The Graph seed, Orbital Materials seed, Prime Intellect seed, Plural Energy seed, Bionaut Labs early stage) and 6 are Series A or later follow-ons (Runway Series A, Tia Series A, Livepeer Series A, Fort Robotics Series B, Talkspace Series D). Signal by NFX lists Dempsey's check size range as $400K–$1.5M with a $1M sweet spot, consistent with seed-stage initial checks [^10]. Dempsey has confirmed the firm does "Seed through Series B follow-on investment" with "typically 4-year deployment periods" [^5].

**Geographic focus:** Compound is New York-based and several portfolio companies are NYC-based (Runway, Talkspace, The Graph). However the verified portfolio is internationally distributed: Wayve (London/Cambridge UK), Orbital Materials (London UK), Prime Intellect (Europe). This is consistent with Dempsey having co-led Wayve's seed alongside Berlin-based Fly Ventures and his stated openness to founders globally [^11].

**Founder profile patterns:** Dempsey favors deeply technical founders, frequently with PhD-level research backgrounds (Wayve's Alex Kendall — Cambridge PhD; Orbital Materials' Jonathan Godwin — DeepMind alum; Prime Intellect — decentralized AI researchers). He has stated he "fundamentally" does not believe "all founders are good founders" and emphasizes founder-product fit [^8].

**Co-investor patterns:** Across the verified portfolio, frequent co-investors include Lux Capital (Runway), Fly Ventures (Wayve, Orbital Materials), Amplify Partners (Runway), Multicoin Capital (The Graph), CoinFund (The Graph), Khosla Ventures (Bionaut Labs), Distributed Global (Prime Intellect), Necessary Ventures (Plural Energy), Volt Capital (Plural Energy), and Maven11 (Plural Energy). Signal by NFX additionally lists frequent network co-investors including John Doerr, Charles Hudson, and Satya Patel [^10].

**Notable patterns vs. stated thesis:** The stated thesis emphasizes equally AI, crypto, biology, and energy/materials. The verified portfolio confirms these but shows AI/ML as the largest single category and a distinct emerging cluster around decentralized AI (Prime Intellect) and AI-for-materials (Orbital Materials) — both bridging two of Compound's stated focus areas. Dempsey has been openly skeptical of humanoid robotics specifically, stating "I'm relatively anti-humanoid robots" — a contrarian stance against a popular 2024-2025 robotics narrative [^6].

**Notable gap:** Despite Compound's website prominently listing materials science as a focus area, the verified portfolio includes only Orbital Materials in that category. Most "frontier" / energy / materials portfolio companies on Compound's published portfolio page lacked the public funding announcements needed for individual citation in this profile.
