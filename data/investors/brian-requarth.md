## Background

Brian Requarth is a serial entrepreneur, angel investor, and co-founder and General Partner of Latitud, a pre-seed venture fund and startup infrastructure platform for Latin America [^1]. He grew up in Sebastopol, a small town of approximately 7,000 people in Northern California [^2]. He studied Spanish and Portuguese in college [^2].

In 2003, Requarth drove from California to Costa Rica and onward through Central America to Colombia, a six-month journey that led him to fall in love with Latin America [^3]. He initially taught English in Colombia and ran a translation and web development business before co-founding VivaReal, a real estate marketplace in Brazil, around 2009 [^2][^4]. He served as CEO of VivaReal from 2009 to July 2016, growing the company to over 500 employees and raising approximately $74 million in venture capital [^4][^5]. He was inspired to enter proptech after reading a case study about Mercado Libre in a coffee shop [^6].

In December 2017, Requarth orchestrated a merger between VivaReal and ZAP Imoveis, owned by Latin America's largest media company Grupo Globo, forming Grupo ZAP [^5]. He became Chairman of the combined entity [^5]. In March 2020, Grupo ZAP was acquired by OLX Brasil for R$2.9 billion (approximately $600 million USD), with the transaction closing on October 30, 2020 [^2][^5]. Requarth has publicly shared that the deal cost him over $100 million in taxes due to a corporate structure mistake made during VivaReal's early days — an experience that directly inspired Latitud's company formation product [^7][^8].

Requarth co-founded Latitud in 2020 alongside Gina Gotthilf (former VP Growth at Duolingo, who helped scale the app to 300 million users) and Yuri Danichenko (former CTO/CPO at a Brazilian B2B tech platform) [^9][^10]. Latitud raised a $13 million seed round in March 2022, co-led by Andreessen Horowitz and NFX, with participation from Endeavor, Canary, FJ Labs, Ganas Ventures, and over 100 founder-angel investors including David Velez (Nubank), Sebastian Mejia (Rappi), Carlos Garcia (Kavak), Daniel Vogel (Bitso), Matias Woloski (Auth0), and Sergio Furio (Creditas) [^9][^10].

Requarth is the author of *Viva the Entrepreneur: Founding, Scaling, and Raising Venture Capital in Latin America* [^4]. He appeared as an investor on Shark Tank Mexico [^11]. He is also co-founder and CEO of Camu, an AI-powered company automating corporate tax processes in Brazil [^12].

## Stated Thesis

Self-reported. Requarth publicly describes his investing focus as Latin American technology startups, particularly at the pre-seed stage [^13]. Through Latitud Ventures, his stated areas of interest include fintech (infrastructure, payments, embedded lending), vertical SaaS, healthtech, marketplaces, e-commerce enablers, and proptech, with special attention to the Brazilian and Mexican markets [^13][^14].

Requarth has stated on his NFX Signal profile: "I am focusing primarily on investing in tech startups in Latin America. I like Marketplaces, PropTech and SaaS startups" [^15].

On fundraising philosophy, he has publicly advised founders: "Stop fundraising pre-traction. Build to $300K ARR. Prove you can execute. Then investors will compete for you. Because the fastest way to raise capital in a capital-scarce market is to not need it" [^16].

The Latitud Ventures manifesto describes Fund II's approach as leading 40 pre-seed rounds and co-investing in 20 seed rounds over three years, with a typical check size of approximately $200K for roughly 5% ownership and implicit dilution under 10% [^14].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 127 active portfolio companies listed on the Latitud Ventures portfolio page [^17], the following patterns emerge. Note: these investments are through Latitud Ventures (the fund), not all personally attributable to Requarth as an individual angel. He claims 150+ personal angel investments [^3], but only a handful are independently verifiable outside the Latitud portfolio.

**Sector breakdown (127 portfolio companies):**
- Fintech: approximately 45 of 127 (35%) — including Pomelo, StarkBank, Yuno, Finkargo, Belo, Jefa, Malga, Kanastra, NG Cash, Silverguard, Dinie, and others [^17]
- E-commerce / Retail: approximately 15 of 127 (12%) — including Clubbi, Diferente, Linkana, Newtail, Zigo [^17]
- Proptech / Real Estate: approximately 10 of 127 (8%) — including aMORA, Duppla, Kocomo, Pulppo, Woba, Pilar [^17]
- Healthcare: approximately 8 of 127 (6%) — including Alinea Health, Clivi, Kuri, Liti, Arvo Saude, Extendeal [^17]
- EdTech: approximately 7 of 127 (6%) — including AnyoneAI, Udocz, Nulinga, BeConfident, Hunty [^17]
- Enterprise / SaaS: approximately 10 of 127 (8%) — including Treble AI, Leadsales, Salvy, Maggu [^17]
- Other (AgTech, Gaming, Insurance, HR, Logistics, Cybersecurity): remainder [^17]

**Stage distribution:** Predominantly pre-seed. Latitud Ventures Fund I invested $13 million across 100+ startups (averaging ~$130K per company) [^13]. Fund II targets $250K average checks at pre-seed, with some seed co-investments [^13][^14].

**Geographic concentration (127 companies):**
- Brazil: approximately 60 of 127 (47%) [^17]
- Mexico: approximately 30 of 127 (24%) [^17]
- Argentina: approximately 15 of 127 (12%) [^17]
- Colombia: approximately 12 of 127 (9%) [^17]
- Other (Chile, Peru, Central America, USA): remainder [^17]

**Founder access model:** Latitud operates a fellowship program that serves as a deal pipeline — 1,250+ fellows have collectively raised $650 million [^14]. The fund often writes the first check in companies that emerge from the fellowship. As Latitud's manifesto states, the team gets to know founders during the equity-free program before deciding to invest [^14].

**Co-investor patterns:** Portfolio companies have gone on to raise from a16z, DST, Founders Fund, General Catalyst, Index Ventures, Insight Partners, Kaszek, Monashees, QED, Ribbit, and Sequoia [^13]. The fund explicitly positions itself as a first-check partner that helps companies graduate to larger institutional rounds.

**Notable gap vs. stated thesis:** While Requarth states interest in proptech and marketplaces, the actual portfolio is heavily skewed toward fintech (35%), significantly more concentrated than his stated sector-agnostic approach suggests.
