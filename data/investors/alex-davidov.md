## Background

Alex Davidov is a General Partner at Abstract Ventures, a seed-stage venture capital firm based in San Francisco managing approximately $1.8 billion in assets [^1][^2]. He joined Abstract in 2017 as the first partner recruited by founder Ramtin Naimi, helping build the firm from an investment vehicle into a full venture platform [^3][^4].

Before joining Abstract, Davidov served as a Principal at Core Innovation Capital, a fintech-focused venture fund, where he invested in early-stage fintech companies [^5][^6]. Prior to Core, he spent approximately one year as an investor at Founder Collective, a seed-stage fund that backed companies including Uber, PillPack, The Trade Desk, and Airtable [^5][^6]. Earlier in his career, he spent four years at Bridgewater Associates, where he worked in internal strategy and led a big data analytics team within the trading strategy group [^5][^6][^7].

Davidov is a two-time founder. He co-founded SkillSlate, a labor marketplace that was acquired by TaskRabbit, and Intellitoys, a company that made interactive children's toys allowing parents to personalize play experiences [^3][^5][^6]. He started his career as a Business Analyst in Technology at McKinsey & Co. and also worked as a Senior Associate at Katzenbach Partners LLC and in software development at Microsoft [^7].

Davidov holds a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from Columbia University and an MBA from Columbia Business School, where he graduated with Dean's Honors and Distinction (top 5% of class) [^3][^5][^7].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Davidov and Abstract Ventures say publicly about their investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Abstract Ventures publicly describes itself as "sector-agnostic," dedicated to backing "transformative founders building exceptional companies across all sectors" [^1][^8]. The firm emphasizes three pillars when partnering with founders: "fierce loyalty, unparalleled connections, and a relentless drive to help them win" [^1].

Davidov has stated that he focuses on applied AI solving tangible problems, digital-first wealth management platforms, early customer traction signals, grid infrastructure and electrification, and augmented reality technologies modernizing legacy industries [^3]. He emphasizes early go-to-market traction and a founder's ability to articulate compelling vision as key evaluation criteria [^3].

Davidov maintains an unusually open approach to deal flow, keeping his DMs open and accepting direct deck submissions from early-stage founders [^3][^4].

Abstract Ventures requires every founder it invests in to sign an "Impact Pact," which is written into investment documents and is legally binding [^4]. The Impact Pact requires founders to pay it forward in three areas: wealth sharing (distributing ownership beyond the executive team), supplier diversity, and community support [^4]. The firm reports that 92% of its founders are BIPOC and 81% of portfolio employees are BIPOC [^4].

## In Their Own Words

Davidov has stated that at Abstract Ventures, investing in a founder means investing in their commitment to pay it forward — through the vendors they choose, the teams they build, and the wealth they share [^4].

On his approach to deal flow, Davidov keeps his DMs open and accepts direct deck submissions, emphasizing accessibility to early-stage founders [^3][^4].

On Abstract's Impact Pact model, the firm has stated that paying it forward is not charity — when founders prioritize diverse suppliers, those suppliers gain revenue and credibility; when founders build inclusive teams, they tap talent pools competitors ignore; and when founders share equity broadly, retention improves and alignment deepens [^4].

On Abstract Ventures' performance, the firm has reported that its portfolio created 436 jobs in 2024 — a 48% compound annual growth rate since 2021 — and generated over $400 million in wealth creation, with $342 million going to BIPOC and women founders plus $65 million in employee equity [^4].

Regarding one of his portfolio companies, Davidov stated on X/Twitter that "Origin's new AI financial advisor beats all leading LLM models on financial planning questions and beats humans on the CFP exam by 20%" [^13].
