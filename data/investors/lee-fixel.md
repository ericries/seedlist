## Background

Lee Jared Fixel was born in 1980 and raised near Fort Lauderdale, Florida [^1][^2]. His father, Norman Fixel, worked as a lawyer, and his mother was a fashion designer [^1]. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration with concentrations in finance and accounting from the Olin Business School at Washington University in St. Louis in 2002 [^2][^3]. He also completed the General Course at the London School of Economics and is a CFA Charterholder [^1].

Fixel began his career as an analyst at Alkeon Capital Management, covering internet companies [^1]. In 2006, he joined Tiger Global Management, where he rose to partner and head of the firm's private equity division over a 13-year tenure [^1][^4]. Under his leadership, Tiger Global's private equity portfolio reached approximately $13 billion by 2019 [^1]. His most notable investment at Tiger Global was in India's Flipkart, where Tiger Global invested over $1 billion across multiple rounds; the 2018 sale to Walmart generated a $3 billion gain, with an additional $1.4 billion from the remaining stake sold in 2023 [^1][^5]. Other major investments he led at Tiger Global included Spotify, Coinbase, Stripe, Roblox, Peloton, Palantir, Freshworks, Mercado Libre, and Ola [^1][^5][^6].

Fixel departed Tiger Global on June 30, 2019, reportedly tired of the multi-partner decision-making process where multiple partners had to approve each deal [^1][^7]. In 2020, he launched Addition, a venture capital firm with a solo decision-maker structure [^1][^8]. Addition has raised five funds in four years: Addition One ($1.3B, 2020), Addition Two ($1.4B, 2020), Addition Three ($1.4B, 2021), Addition Four ($1.5B, 2022), and a fifth fund targeting $1.5B filed in 2023 [^1][^8][^9]. Total assets under management reached $7 billion by 2022 [^1][^10].

As of 2024, Fixel has appeared on the Forbes Midas List twelve times, ranking #76 on the 2025 edition [^1]. He was also named to Business Insider's Seed 100 list of top early-stage investors in 2025 [^1].

Fixel resides in New York City with his wife Lauren and two children [^2]. He co-founded the Lauren and Lee Fixel Family Foundation in 2014, which committed $20 million to the University of Florida in 2019 to establish the Norman Fixel Institute for Neurological Diseases, named after his father who was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease over 20 years ago [^1][^2][^3].

## Stated Thesis

Addition publicly describes its focus as investing in "founder-led, technology-enabled companies" [^3]. The firm targets both early-stage and growth-stage opportunities, with approximately one-third of capital allocated to early-stage investments and two-thirds to growth-stage [^10].

Fixel has emphasized disruption as a core investment lens. In a 2017 talk at Washington University, he described looking for businesses that offer "an equal or higher-quality product at the same price or a lower price with the same or higher gross margins," noting that "when you can provide that quality of product at a lower price point, the disruptive forces are very powerful" [^11].

He has stressed patience as a key investment virtue, citing Amazon: "It's an incredible business, but there was almost a decade where people were doubting them, thinking it's a terrible business" [^11]. He has stated: "That's really important for entrepreneurs to know that we can be very patient and long-term with our support of their businesses" [^11].

Addition's stated strategy emphasizes scalable business models, execution-focused founding teams, clear paths to market leadership, and viable exit strategies [^10]. The firm operates globally across North America, Europe, Asia, and emerging markets [^10].

### Geographic Patterns

Addition invests globally but with strong US concentration. The firm has maintained Fixel's Tiger Global-era interest in India, with investments in Delhivery ($125M+), Inshorts ($35M), and SuperOps.ai ($14M) [^13][^14][^15]. The firm is headquartered in New York [^8].

### Check Size

Addition operates across a wide range: $1M–$50M for early-stage (target $10M), and $30M–$200M for growth-stage [^10][^16]. This is consistent with the firm's multi-stage approach and $1.3B–$1.5B fund sizes.

### Founder Profile Patterns

Fixel strongly favors founder-led companies and has a track record of betting on founders over metrics. His investment in Freshworks via Tiger Global came when the company had only $10K monthly recurring revenue — he told founder Girish Mathrubootham: "If you look at the business numbers, there is nothing much to bet on, I am just betting on you" [^6]. Similarly, his Flipkart conviction was founder-driven: "If we're right, this is going to be absolutely enormous and the valuation's not going to matter. And if we're wrong, who cares what percentage we own?" [^11].

### Co-Investor Patterns

Addition frequently co-invests with top-tier firms. Observed co-investors across the portfolio include Andreessen Horowitz (Base Power), Stripe (Fast), Coatue (Applied Intuition, Alchemy), Lightspeed (Base Power), Tiger Global (Snyk), Accel (Snyk), Greylock (Chronosphere), and General Atlantic (dLocal) [^17][^18][^19][^20][^21].

### Decision Speed

Addition's solo decision-maker structure is a deliberate response to Fixel's frustration with Tiger Global's multi-partner approval process [^7]. Multiple sources describe his decision speed as "tremendous" with "unparalleled instinct" [^22]. The Freshworks deal at Tiger Global was decided the day after a single hotel lobby meeting [^6].

### Notable Gaps and Divergences

Despite the stated thesis of investing in "technology-enabled companies" broadly, Addition has a strong revealed preference for enterprise/B2B software (37% of verified investments) over consumer (9%). The firm's consumer investments (Warby Parker, Allbirds) are largely from its Tiger Global carry-over relationships. Addition's emerging energy investments (Base Power, Zap Energy) represent a newer thesis not prominently featured in public messaging.

## What Founders Say

**Girish Mathrubootham, Founder & CEO of Freshworks:**

Mathrubootham has described how Fixel invested in Freshworks at Tiger Global when the company had only $10K in monthly recurring revenue. After a single meeting in a hotel lobby in Chennai — where Fixel happened to be visiting for another investment — Fixel called the next day with an offer:

> "Girish, the numbers don't make any sense. You are very early for us as a fund. But I am making a bet on you. You have seen the scale. And if you are okay, here are the terms." [^6]

Fixel proposed skipping the formal term sheet entirely:

> "If you like it, we will shake hands in the air and we can skip the term sheet and move directly into due diligence." [^6]

Tiger Global conducted a customer survey as part of due diligence and found overwhelmingly positive results. Mathrubootham recalled: "The survey report was overwhelmingly positive — I think 96% of the survey respondents actually said they would recommend us to their friends" [^6].

**SaaSBoomi community (on Fixel's character):**

According to the SaaSBoomi community, which Fixel supported financially, "the speed at which he moves is tremendous, his instinct unparalleled and his humility unique" and "Lee the investor and Lee the person [are] so intrinsically connected that it is difficult to separate the two." His humility was described as "striking, especially considering his extraordinary achievements" [^22].
