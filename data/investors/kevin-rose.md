## Background

Kevin Rose (born 1977) is an American internet entrepreneur, media personality, and technology investor [^1][^2]. He attended the University of Nevada Las Vegas, majoring in computer science, but dropped out in 1998 [^2].

Rose began his career in media as a production assistant and co-host on TechTV's *The Screen Savers* [^2]. In November 2004, he co-founded Digg, a social news aggregation platform that pioneered user-driven content curation through voting mechanics [^3][^4]. At its peak, Digg reached over 38 million monthly users [^3][^5]. Rose holds six social media and voting patents from his Digg work, which were later acquired by LinkedIn [^5].

In October 2004, Rose also co-founded Revision3, an internet television network that produced shows including Diggnation, Systm, and Tekzilla; Discovery Communications acquired Revision3 in 2012 for over $30 million [^4]. He co-founded Pownce, a micro-blogging platform, in 2007; it was acquired by Six Apart in 2008 [^2][^4].

Rose joined Google Ventures as a General Partner in March 2012 after Google acquired his startup Milk Inc. in late 2011 [^6]. At GV, he focused on consumer internet, mobile technologies, and early cryptocurrency investments, helping back companies including Nextdoor, Ripple (Google's first crypto investment), Medium, Blue Bottle Coffee, Uber, and Slack [^4][^6]. He transitioned from general partner to venture partner in mid-2014 and left GV in January 2015 to found North Technologies, an app development lab [^6].

Rose's relationship with True Ventures dates to 2011, when the firm backed his startup Milk [^7]. True subsequently backed his ventures North (2014), Zero and Oak wellness apps (which became Zero Longevity Science in 2019), and PROOF (2022), a Web3 media company that created the Moonbirds NFT collection and was acquired by Yuga Labs in 2024 [^3][^7]. He served as a venture partner at True Ventures beginning in April 2017 and was promoted to full-time Partner on February 12, 2025 [^3][^7][^8].

Rose served as CEO of HODINKEE, a watch media and e-commerce platform, from 2019 to 2021 [^4][^5]. In March 2025, he and Reddit co-founder Alexis Ohanian re-acquired Digg, with Rose serving as Chairman and Justin Mezzell as CEO [^9][^10]. The relaunch's open beta launched January 14, 2026, but shut down two months later [^2].

Rose has been recognized as a Bloomberg "Top 25 Angel Investor," Forbes "Top 25 Web Celebrity," Time "Top 25 Most Influential People on the Web," and MIT "Top 35 Innovator Under 35," and is a five-time Webby Award winner [^5].

## Stated Thesis

Rose has publicly described his investing approach as product-centric and intuition-driven. He focuses on consumer internet, life sciences, AI, and health/wellness technologies [^3][^5].

On his investment process, Rose has written about starting with emotional intelligence: exploring an idea emotionally and following his intuition before proceeding with traditional due diligence using objective data [^11].

Rose has stated that he invests in "products he personally uses and loves" rather than theoretical opportunities [^12]. He has said: "Most of my products begin with a personal desire to use a product that doesn't exist" [^13]. This product-first philosophy extends to his investing: he backs founders who demonstrate deep craft obsession, citing James Freeman's coffee expertise at Blue Bottle Coffee and Jack Dorsey's design focus at Square as examples [^12].

At True Ventures, Rose focuses on AI innovation across health, consumer, and enterprise technologies [^7]. He has said: "I'm attracted to closed-loop systems, meaning products that produce actionable insights; these insights are then applied by the user and fed back into the system for further analysis" [^13].

On founder qualities, Rose has stated: "I like entrepreneurs with wild, novel ideas that haven't been tested yet" [^14]. He has also said: "A lot of founders will look at a very large problem and get nervous or freak out or think it's too big to take on. The best founders, they look at that as a challenge and not an obstacle" [^14].

Based on 28 verified investments in the portfolio table below, the following patterns emerge. Note: Rose claims approximately 53 total investments (per PitchBook), so this table represents roughly 53% of his known portfolio [^15].

- **Consumer products / DTC**: 3 companies (11%) — Blue Bottle Coffee, Magic Spoon, HODINKEE
- **Enterprise / productivity**: 3 companies (11%) — Uber, Slack, Clever

### Stage Distribution

Rose has primarily operated as a seed and early-stage angel investor, with check sizes of $25K-$50K for personal angel deals [^15]. At Google Ventures, he invested at Series A and beyond with institutional-sized checks. At True Ventures, the firm invests at seed and Series A with $3.8B in assets under management [^5].

### Key Patterns

- **Product-obsessed founders**: Nearly every verified investment involves a company with strong product design emphasis (Square, Blue Bottle Coffee, Oura, Endel, Medium) [^12].
- **Personal usage signal**: Rose has a documented pattern of using products before investing — he was a Digg founder who invested in other social platforms, used Oura Ring before joining the board, and built fasting/meditation apps before investing in health tech [^5][^13].
- **Consumer-first, B2B rare**: The vast majority of Rose's portfolio is consumer-facing. Enterprise investments (Slack, Clever) are exceptions, not the rule.
- **Web3 / crypto exposure**: Rose has meaningful crypto and Web3 exposure (Ripple, Solana, Art Blocks, PROOF/Moonbirds), though this represents a distinct era of focus rather than a consistent thread.
- **San Francisco Bay Area concentration**: Most portfolio companies are headquartered in the SF Bay Area, with occasional exceptions like Endel (Berlin) and Oura (Finland).
- **Co-investor patterns**: Rose frequently co-invests with True Ventures and has overlap with other prominent angels and funds including Andreessen Horowitz, Google Ventures, and Founders Fund across multiple deals.

### Notable Gaps

Rose's stated focus includes "enterprise technologies" at True Ventures [^7], but his personal portfolio shows minimal enterprise investment. His portfolio also skews heavily toward US-based companies despite occasional international bets.
