## Background

Shakil Khan is a British entrepreneur and angel investor based in London [^1][^2]. The son of first-generation immigrant parents from Pakistan, Khan grew up in a working-class environment and demonstrated early entrepreneurial drive [^3].

Khan has had a number of online businesses in the UK since 2000 [^1][^4]. He was part of the founding team at buy.at, an affiliate marketing company that was acquired by AOL [^1][^4]. He co-founded Student.com, a global student accommodation platform [^1].

Khan is best known for his close relationship with Spotify. He served as Head of Special Projects at Spotify for approximately five years and acted as personal advisor to Spotify founder and CEO Daniel Ek [^3][^4]. In this role, Khan helped Spotify navigate its early expansion, particularly in the US market [^3].

In April 2013, Khan founded CoinDesk, a bitcoin and cryptocurrency news site, during a period when Bitcoin was reaching new all-time highs [^4][^5]. He conceived and launched the project in approximately four weeks [^5].

Khan is also Co-Founder of Prima Materia, an investment firm established with Daniel Ek [^1][^6]. Prima Materia focuses on partnering with researchers, engineers, and entrepreneurs to build companies that leverage technology to address societal challenges, particularly in healthcare, clean energy, and AI [^6]. Prima Materia describes itself as "not investors but builders and active owners" [^6].

Khan has also served as an advisor to Horizons Ventures, the tech investment arm of the Li Ka-Shing Foundation [^3].

## Stated Thesis

Khan's publicly stated investment philosophy emphasizes pragmatism over ideology. In the context of bitcoin and crypto investing, he has stated: "We're investors that want to invest in solid business models" [^7].

He has been vocal about the inefficiency of traditional financial systems, asking: "Why is it in 2013 I can send you an email, a voicemail, or text message, but if someone says 'you owe me $10 for my pizza last week' and I try to send it, the bank wants to charge me $27?" [^7]. This frustration with legacy financial infrastructure motivated both his CoinDesk founding and his crypto investments [^5][^7].

On leadership and investing, Khan has stated: "Good leadership is realising that you don't know everything about everything" and emphasized the importance of hiring people smarter than yourself [^3]. He has described inner passion as essential for founders: "without that inner drive, forget about it" [^3].

Through Prima Materia, Khan and Ek take a long-term, hands-on approach, stating their goal is "to partner with exceptional people to build companies that leverage technology to help solve meaningful problems for society" [^6].
