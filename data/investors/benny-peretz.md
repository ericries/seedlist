## Background

Benjamin "Benny" Peretz is the co-founder and Managing Partner of Greenoaks Capital, a San Francisco-based investment firm he founded in 2012 alongside Neil Mehta [^1][^2]. The two started Greenoaks in what has been described as "a foul, borrowed backroom office of an insurance broker in San Francisco" [^3]. The firm is named after the street Mehta grew up on in Atherton, California [^2].

Prior to founding Greenoaks, Peretz invested in public and private businesses globally at D.E. Shaw & Co., L.P., a quantitative investment firm [^1][^4]. He also co-founded WRKSHP, described as a leading mobile gaming company [^1][^4].

Peretz holds a BSc in Economics from the Wharton School and a BAS from the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences at the University of Pennsylvania [^1][^4].

At Greenoaks, Peretz shares final investment authority with Mehta; every investment requires their unanimous agreement [^5]. The firm manages approximately $15 billion in assets with a team of roughly nine investment professionals, maintaining an unusually concentrated portfolio of approximately 55 core companies [^6][^7]. Over its first 13 years, Greenoaks has generated over $13 billion in gross profits with a 33% net IRR [^6].

Peretz has taken board seats at portfolio companies including Deliveroo (joined the board as part of the 2015 Series C) [^8] and Clover Health (joined the board as part of the 2016 Series C) [^9].

## Stated Thesis

Greenoaks describes itself as making "concentrated, long-term investments in generational teams building enduring companies" [^10]. The firm publicly focuses on identifying "generation-defining technology companies with exceptional long-term growth potential" [^7][^11].

The firm's stated approach emphasizes several principles:

- **Concentrated bets**: Greenoaks invests in approximately 15 companies per fund, making large initial investments of $25M-$75M and reserving roughly 50% of each fund for follow-on investments [^7].
- **Long-term partnership**: The firm describes itself as maintaining "decades-long partnerships" with founders, blending venture capital with value investing principles [^11].
- **Global focus**: Greenoaks targets an estimated 50% North America / 50% international exposure [^5].
- **Public equity continuation**: Unlike most venture firms, Greenoaks holds and grows public equity positions rather than exiting at IPO [^7][^12].

Peretz has been described as playing the specific role of "extending the firm's time horizon" within the partnership [^3].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 25 verified investments in the portfolio table below, the following patterns emerge. Note: Greenoaks has made approximately 114-149 total investments [^10][^13], so these 25 represent roughly 17-22% of the full portfolio. Percentages should be treated as indicative rather than definitive given this sample size.

### Sector Distribution (25 verified investments)

- **Fintech / Payments**: 5 of 25 (20%) -- Stripe, Brex, Airwallex, Tipalti, Robinhood
- **E-commerce / Marketplaces**: 4 of 25 (16%) -- Coupang, Flipkart, Deliveroo, Carvana
- **Enterprise Software / SaaS**: 4 of 25 (16%) -- Airtable, Personio, Rippling, Navan
- **Cybersecurity**: 3 of 25 (12%) -- Wiz, Cyera, Clover Health (health-tech/insurance)
- **AI / Machine Learning**: 2 of 25 (8%) -- Safe Superintelligence, Databricks
- **Developer Tools / Infrastructure**: 2 of 25 (8%) -- Figma, Scale AI
- **Consumer / Social**: 2 of 25 (8%) -- Discord, Canva
- **Other (Fleet mgmt, Hospitality, Food)**: 3 of 25 (12%) -- Motive, OYO, Toast

### Stage Distribution

Greenoaks primarily invests at Series A and Series B, with significant follow-on through later stages [^7]. The firm's most notable characteristic is its willingness to make extremely concentrated, high-conviction bets early and then continue investing through multiple rounds. The Coupang investment exemplifies this: Greenoaks invested approximately 40% of its initial $50M fund into Coupang's Series B in 2014, a position that ultimately returned approximately $8 billion [^12][^14].

### Geographic Patterns

The portfolio shows genuinely global coverage: investments span the US (San Francisco Bay Area concentration), India (Flipkart, OYO), South Korea (Coupang), UK (Deliveroo), Australia (Airwallex, Canva), Germany (Personio), Israel (Wiz, Cyera), and others. This is consistent with the firm's stated 50/50 US/international split [^5].

### Notable Gaps vs. Stated Thesis

Greenoaks claims to focus on "generation-defining" companies broadly, but the portfolio reveals a strong bias toward companies with network effects, marketplace dynamics, or infrastructure-layer positions. Pure consumer apps and hardware companies are notably absent from the verified portfolio.

### Co-investor Patterns

Frequent co-investors include Sequoia Capital (Wiz, Stripe), Index Ventures (Deliveroo, Wiz), Insight Partners (Wiz), and Lightspeed Venture Partners (Safe Superintelligence, Cyera).
