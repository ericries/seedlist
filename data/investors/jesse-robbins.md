## Background

Jesse Robbins is an American technology entrepreneur, investor, and certified firefighter best known for co-founding Chef (infrastructure automation) and pioneering chaos engineering at Amazon [^1][^2]. He is currently a General Partner at Heavybit, a developer-focused venture fund [^3].

Robbins began building technology early, constructing ISPs and wireless modems in high school, with an early company IPO in 1999 [^4]. In 2001, he joined Amazon with the manager-approved title "Master of Disaster," where he was responsible for website availability for every property bearing the Amazon brand [^1][^2]. At Amazon, he created "GameDay," a practice of deliberately creating major failures to increase system reliability — a method now known as chaos engineering [^1][^2]. Robbins has said GameDay was inspired by his experience and training as a firefighter combined with lessons from other industries and research on complex systems [^1].

After Amazon, Robbins co-founded the O'Reilly Velocity Conference with Tim O'Reilly, which catalyzed the DevOps movement [^2][^4]. In 2008, he co-founded Opscode (later Chef Software) with Adam Jacob, Barry Steinglass, and Nathan Haneysmith [^4]. Chef became a pioneer in cloud infrastructure automation, adopted by companies including Facebook, Google, Apple, and IBM [^4]. Chef was acquired by Progress Software for over $220 million in 2020 [^3][^4].

Robbins also founded Orion Labs, a voice-first AI platform for frontline teams, which he led from 2013 to 2022 [^5]. He served as Entrepreneur in Residence at Threshold Ventures (formerly DFJ) in 2013 [^5].

Robbins joined Heavybit as a part-time partner in 2016 and became a full-time General Partner in 2022, coinciding with the firm's $80 million Fund IV raise [^3][^6]. He was recognized with the MIT Technology Review TR35 award in 2011 for "transforming the way Web companies design and manage complex networks of servers and software" [^1].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Robbins says publicly about his investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Robbins' publicly stated investment philosophy is deeply informed by his operator background:

- **Founder-first investing**: Robbins invests in "relentless, unstoppable, driven founders solving a problem they know firsthand" [^4].

- **Developer experience**: He champions products that improve the developer experience and transform how people work on first use [^4].

- **Open-source go-to-market**: Robbins favors companies with open-source go-to-market strategies, consistent with his Chef background [^4].

- **"Don't fight stupid, make more awesome"**: A core Robbins principle — build demonstrably better alternatives rather than criticizing incumbents [^4].

- **Data infrastructure as competitive advantage**: Robbins advocates for enterprises to invest in continuous data management processes similar to DevOps practices, viewing robust, secure first-party datasets as "barriers to entry" for competitors [^3].

- **"Delegation is the new automation"**: Robbins views AI agents as the next computing layer, representing a fundamental shift comparable to the DevOps movement [^4].

**Key patterns:**

- **Seed-stage focus**: Robbins' investment range is $500K-$5M with a target of $2.5M, indicating primarily seed and early-stage activity [^5]. He frequently gets involved as an early angel or advisor before formal venture rounds.

- **Operator-to-investor advantage**: Many of Robbins' investments are in categories where his operational experience provides unique evaluation capability — infrastructure automation (his Chef domain), incident management (his Amazon domain), and developer workflows.

- **Strong IPO record**: Robbins claims 5 IPOs from his portfolio: PagerDuty, Instacart, Fastly, Caribou Biosciences, and Zymergen [^4]. His early angel investments in Fastly (entering at a $4.36M valuation, now ~$1.45B) and PagerDuty (entering at $28.98M) generated exceptional returns [^7].

- **Active board involvement**: Robbins serves on the boards of Sanity, Continue, Memgraph, and Mobot, and advises Tailscale, Blockdaemon, Tonic, and LaunchDarkly [^3][^4].

- **Expanding beyond DevOps**: Recent investments in Figure AI (humanoid robots), Shield AI (defense), and Eight Sleep (consumer hardware) suggest Robbins is broadening beyond pure developer tools into AI and deep tech.
