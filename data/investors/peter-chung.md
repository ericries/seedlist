## Background

Peter Y. Chung is the Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer of Summit Partners, a global growth equity firm, positions he has held since 2015 [^1][^2]. He joined Summit Partners in 1994 and focuses primarily on the technology sector [^1]. During his tenure, he has invested in more than 30 companies, including 18 currently or formerly publicly traded companies [^1][^2].

Prior to Summit, Chung worked in the Mergers & Acquisitions department of Goldman, Sachs & Co. [^1][^2]. He holds an AB in economics, magna cum laude, from Harvard University and an MBA from Stanford University Graduate School of Business [^1][^2].

Chung has been named to the Forbes Midas List multiple times [^1][^3]. He is an Emeritus Trustee of the California Academy of Sciences and serves on the Executive Board of Friends of Harvard Football and the Board of Directors of the National Park Foundation [^1][^4].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Chung and Summit Partners say publicly about their investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Summit Partners positions itself as a growth equity investor, partnering with "profitable, category-leading companies and their leadership teams seeking to build best-in-class businesses" [^2][^5]. The firm leverages its "deep industry expertise and their Peak Performance Group value enhancement platform" to support portfolio companies [^5].

Chung has discussed the evolution of the growth equity industry and Summit's 40-year focus on growth investing on the Growth Investor Podcast [^6]. Summit has invested in more than 550 companies across technology, healthcare, financial services, business services, and consumer sectors [^5].

The firm maintains over 80 growth equity professionals across offices in North America and Europe, with an investment sweet spot of approximately $20M and a range of $5M to $50M [^1][^7].

## Inferred Thesis

The analysis below is based on 15 verified investments attributed to Peter Chung at Summit Partners [^1][^2][^3][^8].

**Sector concentration (based on 15 verified investments):**
- Photonics / optical networking: 4 of 15 (27%) — E-TEK Dynamics, Finisar, Acacia Communications, Sirenza Microdevices
- Semiconductors / RF technology: 3 of 15 (20%) — MACOM, Empower RF Systems, AltoCom
- Enterprise software: 2 of 15 (13%) — GoldenGate Software, Striim
- Networking / security: 2 of 15 (13%) — A10 Networks, Ubiquiti
- Healthcare: 2 of 15 (13%) — Sound Physicians, NightHawk Radiology
- Other (insurance, data): 2 of 15 (13%) — SeaBright Insurance, Syndigo

**Key patterns:**

- **Deep technology hardware specialization**: Nearly half of Chung's portfolio (47%) is in photonics, optical networking, and semiconductor/RF technology. This is a distinctive concentration among growth equity investors and reflects deep domain expertise in physical-layer technology.

- **Massive exits in optical/photonics**: Chung's E-TEK Dynamics investment merged with JDS Uniphase in a transaction valued at over $18 billion [^1]. Finisar was acquired by Coherent, and Acacia Communications was acquired by Cisco [^1][^8]. These three exits alone represent enormous returns in the optical networking space.

- **Growth equity, not early stage**: Consistent with Summit's model, Chung invests at the growth stage — typically in companies with established revenue and seeking capital to scale, not at seed or Series A [^5][^7].

- **Public market exits dominant**: 18 of his 30+ investments resulted in publicly traded companies [^1], indicating a preference for companies with clear IPO potential. Current public company board seats include A10 Networks and MACOM [^3][^8].

- **Long tenure, concentrated portfolio**: Over 30 years at Summit, Chung has done roughly one investment per year, suggesting a highly selective approach.

- **Cross-sector diversification into healthcare**: Board positions at Sound Physicians and NightHawk Radiology (acquired by Virtual Radiologic) show strategic expansion beyond pure technology [^1].
