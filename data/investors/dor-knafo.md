## Background

Dor Knafo is a General Partner at Cyberstarts, the cybersecurity-focused venture capital firm founded by Gili Raanan in 2018 [^1]. He joined Cyberstarts in 2025, transitioning from founder to investor [^2].

Before joining Cyberstarts, Knafo co-founded and served as CEO of Axis Security, a cloud-native zero-trust network access (ZTNA) platform, alongside co-founder and CTO Gil Azrielant [^3]. Axis Security was one of Cyberstarts' earliest seed investments [^1]. Founded in 2018, Axis raised over $100 million across three rounds — a $17 million Series A led by Ten Eleven Ventures in March 2020 [^3], a $32 million Series B led by Canaan Partners in September 2020 [^4], and a $50 million Series C led by Spark Capital in March 2021 [^5]. Axis was acquired by Hewlett-Packard Enterprise (HPE) in March 2023 for a reported $500 million, though HPE stated that figure was inaccurate without providing an alternative [^6].

Prior to co-founding Axis, Knafo was a senior security researcher at Fireglass, a web isolation company later acquired by Symantec [^3]. He also presented at Black Hat Asia 2017 and the O'Reilly Security Conference 2016 alongside Fireglass founder Dan Amiga [^7][^8]. Knafo served for five years in Unit 8200 of the Israeli Defense Forces as a senior software engineer for advanced cybersecurity [^3]. He holds a B.Sc. in Computer Science from IDC Herzliya (now Reichman University), graduating cum laude [^3].

## Stated Thesis

Knafo has publicly stated that he brings "a deep conviction that the most impactful cybersecurity companies are built by technical founders who can make the complex feel inevitable" [^1]. He describes himself as "especially drawn to entrepreneurs who not only understand the architecture of modern cyber threats but can clearly articulate the precise problem their technology is solving" [^1].

On his Cyberstarts profile, he is quoted as saying: "My decisions usually start with instinct. The good ones end there too" [^1].

As a founder, Knafo was an advocate for zero-trust security and replacing legacy VPN and remote access technologies, stating: "The time is now to replace legacy access technologies such as VPNs, virtual desktop infrastructure (VDI) and inline cloud access security broker (CASB) services using a single zero trust cloud platform" [^3].
