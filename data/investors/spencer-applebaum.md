## Background

Spencer Applebaum is a General Partner and Co-Head of Venture at Multicoin Capital, a thesis-driven crypto investment firm based in Austin, Texas [^1]. He was promoted to this role alongside Shayon Sengupta in March 2026, having already been "operating at the GP level for some time" [^1]. Applebaum has been with Multicoin for over eight years, making him one of the firm's first hires [^1] [^2].

Applebaum studied accounting and economics at Miami University, where he founded the school's first student organization dedicated to advancing blockchain technology education [^3] [^4]. Before joining Multicoin, he interned at Bitspark, a blockchain remittance platform in Hong Kong, where he assisted with the development of Zephyr, a pegged cryptocurrency solution on the BitShares DEX [^2] [^4]. He also worked as a Multistate Tax Intern at Deloitte [^2] [^4].

Applebaum joined Multicoin Capital as an Analyst in March 2018 and progressed through Associate (2020), Principal (2022), and Investment Partner (March 2023) before his promotion to General Partner in March 2026 [^5] [^6] [^1]. He co-founded Drift Labs in 2021, the entity behind the Drift Protocol decentralized derivatives exchange on Solana [^7]. He was named to the Forbes 30 Under 30 list in 2025 [^7].

He specializes in DeFi protocols, DePIN infrastructure, and crypto-consumer applications, and also contributes to public markets thesis formation and asset coverage [^2].

## Stated Thesis

Applebaum co-authored Multicoin Capital's updated investment thesis in February 2026 alongside Tushar Jain and Shayon Sengupta, which describes the firm's foundational belief that "blockchains are the first-principles-correct technology to move money, coordinate and program capital formation, and power global financial markets" [^8].

The thesis outlines eight investment themes: Fintech 4.0 (stablecoins enabling programmable payments), DeFi Mullet (value capture across the DeFi stack), Financial Globalization, More Efficient Borrow/Lend, Entertainment Finance, Programmable Ownership, Credibly-Neutral Blockchains, and Cryptographic Primitives [^8].

In a December 2025 essay co-authored with Eli Qian, Applebaum argued that fintech is entering a fourth phase driven by stablecoins: previous waves (digital distribution, neobanking, embedded finance) merely layered software atop legacy banking infrastructure without changing how money moves, while stablecoins replace traditional banking functions with open, programmable networks [^9].

Applebaum has also articulated a framework for DeFi token value capture, arguing in a September 2021 essay co-authored with Kyle Samani that DeFi tokens can only justify value capture if they manage and backstop risk in their systems, while tokens that merely extract fees without managing risk are vulnerable to being forked out by competitors [^10].
