## Background

Scott Belsky is an American entrepreneur, author, executive, and prolific seed-stage angel investor [^1][^2]. He co-founded Behance in 2006, the leading creative portfolio platform that grew to serve 55+ million professionals before Adobe acquired it in 2012 [^1][^2]. He holds a bachelor's degree from Cornell University and an MBA from Harvard Business School [^2].

After the acquisition, Belsky served as VP of Products, Mobile and Community at Adobe [^2]. He returned to Adobe in 2017 and rose to Chief Product Officer and Executive Vice President of Creative Cloud, later serving as Chief Strategy Officer and EVP of Design & Emerging Products [^1][^2]. At Adobe, he oversaw the transformation bringing Adobe products to mobile and web, launched AI foundational products, and created the open protocol for content credentials [^2].

Belsky has been an active seed-stage angel investor since 2010, with 242-264 portfolio companies on record across various tracking platforms [^3][^4]. His notable early investments include seed rounds in Pinterest, Uber, Warby Parker, Sweetgreen, Airtable, Ramp, and Periscope [^1][^2][^5]. He holds board seats at Atlassian, the Museum of Modern Art, and COOP Careers [^2].

In January 2025, Belsky joined A24 (the independent film and entertainment studio) as a partner, overseeing the studio's technology and innovation initiatives, and founded A24 Labs [^2]. He was listed on Business Insider's "Best Early-Stage Investors" list in 2025 and was named to Fast Company's "100 Most Creative People in Business" in 2010 [^2][^2].

Belsky is also an author, having written "Making Ideas Happen" (2010) and "The Messy Middle" (2018), and hosted the 99U conference for a decade [^2]. He publishes "Implications," a newsletter for product leaders and designers [^2].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Belsky says publicly about his investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Belsky describes himself as an "operangeltrepreneur" — an operator-angel-entrepreneur hybrid — focused on early-stage companies since 2010 [^5]. He publicly states his focus areas as "consumer, marketplace, and transformation by interface" [^5][^6].

He has stated that he seeks "mission-driven teams that value design" and exceptional products [^5]. His investment philosophy emphasizes teams solving problems through empowerment, empathy-driven motivation, and realistic execution optimism [^5].

Belsky has articulated key principles about how he works with founders: "The best investors know better than to give out advice. They pose the right questions at the right time" [^7]. He has also said: "The only way to help someone out in these moments is to ask questions that the entrepreneur might not have thought about" [^7].

On opportunities, Belsky has stated: "Great opportunities never have 'great opportunity' in the subject line" [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

The analysis below is based on portfolio data from Belsky's own website, Crunchbase (242 portfolio companies), and Mercury's investor database [^3][^4][^5][^6]. Because Belsky is an angel investor making small checks ($50K-$100K) across a very large number of companies, his portfolio is unusually broad.

**Sector concentration (based on categorized portfolio from scottbelsky.com, covering 80+ named companies) [^5]:**
- Enterprise/Dev/Consumer Tech: The largest category, including Airtable, Clay, Supabase, Notion, Replit, Roam, Bubble, Carta — estimated 30%+ of named portfolio
- Fintech: Ramp, Mercury, Public, Runway Financial — significant representation
- Consumer Brands: Blank Street, Liquid Death, Sanzo, Primary
- Health Tech: Whoop, Eight Sleep, Ro, Papa
- Climate Tech: KoBold, Living Carbon, Pachama

*Note: With 240+ total investments and only ~80 named on his website, precise percentages would be misleading. The pattern is clearly skewed toward design-forward software companies and consumer products.*

**Stage distribution:** Almost exclusively pre-seed and seed stage. Mercury's investor database lists pre-seed, seed, and Series A as his stages [^6]. He does not lead rounds [^6].

**Check size:** $50K-$100K per investment [^6]. This is consistent with a high-volume angel approach — many small bets across a wide range of companies.

**Geographic patterns:** Belsky invests globally but with strong representation in New York City and San Francisco, consistent with his base in New York and Adobe/tech connections in the Bay Area [^6].

**Co-investor patterns:** As a small-check angel, Belsky typically invests alongside institutional seed funds rather than leading. His portfolio overlaps significantly with other design-oriented angels and seed funds.

**Founder profile patterns:** Strong bias toward founders with design sensibility and product-first approaches. Companies like Airtable, Notion, Replit, and Figma (via his design network) reflect a pattern of backing tools that empower creators and builders.

**Notable exits:** Pinterest (IPO), Uber (IPO), Warby Parker (IPO), Sweetgreen (IPO), Reddit (IPO), Periscope (acquired by Twitter) [^5].

**Key observation — design as a lens:** Belsky's stated thesis about "transformation by interface" is well-supported by his actual portfolio. The majority of his named investments are products where exceptional design and user experience are core competitive advantages, not just nice-to-haves. This is a genuine differentiator from most angel portfolios.
