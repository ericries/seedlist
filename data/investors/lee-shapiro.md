## Background

Lee Shapiro is Co-Founder and Managing Partner of 7wire Ventures, a Chicago-based venture capital firm focused exclusively on digital health that he co-founded with Glen Tullman in 2013 [^1][^2]. Shapiro and Tullman had previously worked together for more than 30 years across more than 25 companies [^3].

Before entering venture capital, Shapiro had an extensive career spanning law, real estate, banking, and healthcare technology. He earned a BS in Accounting from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign and a JD from the University of Chicago Law School [^4][^5]. He is both a certified public accountant and licensed attorney [^5]. He began his career practicing commercial law at Barack, Ferrazzano, Kirschbaum, Perlman & Nagelberg in Chicago [^4]. He subsequently served as Vice Chairman of City Financial Bancorp and President of SES Properties, Inc. [^4]. He then became Chief Operating Officer of Douglas Elliman-Beitler, a commercial office management and development company, where he directed all business activities throughout the U.S. [^4].

Shapiro joined Allscripts Healthcare Solutions in April 2000 and served as President from 2001 through 2012 [^3][^6]. During his tenure, he oversaw the company's strategy, international operations, business development and partnerships, and was central to over $4 billion in mergers, acquisitions, and financings [^3][^4]. After departing Allscripts in late 2012, he and Tullman devoted their attention to investing through 7wire Ventures [^2].

Beyond his role at 7wire, Shapiro served as Chief Financial Officer of Livongo Health from December 2018 to November 2020 [^3][^7]. Prior to stepping in as CFO, he had served on Livongo's board since its launch in 2013, chairing the audit committee and serving on the compensation committee [^3]. As CFO, he led Livongo's 2019 IPO, a successful secondary offering, a $550 million convertible debt raise, and the company's $18.5 billion merger agreement with Teladoc Health — the highest valuation of any healthcare technology company based on revenue multiples at the time [^3][^7]. The share price of $159 at the time of the merger was over six times the IPO price just 12 months earlier [^3].

Shapiro began a two-year term as Chairperson of the American Heart Association's National Board of Directors on July 1, 2025, after previously serving as Audit Committee Chair and Treasurer [^5]. He had joined the AHA around 2012 through the Qualcomm Life Advisory Council [^5]. He has run 18 marathons, including the Boston Marathon [^5].

## Stated Thesis

Shapiro publicly describes 7wire's investment focus as supporting "an Informed Connected Health Consumer" — the belief that technology and technology-enabled services can empower individuals to make smarter health and care decisions [^1][^8].

At the founding of 7wire, Shapiro stated: "We founded 7wire on the concept of disrupting the status quo — supporting new ideas, technologies, approaches, and business models for solving society's most important problems" [^2].

In a 2024 StartUp Health Masterclass, Shapiro emphasized that understanding healthcare business fundamentals is non-negotiable for founders: "If you don't have an understanding of reimbursement, or how doctors are paid, or what it takes to get integrated into a practice, you're going to have trouble" [^9]. He also stressed the importance of unit economics over top-line growth: "Not every dollar of revenue is the same. What's the margin on that dollar?" [^9].

Shapiro has described 7wire as a relationship-driven investor, stating: "Building relationships takes time; it's a little like dating" and "At 7wireVentures we won't invest in the company unless we've gone out to dinner with a founder at least once if not multiple times" [^9].

## Inferred Thesis

Lee Shapiro's investment behavior is essentially identical to 7wire Ventures' overall thesis — he is not a side-bet investor or angel with a separate portfolio. His investments are made through the 7wire fund, and he personally sits on many portfolio company boards. Based on 7wire's 28 verified portfolio investments (see firm profile for full analysis):

**Sector breakdown (28 verified investments):**
- Chronic disease management / virtual care: 8 of 28 (29%) — Livongo, Transcarent, Parsley Health, Zerigo Health, WellTheory, CirrusMD, Medisafe, Override
- Mental / behavioral health: 4 of 28 (14%) — NOCD, Brightline, Jasper Health, Caraway
- Care navigation / population health: 4 of 28 (14%) — GroundGame.Health, ConsejoSano, Yaro, Lightbeam
- Caregiving / aging in place: 2 of 28 (7%) — Homethrive
- Health insurance / benefits: 2 of 28 (7%) — When Insurance, PayZen
- Other digital health: 8 of 28 (29%) — MedArrive, FOLX Health, CareDox, Ayogo, EosHealth, ZestHealth, Truvian Sciences, Higi

100% of investments are in healthcare. The firm has zero investments outside of health-related sectors [^8].

**Stage distribution:** Primarily seed and Series A for initial investments, with strong follow-on through Series B and beyond. The 2023 $217M Growth & Opportunity Fund was specifically designed for later-stage follow-on and new Series B/C investments [^10].

**Distinctive pattern — the "Hatch" model:** Shapiro personally co-created multiple portfolio companies through 7wire's proprietary incubation program, including Livongo, Transcarent, Homethrive, and Caraway [^11][^12]. Homethrive specifically came from Shapiro's personal experience caring for his aging mother [^13]. This blurs the line between investor and founder — Shapiro is an operator-investor who builds companies, not just funds them.

**Board engagement:** Shapiro personally serves or has served on the boards of Override Health, FOLX Health, Jasper Health, PayZen, Transcarent, Click Therapeutics, Clover Health, Medidata Solutions, and Tivity Health, among others [^5][^14][^4][^15]. This is an unusually high level of direct board involvement for a managing partner.

**Co-investor patterns:** General Catalyst is a key co-investor, particularly through the Livongo and Transcarent partnership (Hemant Taneja co-hatched Transcarent with Tullman and Shapiro) [^12]. The firm's Connected Consumer Health Coalition — strategic LPs including Cigna, Memorial Hermann, Rush, Florida Blue, and Boeing — provides proprietary deal flow and distribution advantages [^1].

**Geographic concentration:** Chicago-based firm with portfolio companies distributed across the U.S., with notable concentration in Illinois and California.

**Founder profile preferences:** The firm targets founders addressing specific underserved populations — LGBTQIA+ community (FOLX Health), Spanish-speaking communities (ConsejoSano), children and teens (Brightline, CareDox), seniors (Homethrive), and people with specific conditions like OCD (NOCD) [^8].
