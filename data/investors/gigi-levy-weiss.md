## Background

Gigi Levy-Weiss (born 1971) is an Israeli serial entrepreneur, venture capitalist, and co-founder of NFX, a seed-stage venture firm managing approximately $1.5 billion in assets [^1][^2]. He serves as General Partner and heads NFX's Israel operations from Rishpon, Israel [^3].

Levy-Weiss served as an attack helicopter pilot in the Israeli Air Force before pursuing higher education [^4][^5]. He earned his MBA from the Kellogg Graduate School of Management at Northwestern University in 2004 [^4][^5].

His corporate career includes serving as Division President for Western Europe, Central and Latin America at Amdocs (NYSE: DOX), a billing and CRM provider, from 2005 to 2006 [^3][^6]. He then served as CEO of 888 Holdings (LSE: 888.L), one of the world's leading online gaming companies, from 2006 to 2011 [^3][^6].

As a founder, Levy-Weiss co-founded Playtika in 2010, the digital entertainment company that later went public on NASDAQ (ticker: PLTK) [^3][^4]. He also founded Beach Bum (acquired by Voodoo), InceptionVR, and Ridge (FKA Tectonic) [^3]. In 2025, he co-founded Line 5, a defense-tech startup, alongside Redis co-founder Yiftach Shoolman, raising a $20 million seed round [^7].

In 2015, Levy-Weiss co-founded NFX with James Currier, Pete Flint, and Stan Chudnovsky [^4][^8]. Prior to NFX, he was an active angel investor in Israeli tech startups, with investments including Global-e, Plarium, MyHeritage, Kenshoo, and others [^3][^6].

Levy-Weiss was appointed to Facebook's EMEA Client Advisory Council in 2014 and joined the supervisory board of Bertelsmann in 2015 [^3][^4]. He has been named top investor in Israel by Geektime and The Marker [^3].

## Stated Thesis

Levy-Weiss publicly describes NFX's investment focus as centered on businesses "that have — or want to have — network effects in their core," spanning any sector (consumer, SMB, enterprise, fintech, healthcare, govtech) and any platform (web, mobile, AR/VR, blockchain, AI, robotics) [^9].

On founder selection, Levy-Weiss has stated he looks for "high-agency, calm-under-pressure founders" with a "data-obsessed, cohort-focused, experiment-driven, AI-first" approach and "ultra-lean operational efficiency" [^5]. He has emphasized that "speed is one of the main character traits founders should have, whilst being willing to accept failure" [^10].

Levy-Weiss and NFX employ a "Ladder of Proof" framework to evaluate startups, assessing progress across six sequential rungs from problem validation through defensibility via network effects or proprietary technology [^11]. He has stated: "The KPIs you have should always aim to move you up the next rung in the Ladder of Proof" [^11].

On data rigor, Levy-Weiss has articulated a hard line: "If they don't have a daily report or tell me it's going to take a few days, the conversation is over" [^11]. He has noted that NFX is currently focused on "the tools layer and on applications" within AI, looking for "profit margins like in the software field, that is, over 60%" and "unique defensibility, through proprietary data, distribution, or differentiated technology" [^12].

Regarding geography, he has stated: "We believe Israeli founders have the speed, resilience and vision to lead the AI era, and we are here to back them" and that NFX deploys approximately 40% of capital to Israeli startups with the remainder primarily to US-based companies [^2].

## Inferred Thesis

The following analysis is based on 28 verified investments from the portfolio table below. Levy-Weiss has reportedly made 177 investments (per Signal by NFX) [^9], meaning this sample represents approximately 16% of his total portfolio. Percentages should be interpreted as reflecting the publicly notable portion of his investments, not the full distribution.

### Sector Distribution (28 verified investments)

- **Gaming**: 7 of 28 (25%) — Playtika, Plarium, Beach Bum, Papaya Gaming, Ace Games, ACID Labs, Moon Active
- **Marketplaces / Consumer**: 5 of 28 (18%) — HoneyBook, A.Team, Incredible Health, Aligned, Bizzabo
- **SaaS / Enterprise**: 4 of 28 (14%) — Komodor, Akooda, Global-e, Kenshoo (Skai)
- **AI**: 3 of 28 (11%) — Hightouch, Darrow, Akooda (also counted under SaaS)
- **Fintech / Payments**: 2 of 28 (7%) — Marqeta, Alterya
- **Biotech / Health**: 2 of 28 (7%) — Mammoth Biosciences, MyHeritage
- **Crypto / Web3**: 2 of 28 (7%) — Anchorage Digital, Alterya (also counted under fintech)
- **Defense Tech**: 1 of 28 (4%) — Line 5
- **Other (AdTech, Consumer)**: 2 of 28 (7%) — Eyeview, SweetIM

### Stage Distribution

Based on the verified portfolio, Levy-Weiss invests almost exclusively at pre-seed and seed stages through NFX, with a stated investment range of $500K to $5M and a sweet spot of $2.5M [^9]. His earlier angel investments (pre-2015) were also early-stage.

### Geographic Concentration

The portfolio skews heavily toward Israel-founded companies. Of 28 verified investments, at least 18 (64%) are Israel-founded or Israel-headquartered companies. The remainder are primarily US-based. This is consistent with his role heading NFX Israel and his stated commitment to the Israeli ecosystem [^2].

### Co-investor Patterns

Through NFX, Levy-Weiss frequently co-invests with the other NFX general partners (James Currier, Pete Flint, Morgan Beller). Notable external co-investors appearing across portfolio companies include Andreessen Horowitz (ACID Labs), Actera Group (Ace Games), and various Israeli-focused funds.

### Notable Patterns Not in Stated Thesis

- **Gaming overweight**: While NFX's stated thesis is sector-agnostic (focused on network effects), Levy-Weiss's personal portfolio reveals a strong gaming bias (25% of verified investments), reflecting his background as Playtika co-founder and 888 Holdings CEO.
- **Israel-heavy allocation**: Despite NFX's San Francisco headquarters, Levy-Weiss's deal flow is predominantly Israeli, consistent with his role heading NFX Israel.
- **Operator-founder preference**: Many of his portfolio founders come from operational backgrounds at scaled companies, mirroring his own career trajectory from military to corporate to founding.
- **Defense tech emergence**: The 2025 founding of Line 5 signals a newer investment interest in defense technology, a sector not previously associated with NFX or Levy-Weiss.

## In Their Own Words

"For a startup, understanding VCs' mindsets is often as important as understanding the mindsets of your customers. With no capital most Founders will not win, so you'd better learn the language." — Gigi Levy-Weiss, NFX essay "How VCs Think" [^18].

"We don't get the luxury of investing in things because we want them to succeed. We need to invest in things because we think they're going to succeed." — Gigi Levy-Weiss, Boundaryless Podcast, July 2022 [^19].

"Games is actually not just part of the same continuum but actually, it's a lot more than that. Games is maybe the most extreme and pure form of Internet businesses." — Gigi Levy-Weiss, Boundaryless Podcast, July 2022 [^19].

"Most Founders I meet with think they're data-driven. In reality, few are." — Gigi Levy-Weiss, NFX essay "How VCs See Your KPIs" [^11].

"The era of just saying in a presentation, 'I have AI inside me,' will no longer be enough to raise money." — Gigi Levy-Weiss, CTech interview, 2025 [^12].

"As investors — they should be grateful to have been chosen by the founders to join their journey. The founders are generally brilliant, hungry, energetic and sacrificing a lot. It's not 'our' company — it's theirs." — Gigi Levy-Weiss, The Human Founder podcast [^10].

"We are seeing the best of the best in Israel and Silicon Valley, and we are super bullish about the Israeli ecosystem." — Gigi Levy-Weiss, Times of Israel, 2024 [^2].
