## Background

Nikolay (Nik) Storonsky is a British-Russian entrepreneur, the co-founder and CEO of Revolut (valued at $45 billion with over 60 million users across 39 countries), and the founder of QuantumLight, an AI-driven venture capital and growth equity firm [^1][^2].

Storonsky was born on July 21, 1984, in Dolgoprudny, a town approximately 20 kilometers north of Moscow [^3]. He graduated from Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology (MIPT) with a Master of Science in General and Applied Physics, and earned a Master of Arts in Applied Economics and Finance from the New Economic School [^3]. He relocated to the United Kingdom in 2004 and later acquired British citizenship [^3].

Storonsky began his career as an equity derivatives trader at Lehman Brothers from 2006 to 2008, and subsequently at Credit Suisse until 2013 [^3][^4]. His experience with the inefficiencies of international financial transactions inspired the concept for Revolut. He founded Revolut in December 2013, investing approximately £300,000 of his own savings [^3]. He recruited Vlad Yatsenko, a former Deutsche Bank software engineer, as co-founder and CTO. The app launched publicly in July 2015 [^3].

Prior to founding QuantumLight, Storonsky made personal angel investments in fintech companies, including Swedish open banking vendor Tink (participating in its Series D in January 2019; Tink was later acquired by Visa for $2.1 billion in June 2021) [^5]. His net worth is approximately $4 billion according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index [^1].

In 2022-2023, Storonsky founded QuantumLight, a London-based venture capital firm that uses a proprietary AI platform called Aleph to source and evaluate investments [^1][^2]. The firm closed its inaugural fund at $250 million (hard cap), with Storonsky personally allocating approximately $60 million [^2][^6].

## Stated Thesis

Storonsky has publicly described QuantumLight's ambition as building "the world's best systematic venture capital and growth equity firm" [^6]. The firm positions itself as "the first truly systematic venture capital and growth equity firm," using algorithmic and AI-driven methods rather than human judgment for deal sourcing [^1][^2].

QuantumLight targets startups across AI, Web3, fintech, SaaS, and healthtech sectors [^7]. Storonsky has stated that Aleph allows QuantumLight to find promising businesses while avoiding a "crows mentality" that often comes from "human judgement" [^7].

Ilya Kondrashov, QuantumLight's CEO, has stated: "We just believe that machines are able to do it better. Not only do they have perfect memories, they are also not swayed by emotion, by fear of missing out on a certain hyped deal" [^7].

In addition to capital, QuantumLight provides a "Scale-up Operating System" — a framework for scaling tech businesses derived from Revolut's growth playbook, shared with founders through published playbooks on topics like hiring and performance management [^6][^8].
