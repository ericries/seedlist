## Background

Chris Pitchford is the co-founder and CEO of Brev, a San Francisco-based AI startup that raised a $3.3M pre-seed round in April 2026 led by Resolute Ventures, with participation from shuckerVC, Duro VC, Gaingels, and FOG Ventures [^1][^2]. He founded Brev in early 2024 with co-founder Vic Hu, a former engineering manager at Indeed who also worked at Meta, Iterative Health, and Panopto [^3].

Pitchford is a two-time founder originally from Toronto who is now based in Seattle/San Francisco [^4]. He was previously a co-founder and VP of Go-to-Market at Shyft, a mobile workforce-scheduling startup that went through Techstars Seattle in 2016, raised $1.5M during the program (and $10M total to date), and signed customers including Gap, Walmart, and L Brands [^4][^5]. After Shyft, he was the opening sales hire at OKR-platform Ally.io and rose to VP of Sales; following Microsoft's late-2021 acquisition of Ally.io he served as Vice President of Sales at Microsoft, where the product became Viva Goals (later discontinued in 2025) [^4][^6]. He subsequently served as CRO-in-Residence at Techstars Seattle, mentoring founders on go-to-market strategy, repeatable sales processes, and business-relationship negotiations [^4]. Earlier in his career, he was an Enterprise Account Executive at Rogers Communications [^6].

Brev's product is positioned as "the AI-native layer between business goals and work" — agents that join standups and business reviews to track progress, surface risk, and update goals across systems like Slack, Jira, and Salesforce. Early customers include operations leaders at Flex, Patlytics, Unified, and RecordPoint [^1][^2].

Pitchford does not publish a formal investing thesis and is primarily an operator at this stage of his career.
