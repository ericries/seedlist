## Background

Byron Knight is President and Chief Operating Officer of Koch Disruptive Technologies (KDT), the venture capital and growth equity arm of Koch, Inc. [^1][^2]. He holds a Bachelor of Industrial Engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology [^3]. Prior to KDT, Knight spent more than a decade at Georgia-Pacific (a Koch subsidiary), most recently serving as Vice President of eCommerce for Georgia-Pacific's North America Consumer Business; earlier in his career he was an early employee of TechDiscovery, a technical due diligence and custom software firm [^1][^4].

Knight joined KDT in its first years of operation. As of January 2020, Global Corporate Venturing reported he had been at KDT for "less than two years" with over 14 years total at Koch Industries, having personally deployed nearly $300M and led four of KDT's eight major investments to that point [^4]. He was named to the Global Corporate Venturing Rising Stars list in 2020 [^4].

In April 2024, Knight was elevated to President of KDT when founder Chase Koch transitioned to Executive Vice President of Origination and Partnerships at Koch, Inc. [^5][^6]. By that point Knight had spearheaded approximately 20 investments across supply chain and manufacturing, cybersecurity, and fintech [^1][^2]. He delivered the Distinguished Speaker keynote at Wichita State University's W. Frank Barton School of Business on October 23, 2024, titled "Harnessing Disruption: Pioneering the Future of Innovation and Growth in the Business World" [^2]. He is based in Wichita, Kansas [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Knight has publicly described KDT's mission as investing "in transformative technology companies that disrupt the traditional paradigms and provide impactful solutions to make the world a better place" [^7]. He frames KDT's role as "to invest in technology companies that disrupt and transform Koch's core businesses and expand into new platforms and capabilities" [^8].

He emphasizes a founder-first orientation grounded in Koch's operating platform rather than just capital: "Our approach of putting these disruptive innovators first and figuring out what we can bring to the table -- to pour rocket fuel on their ideas -- is what really gets our team excited" [^4]. Knight stresses that KDT seeks "win-win opportunities with our partners to create transformative value for society" [^9].

On supply chain and Industry 4.0 -- the sector he has most consistently led for KDT -- Knight states: "There's tremendous opportunity -- and a demonstrated need -- to address the most pressing challenges in the supply chain" [^10]. He has framed KDT's logistics strategy around three areas: yard automation (Outrider), warehouse robotics (Gideon Brothers), and middle-mile autonomy (Gatik) [^10].

## Inferred Thesis

Knight has been at KDT since approximately 2018, ascended to President in April 2024, and concurrently serves as COO [^1][^4][^5]. Two distinct signals are available: (a) deals where Knight is personally quoted in the announcement (strongest signal of personal sourcing), and (b) board seats he personally holds at portfolio companies (strongest signal of ongoing oversight). Both pattern toward supply chain, cybersecurity, fintech, and industrial software -- the verticals KDT and external profiles consistently associate with him [^1][^2][^4][^11].

**Sectors where Knight is personally named or board-attached** (8 verifiable companies):
- Supply chain / autonomy: Outrider (board, lead quote on $62M Series D Oct 2024 [^11]); Trackonomy (board [^1]); Gatik (KDT-led Series B 2021 [^12])
- Cybersecurity: Dragos (board, lead quote on $200M Series D Oct 2021 [^13]); Automox (board [^1]); Strider (board [^3]); Beyond Identity (former board [^14])
- Fintech: Ibotta (board, lead quote on $200M Series D Aug 2019 [^9])
- Healthcare: Cellares (board [^8])
- Manufacturing/Industry 4.0: Desktop Metal (led $150M Series D, former board [^4])
- Enterprise software / AI: Rhino.ai (lead quote on $50M Series A Jan 2025 [^7])
- Semiconductors / optical: Lucidean (KDT co-lead seed Dec 2025 [^15])

**Sector tilt vs. Chase Koch:** Of the 8 companies where Knight is personally named or board-attached, the distribution skews industrial-tech: 3 supply chain (38%), 4 cybersecurity (50% across overlapping seats), 1 fintech (Ibotta), 1 healthcare (Cellares), 1 industrial manufacturing (Desktop Metal), 1 enterprise AI (Rhino.ai), 1 semiconductors (Lucidean). Chase Koch's personally quoted deals concentrated in healthcare and growth-stage life sciences (Insightec, Valo Health) plus marquee industrials (Gatik, Sidecar Health) [^16]. Knight's pattern leans more heavily toward cybersecurity and supply-chain automation -- the operationally-adjacent verticals where Koch Industries is itself a deployment customer.

**Stage distribution:** Knight's named deals span Series A through Series D, with check sizes from KDT's typical $50M-$150M range up to the $200M Dragos co-lead [^13]. The December 2025 Lucidean $18M seed co-led with Entrada Ventures [^15] -- Knight's first publicly attributed seed-stage deal as President -- suggests willingness to write smaller, earlier checks for strategically-relevant deep tech under his leadership.

**Distinctive pattern -- Koch as customer:** Knight personally articulated the dual investor-customer relationship for Dragos: "We believe the Dragos Platform plays a key part in managing risk across our enterprise, delivering the products and services our customers depend on without interruption" [^13]. This pattern (KDT invests; Koch deploys) reflects the operating thesis Knight inherited from Chase but applies most heavily in his own cybersecurity and supply-chain bets.

**Sample size caveat:** This analysis is based on 8 deals where Knight is personally quoted or holds a board seat. KDT under his presidency has deployed across ~70 portfolio companies, but personal attribution to Knight per deal is verifiable only for the subset above. For the firm-wide portfolio see the Koch Disruptive Technologies firm profile.
