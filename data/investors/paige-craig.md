## Background

Paige Craig is the Founder and Managing Partner of Outlander VC, an early-stage venture capital firm [^1]. He grew up in poverty — by his own account, his family "lived in the back of a car" and were "dirt poor" [^2]. His father instilled a strong belief in education and reading despite the difficult circumstances [^2].

Craig attended West Point beginning in 1992 but left in 1995 and enlisted in the United States Marine Corps, where he served in intelligence roles and was promoted to sergeant before leaving the service in 2000 [^3][^4]. He earned a Bachelor's degree in Management Information Systems from the University of Maryland and an MBA from National University [^3].

After the invasion of Iraq in 2003, Craig drove alone into Baghdad with $10,000 and founded the Lincoln Group, a private military and strategic communications firm that won Pentagon contracts for psychological operations [^3][^5]. He expanded operations across the Middle East, Afghanistan, Pakistan, Africa, and Southeast Asia, growing the company to $300 million in revenue within four years [^5][^5]. He sold his share of the Lincoln Group in 2007 and moved to Los Angeles in 2008 [^3][^4].

Craig began angel investing in April 2009, making over 70 personal investments through 2015, including early bets on Lyft, AngelList, Wish, Postmates, Twitter, Gusto, and Scale AI [^6][^7][^8]. In 2010, he co-founded BetterWorks, an employee recognition and rewards platform, serving as CEO until 2012 when the company folded [^3][^9]. He launched Arena Ventures in January 2015 with Jeffrey Lo, raising over $27 million from 35 investors [^4][^10]. He co-founded Outlander VC in 2020, where he currently serves as Founder and Managing Partner [^1][^7].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Craig says publicly about his investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Craig describes Outlander as a "founder first" firm: "We are founder first, it means two things... we really understand that we see startups from the end of a straw" [^2]. He has stated: "People are the most important thing" and that most VCs "don't actually look at people" [^2].

Outlander uses a proprietary 38-point "Founder Framework" that evaluates founders across four categories: Vision, Intelligence, Character, and Execution [^11]. Craig has said: "We dig into your life from middle school, even elementary school onward" and "we really don't care that much about reference calls. We really look at life and talk to you about your life" [^2].

On the types of founders he targets, Craig has stated: "We are looking for extremely unique people who are highly inclined to build fast growing highly scalable tech companies" [^2]. He specifically seeks "unknown, under networked, underrepresented" founders rather than "hot deals" [^2], and has said Outlander targets "ideas from innovators that have historically been marginalized in the investment industry, women and people of color" [^5].

On sectors, Craig has described Outlander's next decade focus as "Robotics, industry, autonomy and deep tech" along with "AI and software embedded in a robot that does agriculture, does construction, does healthcare" [^2]. The firm describes itself as "category agnostic" but avoids video games, crypto, medical devices, and physical products [^12].

Craig invests at the $3M-$10M valuation range and looks for companies "exiting at Five to $30 billion or greater" [^2].

## Inferred Thesis

The analysis below is based on 28 verified investments from Craig's angel portfolio and Outlander VC combined, drawn from Signal NFX, Crunchbase, press coverage, and Outlander's own publications [^1][^5][^6][^7][^8][^13][^14].

**Sector concentration (based on 28 verified investments):**
- Marketplaces / consumer platforms: 9 of 28 (32%) — Lyft, Postmates, Wish, Couchsurfing, StyleSeat, CourseHorse, Headout, Omaze, Rinse
- Enterprise SaaS / productivity: 5 of 28 (18%) — Gusto, AngelList, Mattermark, Livefyre, Qualified
- AI / deep tech: 3 of 28 (11%) — Scale AI, Imbue, Prevoty
- Fintech / payments: 3 of 28 (11%) — Dwolla, ID.me, Bird Rides
- Social / media: 3 of 28 (11%) — Twitter, Plain Vanilla (QuizUp), Mobile Roadie
- HR / workforce: 2 of 28 (7%) — Andela, Scripted
- Other (consumer brands, edtech): 3 of 28 (11%) — Plated, Digital Brands Group, One Month

**Key patterns:**

- **Marketplace-heavy portfolio**: Despite the stated focus on deep tech and robotics for Outlander's future, Craig's historical portfolio is dominated by marketplace and consumer platform companies (32%). This is consistent with his early LA-centric angel investing era when he saw "great potential in LA — the massive creative class, diversity and hustle" [^10].

- **True early-stage investor**: Craig's angel investments were typically $50K-$250K at the earliest stages [^7]. Under Outlander, initial checks range from $500K to $2M in pre-seed and seed rounds [^12]. He has described investing "in these companies that three to $10 million evaluations" [^2].

- **Founder-over-thesis investing**: Craig has stated "The false negatives are far more damaging than false positives at this stage" [^2], and the portfolio's sector diversity reflects this approach — he backs founders he finds exceptional regardless of category, rather than concentrating in a narrow thesis.

- **Prolific angel turned institutional**: Craig made over 70 personal angel investments from 2009 to 2015 [^6], transitioned through Arena Ventures ($27M fund, 2015) [^4], and is now raising Outlander Magellan, a $250M third fund [^5][^12]. The progression shows a substantial scaling of check sizes and ambition.

- **Strong unicorn hit rate**: Craig claims a 10% unicorn rate across his portfolio versus the 2% global average [^5]. Verified unicorns from his investments include Lyft, Wish, Gusto, Scale AI, and ID.me [^7][^13][^14].

- **Rapid decision-making**: Craig has described committing to investments after just 2 hours and stated "A week with a new team is a lot of time" [^6]. He has said the best seed investors are hunters: "I'm naturally a hunter. I love going out to hackathons and house parties" [^6].

- **Co-investor network**: Craig is connected to 183 investors on Signal NFX, including James Currier (NFX), Satya Patel (Homebrew), and Aileen Lee (Cowboy Ventures) [^7].
