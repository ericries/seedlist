## Background

Mark Goldberg is the Co-Founder and Managing Partner of Chemistry, a $350M early-stage venture firm he launched in October 2024 alongside Kristina Shen (formerly Andreessen Horowitz) and Ethan Kurzweil (formerly Bessemer Venture Partners) [^1][^2]. Chemistry's debut fund was 2x oversubscribed [^2].

Before Chemistry, Goldberg was a Partner at Index Ventures for nearly a decade (2015-2024), where he led early-stage investments in software and fintech businesses [^3][^4]. At Index, he led investments in Plaid, Bridge, Pilot, Anrok, and Persona, and sat on the boards of Plaid, Pilot, Intercom, Nova Credit, Built Technologies, and Motive (formerly KeepTruckin) [^3][^5][^6].

Prior to Index, Goldberg was one of the first business hires at Dropbox, where he ran Business Strategy and helped the company grow from 250 to 1,500 employees [^3][^7]. Before Dropbox, he was an investment banker at Morgan Stanley, where he worked on the IPO for Tesla Motors, and then joined Hudson Clean Energy Partners, a cleantech-focused venture fund, where he led investments in solar energy, energy efficiency, and storage technologies [^3][^7].

Goldberg holds a degree in International Relations from Brown University [^3].

## Stated Thesis

Chemistry publicly describes its focus as leading "high conviction investments at the Seed and Series A" in software companies [^1]. Goldberg has stated that Chemistry operates on a "conviction, not consensus" model, optimizing for outliers rather than averages [^8]. He has emphasized that "venture doesn't scale well" and that the firm's strategy is to "take great multi-stage DNA, reconstitute it with focus" [^8].

Goldberg personally focuses on financial services, insurance, real estate, and enterprise software [^3]. He has described the current market as a period of "fintech maximalism," noting that fintech companies that "quietly executed through the winter of the 2021 to 2024 period have emerged as compounders" [^9].

Chemistry's fund design follows a pre-seed through Series A model with light reserves and concentrated doubling-down, with each partner aiming to make two to three deals per year [^2][^8].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 17 verified investments across Goldberg's career at Index Ventures and Chemistry:

**Sector breakdown:** 11 of 17 investments (65%) are in fintech or financial services (Plaid, Bridge, Pilot, Anrok, Lithic, Nova Credit, CoverWallet, Newfront, Capitolis, Built Technologies, Moov). 2 of 17 (12%) are in identity/security (Persona, Stytch). 2 of 17 (12%) are in enterprise software/data (Datacurve, Motive). 2 of 17 (12%) are in proptech/other (Atmos, Intercom). Note: some companies span categories; primary classification used.

**Stage distribution:** Goldberg has primarily led or joined seed through Series B rounds. At Index, he frequently entered at Series A or B and followed through to later stages (Plaid Series C in 2018, Persona Series B in 2021, Built Series A in 2017). At Chemistry, the focus narrows to seed and Series A.

**Geographic concentration:** The majority of verified portfolio companies are based in the San Francisco Bay Area, with some exceptions (Built Technologies in Nashville, Bridge in San Antonio).

**Founder profile patterns:** Goldberg has backed both technical and non-technical founders, but shows a preference for founders attacking regulated industries (financial services, insurance, tax compliance) with software-first approaches.

**Co-investor patterns:** Frequent co-investors include Sequoia Capital (Anrok, Bridge), Coatue (Persona), Goldman Sachs (Built Technologies), and Stripe (Pilot). At Chemistry, he co-founded alongside alumni from a16z and Bessemer, suggesting continued access to those networks.

**Notable pattern:** Goldberg's portfolio is overwhelmingly concentrated in fintech and financial infrastructure — far more concentrated than a typical generalist VC. His stated focus on "financial services, insurance, real estate, and enterprise software" closely matches his actual behavior, which is relatively uncommon among VCs.

**Sample size caveat:** This analysis is based on 17 verified investments. Goldberg likely has additional investments not captured here, particularly smaller checks and angel investments.
