## Background

Romit Shah is the Founder and Managing General Partner of Tensor Edge Capital, a concentrated long/short equity investment firm focused on semiconductors and select areas of software, headquartered in Weston, Massachusetts [^1][^2].

Shah's career spans approximately 25 years across three phases in the semiconductor investment ecosystem [^3]. He began his career in equity research at J.P. Morgan and Franklin Templeton Funds before 1998 [^4]. From 2001 to 2007, he served as Senior Vice President in equity research at Lehman Brothers [^4]. When Barclays acquired Lehman's investment banking operations, Shah joined Barclays Capital as a Director covering the semiconductor sector in September 2008, a position he held through roughly 2010 [^4][^5].

From approximately 2010 through January 2019, Shah served as Managing Director at Nomura (later branded Nomura Instinet), where he established himself as one of the most prominent semiconductor equity analysts on Wall Street [^3][^6]. His coverage universe included Intel (INTC), Broadcom (AVGO), Micron (MU), NVIDIA (NVDA), Qualcomm (QCOM), Texas Instruments (TXN), Analog Devices (ADI), Applied Materials (AMAT), NXP Semiconductors (NXPI), and Tesla (TSLA) [^6]. Over his analyst career at Nomura, he achieved a 66.67% success rate across 69 rated positions, with an average return of 27.01% per rating [^6].

After Nomura, Shah moved to the buy side as Senior Managing Director and Semiconductor Sector Head at Matrix Capital Management Company, L.P., a multi-billion-dollar technology-focused hedge fund founded by David Goel [^7][^8]. At Matrix, Shah helped lead the firm's semiconductor investment strategy, including serving as a founding investor in Rivos Inc. (alongside Matrix co-founder David Goel) beginning in 2021 [^9].

In 2025, Shah founded Tensor Edge Capital — registering the entity (formerly named Matrix Capital Partners, LLC) on April 3, 2025 — to manage a concentrated long/short semiconductor equity strategy independently [^1][^2][^10]. The firm manages approximately $109.5 million in fund assets as of its SEC registration [^11].

Shah holds a Bachelor of Arts in Biology from the University of California, Berkeley (class of 1998) [^4][^12].

## Stated Thesis

Tensor Edge Capital publicly describes its focus as "a fundamental, long/short equity investment firm focused on semiconductors and select areas of software," built on "focus, discipline, and alignment" [^2]. The firm's stated approach is "concentrated, research-intensive" and grounded in "bottom-up conviction and a deep understanding of industry dynamics, business models, and the technologies that matter" [^2].

The firm also integrates alternative data and AI tools into its investment process, and retains flexibility for opportunistic positions outside its core semiconductor/software focus, including blockchain and digital-asset-related businesses, companies with significant AI exposure, and Japanese issuers [^11].

Shah has described his personal investment edge as having "built deep domain expertise in the semiconductor ecosystem — from core technologies to supply chains to industry leadership" and using that expertise "to identify variant views and generate alpha in one of the most dynamic and consequential sectors in the market" [^3].

During his Nomura analyst years, Shah publicly articulated views on semiconductor cycles, supply constraints, and competitive dynamics. On Intel's 2018 upgrade, he stated: "There isn't a company in our semiconductor coverage that we see raising estimates in October or November. Intel is the only exception" [^13].

## Inferred Thesis

Tensor Edge Capital is a newly launched fund (registered April 2025) with limited publicly disclosed portfolio holdings. The firm operates as a long/short equity hedge fund, not a venture capital firm, so its "portfolio" consists primarily of public equity positions rather than startup investments.

The one documented venture/private investment during Shah's career is Rivos Inc. (made while at Matrix Capital Management), which was acquired by Meta in late 2025 [^14][^15]. This single data point suggests Shah's private investment activity is concentrated in deep-tech semiconductor startups with AI applications — consistent with his entire public-market career focus.

Based on the ADV filing and LinkedIn description, the inferred thesis appears to be:

- **Primary focus:** Public equity long/short in semiconductors (fabless chip companies, semiconductor equipment, EDA software)
- **Secondary exposure:** Software companies with significant semiconductor/AI intersections
- **Stage:** Public markets with selective private/venture positions (as demonstrated by Rivos)
- **AI angle:** Strong — the fund explicitly calls out AI-exposed companies as a focus, and Rivos was an AI chip company
- **Geographic lean:** US-listed companies with some Japan exposure (explicitly cited in ADV)
