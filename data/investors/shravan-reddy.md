## Background

Shravan Reddy is a Partner at Pear VC, the Palo Alto-based pre-seed and seed fund, where he leads the PearX accelerator program [^1]. He joined Pear in August 2024 [^2]. He grew up between the Bay Area and Hyderabad and holds a B.S. in Computer Science with an Economics minor and an M.S. in Management Science and Engineering (Tau Beta Pi honors), both from Stanford University [^3].

Reddy's career spans three major chapters as an operator before he moved into venture. He co-founded Pixelapse, a designer-focused visual version control and collaboration platform, in 2011 while participating in Y Combinator and StartX [^4]. He grew Pixelapse to profitability with tens of thousands of designers on the platform before Dropbox acquired the company in January 2015 [^4][^5]. Post-acquisition, he spent approximately four years at Dropbox as a Senior Engineering Manager, leading the Design Systems team and Core Desktop Experience team, and building collaboration features including file previews and version history [^2].

From 2020 to 2024, Reddy was an Engineering Leader at Stripe. He grew Stripe Treasury from inception to general availability with hundreds of millions of dollars in deposits, led Stripe Issuing's integrations with multiple banking partners, and led engineering for Stripe Opal, the firm's startup banking platform [^2][^3]. He holds two patents related to content management systems and real-time collaboration features [^3].

Prior to Pear, Reddy was also an active angel investor backing technical founders in companies including Flow Club, Puzzle.io, Scanner.dev, Outerbase, Acre Rewards, and Daimo [^2].

## Stated Thesis

Reddy publicly describes his investment focus as early-stage AI, B2B software, and fintech [^1]. He articulates three domain preferences: companies building new ways for people to work together, fintechs serving underserved markets, and application-layer vertical AI companies [^3].

He emphasizes intrinsic founder motivation as a primary signal: "Achieving escape velocity can be incredibly difficult, and the best founders know exactly why they've made it their life's work." [^3] He also prizes speed of execution and a customer-centric product approach [^3].

On community as a competitive advantage for early founders, he has stated: "There are a few hard truths about startups. Companies don't die. Founders give up. Being a founder can be a lonely experience. Building a community gives you the best possible odds of success, which guides everything we do at Pear." [^1]

In his writing on the PearX interview process, Reddy states that he looks for founders who can articulate what makes them outliers in their field, who have deep insight into the problem they are solving, and who bring genuine conviction about the challenges they are tackling [^6].

## Inferred Thesis

**Based on 8 verified Pear VC investments where Shravan Reddy's involvement is attributable through his PearX leadership role (joined August 2024; all PearX cohorts since then are within his tenure). Sample is too small for reliable sector percentages; qualitative patterns are noted below.**

All 8 documented investments are at the pre-seed stage through the PearX accelerator, consistent with his stated focus on PearX as the vehicle for Pear's pre-seed investing [^1]. Check sizes for PearX companies range from $250K to $2M, with over $1M in cloud credits added [^7].

Sector breakdown across documented investments: developer tools and AI infrastructure (Tanagram, Shiplight, NomadicML, Outspeed — 4 of 8, 50%), fintech/payments (Piston, Andera — 2 of 8, 25%), energy/climate AI (Pravāh — 1 of 8, 12.5%), scientific computing (Camber — 1 of 8, 12.5%). This reflects a strong skew toward technical infrastructure and developer-facing products, consistent with Reddy's own background as a deeply technical engineer and operator.

Founder profile patterns: notable concentration of founders with prior operator experience at major tech companies. Tanagram's founder was an early Stripe Issuing engineer — directly overlapping with Reddy's Stripe Issuing work [^8]. Camber's founder is a former theoretical astrophysicist from the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton [^9]. Piston's founders were former fleet operators with direct domain pain [^10]. This pattern suggests Reddy weights domain credibility and lived problem experience heavily.

Co-investor patterns: Piston's seed round was led by Spark Capital with participation from BOND [^10]; Camber's seed was led by Base10 Partners [^9]; Pravāh is backed by Khosla Ventures and Conviction [^11]. These suggest Pear/Reddy co-invests with top-tier generalist funds at seed, while leading or co-leading at pre-seed through PearX.

Geographic focus: Bay Area (San Francisco, Palo Alto) dominates, consistent with PearX being an in-person accelerator program at Pear's San Francisco office [^7].

One pattern not foregrounded in stated thesis: scientific computing and climate/energy AI. Both Camber and Pravāh are in domains Reddy does not explicitly name in his stated focus, suggesting the vertical AI interest extends beyond enterprise SaaS into hard-science applications.

## In Their Own Words

On community and the founder journey, from the Pear VC team page:

> "There are a few hard truths about startups. Companies don't die. Founders give up. Being a founder can be a lonely experience. Building a community gives you the best possible odds of success, which guides everything we do at Pear." [^1]

On what he looks for during the PearX interview process:

On the Piston investment (July 2025), from the Pear VC blog:

> "Vikram and Shivam have both operated fleets themselves and lived the pain of skyrocketing fuel expenses and rampant abuse on fleet cards. They identified a huge problem impacting an underserved market and we believe there is enormous opportunity." [^10]

On what he values in founders, from his personal website:

On his investment domains, from his personal website:

> "In terms of domains, I'm most excited about companies building new ways to work together, fintechs going after underserved markets, and application-layer vertical AI companies." [^3]
