## Background

Kristina Shen is Co-Founder and Managing Partner at Chemistry, a venture capital firm she launched in October 2024 with a $350 million debut fund focused on seed and Series A investments [^1][^2].

Before founding Chemistry, Shen was a General Partner at Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) for four years (2019-2024), where she led the B2B software investing practice [^3][^4]. Prior to a16z, she was a Partner at Bessemer Venture Partners for seven years (2013-2019), where she co-authored the influential "State of the Cloud" report and Bessemer Cloud Index alongside Byron Deeter [^3][^5].

Shen holds a B.A. in Operations Research and Management Science from the University of California, Berkeley [^4]. She is a founding member of All Raise, a nonprofit focused on accelerating the success of female founders and funders in tech [^4].

At Chemistry, Shen is joined by co-founders Mark Goldberg (formerly Index Ventures) and Ethan Kurzweil (formerly Bessemer Venture Partners) [^1][^2]. The debut fund was reported to be 2x oversubscribed [^2].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Shen says publicly about her investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Shen is known for a structured, metrics-driven approach to SaaS investing that emphasizes actionable metrics over vanity numbers [^5][^6]. At Chemistry, she focuses on early-stage B2B software including fintech, work software, and developer/infrastructure tools [^2].

On SaaS metrics, Shen has stated: "When you're at $1M in ARR, all your metrics are made-up numbers because your business is still changing so rapidly. In the early stages, it's more important to understand your levers than the output" [^6].

On benchmarking, Shen has cautioned: "By definition, benchmarks are average... we kind of have to figure out for your company... what is that superpower that makes you really stand out?" [^6]. She has argued that "we have over indexed on metrics and benchmarks" in the industry [^6].

On retention metrics, Shen has stated: "Net dollar retention is actually the most incorrectly used number and cited number" and emphasizes that benchmarks differ dramatically by customer segment — "If you're selling to the SMB... 70 or 75% annual retention is considered pretty good... if you are selling like a 50K or 100K ACV deal... you better be 90% retention or higher" [^6].

On growth standards, she has noted: "The best cloud companies can get from $1-$10 million ARR in two or three years" [^6]. She has also pushed for higher standards: "We talk about Rule of 60. We think Rule of 40 isn't good enough anymore" [^6].

## Inferred Thesis

The analysis below is based on 17 investments attributed to Shen across her tenures at Bessemer, a16z, and Chemistry [^3][^4][^7][^8].

**Sector concentration (based on 17 verified investments):**
- B2B SaaS (horizontal): 7 of 17 (41%) — ServiceTitan, Gainsight, Pave, Sprig, Equals, Wrapbook, Macro
- AI/ML: 4 of 17 (24%) — Decagon, Didero, Serval, Assort Health
- Developer tools/infrastructure: 3 of 17 (18%) — Mux, Instructure, Matik
- Healthcare AI: 2 of 17 (12%) — Assort Health, Tennr
- Cybersecurity: 1 of 17 (6%) — WhiteHat Security

*Note: Some companies span multiple categories; classified by primary function. Assort Health counted once in AI.*

**Key patterns:**

- **SaaS-dominant portfolio**: Shen's investments are overwhelmingly B2B SaaS companies, consistent with her stated expertise and her role co-authoring Bessemer's State of the Cloud reports.

- **Stage distribution**: Primarily seed and Series A investor. At a16z she invested with check sizes from $500K to $40M [^7]. At Chemistry, the $350M fund targets seed and Series A [^2].

- **Long-term founder relationships**: Shen has described her involvement with ServiceTitan as spanning from seed through IPO milestone [^4][^9]. She first invested in ServiceTitan at Bessemer around 2014, reflecting on the investment later: "My favorite part is we thought it'd only be a $1bn outcome" — the company went on to IPO at a much higher valuation [^9].

- **Metrics-driven diligence**: Shen's analytical background (Berkeley ORMS degree, State of the Cloud co-author) suggests she applies quantitative rigor to SaaS company evaluation, looking at cohort analysis, net dollar retention by segment, and sales efficiency [^5][^6].

- **AI pivot in recent investments**: Her Chemistry portfolio includes multiple AI companies (Decagon, Didero, Serval, Assort Health), suggesting she sees AI-native B2B software as the next major wave, consistent with her pattern of investing in enterprise software evolution.

- **Board-level engagement**: Shen sits on boards of Matik, Mux, Pave, Sprig, and Wrapbook [^4], indicating hands-on involvement with portfolio companies.

- **Co-investor patterns**: Through Chemistry, she co-invests with her partners Mark Goldberg and Ethan Kurzweil, who bring networks from Index Ventures and Bessemer respectively.

- **Geographic focus**: Primarily San Francisco/Bay Area based investments.
