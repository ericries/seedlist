## Background

Raj Ganguly is Co-Founder and Co-CEO of B Capital Group, a global multi-stage venture and growth equity firm he co-founded in 2015 alongside Facebook co-founder Eduardo Saverin and Howard Morgan [^1]. Under his leadership, B Capital has grown into a firm with nine office locations globally, more than 100 employees, and over $9 billion in assets under management [^2].

Ganguly grew up outside Calcutta, India, and moved to the United States at age five [^3]. He earned a BSE (engineering) and BAS (economics) from the University of Pennsylvania's School of Engineering and Applied Science and Wharton School, graduating summa cum laude as a Benjamin Franklin Scholar and Joseph Wharton Scholar, and later earned an MBA from Harvard Business School [^4].

Before founding B Capital, Ganguly spent six years at Bain Capital investing in technology-driven companies across the U.S. and Asia and co-led the firm's global technology initiative [^1]. Earlier in his career he worked at McKinsey & Company, Diageo plc, and Miradiant — one of the first internet payment startups, which was later acquired by J.P. Morgan [^1]. He is also a Senior Advisor to the Boston Consulting Group, B Capital's strategic partner [^4].

Prior to B Capital, Ganguly co-founded Velos Partners in 2013, a venture capital firm with offices in Los Angeles, Singapore, and London, where he led investments and served on the boards of Surf Air, Silvercar, and Pong [^5]. The Silvercar investment was co-led with Eduardo Saverin in 2014, which led to Ganguly and Saverin meeting and eventually co-founding B Capital [^6].

Ganguly serves or has served on the boards of Icertis, Atomwise, Evidation Health, Journera, Winmore, and INTURN [^7] [^8].

## Stated Thesis

Ganguly publicly describes B Capital's focus as backing technology-enabled companies digitizing large traditional industries globally. He articulates the firm around three pillars: treating technology as a global enterprise (not U.S.-centric), investing in the digitization of large traditional industries such as healthcare and finance, and being actively engaged, value-add partners to portfolio companies [^3].

He emphasizes enterprise and B2B technology at growth stage, stating publicly that "gone are the days in 2020 and 2021 when it was really growth at all costs" — signaling a focus on unit economics and profitability fundamentals [^9]. For B2B investments, he says "the biggest thing we're really focused on is high re-up rates with early customers" — companies whose earliest enterprise customers increase spend year over year [^10].

On AI, Ganguly has called it a 15-to-20-year super cycle: "We're in the very early stages, the third or fourth year of this AI super cycle. And it's probably a 20-year super cycle. Maybe it's a 15-year super cycle, but it is going to change the world." He sees the current opportunity in the "AI native app layer" rather than in infrastructure alone [^11].

Looking forward, Ganguly has publicly stated that healthcare, fintech, and climate tech are the three verticals where B Capital is most actively deploying capital [^3]. In China specifically, B Capital's scope is deliberately narrow: "Our focus in China is only B2B, enterprise software, and healthcare. We've been very narrowly focused" [^12].

## Inferred Thesis

**Based on the B Capital portfolio page and publicly documented investments, this analysis covers approximately 20-25 verifiable individual deals where Ganguly had a direct board or lead role, out of 200+ total B Capital investments. Sample size is too small to compute reliable firm-wide percentages, but patterns within Ganguly's personal deal record are consistent.**

**Sector distribution (personal board/lead deals, ~20 verified):**
- Enterprise/B2B SaaS: Icertis (contract management), Winmore (logistics SaaS), INTURN (inventory), Journera (travel data) — approximately 40% of personal deals
- Healthcare/biotech: Evidation Health (health data), Atomwise (AI drug discovery), Notable Labs (oncology), Centivo (health benefits) — approximately 35%
- Fintech: Evidation's payer/provider model, Khatabook (India SMB finance), Payfazz (Indonesia) — approximately 15%
- Logistics/transportation: Ninja Van (Southeast Asia last-mile), Blackbuck (India trucking) — approximately 10%

**Stage distribution:** Ganguly leads predominantly Series B and C investments, with documented check sizes of $10M–$60M per deal [^2]. B Capital's growth funds (the primary vehicles Ganguly manages) target Series B through late-stage. B Capital also deploys seed/Series A capital through its Ascent Fund, but Ganguly's personal board seats are clustered at Series B and beyond.

**Geographic concentration:** North America (50–60%) and South/Southeast Asia (30–40%) — particularly India and Indonesia — with more limited exposure to Southeast Asia (Singapore, Indonesia, Vietnam) and China (B2B/healthcare only) [^12]. Ganguly is LA-based and the firm's Americas operations anchor in Los Angeles, San Francisco, and New York.

**Co-investor patterns:** B Capital frequently co-invests with Andreessen Horowitz (Journera Series B [^13]), Sanabil Investments (Atomwise Series B [^14]), Greycroft, Eight Roads Ventures (Icertis Series C [^15]), SV Health Investors (Evidation [^16]), and Alibaba/strategic investors for Southeast Asia growth rounds.

**Founder profile:** Ganguly has shown a strong preference for technical co-founders with deep domain expertise in large, underserved enterprise markets — particularly where the founder is also an active operator/user of the product (e.g., Ninja Van's logistics founders, Lambda's AI infrastructure founders). He has repeatedly backed immigrant founders and first-generation entrepreneurs.

**BCG partnership as differentiator:** B Capital's relationship with Boston Consulting Group is a structural differentiator Ganguly consistently emphasizes: portfolio companies receive access to a dedicated BCG partner who opens resources and enterprise networks, potentially accelerating enterprise sales [^10].

**Notable gaps vs. stated thesis:** Despite public statements about climate tech being a priority, verified climate-related investments with Ganguly's direct involvement are sparse in publicly available data (Lunar Energy cited at B Capital's 2025 AGM but without Ganguly named as lead). The AI investments (Atomwise, Axiom, Goodfire, Apptronik, Lambda) are recent (2020–2025) and may represent a strategic shift rather than a longstanding pattern.

## In Their Own Words

**On B Capital's founding vision:**
"We built the firm around three key pillars: how tech was going global, the rise of digitization of large traditional industries, and being really engaged with our companies." [^3]

**On what makes a great B2B investment:**
"Great companies are not great investments... we'll meet with founders who blow us away... but valuations [make] the risk really tough." [^3]

**On the AI super cycle:**
"We're in the very early stages, the third or fourth year of this AI super cycle. And it's probably a 20-year super cycle. Maybe it's a 15-year super cycle, but it is going to change the world." [^11]

**On where AI profits will be captured:**
"The opportunity now, and where I think the profit pools will be, [is] in that AI native app layer." [^11]

**On the shift from growth-at-all-costs:**
"Gone are the days in 2020 and 2021 when it was really growth at all costs." [^9]

**On profitability:**
"I think investors care a lot about profitability and frankly, a return to profitability is a good thing for everyone." [^12]

**On digitization opportunity post-COVID:**
"COVID-19 shows extensive unmet need when it comes to digitization. The next ten years will see a major shift in innovation as entrepreneurs examine industries and practices from new angles." [^25]

**On the Icertis investment:**
"When a market leading company doubles revenues every year and maintains positive cash flow, it's an extremely compelling investment." [^15]

**On supporting founders:**
"Our strategy is to back great founders early and support them throughout their entrepreneurial journey." [^26]

**On building B Capital as a firm:**
"I like to build stuff... It happens to be the business that we're building is where we invest in founders and entrepreneurs." [^3]

**On the BCG partnership advantage:**
"Companies [in our portfolio] get access to a dedicated BCG partner who then opens up their resources and network." [^10]

**On B Capital's community value:**
"Our CEO Summit exemplifies the highly curated ecosystem we've built at B Capital, bringing together the brightest minds in technology and business to exchange ideas that shape industries. Innovation is never a solo act – it's learned, shared and constantly evolving." [^2]
