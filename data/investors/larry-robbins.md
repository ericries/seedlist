## Background

Lawrence "Larry" Michael Robbins (born October 22, 1969) is an American hedge fund manager, activist investor, and philanthropist [^1]. He grew up in Cook County, Illinois, a suburb of Chicago [^1]. He graduated with honors from the Jerome Fisher Program in Management and Technology at the University of Pennsylvania in 1992, earning a B.S. in Economics from the Wharton School (with concentrations in accounting, finance, and marketing) and a B.S. in Engineering from the Moore School (with a major in systems engineering) [^2] [^3]. He became a Certified Public Accountant in Illinois in 1991 [^3].

Robbins began his career at Gleacher & Company, a merger and advisory boutique in New York, where he worked for three years [^2] [^3]. He then joined Leon Cooperman's Omega Advisors, where he spent six years as an analyst and partner on their US equity long/short team [^2] [^3].

In 2000, Robbins left Omega to found Glenview Capital Management, naming the firm after Glenview, Illinois, the Chicago suburb where he first played hockey at age five [^1] [^3]. Glenview launched its flagship long/short funds in 2001 and added the opportunistic Glenview Opportunity ("GO") funds in 2007 [^4]. As of its March 2025 Form ADV, Glenview manages $5.6 billion in discretionary assets across six client accounts [^5]. The firm employs approximately 73 professionals [^6].

Robbins also chairs Longview Acquisition Corp., a SPAC sponsor affiliated with Glenview that has raised over $1 billion in capital [^3]. Longview's first SPAC merged with Butterfly Network (a handheld ultrasound company) in a $1.5 billion deal completed in February 2021 [^7]. Robbins serves on the board of Butterfly Network [^3].

Outside of investing, Robbins is a prominent education philanthropist. He chairs the boards of KIPP New York and Relay Graduate School of Education, and serves on the board of the Robin Hood Foundation [^1] [^8]. Through the Robbins Family Foundation (with his wife Sarahmay), he supports education reform nationally [^3]. He donated $7.5 million to endow the renovation of Penn's M&T program headquarters [^9].

In 2024, Robbins relocated his family from Alpine, New Jersey to South Florida, while maintaining Glenview's Manhattan headquarters and commuting to the office three days a week [^10].

## Stated Thesis

Glenview Capital publicly describes its approach as Growth at a Reasonable Price (GARP), seeking companies in stable industries with recurring revenue streams or entrenched market positions [^4]. The firm states it drives performance through "deep fundamental research and a long-term orientation with a philosophy to think and act like owners" [^3].

Robbins has stated that Glenview's theory is to "invest in businesses that can be adequately described in minutes, where they can analyze past and present fundamentals to predict future fundamentals including earnings growth, cash flow growth, and shareholder returns" [^11].

In a Graham and Doddsville interview, Robbins described his approach: "We look for businesses that have a good medium and long-term growth outlook and are cheap relative to the cash flows that they are currently generating. In general, we're looking for low-teens or better growth" [^12].

The firm offers three strategies: equity and fixed-income investments through bottom-up fundamental research (Glenview Funds); opportunistic short-term market positioning (Opportunity Funds); and long/short strategies focused on healthcare sector investments (GCM Healthcare Funds) [^6].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on Glenview Capital's Q4 2025 13F filing disclosing 57 equity positions valued at $4.91 billion [^13], the portfolio reveals the following patterns:

**Sector Concentration:** Healthcare dominates. Based on the disclosed holdings: CVS Health ($650M), Teva Pharmaceutical ($522M), Tenet Healthcare ($394M), Viatris ($138M), Thermo Fisher Scientific ($130M), and several smaller healthcare positions account for approximately $1.9 billion, or roughly 39% of the 13F portfolio value. This is consistent with reports that over 70% of Robbins' equity portfolio historically has been invested in healthcare [^14]. Technology represents a significant secondary allocation with Amazon ($210M), ZoomInfo ($203M), DXC Technology ($122M), DigitalOcean ($96M), AMD ($84M), and others. Financial/payments technology is notable with Global Payments ($458M) as the third-largest position.

**Investment Style:** Robbins is a concentrated, high-conviction public equity investor. His top 10 positions represent 60.46% of the 13F portfolio [^13]. Unlike most hedge funds, he holds positions for years and does not employ stop-losses [^1]. The average position size in the top 10 exceeds $200 million.

**Activist Engagement:** The portfolio shows a pattern of taking large stakes and pursuing activist campaigns. At CVS Health, Glenview holds $650 million (13.2% of portfolio) and won four board seats in November 2024, with Robbins himself joining the board [^15]. At Tenet Healthcare, Glenview became the largest shareholder in 2016 with a 17.8% stake and placed four board designees [^16]. At Health Management Associates, Glenview acquired a 14.6% stake and replaced the entire board, ultimately facilitating HMA's $7.6 billion sale to Community Health Systems in 2014, generating a $396 million return [^17].

**Geographic Focus:** Portfolio companies are predominantly large-cap US-listed companies. No discernible geographic concentration beyond US public markets.

**Check Size:** Positions range from under $50 million for smaller holdings to over $650 million for top positions, with a median top-20 position of approximately $130 million.

**Pattern Not in Stated Thesis:** While Glenview describes itself as a GARP investor focused on fundamental research, the activist component is a core driver of returns that goes beyond passive stock-picking. The firm regularly takes board seats and pushes for operational or strategic changes at portfolio companies.

## In Their Own Words

On his investment philosophy, from a Graham and Doddsville interview: "You should be a voracious reader and sponge of information from other investors... If you really want to be a good investor, you cannot just be involved, you have to be committed. It really is not just a job, it is a passion" [^12].

On consistency, from the same interview: "What differentiates people in hockey who win games and who don't is the consistency of effort" [^12].

On evaluating people versus stocks: "I spent ten times the amount of time evaluating an investment than I did interviewing a person. Yet that person will impact ten, twenty, or thirty stocks in your portfolio, so you should do much more diligence on that person than you do on any individual investment" [^12].

On his 2015 losses, from a letter to investors: "I've failed to protect your capital, and mine... despite a flat market." Robbins pledged to open a new fund without management or performance fees, stating: "My goal is your capital, not my income" [^19].

On why he founded Glenview, from a Capitalize for Kids interview: Robbins stated he started Glenview because while at Omega he was looking to invest "in a way that would have low risk over time, provide real returns over time, and allow him to focus his own capital on that which was known and knowable from a fundamental perspective" [^11].

On wealth migration from New York, from a 2024 Fortune interview: "I know of no business that has generated long term success by driving away its highest paying customers" [^10].

On New York philanthropy, from the same interview: "I'm going to continue to dedicate to investing in high quality education in New York City, because unfortunately that job is nowhere near done" [^10].
