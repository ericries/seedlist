## Background

Chris Shumway is the Founder and Managing Partner of Shumway Capital, a private investment firm headquartered in Greenwich, Connecticut [^1]. He graduated from the University of Virginia's McIntire School of Commerce in 1988 and earned an MBA from Harvard Business School in 1993 [^2].

After business school, Shumway joined Tiger Management, the legendary hedge fund founded by Julian Robertson Jr. [^1]. He became a Senior Managing Director and Management Committee member by age 30, serving from 1992 through 1999 [^2]. Prior to Tiger, he had worked as an analyst at Morgan Stanley & Co. and at Brentwood Associates [^2].

In 2001, Shumway founded Shumway Capital Partners, a global investment firm that grew from $70 million in assets to over $9 billion [^1]. From 2002 to 2011, the firm significantly outperformed the S&P 500 and received multiple recognitions from *Barron's* as a top industry performer [^1]. In 2011, Shumway converted the firm from a hedge fund into a family office, stating he felt "responsible to correct this mismatch of investment style and investor needs" and that "the right decision for all of us is to return investor capital" [^3]. The conversion allowed for a more flexible investing mandate focused on long-term, opportunistic investments in both private and public securities [^1].

Shumway has provided seed capital to startup investment managers including JAT Capital Management, Glade Brook Capital Partners, Tide Point Capital Management, and Trend Capital Management, which collectively manage in excess of $4 billion [^4]. He co-founded The Chris and Carrie Shumway Foundation in 2007, focused on poverty alleviation through education [^2]. He donated $25 million to the University of Virginia's McIntire School of Commerce in 2018 and $25 million to Harvard Business School and Harvard's Department of Stem Cell and Regenerative Biology in 2020 [^2] [^4].

## Stated Thesis

Shumway Capital publicly describes its approach as targeting "high-quality businesses with strong management teams" focused on "long-term, sustainable growth and attractive reinvestment economics" [^5]. The firm states that as entrepreneurs themselves, they "understand the passion, vision and execution required to achieve sustainable growth" [^1].

The firm emphasizes that its capital comes entirely from partners, providing "a permanent capital base that enables long-term investments and decision making" [^6]. Investment horizons can "extend well beyond typical funds' and may last decades" [^5]. Shumway Capital states it works with management teams to "unlock growth through organic business opportunities and acquisitions" [^5].

**Geographic Focus:** All verified private investments are in United States-based companies [^7].

**Notable Pattern — Healthcare Concentration:** Despite claiming to invest across "all industries," 41% of verified private investments are in healthcare, particularly healthcare services companies (not biotech/pharma). This is a significant stated-vs-actual gap.

**Seed Capital to Fund Managers:** Shumway has a distinct track record of seeding other hedge fund managers (JAT Capital, Glade Brook, Tide Point, Trend Capital), a "Tiger cub" pattern common among Julian Robertson's alumni [^4].
