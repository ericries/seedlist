## Background

Jack Altman is a General Partner at Benchmark, which he joined in February 2026 [^1]. Prior to Benchmark, Altman founded and led Alt Capital, a solo-GP early-stage venture firm that raised $150 million for Fund I in February 2024 and $275 million for Fund II in September 2025, completing the second raise in approximately one week [^2][^3]. When Altman joined Benchmark, his Alt Capital teammates — Nate Collins, Vivek Katara, and Allie Meyer — followed him to the firm [^1][^4].

Before becoming a full-time investor, Altman co-founded Lattice, an HR and people-management platform, in September 2015. He served as CEO until January 2024, growing the company to over 2,500 customers and a $3 billion valuation before transitioning to Executive Chairman [^5][^6]. Altman is the younger brother of Sam Altman, co-founder and CEO of OpenAI [^2].

Altman's career began with an analyst role at Gleacher & Company (2011-2012), followed by co-founding Hydrazine Capital, an early-stage venture firm, with his brother Sam Altman in 2012 [^5][^7]. He then served as VP of Business Development at Teespring from 2013 to 2015 before founding Lattice [^5][^7]. He holds an A.B. in Economics from Princeton University (class of 2011) [^5][^7].

## Stated Thesis

Altman has publicly described his investment approach as centered on product durability. He has stated that his core test is whether a product will still be used 10 years from now, rather than chasing shorter-term trends [^8][^9].

Alt Capital described itself as prioritizing "exceptional founders solving important problems" rather than targeting specific sectors, with emphasis on hands-on operational support and founder mentorship [^4]. Altman has said one of his motivating goals was to build a firm and be the kind of partner he most would have wanted as an entrepreneur [^3].

On AI, Altman has stated his bullishness on AI applications rather than infrastructure or foundation models, noting that model companies will become very large but that his focus is on investing in the application layer [^9]. He has said he believes AI capabilities will progressively expand to encompass more professional domains [^9].

Altman has described his approach as "conviction-led investing," emphasizing backing founders he truly believes in regardless of market fluctuations [^3]. He has said that when founders ask for help, he responds immediately, and when they do not want help, he respects their space [^8].

### Stage Distribution

Altman invests primarily at seed and Series A. Alt Capital's check sizes ranged from $1M to $25M, with a target around $2.5M, and follow-on capacity up to $40M+ [^4][^11]. His earlier angel investments were likely smaller checks. At Benchmark, he will invest at Series A and Series B per the firm's established focus.

### Geographic Focus

Investments are concentrated in San Francisco and the broader Bay Area, consistent with both Alt Capital's base and Benchmark's location [^11].

### Co-investor Patterns

Alt Capital was described as a "preferred co-investor for top-tier firms like Sequoia and Y Combinator" [^12]. Given Altman's move to Benchmark, his co-investor network now includes the Benchmark partnership (Peter Fenton, Eric Vishria, Chetan Puttagunta, Everett Randle) [^1].

### Founder Profile Patterns

Altman gravitates toward technical founders building B2B software, particularly in enterprise applications and AI [^3][^4]. His portfolio shows a strong preference for companies building products for professional end users (HR, compliance, developer tools, financial workflows).

### Notable Gaps

Altman claims a generalist approach but his portfolio reveals a strong B2B enterprise tilt. Consumer-facing companies are rare (Product Hunt and Owner.com being exceptions). Hardware and deep tech are limited to Antares Nuclear as an outlier [^2].

## In Their Own Words

Jack Altman on his investment filter: "If your product won't be used 10 years from now, you'll never build a lasting, valuable SaaS business" [^8].

On learning from founders: "To me, talking to the founders is actually where I learn the most most of the time. The founders who are running companies are the ones who are close to the metal" [^9].

On his investing philosophy: "I learned a ton from seed-stage founders...many are way better at things than I am" [^8].

On joining Benchmark: "The past two years as a full time investor have been the most rewarding of my career. I really love venture capital, which is not something I ever imagined I'd say when I was kid, but here we are. I love new ideas and being part of a team with a mission" [^1].

On AI applications: "Model companies will be like ridiculously big, but we're not investing in them." Regarding AI's expanding scope: "It's gonna just keep moving down the list and become good at being a lawyer... a doctor... a structural engineer" [^9].

On speed and startups: "Move fast. Speed is one of your main advantages over large companies" [^8].

On the investor-founder relationship: "You're literally stuck with each other longer than most marriages" [^8].

On contrarian investing: Altman has argued that true contrarian bets involve believing harder in something, not just disagreeing [^8].
