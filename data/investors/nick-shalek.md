## Background

Nick Shalek is a General Partner at Ribbit Capital, where he has worked since the firm's founding in August 2012 [^1]. He is one of the founding partners of the firm alongside Micky Malka and Nikolay Kostov [^2].

Shalek graduated from Yale University (Class of 2005) with a BA in Economics and Political Science [^3]. After Yale, he worked as a Senior Analyst at Yale's Investment Office, the endowment managed by legendary investor David Swensen, where he contributed to over $800 million in new investment commitments across U.S. public equities, venture capital, and natural resources [^4].

Following Yale Investments, Shalek spent time at General Catalyst — one of the fund investments in Yale's portfolio — focused on incubations [^2]. He also worked at Facebook developing partnership and risk management strategies for Facebook Credits [^4], and served as a Director at Verne Global, a carbon-neutral data center company [^1].

Before joining Ribbit, Shalek served as Entrepreneur in Residence at Aspire Public Schools, where he incubated Schoolzilla, a cloud-based educational data management and analytics platform that was later acquired by Renaissance in 2019 [^5]. Schoolzilla grew out of tools built for Aspire's own school data needs and was eventually spun out to serve districts nationwide [^6].

Shalek holds an MBA from Stanford Graduate School of Business and an MA from Stanford Graduate School of Education [^3].

His board seats as of early 2026 include Next Insurance, Coalition, Root Insurance (former, departed November 2022), and Vouch [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Shalek describes his investment approach as drawing directly on lessons from David Swensen and the Yale endowment model, emphasizing first-principles thinking over pattern matching [^4].

He has publicly stated that incentive alignment is foundational to his investment decisions: "The foundation of great investing is really about getting incentives right" [^7].

Ribbit Capital, where Shalek is a GP, publicly describes itself as a venture fund founded exclusively to invest in financial services and fintech globally — backing entrepreneurs who are using technology to fundamentally reshape how money works [^8]. Shalek has articulated that the firm looks for founders building "impactful new financial brands" [^9].

Shalek's stated focus encompasses fintech, insurtech, payments, crypto infrastructure, and global financial services. He was an early public champion of Bitcoin and crypto infrastructure from a financial services perspective, explaining in 2013 why Ribbit was actively seeking Bitcoin companies while other VCs on the same panels said they would only touch equity [^4].

On ownership and pricing, Shalek has stated: "Ownership definitely matters to me, that's like almost immutable law" and "I'm always concerned about pricing. I always have been since the start" [^7].

**Crypto conviction:** Shalek was among the earliest institutional voices advocating for Bitcoin and crypto infrastructure investing in 2013. Ribbit's direct Bitcoin position in Fund I and early Coinbase backing reflect this. His 2026 Empire podcast appearance on tokenization confirms ongoing crypto conviction [^10].

**Founder profile preference:** Shalek's quoted investments emphasize teams with deep operational experience in regulated industries (insurance, lending, crypto compliance). Justos founders came from Netflix, Airbnb, and ClassPass; Capital Float founders came from Goldman Sachs and Stanford; Root Insurance founders built auto telematics technology.

**Check sizes:** Signal NFX data shows $2M–$15M range with $8M target [^11]. Press coverage confirms led rounds from $10M (Coalition Series A) to $45M (Capital Float Series C).
