## Background

Amanda Niu is General Partner and CFO at Shine Capital, the New York-based early-stage venture firm founded by Mo Koyfman in 2019 [^1][^2]. She joined Shine in July 2021 and serves a hybrid investing/operating role: she invests in early-stage companies and runs the firm's finance, operations, and back-office functions [^1][^3]. Public sources describe her as bringing "an unusually broad executive-operator perspective" to the partnership [^3].

Prior to Shine, Niu spent approximately eight years at Kickstarter (April 2013 - May/July 2021), where she advanced through Operations Manager, Controller, VP of Finance, SVP of Finance, and ultimately CFO from July 2019 to mid-2021 [^4][^5]. In her final years at Kickstarter she also led the People and Legal teams in addition to Finance [^4][^5]. A LinkedIn recommendation from then-CEO Aziz Hasan describes her demonstrating "flexibility, range and innate sense of duty" while guiding the organization through the COVID-19 pandemic [^5]. She left Kickstarter in 2021 to join Shine [^4][^5].

Earlier in her career, Niu was Business Director at WIRED (April 2011 - March 2013) and Business Manager at WIRED (June 2010 - April 2011) [^4][^5]. Before that she held progressive sales and marketing roles at Condé Nast Traveler from 2006 to 2010, starting as a Sales Assistant and rising to Associate Marketing Manager [^4]. She began her career as a Legal Assistant at Willkie Farr & Gallagher in 2005-2006 [^4].

Niu earned a B.A. from Williams College in 2005 with a concentration in English Literature and Psychology [^4][^6]. She is also a founding member of Level (Level Leaders), a multiracial network of women that pools capital to angel-invest in Black women entrepreneurs and to discuss the racial wealth gap [^7].

## Stated Thesis

Niu's published investing focus is broad-band early-stage technology investing aligned with Shine's overall manifesto. VCSheet lists her sector focus as AI, Fintech, SaaS, Web3/Crypto, and Enterprise, with stage focus on pre-seed, seed, and Series A and check sizes in the $1M-$10M range [^3]. Signal by NFX records her investment range at $1M-$7M with a $5M sweet spot, and ranks her among top investors at the seed and pre-seed stages specifically for Gaming/eSports, Web3/Blockchain, Web3/Crypto, and FinTech [^8]. VCSheet summarizes her "primary focus areas" as gaming/eSports, web3/blockchain/crypto, and fintech [^3].

Shine's firm-level manifesto, which Niu invests under, frames the firm as "lateralist" and "non-linear," states "We invest thematically," and asserts "We are willing to be your first check; we, in fact, embrace it" [^9]. Shine's website describes the firm as partnering with "creative, convincing, relentless entrepreneurs to build iconic businesses" [^1].

No long-form personal investing-thesis essay or podcast appearance authored by Niu was located in available sources within the research time budget.

## Inferred Thesis

**Sample-size caveat: this section is qualitative.** Within available primary sources, no Shine portfolio rounds were located in which Amanda Niu is named individually as the lead partner, board member, or quoted partner of record. Shine's Series A and seed announcements where a Shine partner is named typically credit Mo Koyfman (e.g., Bliss, Two, Hivemapper, Plaid-era) or Alex Hartz (e.g., iVerify, Visia/BinIt, AudioShake) [^10][^11][^12][^13]. This is consistent with Niu's hybrid role as GP & CFO — she contributes to Shine's collective investment decisions and operates the firm's financial infrastructure rather than running a public sourcing-and-leading book like a sector-specialist GP.

What can be inferred from her aggregator-listed focus and Shine's public portfolio:

**Sector orientation (per aggregator profiles):** Niu is ranked by Signal NFX in seed/pre-seed for Gaming/eSports, Web3/Blockchain, Web3/Crypto, and FinTech — a more crypto-and-gaming-tilted set than either Koyfman's broader-spectrum profile (AI, fintech, SaaS, web3, enterprise) or Hartz's lending/embedded-finance/applied-AI book [^8][^14][^15]. Within Shine's portfolio, the crypto/web3 cluster includes Goldsky and Hivemapper, and the consumer/gaming-adjacent positions include Notion's broader Shine portfolio book [^16][^10].

**Stage:** Pre-seed through Series A, consistent with Shine's manifesto and the rest of the partnership [^3][^9].

**Check size:** $1M-$7M with a $5M sweet spot per Signal [^8].

**Geographic focus:** New York-based [^4][^5]; Shine itself is headquartered at 100 Crosby Street in NYC with a San Francisco satellite [^1].

**Differentiation from Koyfman and Hartz:** Niu is the firm's only partner with a dedicated CFO mandate, having operated finance and people functions at scale at Kickstarter [^4][^5]. Where Koyfman is the firm's external face and primary sourcer of Shine's flagship Series A deals, and Hartz brings the SciFi-era embedded-finance and applied-vertical-AI book from San Francisco, Niu's distinctive contribution is operator credibility on finance, people, and legal — useful for portfolio companies navigating their own finance/HR/legal infrastructure decisions, especially platform/marketplace businesses (the closest public analog to her Kickstarter operating experience). Her aggregator-listed lean toward gaming and crypto is the largest sector-level delta versus the rest of the partnership.

**Honest gap:** Round-level attribution to Niu in publicly available press coverage is thin. A more comprehensive inferred thesis would require access to Shine's internal deal-by-deal partner attribution or direct disclosure of board seats, which were not located in available sources.
