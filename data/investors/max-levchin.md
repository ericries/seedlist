## Background

Max Levchin is a Ukrainian-American software engineer, entrepreneur, and investor [^1]. He co-founded PayPal in 1998, serving as CTO until its acquisition by eBay in 2002 [^1][^2]. He is a core member of the "PayPal Mafia," the group of early PayPal employees who went on to found or invest in major technology companies [^1].

After PayPal, Levchin founded Slide in 2004, a personal media-sharing service for social networking sites like Myspace and Facebook [^1]. Slide was acquired by Google in August 2010 for $182 million, after which Levchin served briefly as VP of Engineering at Google (2010-2011) [^1][^2].

In late 2011, Levchin started HVF (standing for "Hard, Valuable, and Fun"), a startup studio and data lab intended to explore projects leveraging underutilized data [^1][^2][^3]. HVF spawned two major companies: Affirm, spun out in early 2012 as a financial technology company building a next-generation credit network, where Levchin serves as Founder and CEO [^1][^2]; and Glow, launched in 2013 as a fertility tracking app [^1].

Levchin was a key early investor in Yelp, an online review platform founded in 2004, and served as Chairman of Yelp's board of directors from its founding until July 2015 [^1][^2]. He holds a BS in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign [^1][^4].

In January 2017, Levchin founded SciFi VC (which evolved from HVF's investment arm), an operator-led early-stage venture fund, with his wife Nellie Levchin [^4][^5]. SciFi VC focuses on founders tackling difficult problems requiring scientific rigor and intellectual depth [^5]. He currently holds board seats at Mixpanel and Polywork [^3].

## Stated Thesis

SciFi VC publicly describes its thesis across four areas: fintech (credit, infrastructure, insurance, payments, real estate), artificial intelligence (from infrastructure to applications), science (commercializing CRISPR, robotics, quantum computing), and "everything else" — exceptional founders in hard, regulated, or technical markets like enterprise software, healthcare, and security [^5].

Levchin's personal investment philosophy is captured by the "HVF" framework — "Hard, Valuable, Fun" [^3][^6]. He has publicly stated that HVF's core thesis is that "we live in a world of data" and the lab's purpose is to address under-utilization of data [^6]. He has also stated: "In the next decades we will see huge number of inherently analog processes captured digitally. Opportunities to build businesses that process this data and improve lives will abound" [^6].

SciFi VC describes its approach as bringing "unprecedented intellectual rigor to early stage investing" with "deep domain expertise" [^5][^6]. Levchin applies a founder-engineer's lens to investing by examining code quality, infrastructure robustness, and data flow during diligence, rather than staying at the market or financial level [^5].

## Inferred Thesis

**Based on 28 verified investments from Levchin's personal angel portfolio and SciFi VC combined; this represents a subset of his reported 86-132 total investments.**

Sector breakdown of 28 verified investments: 10 fintech/payments (36%), 5 consumer internet/social (18%), 4 data analytics/infrastructure (14%), 3 AI/ML (11%), 3 security/enterprise (11%), 3 other (health, food, media) (11%).

The fintech concentration (36%) is Levchin's strongest and most consistent theme, spanning payments (PayPal co-founder, WePay, Marqeta), lending/credit (Affirm founder, Brex, Capchase), and financial infrastructure (Stripe, Plaid, Very Good Security). This aligns with his stated thesis but the concentration is even heavier than his stated "four pillar" framing suggests.

Stage distribution: Levchin invests primarily at seed and Series A, with a stated check size range of $100K-$1M and target of $1M [^4]. SciFi VC also makes opportunistic growth-stage investments [^5]. His Yelp investment and Mixpanel involvement demonstrate willingness to be deeply engaged as an early investor and board member over long time horizons.

Co-investor patterns: Levchin frequently co-invests with other PayPal Mafia members (Peter Thiel invested alongside him in Brex) [^7]. SciFi VC co-invests with Sequoia, Andreessen Horowitz (Mixpanel Series A), and Insight Venture Partners [^8].

Founder profile preferences: Levchin strongly favors technically deep founders over MBA credentials, looking for founders with obsession over specific problems rather than opportunistic market-chasing [^6]. His portfolio reflects this — Mixpanel (Suhail Doshi, technical founder), Brex (Henrique Dubugras, built Pagar.me in Brazil), Yelp (Jeremy Stoppelman, ex-PayPal engineer) [^1][^7].

Notable pattern: Levchin's investments consistently target regulated industries where data-driven approaches can transform analog processes — this pattern spans fintech (PayPal, Affirm, Brex), health (Glow, Virta Health), and enterprise security (Sift Science, Very Good Security). The "analog-to-digital" pattern is more consistent than any sector label.

## In Their Own Words

On his entrepreneurship philosophy:

> "I think the hallmark of a really good entrepreneur is that you're not really going to build one specific company. The goal — at least the way I think about entrepreneurship — is you realize one day that you can't really work for anyone else. You have to start your own thing. It almost doesn't matter what that thing is." [^10]

On the future of data-driven businesses:

> "In the next decades we will see huge number of inherently analog processes captured digitally. Opportunities to build businesses that process this data and improve lives will abound." [^6]

On what he looks for in founders:

Levchin has stated he values technical depth over MBA credentials, obsession with specific problems rather than opportunistic market-chasing, and willingness to grind through complexity [^6]. He applies a founder-engineer's lens to investing, examining code quality, infrastructure robustness, and how data flows through systems during diligence [^5].

## What Founders Say

Suhail Doshi, founder of Mixpanel, on Levchin's role in the company's survival:

> "If Max and Michael didn't put in $500,000, there would be no Mixpanel." [^8]

> "Max has helped us with every funding round in the history of the company. I don't know if we'd be here without him, honestly." [^8]

Doshi has described how Levchin invested $500K (alongside Michael Birch) in Mixpanel's 2010 seed round after using the product at Slide, then brokered a deal with Sequoia for a $1.25M bridge round when Mixpanel struggled to find Series A investors in 2011, and later helped connect them with Andreessen Horowitz for a $10.25M Series A [^8].

Sadi Khan, CEO of Aven (SciFi VC portfolio company), noted the SciFi team was instrumental during formative stages, providing strategic guidance and network connections [^5].

Henrique Dubugras, Brex Founder/CEO, credited SciFi VC with helping structure scalable company operations and providing expertise across fintech, fundraising, and operational decisions [^5].

Mate Pencz, Loft Co-CEO, highlighted SciFi VC's understanding of marketplaces and lending dynamics across unit economics and capital markets [^5].

Note: The Aven, Brex, and Loft testimonials are sourced from the SciFi VC website and reflect firm-curated content rather than independently sourced quotes.
