## Background

Ethan Daly is a General Partner at Shine Capital, an early-stage venture capital firm based in New York and San Francisco [^1]. He joined Shine in 2020 as the firm's first investor hire and was promoted to General Partner in September 2025 [^1][^2].

Before Shine, Daly spent two years (2018-2020) at Allen & Company, the boutique investment bank, where he advised on M&A, business development, and equity financings for major technology and media clients including Snowflake, Twitter, Adobe, and Intel [^2][^3]. He graduated summa cum laude from The Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania with a B.S. in Economics and a Finance concentration [^2][^3].

Outside of investing, Daly runs Earshot, a creative studio and gallery space in New York City, alongside artists and curators [^2][^4]. This creative community hosts monthly gatherings described as "bookclub for artists & makers" and includes EarshotFM, a radio station [^4].

Shine Capital manages over $600 million across three early-stage funds and one opportunity fund, with offices in New York and San Francisco [^1]. The firm was founded by Mo Koyfman, a former General Partner at Spark Capital who led early-stage investments in Plaid and Warby Parker [^2].

## Stated Thesis

Daly has publicly described his investing approach as focused on "the intersection of technology and culture," stating: "Shine has given me the platform to do what I love — be a loyal partner to singular entrepreneurs, innovating at the intersection of technology and culture" [^1].

Mo Koyfman, Shine's founder, has described Daly as having "his finger on the pulse of the latest consumer trends" and being "equally thoughtful in crypto as he is in artificial intelligence" [^1]. Koyfman also noted that Daly is "well-respected in the entrepreneurial and creative communities" and has "unflinching loyalty and integrity" along with "a deliberate yet easy way with people, which enables him to strike a terrific balance with founders" [^1].

Shine Capital more broadly describes itself as "an early-stage venture capital firm that partners with creative, convincing, relentless entrepreneurs to build iconic businesses" [^5]. The firm invests in inception, seed, and Series A financings [^1]. According to Signal by NFX, Daly's investment range is $1M-$7M with a sweet spot around $5M, and his sector focus includes AI, Web3/Crypto, Payments, SaaS, SMB Software, FinTech, Enterprise Applications, and Enterprise Infrastructure [^3].

*With only 15 of ~49 investments verified in detail, the actual distribution may differ. The crypto/Web3 concentration is notable and consistent with Shine's stated focus. The AI and frontier technology investments suggest a broader "technology-meets-culture" thesis extending into deep tech.*

### Stage Distribution

- **Seed**: Approximately 10 of 15 verified investments (~67%) — consistent with Shine's stated focus on inception and seed
- **Series A**: Approximately 4 of 15 verified investments (~27%) — Gizmo, AudioShake, iVerify, Orchard Robotics
- **Series B+**: 1 of 15 verified investments (~7%) — Antares Series B, Numerai Series C

Shine Capital has made 14 investments in Seed stage with an average round size of $6.4M, 12 investments in Series A stage with an average round size of $17.6M, and 3 investments in Series B stage with an average round size of $26M, according to Tracxn [^6].

### Check Size and Round Leadership

The firm frequently leads or co-leads rounds. Verified lead investments include Bliss ($17.5M seed) [^7], Antares ($96M Series B) [^8], AudioShake ($14M Series A) [^9], Gizmo ($22M Series A) [^10], and co-led investments include Blackbird ($11M seed, co-led with Union Square Ventures and Multicoin) [^11] and Turnout ($21M seed, co-led with LGVP) [^12].

### Geographic Concentration

The portfolio is predominantly US-based, with companies in New York, San Francisco, and other US cities. Signal by NFX also notes activity in Canada and Israel [^3].

### Co-investor Patterns

Frequent co-investors across verified deals include Multicoin Capital (Blackbird, Hivemapper), NFX (Gizmo), General Catalyst (Orchard Robotics, Polimorphic), and a range of crypto-native funds for Web3 deals.

### Notable Gaps

Despite Shine's broad stated thesis, the verified portfolio shows limited healthcare/biotech investments (though the portfolio page lists Counter Health and Curie Bio). The consumer sector appears in the stated thesis but is underrepresented relative to crypto/Web3 in verified investments.
