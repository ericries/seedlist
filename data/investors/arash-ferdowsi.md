## Background

Arash Ferdowsi is an Iranian-American entrepreneur, angel investor, and Visiting Partner at Pear VC [^1][^2]. He is best known as the co-founder and former CTO of Dropbox, the cloud-based file storage and collaboration platform [^1][^3].

Ferdowsi grew up in Kansas, where he was valedictorian, before attending MIT to study Electrical Engineering and Computer Science [^1][^4]. He dropped out of MIT in 2007 to co-found Dropbox with Drew Houston after Houston's Y Combinator application caught his attention [^1][^3]. Ferdowsi served as Dropbox's CTO from 2007 to 2016, overseeing the company's technical development as it scaled to hundreds of millions of users [^1][^2]. He helped guide Dropbox through its 2018 IPO [^2].

Ferdowsi departed Dropbox in March 2020 [^2][^3]. Since leaving, he has become a prolific angel investor, building a portfolio of 80+ investments across pre-seed, seed, and Series A stages [^1][^4]. His portfolio includes 3 unicorns: Speak, Anysphere (maker of Cursor), and at least one other [^5].

In August 2023, Ferdowsi joined Pear VC as a Visiting Partner, where he works directly with Pear's portfolio founders [^2]. Pear's founding partner Pejman Nozad has known Ferdowsi since 2007 when Dropbox was just two people, and Pear was an early backer of Dropbox [^2]. Nozad described the relationship: "Over the last 15 years, Arash and I have truly built a great relationship on a foundation of true partnership" [^2].

## Stated Thesis

Ferdowsi's publicly stated investment focus centers on companies where complex engineering disappears behind an almost "magical" user experience — a philosophy directly rooted in his Dropbox experience [^1][^4]. He has described his core investment pillars as: technical leverage in AI, developer tooling, and infrastructure enabling 10x productivity; complex engineering hidden behind simple UX; and founders demonstrating strong EQ, recruiting craft, and growth mindset [^4].

He is known for integrating psychology tools like the Enneagram into his leadership approach and emphasizing mindfulness and sustainable founder performance [^1][^4]. He screens out "brilliant jerks" and looks for what he calls "conscious leadership" [^4].

Ferdowsi has stated his conviction that "the next giants will blend deep infrastructure leverage with founder cultures built for long-term learning and resilience" [^4].
