## Background

Dylan Field (born 1992) is an American technology entrepreneur and angel investor, best known as the co-founder and CEO of Figma, the collaborative web-based design tool [^1][^2]. Field grew up in Penngrove, California, the son of a respiratory therapist and a teacher [^1]. He was interested in computer science from a young age, participating in FIRST Robotics and acting in TV commercials as a child [^1][^2].

Field enrolled at Brown University in 2009 to study computer science [^1][^2]. While at Brown, he interned at LinkedIn and Flipboard (twice, through the Kleiner Perkins Fellows Program) [^1][^2]. At Brown he met Evan Wallace, who would become his co-founder [^1].

In May 2012, Field received a Thiel Fellowship -- a $100,000 grant conditioned on leaving school -- and dropped out of Brown to work on Figma full-time [^1][^2]. Field and Wallace spent four years building the product before its public launch in September 2016 [^2]. In September 2022, Adobe announced a $20 billion acquisition of Figma, but the deal was abandoned in December 2023 due to regulatory concerns [^1]. Figma went public in July 2025, reaching a valuation of approximately $68 billion [^2][^3].

Outside Figma, Field is an active angel investor with approximately 64 portfolio companies and 118 total investments across sectors including developer tools, fintech, AI, and crypto [^4][^5].

## Stated Thesis

Field has described his investing philosophy as rooted in design, product craft, and AI integration. He emphasizes "taste as a competitive advantage" and "multiplayer-native product design," drawing from his experience building Figma around real-time collaboration [^4]. He has stated that he works closely with founders on what he calls "vibe coding" and "exploring the idea maze," helping teams integrate AI into their core creation loops rather than treating it as a feature add-on [^4].

In a 2025 interview, Field said: "I like the investors; they are smart people. But what keeps me up at night is thinking about product, not share price" [^6]. He has also stated: "You don't go all AI or ignore AI; it's a hybrid approach where you work together. You steer the AI, adding your own taste and curation, pointing it in a direction" [^6].

Field's approach reflects an operator-investor model: he invests in companies where his experience building a design-centric, collaborative SaaS platform can provide direct value to founders [^4].
