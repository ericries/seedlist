## Background

Katelin Holloway is a Founding Partner at Seven Seven Six (776), the venture firm she co-founded with Reddit co-founder Alexis Ohanian in 2020 [^1]. She brings over 20 years of operational experience spanning creative leadership, hyper-growth startups, and institutional investing [^1].

Holloway's career began in unconventional roles: she worked as a public education teacher, a fingerprint specialist and assistant to a police chief in Humboldt County, and held positions in hedge funds and real estate before entering entertainment [^2]. She joined Pixar Animation Studios as a script supervisor, taking a pay cut for the opportunity to work at the studio during the era of Steve Jobs and Ed Catmull [^2]. She described the Pixar workplace as "quite literally puppy dogs and rainbows and candy" — a creative, childlike environment [^2].

After Pixar, Holloway worked at social influence platform Klout as an early employee (employee #21) before joining Reddit as Vice President of People & Culture in 2016 [^2] [^3]. At Reddit, she led the HR, Recruiting, and Experience teams, growing the company from 75 to nearly 750 employees internationally [^1]. Under her leadership, Reddit earned recognition as a Top 20 Company to Work For in Tech [^1].

In 2018, Holloway joined Initialized Capital (co-founded by Alexis Ohanian and Garry Tan) as an advisor to the portfolio, moving into a full-time Investing & Operating Partner role in January 2020 [^4] [^5]. She served as a strategic counselor to Initialized's founders through the early months of the COVID pandemic [^5]. In late 2020, she and Ohanian departed to launch Seven Seven Six [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Holloway publicly describes herself as a people-first investor who translates her operational HR background into early-stage venture capital. She has stated, "I pitched him [Ohanian] my thesis of having people really at the centre of early stage investing" [^6].

She frames early-stage investing as analogous to HR work: talent selection, development, team building, and exits [^6]. On her personal website, she says she is "passionate about funding and coaching the people-first companies that will shape the future of work, life, sustainability, and the delicate balance that enables us to thrive" [^1].

776 publicly describes itself as "thesis agnostic," prioritizing exceptional founders across industries rather than restricting investments to specific sectors [^7]. Holloway has stated: "My thesis around HR translating well to VC was proven to be correct. Early-stage investing is so much about the founders' agility and their ability to build a great team" [^3].

She has identified specific areas of interest including SpaceTech, DeepTech, ClimateTech, HRTech, HealthTech, FinTech, SportsTech, Creator Economy, AI, Consumer, and Next Gen Media & Entertainment [^1].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 18 verified investments where Holloway has personal involvement as lead investor or board member, the following patterns emerge. Note: 776 as a firm has made 147 investments per Tracxn [^8], but attributing specific deals to Holloway vs. Ohanian is only possible for companies where she is named as lead or board member. This analysis is based on those 18 verified Holloway-attributed investments.

**Sector distribution (18 verified investments):**
- Space/DeepTech: 5 of 18 (28%) — Stoke Space, Interlune, LambdaVision, Starcloud, Monumental Labs
- Climate/Sustainability: 1 of 18 (6%) — Mast Reforestation
- Health/Women's Health: 2 of 18 (11%) — Elektra Health, Poppy Seed Health
- Education: 1 of 18 (6%) — Prenda
- Media/Entertainment/Consumer: 3 of 18 (17%) — Invisible Universe, Doodles, Los Angeles Golf Club
- Enterprise/SaaS: 2 of 18 (11%) — Lattice, Databricks (angel)
- Consumer Tech: 3 of 18 (17%) — GoPuff, Clubhouse, Intro
- Other: 1 of 18 (6%) — Hgen

**Stage distribution:** Holloway writes checks ranging from $750K to $4M, with a target of $2M per Signal NFX [^9]. The firm overall has made 44 seed investments, 26 Series A investments, and 7 Series B investments [^8]. Her personal board seats span seed through Series A companies.

**Notable patterns:**
- **Deep tech conviction:** Space and deep tech represent the largest single category (28%) of her attributed investments, despite 776 claiming to be thesis-agnostic. This is a significant gap between stated and revealed preference.
- **Board-heavy approach:** Holloway holds board seats at 8 portfolio companies (Intro, Los Angeles Golf Club, Interlune, Doodles, Invisible Universe, Mast Reforestation, Prenda, Elektra Health), suggesting a high-touch, operator-involved investing style [^1].
- **Women's health and family focus:** Investments in Elektra Health (menopause care) and Poppy Seed Health (pregnancy/maternal health) reflect a personal interest area. Poppy Seed Health was her first-ever angel investment, made alongside her mother [^10].
- **Angel-to-institutional pipeline:** Several investments (Databricks, Lattice, GoPuff, Clubhouse, Poppy Seed Health) began as personal angel investments before 776 existed. Poppy Seed Health was her first angel check and later her first institutional 776 check [^10].

**Co-investor patterns:** 776's frequent co-investors include Aurelia Foundry (LambdaVision), Social Capital (Mast Reforestation), Flare Capital Partners (Elektra Health), and Learn Capital (Prenda).

**Geographic focus:** Primarily US-based companies, with concentration in San Francisco Bay Area and broader US.

**Sample size caveat:** This analysis covers only 18 investments attributable to Holloway personally out of 776's 147 total firm investments. The true distribution of her personal focus may differ.
