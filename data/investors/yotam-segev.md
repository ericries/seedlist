## Background

Yotam Segev is the co-founder and CEO of Cyera, a data security company valued at $9 billion as of January 2026 [^1]. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Physics from The Hebrew University of Jerusalem (2010-2013) [^2]. He attended the Mandel Leadership Institute for Educational Leadership in the Military (2017-2018) and was a Nativ Program Fellow at IDEA - Israeli Center for Liberal Democracy, studying Political Science and Government (2019-2020) [^2].

Segev's career began in the Israeli Defense Forces, where he served in the elite Talpiot Leadership Academy as a Senior Class Commander [^3]. He then served in Israeli Military Intelligence Unit 8200, where he held roles including Cyber Team Lead (2015-2016), Cyber Course Instructor (2015), and Head of the Cyber Department (2018-2020) [^2]. Together with his future co-founder Tamar Bar-Ilan, he founded, built, and ran Unit 8200's cloud security division [^3].

In 2021, Segev and Bar-Ilan co-founded Cyera [^3]. Before building the product, they interviewed over 100 CISOs to understand pain points in data security [^4]. Cyera emerged from stealth in March 2022 with $60 million in funding led by Sequoia Capital, alongside Accel and Cyberstarts [^3]. The company has since raised over $1.7 billion in total funding across six rounds, reaching a $9 billion valuation in its January 2026 Series F led by Blackstone Growth [^1]. Investors include Sequoia Capital, Accel, Cyberstarts, Coatue, Redpoint, Lightspeed Venture Partners, Greenoaks, Georgian, Sapphire Ventures, and Spark [^1]. Segev was named a Sequoia Capital founder in 2022 [^5]. Cyera employs approximately 1,100 people as of January 2026 [^6].

Segev is primarily known as an operator and founder rather than a professional investor. His angel investing activity is limited and focused exclusively on Israeli cybersecurity startups founded by fellow Unit 8200 and Talpiot alumni.

## Stated Thesis

Segev has not published a formal investment thesis. His angel investments appear to be driven by personal relationships within the Israeli cybersecurity community rather than a systematic investment strategy. Based on his public statements, his worldview centers on data security as the foundational layer of enterprise AI adoption.

On the importance of data security, Segev has stated: "Data needs to be everywhere. Data is being used by every business unit, and data is driving tremendous business value" [^7]. He has described AI security as "where the biggest gap is today for enterprises," noting that companies "just have no control over their data, and AI runs on data" [^8].

His angel investments in companies like Daylight Security (AI-native managed security) and Spirit (enterprise information security) suggest a preference for cybersecurity companies that apply AI to security operations, founded by teams with Israeli military intelligence backgrounds.

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 3 verified angel investments, the sample size is too small for reliable percentage breakdowns. Qualitative observations only:

**Sector focus:** All 3 verified investments are in cybersecurity/security operations. No investments found outside the security domain.

**Stage distribution:** All verified investments are at seed stage, though Segev also participated in Daylight Security's Series A follow-on [^9].

**Geographic focus:** All 3 portfolio companies are Israeli-founded cybersecurity startups.

**Founder profile pattern:** All known portfolio company founders are graduates of the IDF's Talpiot program and/or Unit 8200 alumni — the same military programs where Segev served. This is a strong signal of network-driven investing.

**Co-investor patterns:** Segev frequently co-invests alongside other Israeli cybersecurity founder-CEOs, particularly Assaf Rappaport (Wiz) and the Torq co-founders (Ofer Smadari, Leonid Belkind, Eldad Livni). His Cyera co-founder Tamar Bar-Ilan also participates in the same rounds [^9][^10].

**Investment pace:** With only 3 verified investments over approximately 2 years (2025-2026), Segev appears to be a very selective, occasional angel investor rather than an active or prolific one. His primary focus remains building and scaling Cyera.

**Notable context:** Segev's angel investing is best understood as a byproduct of his position as a leading Israeli cybersecurity CEO. He invests in founders he knows personally from military service, not as a deliberate portfolio-building exercise.
