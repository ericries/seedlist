## Background

Nathaniel Dourif Friedman was born on August 6, 1977, and grew up in Charlottesville, Virginia [^1][^2]. He has been online since 1991 and befriended Miguel de Icaza in 1996 while a freshman at MIT on LinuxNet, an IRC network that Friedman had created to discuss Linux [^1][^2].

Friedman studied Computer Science and Mathematics at MIT, graduating with a Bachelor of Science degree in 1999 [^1][^3].

In 1999, Friedman co-founded Ximian (originally called International Gnome Support, then Helix Code) with Miguel de Icaza to develop applications and infrastructure for the GNOME desktop environment [^1][^2]. Ximian was acquired by Novell in 2003, where Friedman served as CTO of Open Source from 2003 to 2009 [^1][^3].

In May 2011, Friedman and de Icaza co-founded Xamarin, with Friedman as CEO [^1][^2]. Xamarin developed cross-platform mobile development tools using Mono. Xamarin was acquired by Microsoft in 2016 [^1][^2].

Following the Xamarin acquisition, Friedman served as Corporate Vice President of Developer Services at Microsoft from 2016 to 2018 [^3]. When Microsoft acquired GitHub for $7.5 billion in 2018, Friedman was named GitHub's CEO, assuming the role on October 29, 2018 [^1][^2].

During his tenure as GitHub CEO, Friedman introduced GitHub Copilot, GitHub Codespaces, a native mobile app for iOS and Android, GitHub Advanced Security, GitHub Sponsors, and a new GitHub CLI [^2]. He stepped down as CEO in November 2021 and served as Chairman Emeritus through November 2022 [^1][^3].

After leaving GitHub, Friedman became a prolific angel investor and co-founded NFDG with Daniel Gross in 2023, a $1.1 billion AI-focused venture fund [^4][^5]. With only approximately 50% of the fund deployed, NFDG achieved roughly 4x returns — from approximately $550 million deployed to approximately $2.2 billion in portfolio value [^5].

In June 2025, Friedman joined Meta Platforms as Vice President of Product and Applied Research at the newly established Meta Superintelligence Labs, alongside Alexandr Wang [^6][^7]. Meta agreed to acquire a substantial portion of NFDG's holdings — potentially more than $1 billion — to provide liquidity to the fund's limited partners [^5][^6].

Friedman is also co-founder and chairman of California YIMBY, a housing advocacy organization, and serves on the board of the Arc Institute [^3]. He is involved in the Herculaneum Papyri project, using AI to read ancient scrolls [^2].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Friedman says publicly about his approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Friedman has articulated his investing and building philosophy through his personal website and interviews:

- **Speed as core principle:** Friedman has written: "It's important to do things fast. You learn more per unit time because you make contact with reality more frequently" and "Going fast makes you focus on what's important; there's no time for bullshit" [^8][^9].

- **Small teams:** He advocates for smaller organizations: "Smaller teams are better. Faster decisions, fewer meetings, more fun" [^2][^8].

- **Excellence over safety:** Friedman has stated: "The goal is not to avoid mistakes; the goal is to achieve uncorrelated levels of excellence in some dimension" [^8][^9].

- **Market inefficiency:** He believes "the efficient market hypothesis is flawed" and the best opportunities exist where markets misjudge value [^2][^8].

- **AI conviction:** Friedman has stated that "AI is clearly more capable than humans now" and that founders who combine high-quality data, compute, and talent will outpace competitors [^8][^9].

- **Enthusiasm as signal:** He has written: "Enthusiasm matters! It's much easier to work on exciting things" and "It might be easier to do big things than small things" [^8].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on analysis of 22 verified investments in the table below. CB Insights reports 97+ companies in Friedman's portfolio; other sources cite 100+ [^10][^11].

**Stage focus:**
- Through NFDG, Friedman invested across seed through growth stages, with check sizes ranging from $1 million to $100 million [^3][^4]. As an individual angel, he made smaller early-stage bets.

**Sector concentration (of 22 verified investments):**
- AI & machine learning: 11 companies (50%) — Perplexity, Character.ai, Safe Superintelligence, ElevenLabs, Pika, Magic, Suno, EvolutionaryScale, The Bot Company, Keen Technologies, Pulse [^4][^10]
- Developer tools & infrastructure: 6 companies (27%) — Retool, Vercel, Sentry, Streamlit, Weights & Biases, ggml.ai [^10]
- Enterprise SaaS: 3 companies (14%) — Scale AI, Klarity, Pilot [^10]
- Other (fintech, design): 2 companies (9%) — Stripe, Figma [^4]

**Note:** This sample represents approximately 22% of Friedman's 100+ investments, so percentages are indicative. However, the heavy AI concentration (50% of verified investments) strongly reflects NFDG's explicit AI focus.

**Co-investor patterns:**
- Friedman's primary co-investing partner is Daniel Gross (NFDG co-founder and former Y Combinator partner) [^4][^5].
- Frequent co-investors include: John and Patrick Collison, Elad Gil, Sequoia Capital, Andreessen Horowitz [^10].

**Geographic patterns:**
- Predominantly San Francisco Bay Area companies, consistent with Friedman's location and NFDG's SF headquarters [^3].

**Distinctive patterns:**
- **AI-first strategy:** NFDG was among the most concentrated AI-focused funds at the $1B+ scale, betting heavily on the AI wave during 2023-2024 [^4][^5].
- **Infrastructure provision:** Beyond capital, NFDG built the Andromeda Cluster — a supercomputer with 3,200 H100 GPUs — for portfolio companies to use, addressing the GPU shortage [^5].
- **AI Grant program:** Friedman and Gross ran an AI grant program offering $250K in funding and $250K in Microsoft Azure credits to AI startups [^5].
- **Operator-investor hybrid:** Friedman's deep technical background (founding two developer tools companies, running GitHub) gives him distinctive credibility with technical founders.
