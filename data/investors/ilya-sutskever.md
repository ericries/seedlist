## Background

Ilya Sutskever was born in 1986 in Nizhny Novgorod, Russia, and emigrated to Israel at age 5 before moving to Canada at age 16 [^1]. He studied at the University of Toronto, earning a bachelor's degree in mathematics (2005), a master's in computer science (2007), and a PhD in computer science (2013) under Geoffrey Hinton [^1].

With Alex Krizhevsky and Hinton, Sutskever co-invented AlexNet, the convolutional neural network that catalyzed the deep learning revolution [^1]. He co-founded DNNresearch, which was acquired by Google in 2013, after which he became a research scientist at Google Brain [^1]. At Google Brain, he co-created the sequence-to-sequence learning algorithm and contributed to TensorFlow [^1].

In late 2015, Sutskever left Google to co-found OpenAI, where he served as chief scientist [^1]. He oversaw the research breakthroughs behind GPT models and ChatGPT [^1]. He has won the NeurIPS Test of Time Award three consecutive times (2022-2024) [^1].

In November 2023, Sutskever was among OpenAI board members who briefly ousted CEO Sam Altman; Altman was reinstated a week later, and Sutskever stepped down from the board [^1]. In May 2024, he announced his departure from OpenAI [^1]. In June 2024, Sutskever co-founded Safe Superintelligence Inc. (SSI) with Daniel Gross and Daniel Levy, focused on developing safe superintelligent AI [^2] [^3]. SSI raised $1 billion in September 2024 from Andreessen Horowitz, Sequoia Capital, DST Global, and SV Angel at a $5 billion valuation [^3] [^4]. In early 2025, SSI raised an additional $2 billion at a $32 billion valuation, reportedly led by Greenoaks [^5].

Sutskever is primarily an AI researcher and company builder rather than an active angel investor. His small angel portfolio reflects personal conviction bets in AI-adjacent hardware and tools [^6].

## Stated Thesis

Sutskever has not publicly articulated a formal angel investment thesis. His public statements focus on building safe superintelligence rather than investing.

On his company's mission, Sutskever has described SSI as focused on a single goal: safely developing superintelligent AI [^2]. He has stated that the commercial "rat race" in AI forces compromises on safety and research depth, and that by insulating his team from market pressures, they can solve harder problems [^7].

On AI development, Sutskever has said: "2012 to 2020 was an age of research, 2020 to 2025 was an age of scaling, and 2026 onward will be another age of research" [^7]. He believes that scaling alone is insufficient and that fundamental research breakthroughs are still needed for true intelligence [^7].

## In Their Own Words

Sutskever has stated that the era of pure scaling is ending: "Scaling sucked the air out of the room" [^7].

On LLM limitations, he has said that LLMs' generalization ability is "inadequate" and expressed confusion about why they perform well on benchmarks yet "fail so badly in practical applications" [^7].

On the future of AI, Sutskever has stated: "Scaling the current thing will keep leading to improvements...But something important will continue to be missing" [^7].

On SSI's approach, he has said: "Right now, we just focus on the research, and then the answer to this question will reveal itself" regarding future monetization [^9].

On avoiding competitive pressures: "It's very nice to not be affected by the day-to-day market competition" [^9].

He has described his vision for superintelligence as producing "a superintelligent 15-year-old that's very eager to go" — a system capable of learning rather than one that is omniscient [^9].
