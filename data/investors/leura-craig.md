## Background

Leura Craig (née Fine) is the Managing Partner and co-founder of Outlander VC, an early-stage venture capital firm she co-founded in 2020 with her husband Paige Craig [^1][^2]. She is based in Atlanta, Georgia, where Outlander is headquartered [^1].

Before entering venture capital, Craig was a professional interior designer who worked as a senior designer at a leading interior design firm [^3][^4]. She founded Leura Fine Interiors in 2013 before launching Laurel & Wolf in late 2014, an online interior design marketplace that connected clients with professional designers through a digital platform [^3][^4][^5]. Laurel & Wolf raised over $25 million in venture capital from Benchmark, Charles River Ventures (CRV), and Upside Partnership [^3][^5][^6]. The company scaled to over 60 employees and more than 1,000 designers within two years of launch [^6]. Laurel & Wolf shut down in March 2019 after struggles with customer satisfaction and operational challenges [^7].

Craig was recognized as a Forbes 30 Under 30 honoree in the Retail & eCommerce category and was named one of Goldman Sachs' Builders + Innovators 100 "Most Intriguing Entrepreneurs" [^3][^4]. She attended McGill University [^8].

After a decade in Los Angeles building companies and investing, Craig and Paige Craig relocated to Atlanta, where they discovered an underserved startup ecosystem with founders lacking early-stage capital and connections to major tech hubs [^1]. At Outlander, Craig focuses on identifying and supporting early-stage founders, with particular emphasis on backing entrepreneurs from underrepresented groups and non-traditional markets [^1][^9]. Since 2015, she has invested in dozens of early-stage companies and advises founding teams on product strategy, operations, customer growth, design, hiring, and achieving product-market fit [^9].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Craig says publicly about her investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Craig publicly describes her investment focus areas as including the future of work, future of commerce, hardware and robotics, and AI [^9][^10]. She has stated: "The future of commerce will not be won through keywords and impressions, but through influence, context, and trust" [^11].

Craig has emphasized the importance of diversity in investment decision-making: "At Outlander, we believe that who sits at the decision-making table matters. We believe that one of the best ways to diversify the tech ecosystem is to diversify who is writing the checks, so that is exactly what we did" [^12].

As a former founder, Craig brings an operator's perspective to investing. She has said: "one of the things I work on with Outlander's portfolio companies that I learned as a founder is _ruthlessly_ prioritizing where you invest your energy" [^12]. She advises founders that "the most successful pitches all boil down to one major imperative: a compelling narrative arc" and that "who you're pitching to should inform your tone and diction in each iteration" [^13].

Craig and Outlander use a proprietary "Outlander Founder Framework" — originally 38 characteristics, now refined to approximately 20 points — organized across four dimensions: Vision, Intelligence, Character, and Execution [^2][^14]. The firm writes initial checks of $500K to $2.5M at pre-seed and seed stages [^2].

## Inferred Thesis

Craig's personal investment behavior is difficult to separate from the broader Outlander VC portfolio, as she operates as co-founder and Managing Partner alongside Paige Craig. The firm-level portfolio analysis (see the Outlander VC firm profile) provides the most complete picture. Based on the firm's 35 verified investments and Craig's publicly highlighted deals, several patterns emerge specific to her focus:

**Sector emphasis:** Craig's stated focus on "future of commerce" and consumer/marketplace businesses aligns with her background as a consumer startup founder. Her highlighted investments include Talli (baby care hardware + software), City Shoppe (local commerce, OutPitch 2021 winner), and Barometer (AI-powered brand suitability for advertising) [^15][^16][^11]. These suggest a particular affinity for commerce infrastructure, consumer marketplaces, and consumer hardware — areas informed by her experience building Laurel & Wolf.

**Stage distribution:** Overwhelmingly pre-seed and seed, consistent with the firm's stated focus. Signal by NFX lists her check size range as $750K to $2M with a target of $1M [^17].

**Founder profile preferences:** Craig's operator background shapes her preference for founders with deep domain expertise and personal connection to the problem they are solving. She noted about Talli founder Lauren Longo: "When you're solving a problem you've faced, the motivation and drive behind your venture is extremely personal" [^15]. The firm reports that 62% of its portfolio companies have diverse founders [^18].

**Geographic focus:** Craig and Outlander invest nationally but have particular strength in markets outside traditional tech hubs, reflecting their Atlanta base. OutPitch winners have come from Austin, TX (City Shoppe) and other non-coastal markets [^16].

**Co-investor patterns:** As a firm, Outlander frequently co-invests with defense-focused firms like Harpoon Ventures and deep-tech investors, though Craig's personal focus areas skew more toward commerce and consumer deals.

**Notable observation:** Craig's stated interest in "future of commerce" and consumer/marketplace sectors is a genuine differentiator from the broader Outlander portfolio, which has increasingly tilted toward defense tech and hardware under Paige Craig and Jordan Kretchmer's influence. Her commerce and consumer expertise provides balance to the firm's thesis.
