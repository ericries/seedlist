## Background

Joel Larsson is a General Partner and Co-Founder of Pale Blue Dot, a seed-stage climate tech venture capital firm based in Malmö, Sweden [^1][^2]. He holds an MSc in environmental engineering from Lund University [^3][^4]. Before founding Pale Blue Dot, Larsson was a software engineer who transitioned into startup founding [^5]. He co-founded Fast Track Malmö, a startup accelerator launched in 2015 in collaboration with ALMI Invest, Minc, Malmö Startups, and Invest in Skåne [^6]. The accelerator invested 200,000 SEK (approximately €21,000) in each cohort company and ran five-month programs [^6].

Larsson co-founded Pale Blue Dot in 2020 alongside Hampus Jakobsson (formerly co-founder of The Astonishing Tribe, sold to BlackBerry, and venture partner at BlueYard Capital) and Heidi Lindvall (former startup founder with a background in human rights and former head of the accelerator and investment team at Fast Track Malmö) [^2][^7][^8]. Jakobsson had previously angel-invested in one of Larsson's companies, which is how the two met [^8].

Larsson has spoken at the NORDEEP (Nordic Deep Tech Business Summit) in 2022 [^5].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Larsson and Pale Blue Dot say publicly about their investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Pale Blue Dot describes itself as backing "the most exciting climate tech startups across Europe and the US" at the pre-seed and seed stage [^1]. The firm organizes investments under three pillars: Optimize (decarbonizing existing systems), Adapt (climate resilience technologies), and Pioneer (breakthrough sustainability innovations) [^9][^10].

Larsson and his co-founders have stated that they view climate not as a sector but as a worldview and "aperture" through which to evaluate investments [^11]. In a 2026 newsletter, the co-founders collectively stated: "Climate as a concept has become politicised, limiting and narrow" and "The word climate has become synonymous with a very narrow set of problems," explaining their decision to move away from the "climate-only" label [^10].

Larsson has stated that he believes "we need both degrowth ideas and venture-scale technical solutions to solve the climate crisis" [^3][^4]. The firm looks for "highly scalable tech companies with a positive climate impact" and emphasizes that the startups they invest in "are powered by the same forces as many internet companies; software, data and network effects" [^7].

The firm prefers to lead rounds, writing checks between €500,000 and €2 million, targeting approximately 10% ownership at seed stage [^7][^11]. Within the partnership, Larsson's investment focus is described as seeking solutions that "unlock adjacent markets" [^11].

During Fast Track Malmö, Larsson emphasized inclusive investing: "We are working to have a program that is directed as much to men as to women and to different nationalities" [^6].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 25 verified investments by Pale Blue Dot (see Portfolio below), the following patterns emerge. Note: individual deal attribution to specific GPs is not publicly available for most investments — this analysis reflects the firm's collective portfolio, which Larsson co-leads.

**Stage distribution:** Predominantly seed-stage. Of 25 verified investments: approximately 19 at Seed (76%), 3 at Pre-Seed (12%), and 3 at Series A (12%). The Series A investments (Monta, Paebbl, Solarock) appear to be follow-on rounds from earlier seed investments, consistent with the firm's stated 50% follow-on reserve [^7].

**Sector breakdown (based on 25 verified investments):**
- EV/mobility/transport: 5 of 25 (20%) — Monta, Pelikan Mobility, PIONIX, JaroFleet, Ember
- Climate data/analytics/nature tech: 5 of 25 (20%) — Overstory, Climate X, Pivotal, Nala Earth, ClimateAligned
- Food/agriculture: 3 of 25 (12%) — Phytoform Labs, Improvin', AgroLeague
- Circular economy/materials: 3 of 25 (12%) — Cirplus, Paebbl, CBAMBOO
- Energy: 2 of 25 (8%) — Solarock, Podero
- Sustainable finance/fintech: 2 of 25 (8%) — Green-Got, Sphere
- Supply chain/logistics: 2 of 25 (8%) — HIVED, Infyos
- Other (consumer, fashion, etc.): 3 of 25 (12%) — Atlas.co, Varm, Maiven

**Geographic focus:** Heavily European with a Nordic tilt. Portfolio companies are based in the UK (HIVED, Climate X, Infyos, Phytoform), France (Green-Got, Pelikan Mobility, Solarock), Denmark (Monta), Germany (Cirplus), Netherlands (Overstory), and various Nordic countries. The firm accepts US deals but the portfolio skews strongly European.

**Check size:** €200K-€2M at seed, with the firm targeting 10% ownership [^7][^11]. Average seed round size across portfolio is approximately $3.56M, suggesting Pale Blue Dot typically co-invests rather than funding entire rounds solo [^12].

**Founder profile patterns:** The co-founders are all former startup founders themselves, and the firm emphasizes backing founders they view as "leaders of tomorrow" and people they would "entrust their children with" [^11]. The firm tracks diverse founding teams and female founders as explicit priorities [^5][^6].

**Co-investor patterns:** Frequent co-investors include Seedcamp (Pelikan Mobility), byFounders (Monta), Eka Ventures (HIVED), and Eniac Ventures (Phytoform). The LP base includes Prima Materia (Daniel Ek's investment vehicle), Atomico, Ramsbury Invest (H&M family office), and founders from Contentful, Supercell, Zendesk, Wise, and Delivery Hero [^7][^13].

**Notable gaps:** Despite stating an openness to US deals, the portfolio is overwhelmingly European. The "Pioneer" pillar (breakthrough innovations) appears underrepresented relative to "Optimize" (decarbonization of existing systems), which dominates the portfolio.

## In Their Own Words

Larsson and his co-founders have jointly stated: "Climate as a concept has become politicised, limiting and narrow" and "The word climate has become synonymous with a very narrow set of problems" — explaining their decision to broaden their thesis framing beyond the climate label [^10].

Larsson has stated that he believes "we need both degrowth ideas and venture-scale technical solutions to solve the climate crisis" [^3][^4].

Co-founder Hampus Jakobsson, speaking about the firm's approach, stated: "Receiving interest and recognition from the industry is good, but the fact that amazing founders keep picking us to be their partners on their journeys is the biggest vote of confidence and why we get up in the morning" [^13].

Jakobsson also stated that the firm aims to "pick the people who will run the future of the world" and that they must be "hyper responsible" in doing so [^7].

On the firm's decision-making process, the partners use a voting system: -1 (hell no), 0 (no), 1 (yes), 2 (hell yes). An investment requires one "hell yes" and zero "hell no" votes, emphasizing individual GP conviction over consensus [^11].

On Fast Track Malmö, Larsson stated: "We are working to have a program that is directed as much to men as to women and to different nationalities" [^6].
