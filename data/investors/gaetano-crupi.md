## Background

Gaetano Crupi is the Managing Director at US Innovative Technology Fund (USIT), a position he has held since 2023 [^1][^2]. USIT is a Pittsburgh-based venture capital firm founded by Thomas Tull that targets $3-5 billion to invest in dual-use technology companies serving both commercial and defense markets [^3].

Prior to USIT, Crupi was a Partner at Prime Movers Lab, a deep-tech venture capital firm, where he focused on growth-stage investments from January 2021 through the end of 2022 [^4][^5]. At Prime Movers Lab, he led or participated in investments across quantum computing, materials science, biotech, food-tech, and climate technology [^6][^7][^8].

Before entering venture capital, Crupi was an entrepreneur. He co-founded Cabin Technologies, a hospitality transportation startup that offered overnight sleep-pod bus service between cities, and served as its CEO [^9][^10]. Cabin, which was itself a Prime Movers Lab portfolio company, transported over 10,000 passengers and developed patented "Cabin Cloud" suspension technology before pausing service [^11][^12]. Prior to Cabin, Crupi served as Chief Operating Officer of Betable, a real-money gaming platform, from September 2012 to August 2016 [^13]. He began his career as an Analyst in consumer and retail investment banking at Goldman Sachs from 2004 to 2006 [^13][^14].

Crupi holds a BA in International Studies and a BS from the Huntsman Program at the Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania (2000-2004), and an MBA from Stanford Graduate School of Business (2011-2012) [^13][^14].

## Stated Thesis

At USIT, Crupi has publicly emphasized infrastructure as a defining investment theme. Regarding AI compute, he stated: "The most valuable infrastructure will convert kilowatts into tokens with minimal friction as AI scales" [^15]. On sovereign compute, he noted: "Compute infrastructure stands at a major inflection point, with many organisations being forced to rethink the viability of outsourcing such a critical part of their value chain to public clouds" [^16].

During his time at Prime Movers Lab, Crupi described his investment philosophy as centered on transformative deep technology. On quantum computing, he stated: "Quantum computing will impact billions of lives, revolutionizing everything from drug discovery and financial modeling, to predicting climate change and the development of new, life-changing materials" [^6]. On materials science, he wrote: "What MycoWorks has achieved with its Fine Mycelium platform is not just a breakthrough, it is a revolution for industries that are ripe for change" [^7].

In his blog series "What Founders Get Wrong About Series B," Crupi described himself as having "been an investor for two years after 15 years of entrepreneurship" and emphasized that Series B fundraising requires emotional conviction: "Every deal that someone is championing means less attention in other places. We have never done a deal that one partner was not truly PASSIONATE about" [^17].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 10 verified investments across two firms (USIT and Prime Movers Lab):

**Stage distribution:** Crupi invests across a wide range of stages but skews toward growth. At USIT, his verified investments are all Series B or later: Gecko Robotics (Series C extension), Oxide Computer (Series B and Series C), and Lambda (Series E). At Prime Movers Lab, he invested earlier: Bandit (Seed), Atom Computing (Series B), MycoWorks (Series C), and Gilgamesh Pharmaceuticals (Series B). Of 10 verified investments: 1 seed (10%), 3 Series B (30%), 3 Series C (30%), 1 Series D (10%), 1 Series E (10%), 1 growth equity (10%).

**Sector breakdown:** Deep technology dominates. Of 10 verified investments: 3 are defense-tech/infrastructure (Gecko Robotics, Oxide Computer x2 — 30%); 2 are AI infrastructure/compute (Lambda, Oxide Computer counted above); 1 is quantum computing (Atom Computing — 10%); 1 is materials science (MycoWorks — 10%); 1 is biotech/pharma (Gilgamesh Pharmaceuticals — 10%); 1 is food-tech (Bandit — 10%); 1 is climate/floating infrastructure (OCEANIX — 10%); 1 is AI compute (Lambda — 10%). The common thread is hard-tech companies building physical products or infrastructure, not pure software.

**Check size:** Varies enormously by platform. At Prime Movers Lab, investments ranged from $1.5M (Bandit seed) to participation in a $125M round (MycoWorks Series C). At USIT, he has led or participated in rounds from $100M (Oxide Computer Series B, Gecko Robotics Series C extension) to $200M (Oxide Computer Series C) and $1.5B+ (Lambda Series E) [^15][^16][^18].

**Board involvement:** High. Crupi takes board seats at portfolio companies. Verified board seats include Gecko Robotics, Bandit, Atom Computing, MycoWorks, OCEANIX, and Gilgamesh Pharmaceuticals [^8][^19][^6][^7][^20][^21]. This is 6 board seats across 10 verified investments, indicating an active, hands-on approach.

**Founder profile pattern:** Crupi gravitates toward technical founders building category-defining physical products: Jake Loosararian at Gecko Robotics (robotics for infrastructure inspection), Rob Hays at Atom Computing (nuclear physicist turned quantum CEO), Matt Scullin at MycoWorks (materials science PhD), and Bryan Cantrill and Steve Tuck at Oxide Computer (systems engineering veterans) [^6][^7][^16][^19].

**Geographic concentration:** All verified investments are U.S.-based companies.

**Co-investor patterns:** At USIT, frequently co-invests with Founders Fund (Gecko Robotics, Anduril), Eclipse (Oxide Computer), and ARK Invest (Shield AI). At Prime Movers Lab, co-invested with Third Point Ventures (Atom Computing), Innovation Endeavors, and Venrock [^6][^19].

**Notable shift:** Crupi's move from Prime Movers Lab to USIT in 2023 marked a significant shift in check size and sector focus — from seed-to-Series-B deep tech across diverse sectors (food-tech, biotech, materials) to growth-stage defense-tech and AI infrastructure with check sizes 10-100x larger.
