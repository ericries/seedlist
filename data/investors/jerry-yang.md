## Background

Jerry Chih-Yuan Yang is a Taiwanese-born American entrepreneur, investor, and co-founder of Yahoo! [^1][^2]. Born on November 6, 1968, in Taipei, Taiwan, Yang's father died when he was two [^1]. In 1978, his mother — a professor of English and drama — moved the family to San Jose, California, where extended family helped raise the boys while she taught English to other immigrants [^1].

Yang earned a B.S. and M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Stanford University [^1][^2]. While pursuing doctoral studies at Stanford in 1994, Yang and fellow student David Filo co-created "Jerry and David's Guide to the World Wide Web," an Internet directory that became Yahoo! Inc. in 1995 [^1][^2].

Yang served in various roles at Yahoo!, including as CEO from June 2007 to January 2009 [^1]. One of his most consequential decisions at Yahoo! was directing the 2005 purchase of a 40% stake in Alibaba Group for $1 billion plus Yahoo! China's assets (valued at $700 million) — an investment that would prove to be worth tens of billions of dollars [^1].

After leaving Yahoo!, Yang founded AME Cloud Ventures, a venture fund backed entirely by his personal capital rather than traditional limited partners [^3][^4]. The fund has a reported size of approximately $500 million [^4]. AME Cloud Ventures is headquartered in Midtown Palo Alto [^3].

Yang serves on the boards of Workday, Lenovo Group, and Alibaba Group [^1][^2]. He is chair of The Stanford Board of Trustees [^1][^2]. He is also a member of the Committee of 100, an organization of distinguished Chinese Americans [^5].

## Stated Thesis

AME Cloud Ventures publicly focuses on "seed to later stage companies building infrastructure and value chains around data" [^3]. The firm targets "technology heavy companies gathering or creating unique data at every stage of the data stack — from infrastructure all the way to applications, mobile, and sensors" [^3].

Yang started AME Cloud Ventures with the belief that "data, cloud, and hardware advances will create unprecedented opportunities for great companies to be built" [^3].

The fund follows what has been described as an "anti-Yahoo playbook" — three strict rules: avoid consumer internet, invest at the earliest stages, and back only data-driven businesses with deep science or tech [^4].

On AI, Yang has stated: "I think the AI wave is coming" and has reflected that "we've seen a few waves of technology, and with each one of these waves, [we] go through this hype cycle" [^6].

- AME Cloud Ventures has achieved 17 unicorns, 15 IPOs, and 82 acquisitions across its portfolio [^8].

**Co-investor patterns:** AME Cloud Ventures' portfolio companies have raised follow-on financing from Benchmark Capital, Khosla Ventures, Accel Partners, and other top-tier firms [^3].
