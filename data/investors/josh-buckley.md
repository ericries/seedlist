## Background

Josh Buckley is an entrepreneur and investor originally from Kent, England, now based in San Francisco [^1]. He sold his first company at age 15 [^2]. In 2011, he co-founded Mino Games, a mobile gaming studio backed by Y Combinator (W11) and Andreessen Horowitz, where he served as CEO until 2019 [^1][^2]. Mino Games grew to 70 employees and over $20 million in annual revenue, with its flagship title Mino Monsters reaching the top 25 on the App Store with 40 million-plus downloads [^2].

Buckley served as Chairman of Clearbit from 2014 to 2020; Clearbit was later acquired by HubSpot [^1][^3]. He served as CEO of Product Hunt from 2020 to 2021 [^1][^3].

Buckley now runs Buckley Ventures, which manages over $1 billion in assets across multiple funds and makes concentrated investments in early-stage companies across internet, software, and frontier technology [^4]. In October 2024, Buckley Ventures filed to raise $250 million for its fourth fund [^3]. The firm operates offices in New York and San Francisco [^4].

## Stated Thesis

Buckley Ventures describes its approach as partnering closely with founders and leveraging a diverse network including "entrepreneurs, investors, executives, engineers, and academics to help companies solve problems and grow their businesses" [^4]. The firm focuses on concentrated investments in early-stage companies across internet, software, and frontier technology sectors [^4].

In a 20VC podcast interview, Buckley described his belief that the best entrepreneurs are "cockroaches" — resilient founders who survive and adapt. He stated: "I think at least the number one rule of success, at least I take with business, is you just need to survive first to get there" [^5].

On an Invest Like the Best podcast appearance, Buckley discussed his investment framework centered on system design, flywheel dynamics, and identifying self-reinforcing business loops [^6]. He has observed that the video game industry typically innovates 2-3 years ahead of other sectors in performance marketing, user acquisition, and monetization models [^2][^6].

### Stage Distribution

Buckley typically writes $300K checks at seed and Series A stages [^2][^8]. He takes board seats at select companies and provides hands-on operational support, a more concentrated approach than typical angel investing [^2].

### Notable Patterns

- **Gaming-to-enterprise thesis**: Buckley's gaming background informs his investing — he looks for enterprise software that applies gaming principles such as instant value delivery, viral loops, and engagement mechanics [^2].
- **Board involvement**: He serves or has served on boards at Placer.ai, NexHealth, Clearbit, Mino Games, Product Hunt, Vial, PlayCo, Limit Break, Fuse, and Toku [^1][^3].
- **Four unicorns**: His portfolio includes at least 4 unicorns, including ClassDojo and Speak [^2][^7].
- **Fund progression**: Buckley has scaled from a $50M early-stage fund to $150M to $250M+, indicating strong LP demand and returns [^3][^5].

## In Their Own Words

"I think at least the number one rule of success, at least I take with business, is you just need to survive first to get there." — Josh Buckley, 20VC podcast interview [^5]

Buckley has stated that gaming companies figured out freemium business models before SaaS companies understood product-led growth, mastered data-driven user acquisition before "growth hacking" became mainstream, and understood network effects and viral loops before enterprise software adopted bottoms-up distribution [^2].
