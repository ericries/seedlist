## Background

Alex Felix is the Co-Founder, Managing Partner, and Chief Investment Officer of CoinFund, one of the earliest cryptonative investment firms, founded in 2015 [^1]. Felix joined CoinFund after co-founder Jake Brukhman established the firm at a Brooklyn kitchen table that same year [^2]. Felix leads investments in companies and protocols from pre-seed through Series A [^1].

Before CoinFund, Felix spent a decade in private equity and leveraged finance. He arranged leveraged buyouts at American Capital, a New York-based private equity firm, and previously held roles at Bank of America Merrill Lynch, Royal Bank of Scotland (RBS), and Guggenheim Partners [^1][^3]. He is also an active technology angel investor and has built e-commerce algorithms [^3].

Felix holds a degree in Economics and Earth Sciences from Dartmouth College (2004-2008) [^3]. He is a former world-class ski racer and was the 2007 NCAA Skiing National Champion [^1][^4].

Felix has personally and professionally invested early in over 100 blockchain-focused companies since 2013, including seed rounds of Ethereum and Blockdaemon [^1][^4].

## Stated Thesis

Felix and CoinFund publicly describe their investment focus as backing foundational technologies in the blockchain and web3 ecosystem before they become mainstream trends. In a 2024 press release commemorating CoinFund's ninth anniversary, Felix stated: "CoinFund continues to invest early in emerging, foundational technologies before they become trends" [^2].

CoinFund's stated areas of focus include layer-1 blockchains, web3 infrastructure, DeFi, NFTs, gaming, asset management, payments, and decentralized applications [^5]. More recently, the firm has emphasized the intersection of AI and crypto, with Felix articulating a thesis that crypto needs AI just as much as AI needs crypto [^4].

Felix has described CoinFund's investment strategy as long-term oriented and long-only, noting that shorting is dangerous in crypto due to volatility and counterparty risk [^4]. His focus spans the decentralized stack: base layer protocols and platforms, resource marketplaces, middleware and on-chain services, and applications [^4].

On social identity and verification, Felix has stated: "One of the next significant use cases for crypto is to develop foundational social graphs for applications to leverage" [^6].

On stablecoins and payments, Felix noted that by 2024 the total volume of stablecoin transactions on public blockchains exceeded the combined annual volume of Visa and Mastercard at $28 trillion [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 25 verified investments below, Felix and CoinFund demonstrate a clear pattern of investing across the blockchain infrastructure stack with increasing emphasis on AI-crypto convergence and financial applications.

**Sector breakdown** (based on 25 verified investments):
- Blockchain infrastructure and protocols: 9 of 25 (36%) — Blockdaemon, The Graph, Solana, Polkadot, LayerZero, Hyperlane, Fuel Network, Archway, Flare
- DeFi and financial services: 6 of 25 (24%) — Ondo Finance, ether.fi, Dakota, Drop Money, Finblox, LI.FI
- AI and decentralized compute: 3 of 25 (12%) — Recall Network, Pluralis Research, Prime Intellect
- Gaming and NFTs: 3 of 25 (12%) — Dapper Labs, Gunzilla Games, Decentraland
- Social and identity: 1 of 25 (4%) — Icebreaker
- Data and middleware: 2 of 25 (8%) — Irys, Geodnet
- Other: 1 of 25 (4%) — Manta Network

**Stage distribution:** CoinFund invests primarily at seed and Series A, with check sizes of $6M-$10M from the Ventures fund [^8]. The firm also participates in pre-seed rounds (e.g., Pluralis Research) and follow-on rounds at later stages.

**Geographic focus:** CoinFund is headquartered in New York [^2], with Felix based in Boston [^3]. Portfolio companies are globally distributed, reflecting the borderless nature of crypto and web3.

**Co-investor patterns:** Frequent co-investors include Multicoin Capital (LI.FI, Recall Network), Avalanche/Blizzard Fund (Gunzilla Games), and Union Square Ventures (Pluralis Research). The firm often co-leads rounds rather than participating as a follow-on investor.

**Notable pattern — AI-crypto convergence:** Since 2024, CoinFund has made a notable pivot toward the intersection of AI and blockchain, investing in Recall Network (AI agent reputation), Pluralis Research (decentralized AI training), and Prime Intellect (decentralized compute) [^9][^10][^11]. This represents a newer thesis area not historically part of CoinFund's stated focus.

**Notable pattern — stablecoin infrastructure:** The Dakota investment (stablecoin-powered business banking) reflects a thesis on stablecoins replacing traditional payment rails, a theme Felix has emphasized publicly [^7][^12].

**Founder profile preferences:** CoinFund backs teams with deep technical expertise and prior experience at major crypto-native or tech companies. Icebreaker was founded by Coinbase and Google veterans [^6], Dakota by veterans of Coinbase, Square, and Airbnb [^12].
