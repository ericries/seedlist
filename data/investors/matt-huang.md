## Background

Matt Huang is co-founder and Managing Partner of Paradigm, a crypto-native investment firm he founded with Coinbase co-founder Fred Ehrsam in June 2018 [^1][^2]. Huang holds a B.S. in Mathematics from MIT [^1].

Huang's father, Chi-fu Huang, was an MIT economics professor and LTCM managing director; his mother, Marina Chen, was a parallel computing researcher at Caltech [^3]. The family relocated to Tokyo when Matt was nine [^3].

Huang founded Hotspots, a Y Combinator-backed company acquired by Twitter in 2012 [^1][^2]. While at Twitter, he made early angel investments in ByteDance at $20-30 million valuations and in Instacart, among others [^1][^2][^3]. His ByteDance investment reportedly returned approximately 10,000x [^3]. He purchased his first Bitcoin from MtGox in 2012 [^1].

From 2014 to 2018, Huang was a partner at Sequoia Capital, where he focused on early-stage venture investments and led the firm's cryptocurrency efforts [^1][^2]. Sequoia's Doug Leone called him "the only regrettable loss in Sequoia's history" [^3]. In 2021, Huang joined the board of directors of Stripe [^4].

In August 2025, Huang was appointed CEO of Tempo, a payments-focused blockchain incubated by Stripe and Paradigm, while retaining his position at Paradigm [^5].

## Stated Thesis

Paradigm publicly describes itself as a "research-driven technology investment firm" focused on crypto and frontier technologies [^1][^6]. Huang has stated that crypto is "a very important technology needing defenders" and that Paradigm is committed to ensuring "it succeeds" [^3].

Huang frames cryptocurrencies as "multiplayer technology," emphasizing that their value lies in collective adoption, unlike AI, which can function independently [^7]. He has compared the current state of crypto to the launch of Netscape Navigator in the 1990s and the iPhone in 2007, saying the industry is at "that inflection point where crypto goes from niche to ubiquitous" [^7].

Huang envisions three phases for crypto: (1) currency (established; Bitcoin worth ~$2 trillion), (2) financial system (emerging; stablecoins processing $5.6 trillion annually), and (3) internet platform (nascent; enabling true digital ownership) [^3].

In 2026, Paradigm announced it was expanding its focus to include AI, robotics, and other frontier technologies alongside crypto [^8].
