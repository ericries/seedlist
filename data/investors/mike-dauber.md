## Background

Mike Dauber is a General Partner at Amplify Partners, the early-stage venture firm he has co-led alongside founder Sunil Dhaliwal since joining in August 2014 [^1][^2]. The two previously worked together at Battery Ventures, where Dhaliwal was Dauber's manager [^3].

Dauber grew up outside Washington, D.C. and attended Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology [^4]. He earned a B.S.E. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Michigan and an MBA from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania [^1][^5].

Dauber began his career in 2001 as a hardware engineer at Velio Communications, a semiconductor startup, where he spent approximately two years [^1][^5]. He has said that he found it frustrating to spend so much time building products that ultimately proved to be wrong [^5]. He subsequently held product, sales, solutions engineering, and marketing roles at Altera and Xilinx over approximately five years, including serving as an account manager for Cisco [^2][^5][^6].

In 2008, Dauber transitioned to venture capital at Battery Ventures, where he spent more than six years as a principal focusing on early-stage enterprise infrastructure and applications on the West Coast [^2][^3]. At Battery, he led investments in Duetto, RelateIQ (acquired by Salesforce), Splunk (NASDAQ: SPLK), Platfora (acquired by Workday), Continuuity, and Vera, and sat on the boards of Cask, Duetto, Interana, and Platfora [^2][^3][^5]. He was named to the Forbes Midas Brink List in 2014 as an up-and-coming venture investor [^2][^3].

Dauber joined Amplify in 2014 to help Dhaliwal build the firm. He has described the decision as fulfilling a longstanding desire: "I've always wanted to go off and do my own thing, and you couple that with the idea that $1.5 trillion in market cap is shifting in the space where Amplify focuses" [^3]. At the time he joined, Amplify had raised nearly $50 million for its debut fund with approximately half already committed [^3]. He authors an annual AI Engineering Report as part of his thought leadership at the firm [^1][^7].

## Stated Thesis

Dauber publicly describes his focus as backing "technical entrepreneurs who are solving technical problems in a domain in which they are deeply immersed" [^5]. He has stated that he enjoys working with founders who are "deeply steeped in their domain, feel like they've got something to prove and are borderline obsessed with their idea," noting that this passion "touches all the key aspects of company formation: recruiting, selling, and fundraising" [^1].

On his investing philosophy, Dauber has said: "Early stage investing is deeply personal. It's not about spreadsheets and customer references" [^1]. He has also emphasized lessons from his engineering background: "The core lesson I learned as an engineer — everything is about tradeoffs. You get nothing for free" [^1].

In a June 2025 post on X, Dauber described his mission at Amplify: "When I joined @dhaliwas on this journey 11 years ago it was to build *the* haven for technical founders. Nothing has changed except we have far more resources than I ever imagined to help the people we're built to serve" [^8].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 22 verified investments personally attributed to Dauber across his Amplify Partners tenure (from his Amplify bio, Stanford NVCA speaker profile, Evalyze, and Crunchbase cross-references), the following patterns emerge. Note: as co-leader of Amplify, Dauber is involved in a broader set of the firm's 162 total investments, but these 22 are specifically attributed to him as deal lead or active board participant.

**Sector breakdown (22 verified investments):**
- Developer tools and infrastructure: 8 of 22 (36%) — Gremlin, Abstract, Temporal, Archera, Scribe, Maze, OctoML, Primer
- Cybersecurity and supply chain security: 4 of 22 (18%) — StackRox, LightCyber, Chainguard, Integris Software
- AI/ML infrastructure and applications: 4 of 22 (18%) — OctoML, Lamini, SiMa.ai, Intellimize
- Enterprise software: 3 of 22 (14%) — Intellimize, Archera, Scribe
- Hardware/semiconductors: 2 of 22 (9%) — SiMa.ai, Sonera Magnetics
- Other: 1 of 22 (5%) — Buddybuild (mobile developer tools, acquired by Apple)

Note: some companies span multiple categories; primary category used above.

**Stage distribution:** Predominantly seed-stage investments, consistent with Amplify's first-check focus. Follow-on participation through Series B is documented for several companies (Archera Series B, Scribe Series B, Intellimize Series B, SiMa.ai Series B) [^9].

**Geographic concentration:** Dauber's portfolio is heavily concentrated in the San Francisco Bay Area, consistent with Amplify's Menlo Park and San Francisco offices [^4].

**Check size:** $500K to $5M at initial check, with a typical seed investment around $2.75M [^4][^7].

**Founder profile patterns:** Dauber has a strong stated and demonstrated preference for deeply technical founders with domain expertise. Multiple portfolio founders came from engineering backgrounds at major infrastructure companies (e.g., Temporal founders from Uber's Cadence team, Chainguard founders from Google's container security work).

**Co-investor patterns:** Based on downstream funding rounds of Dauber's portfolio companies, frequent co-investors include Sequoia Capital (StackRox Series A, Temporal Series A), Redpoint Ventures (StackRox Series B), Dell Technologies Capital (SiMa.ai Series A), and Andreessen Horowitz (Temporal Series B) [^10][^11][^12].

**Exit patterns:** Multiple successful exits including StackRox (acquired by Red Hat, ~$100M, 2021), Buddybuild (acquired by Apple), LightCyber (acquired by Palo Alto Networks), CMD/Cmd (acquired by Elastic), Determined AI (acquired by HPE), and Platfora (acquired by Workday) from his Battery days [^1][^10]. RelateIQ (acquired by Salesforce) and Splunk (IPO) are notable Battery-era outcomes [^2][^3].

**Notable gaps vs. stated thesis:** Dauber's stated focus on "technical founders solving technical problems" is broadly consistent with his actual portfolio, which is almost entirely B2B infrastructure and developer-facing companies. There is limited consumer exposure and no fintech, which is consistent with Amplify's enterprise-only positioning.
