## Background

Charlie Songhurst is a British-born technology investor based in Kirkland, Washington [^1][^1]. He earned a bachelor's degree in Politics, Philosophy, and Economics (PPE) from Oxford University in 2001 [^2][^3]. He began his career as an analyst at McKinsey & Company in London from approximately 2003 to 2005 [^1][^4].

Songhurst then joined Microsoft, initially as General Manager before becoming Head of Global Corporate Strategy, a position he held from approximately 2005 to 2013 [^1][^4]. During his tenure at Microsoft, he led over 80 acquisitions and played a central role in the company's $8.5 billion acquisition of Skype and the Yahoo acquisition [^2][^3]. He also collaborated with Bill Gates on Microsoft's investment in Facebook [^5].

After leaving Microsoft in 2013, Songhurst transitioned to full-time investing [^4]. He founded Katana Capital, a long-short equity hedge fund, and the Songhurst Group, a private investment firm with holdings across enterprise software, AI, and deep technology [^5][^6]. He serves as Senior Venture Partner at Acequia Capital [^7], and as an Investment Partner at Nyca Partners [^4][^6].

In January 2025, Songhurst was elected to the board of directors of Meta Platforms, alongside Dana White and John Elkann [^2]. He had previously served on Meta's product advisory council, advising on AI products [^2]. He also holds board seats at Invisible Technologies, Beauhurst, CodeSignal, and Rigetti Computing [^4][^6][^8].

## Stated Thesis

Songhurst has publicly described his investment approach as "edge-of-knowledge investing" — focusing on spaces where even domain experts lack clear answers, using pattern recognition drawn from hundreds of startup interactions [^9]. He emphasizes seeking out "boring and complex" businesses, a framework he articulated on the Invest Like the Best podcast: markets with high complexity and low glamour attract fewer entrepreneurs, creating favorable supply-demand dynamics for those who do enter [^10][^11].

On the Invest Like the Best podcast in 2020, Songhurst stated that he looks for founders who are motivated by power (execution-oriented) or money (capital-efficiency-oriented), and tends to avoid those primarily motivated by fame [^11]. He has publicly described his focus areas as enterprise SaaS, artificial intelligence, deep technology, fintech, biotech, and quantum computing [^2].

Acequia Capital, where Songhurst is Senior Venture Partner, is led by former senior Microsoft strategy executives and describes itself as "an experienced and market-tested partnership" with deep background in global platform strategy and M&A [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

Songhurst is one of the most prolific individual angel investors globally, with a portfolio spanning approximately 500 companies [^2][^9]. Analysis of 97 tracked investments (from VCBacked data, representing approximately 20% of his claimed total portfolio) reveals the following patterns [^12]:

**Stage distribution:** Predominantly pre-seed and seed, with some Series A participation. Of the 97 tracked investments, the majority are seed-stage (approximately 60%+), with a significant number of pre-seed deals, consistent with his stated $10K-$500K angel check size [^1][^12].

**Sector breakdown:** Software and AI dominate. Nordic9 data shows 119 of 140 tracked investments (85%) are in Software Services [^13]. Within that, AI/ML is the most common subsector, followed by enterprise SaaS, fintech, and deep tech. His portfolio also includes robotics (Bear Robotics), quantum computing (Rigetti Computing, Entropica Labs, Rahko), biotech (Bit Bio, LabGenius, Arctoris), and space technology (Open Cosmos, STOKE Space Technologies) [^12][^14].

**Geographic distribution:** Songhurst claims approximately 90% of his investments are evenly split across the US, UK, and continental Europe, with the remaining 10% in Asia, Africa, and Latin America [^9]. Nordic9 data shows 78 of 140 tracked investments (56%) are in the United Kingdom, confirming a strong UK bias [^13]. He is described as one of the most prolific US-based investors in Europe, with active shareholdings in over 85 private UK tech companies [^9][^15].

**Check size:** NFX Signal reports an investment range of $10K-$500K with a typical target of $100K [^1]. The average deal size across larger tracked rounds is $4.53M, reflecting that some investments are through Acequia Capital or other co-investment vehicles [^13].

**Unicorn track record:** His portfolio includes six current or former unicorns: Rigetti Computing, Formlabs, Convoy, Tractable, Karat, and Side, with ClassPass and Grove Collaborative as exited unicorns [^9]. The Arete Index also lists Affirm, Public.com, Cargomatic, and Flexe among early-stage unicorn investments [^4].

**Co-investor patterns:** Songhurst frequently co-invests with European seed funds including Cherry Ventures, and with other prolific angels. He is closely associated with Entrepreneurs First, where he has served as a board member for over a decade [^16][^17].

**Notable gap:** Despite claiming 500+ investments, only approximately 140-313 are publicly tracked across major databases, suggesting a significant portion of his angel activity is undisclosed or made through private vehicles [^1][^13].
