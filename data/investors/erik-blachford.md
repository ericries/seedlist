## Background

Erik Blachford is a San Francisco-based angel investor and independent venture investor with deep roots in the online travel industry. He holds a BA in English (with a theater certificate) from Princeton University (Class of 1989) and an MBA from Columbia Business School [^1].

Blachford joined Microsoft in 1995 as one of the founders of Expedia, where he led the creation of the brand as Senior Vice President of Marketing. He later served as President of Expedia North America before being named President and CEO of Expedia in 2003. When IAC acquired Expedia, he became President and CEO of IAC Travel, overseeing Expedia, Hotels.com, Hotwire, Classic Custom Vacations, and Interval International [^2].

After leaving IAC Travel, Blachford served as CEO of Butterfield & Robinson, a premium active-travel operator, and as CEO of TerraPass, a retailer of carbon offsets and consumer energy efficiency products [^2]. In 2014 he became Executive Chairman of Couchsurfing, the hospitality exchange and social networking website [^3].

Blachford began angel investing in 2007, with his first known investment in Farecast [^4]. He served as Venture Partner at Technology Crossover Ventures (TCV) from March 2011 until September 2021, during which time he joined the boards of multiple TCV portfolio companies including Zillow, Peloton, SiteMinder, TourRadar, and Varsity Tutors [^5][^6]. In 2021 he formalized his independent investing activity by founding Blachford Capital LLC [^7]. He also operates Camp Nominingue, a wilderness adventure camp in Québec [^8].

His investment network overlaps heavily with other travel-industry veterans, including Rich Barton (Zillow/Expedia), Brad Gerstner (Altimeter), Sam Shank (Hotel Tonight), and Hugh Crean; this cohort was described in 2013 as an informal "fab five" of travel angel investors [^9].

## Stated Thesis

Blachford describes himself as "an internet investor and advisor… with a primary focus on consumer technology and travel companies" [^10]. His stated focus areas, as presented through Blachford Capital LLC, are online travel, health and fitness, and clean energy [^7].

On what he looks for in founders, he has stated (self-reported, PhocusWire interview): "You are investing so much in the people. You want to see strategy, you want to see a market that makes sense. But from the moment you write a check, you know the plan you saw isn't going to come true — it just never does — so you have to have faith in the founders." He adds: "It helps an awful lot to have spent a lot of time in operating environments… You are able to see if they are able to do the stuff you hired them to do. So you calibrate against candidates pretty well." [^11]

On market opportunity, he has emphasized mobile-first validation and real-world product-market fit: "I can't speak for anyone else, but to me there's a bright shining line between the entrepreneurs who are willing to ask themselves whether real travelers in the real world would use their services." [^9]

On multi-day tour travel, he stated in 2018: "Multi-day tours are the last frontier in online travel." [^12]

On AI in travel (2025 podcast): Smart teams use AI to compress product cycles, improve workflows, and sharpen marketing efficiency; however, current AI gains focus primarily on cost savings rather than generating new revenue streams. He sees a growing role for human travel advisors as "human in the loop AI" [^13].

Regarding his AngelList syndicate, Blachford has stated: "I will only syndicate deals in which the company founders are enthusiastic supporters of the syndicate. Otherwise I will invest as an individual." [^14]

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 19 verified investments with publicly documented years and rounds, Blachford's actual investment behavior skews heavily toward travel and hospitality technology, with secondary clusters in clean energy and real-estate/proptech:

**Sector breakdown (19 verified investments):**
- Travel / hospitality tech: 14 of 19 (74%) — Farecast, Liftopia, Hipmunk, HotelTonight, Ostrovok, Room 77, TourRadar, Vacatia, Vayable, Tripping.com, Busbud, Traveling Spoon, Glassdoor (employment review platform with strong travel brand), Otto
- Clean energy / sustainability: 2 of 19 (11%) — TerraPass (operator role), Mosaic
- Real estate / proptech: 1 of 19 (5%) — Zillow (board/TCV role)
- Consumer fitness: 1 of 19 (5%) — Peloton (TCV board role)
- EdTech: 1 of 19 (5%) — Varsity Tutors (TCV board role)

The clean energy and proptech/EdTech entries largely reflect TCV board assignments rather than independent angel conviction. Blachford's independent angel work is almost entirely concentrated in travel and hospitality.

**Stage distribution (19 verified investments):**
- Seed / angel: 9 of 19 (47%) — Farecast, Liftopia (pre-2011), TourRadar (2013), Vacatia (2013), Vayable (~2012–13), HotelTonight (2011), Hipmunk (2011), Ostrovok (2012), Otto (2024)
- Series A: 3 of 19 (16%) — Tripping.com (2014), Busbud (2018 Series B counted below), Glassdoor (early stage)
- Series B or later / growth: 4 of 19 (21%) — Tripping.com Series B (2015), Busbud Series B (2018), Ostrovok Series C (2013), SiteMinder Series B (2013/TCV)
- Board/TCV roles without confirmed independent check: 3 — Peloton, Varsity Tutors, Zillow (board since 2005)

Sample size is 19; Blachford has reportedly made 30 total investments (Ascension VC bio) with 13+ exits. This table covers roughly 63% of the claimed portfolio. Percentages for the full portfolio may differ.

**Geographic focus:** Almost entirely US-headquartered companies with some exceptions: Ostrovok (Russia), TourRadar (Vienna), SiteMinder (Sydney/Australia), Busbud (Montreal/Canada).

**Co-investor patterns:** Frequently co-invests with Rich Barton, Sam Shank, Brad Gerstner, and Hugh Crean (travel "fab five"). TCV deal flow was his institutional channel 2011–2021. At the seed level, he has co-invested with First Round Capital (Liftopia), Y Combinator-backed companies (Vayable), and Madrona Ventures (Otto).

**Founder profile patterns:** Strong preference for founders with prior domain expertise in online travel or consumer marketplaces. Many portfolio founders had prior stints at Expedia, IAC, or adjacent OTAs (Otto's CEO is ex-Expedia; TourRadar founders met Blachford at a travel conference).

**Notable gaps vs. stated thesis:** Health and fitness appears heavily in his stated thesis but only Peloton (a TCV board seat, not an independent angel bet) is verifiable in the portfolio. No SaaS enterprise or developer-tools investments found.
