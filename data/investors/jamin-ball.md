## Background

Jamin Ball is a Partner at Altimeter Capital, where he has invested since 2021 [^1]. He focuses on growth-stage investments in enterprise software, cloud infrastructure, and AI-driven companies [^2].

Before joining Altimeter, Ball was a Vice President at Redpoint Ventures from 2016 to 2021, where he led deals across both early and growth stages in companies including Workato, Monte Carlo, Cityblock Health, and Root Insurance [^1] [^3]. He began his career in investment banking, working at Bank of America Merrill Lynch and then Morgan Stanley from 2014 to 2016, where he participated in IPOs for Pure Storage and Square [^1] [^3].

Ball holds a Bachelor of Science degree in Management Science & Engineering from Stanford University [^1]. He is also the author of "Clouded Judgement," a widely followed weekly newsletter analyzing public SaaS and cloud company metrics, launched in June 2020, with over 86,000 subscribers [^4]. The newsletter provides data-driven frameworks for evaluating cloud and SaaS businesses based on valuation trends and operating metrics [^4].

## Stated Thesis

Ball has publicly described his investment approach as centered on "essential, long-term businesses" that remain valuable across economic cycles [^3]. In his own words, Altimeter invests in companies solving "foundational infrastructure problems" [^5].

On the Clouded Judgement newsletter about page, Ball states he has "developed frameworks (many of them data driven) to evaluate private SaaS businesses" and aims to help entrepreneurs "build similar frameworks for how to measure their own business, and ultimately put together a blueprint of how to become a successful public company" [^4].

Ball has emphasized a metrics-first approach that prioritizes unit economics and free-cash-flow generation even for high-growth software businesses [^2]. He has identified net revenue retention as one of his favorite SaaS metrics, noting that anything above 130% is "top decile" [^6]. His second-favorite metric is Gross Margin Adjusted CAC Payback [^6].

Regarding AI infrastructure, Ball has stated that data is a critical enabler, noting at Altimeter that "You don't have an AI strategy without a data strategy" [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 15 verified investments where Ball has been personally attributed (across Altimeter and Redpoint), the following patterns emerge. Note: this represents investments specifically tied to Ball rather than Altimeter's full portfolio, so percentages should be interpreted as directional rather than exhaustive.

**Sector distribution (15 verified investments):**
- Data infrastructure: 6 of 15 (40%) — dbt Labs, ClickHouse, Airbyte, Tabular, Starburst, Hammerspace
- Data observability: 1 of 15 (7%) — Monte Carlo
- Enterprise software / HR: 3 of 15 (20%) — Deel, Personio, Workato
- Developer tools: 1 of 15 (7%) — Prisma
- Real-time infrastructure: 1 of 15 (7%) — LiveKit
- Healthcare: 1 of 15 (7%) — Cityblock Health
- Insurtech: 1 of 15 (7%) — Root Insurance
- AI / biotech: 1 of 15 (7%) — Profluent

**Stage distribution (15 verified investments):**
- Series B: 9 of 15 (60%) — Airbyte, ClickHouse, Prisma, Tabular, LiveKit, Hammerspace, Profluent, Monte Carlo, Cityblock Health
- Series C: 2 of 15 (13%) — Workato, Root Insurance
- Series D: 3 of 15 (20%) — dbt Labs, Deel, Starburst
- Series E: 1 of 15 (7%) — Personio

**Key patterns:**
- **Data infrastructure dominance:** Nearly half of Ball's verified investments are in the data infrastructure space, making this a clear area of concentration that aligns with his stated thesis.
- **Growth-stage focus:** The majority of investments are Series B and later, with typical check sizes of $1M-$25M [^2]. Ball tends to invest in companies that have already demonstrated product-market fit.
- **Open-source and developer-first models:** Several portfolio companies (Airbyte, dbt Labs, ClickHouse, Prisma) have strong open-source components, suggesting a preference for companies with community-driven adoption.
- **Public-market fluency as a differentiator:** Ball's dual role as a growth investor and public SaaS analyst (via Clouded Judgement) gives him an unusual vantage point on IPO readiness and public-market benchmarking, which likely informs his private market investments.
- **Expanding into AI infrastructure:** Recent investments in Hammerspace (April 2025) and Profluent (November 2025) indicate an expanding thesis into AI data infrastructure and AI-driven biology.
- **Co-investor patterns:** Coatue appears alongside Altimeter in multiple deals (Airbyte, ClickHouse, Deel). Andreessen Horowitz also appears frequently (MotherDuck, Tabular, Starburst).

**Notable gaps:** Despite Altimeter's broader portfolio including consumer companies (Airbnb, Uber, Roblox), Ball's personal investments are exclusively in enterprise/infrastructure/developer categories. His stated thesis about "essential businesses" is well-matched by his actual behavior — there is no significant divergence between stated and inferred thesis.
