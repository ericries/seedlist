## Background

Brandon Reeves is a General Partner at Lux Capital, where he has been investing since February 2017 [^1][^2]. He grew up in Fort Lauderdale, Florida [^1]. He earned a BS in electrical engineering from the University of Central Florida and an MBA from Harvard Business School [^1][^3].

Before joining Lux, Reeves was on the investment team at Capricorn Investment Group in Palo Alto, California, where he focused on autonomy, mobility, semiconductors, and energy, investing in companies including Joby Aviation and Navitas [^1][^4]. Prior to that, he worked at Texas Instruments in Sunnyvale, California, as a Field Applications Engineer supporting Tesla Motors, where he worked on autopilot designs, next-generation infotainment, and the sensor system for the Model X falcon wing doors [^1][^4].

Since joining Lux in 2017, Reeves was promoted to Partner and has led the firm's investment efforts in open source infrastructure, data applications, and machine learning technologies [^5]. He currently serves as a board member at Hugging Face, Primer, and Hadrian [^2][^6].

## Stated Thesis

Reeves publicly describes his investment goal as identifying "companies that are turning science fiction into science fact, especially in areas of autonomous systems, robotics, machine intelligence, and space" [^1]. He focuses on companies in the areas of data and machine learning infrastructure, cryptonetworks, robotics and automation, and new compute architectures [^4].

His stated investment range is $1M to $50M, with a sweet spot around $10M [^2]. He invests across seed through Series B stages [^2].

Reeves has also described his thesis as centering on open source infrastructure and AI/ML tooling, noting about Hugging Face that "the market for bringing NLP to enterprises and consumers is incredibly exciting — very few professions deal with images...but nearly all jobs work with text and language" [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 20 verified investments, Reeves' actual portfolio reveals the following patterns:

**Sector breakdown (20 investments):**
- AI/ML infrastructure and tools: 9 of 20 (45%) — Hugging Face, Together AI, Sakana AI, Cognition, Fiddler, Tecton, Chronosphere, Physical Intelligence, Ahana
- Defense and manufacturing: 3 of 20 (15%) — Anduril, Hadrian, Primer
- Autonomous vehicles/simulation: 2 of 20 (10%) — Applied Intuition, Joby Aviation (at Capricorn)
- Crypto/Web3: 2 of 20 (10%) — Anchorage, Oasis Labs
- Life sciences/biotech: 2 of 20 (10%) — Benchling, LatchBio
- Digital health: 1 of 20 (5%) — Elektra Labs
- Fintech: 1 of 20 (5%) — Ramp

**Stage distribution (18 Lux investments, excluding 2 Capricorn-era):**
- Seed: 8 of 18 (44%) — Together AI, Sakana AI, Ahana, LatchBio, Elektra Labs, Lekko, Magie, and others
- Series A: 5 of 18 (28%) — Hugging Face, Chronosphere, Fiddler, Anchorage, Oasis Labs
- Series B and later: 5 of 18 (28%) — Benchling, Ramp, Applied Intuition, Hadrian, Cognition

**Notable patterns:**
- Heavy concentration in AI/ML (45% of portfolio), significantly exceeding his stated focus on "autonomous systems, robotics, machine intelligence, and space." His actual portfolio is far more weighted toward software infrastructure for AI than toward robotics or space.
- Strong affinity for open source business models: Hugging Face, Chronosphere (built on M3), Together AI, and Ahana (Presto) all have significant open source components.
- Willingness to invest internationally: Sakana AI (Tokyo) and Magie (Brazil) show geographic range beyond the typical Bay Area focus [^8][^9].
- Technical founder preference: nearly all portfolio companies have founders with deep engineering backgrounds — ex-Uber (Chronosphere, Tecton), ex-Google (Sakana AI), competitive programming (Cognition).
- Defense and national security is a meaningful but secondary theme (15%), with board seats at Hadrian and investments in Anduril and Primer.

**Gaps between stated and actual thesis:**
- Space is mentioned in his stated thesis but no verified space-specific investments were found (Hadrian serves space/defense but is primarily a manufacturing company).
- Robotics is mentioned in stated thesis; Physical Intelligence is the one direct robotics investment.
- Crypto/Web3 appears in earlier portfolio (2018-2019) but no recent investments in the space.
