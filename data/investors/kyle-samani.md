## Background

Kyle Samani is the co-founder and former managing partner of Multicoin Capital, a thesis-driven crypto investment firm based in Austin, Texas [^1]. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Finance and Management from New York University's Leonard N. Stern School of Business [^1]. Samani has been programming since the age of 10 [^2].

Before entering crypto investing, Samani co-founded Pristine in May 2013 with Patrick Kolencherry, a company that developed software for Google Glass in healthcare settings [^3]. As CEO, he led Pristine to millions in revenue before the company was acquired by Upskill in 2017 [^4]. Upskill was subsequently acquired by TeamViewer in 2021 [^4].

After exiting Pristine, Samani entered crypto via Ethereum in 2016 but became disillusioned with Ethereum's scaling approach [^5]. In May 2017, he co-founded Multicoin Capital alongside Tushar Jain [^1]. The firm grew from a $75 million first fund to managing approximately $6 billion in AUM by mid-2025 [^6][^7]. Multicoin operates as both a venture fund and a hedge fund, investing in tokens, equity, and blockchain companies [^1].

On February 4, 2026, Samani announced he was stepping down from Multicoin Capital after nearly a decade to explore other areas of technology including AI, longevity, and robotics [^8]. He remains chairman of Forward Industries (NASDAQ: FWDI), a Solana treasury company [^8]. Day-to-day operations at Multicoin continue under Tushar Jain and CFO/COO Brian Smith [^8].

## Stated Thesis

Multicoin Capital publicly describes itself as "a thesis-driven investment firm that invests in cryptoassets, tokens, and blockchain companies reshaping trillion-dollar markets" [^1]. The firm states it invests in "technical endeavors that push the boundaries of computer science and technology, infrastructure that supports trust-minimized computation at global scale" [^9].

Samani has publicly articulated investment focus across ten categories: Execution (Layer 1 protocols), Stablecoins, DeFi, Real-World Assets, Payments, DePIN, Applications, FHE and Zero-Knowledge Proofs, MEV, and AI/Machine Learning [^1]. The firm claims to have pioneered the DePIN category in 2019 [^1].

On the 20VC podcast, Samani stated that Multicoin prioritizes market analysis over founding team evaluation, noting that approximately 93% of their discussion time focuses on market dynamics [^10]. He has argued that Solana would surpass Ethereum as the dominant smart contract platform, stating that "Ethereum does not have a scaling plan. Seven years later, it still does not have one today" [^10].

On stablecoins, Samani has described the opportunity to distribute USD-denominated stablecoins globally as "the single largest arbitrage in human history," arguing that if you asked the world's 8 billion people what asset they would want to denominate their wealth in, five to seven billion would say U.S. dollars [^11].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 30 verified investments tracked below, Multicoin Capital's actual portfolio reveals several clear patterns. Note: Multicoin has reportedly made 115+ total investments [^12], so this table represents approximately 26% of the full portfolio. Percentages should be treated as directional rather than definitive.

**Sector distribution (30 verified investments):**
- Layer 1 blockchains: 7 of 30 (23%) — Solana, Algorand, NEAR, DFINITY, Kadena, Sei, Aptos
- DeFi protocols: 7 of 30 (23%) — Serum, Perpetual Protocol, MCDEX, Saber, UXD Protocol, dFlow, Drift Protocol
- DePIN / infrastructure: 5 of 30 (17%) — Helium, Hivemapper, io.net, Render, Livepeer
- Data / storage: 3 of 30 (10%) — The Graph, Arweave, Dune Analytics
- Interoperability / messaging: 2 of 30 (7%) — Wormhole, StarkWare
- Consumer / creator: 3 of 30 (10%) — Audius, Metaplex, Coin98 Labs
- Other (wallets, DAOs, analytics): 3 of 30 (10%) — MathWallet, Grape Network, Filebase

**Stage distribution (30 verified investments):**
- Seed: 14 of 30 (47%) — including The Graph, dFlow, Sei, Drift, UXD Protocol, Saber, Grape Network, Filebase
- Series A / strategic: 12 of 30 (40%) — including Solana, StarkWare, Coin98 Labs, MathWallet, Perpetual Protocol
- Series B+: 4 of 30 (13%) — including Wormhole ($225M round), Helium ($111M Series C), Aptos ($200M strategic)

**Solana ecosystem concentration:** A striking pattern is heavy concentration in the Solana ecosystem. Of 30 verified investments, at least 12 (40%) are Solana-native or Solana-aligned projects (Solana, Serum, Saber, UXD Protocol, Drift Protocol, dFlow, Metaplex, Grape Network, Wormhole, Pyth, Hivemapper, Forward Industries). This far exceeds any stated preference for a single ecosystem.

**Check sizes:** Range from $500K to $25M for early-stage ventures, with capacity up to $100M+ for later-stage opportunities by combining venture fund and liquid fund capital [^13].

**Geographic patterns:** Austin, Texas-based firm with a stated emphasis on Indian Web3 startups as a focus area in Fund III [^13].

**Co-investor patterns:** Frequent co-investors include a16z, Coinbase Ventures, Pantera Capital, Jump Crypto, and Framework Ventures based on portfolio overlap.

**Notable gaps:** Despite the firm's stated thesis covering NFTs, Metaverse, and Gaming as investment categories, the verified portfolio shows minimal investment in pure gaming or metaverse projects. The firm's actual behavior is more concentrated in financial infrastructure, DePIN, and Layer 1 protocols than its broad category list suggests.
