## Background

Talia Goldberg is a Partner in Bessemer Venture Partners' San Francisco office [^1]. She joined Bessemer in 2013 as an analyst in the firm's program designed to cultivate next-generation investors [^2]. She was promoted to Vice President in 2017 [^3], to Principal in 2018 [^4], and to Partner in February 2019, becoming the youngest elected partner in Bessemer's history at that time [^2]. She had sourced and led 13 term sheets by the time of her partner promotion [^2].

Goldberg graduated from the University of Pennsylvania, where she self-designed a major in technology [^2]. While at Penn, she was a founding partner of First Round Capital's Dorm Room Fund [^2]. Before joining Bessemer, she was an early employee at Foursquare and Simple [^2]. She grew up in Portland, Oregon, surrounded by medical professionals rather than finance — she has described herself as unaware of venture capital during her childhood [^5]. She is a mentor at Parity Partners, a nonprofit focused on workplace diversity [^2].

She was featured on Forbes 30 Under 30 for Venture Capital [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Goldberg publicly describes her focus as supporting "teams that leverage AI to create new categories and progress the way we live and work" and seeking "founders building radically better products with unique distribution models" [^1]. She has stated she is drawn to entrepreneurs who challenge the status quo and believes in backing underdogs taking on established competitors [^1].

On her investing philosophy, Goldberg has said: "Most things that seem insane are totally insane. But every year, one of those crazy ideas becomes one of the most valuable and important businesses" [^2]. She has also stated: "The world is shaped by small teams of extremely ambitious people who move fast and think differently" [^1].

On working with founders, she has described her approach: "Our job as investors is to figure out when to speak up and when to sit tight, so we can best help a team achieve its potential" [^6]. She has emphasized that she is not a "fair weather fan" and rolls up her sleeves alongside teams facing obstacles [^1].

**Stage distribution:** Goldberg invests across stages, from growth equity (ServiceTitan, Discord) to earlier-stage rounds. Her check size range of $1M-$250M as reported by Signal NFX [^7] reflects Bessemer's multi-stage platform.

**Geographic concentration:** Portfolio is concentrated in San Francisco and New York, with notable international investments including DeepL (Germany), Toss (South Korea), and Papaya Global (Israel).

**IPO track record:** ServiceTitan went public in December 2024 (NASDAQ: TTAN) [^1]. StubHub went public in September 2025 (NYSE: STUB) [^1].

**M&A exits:** Supermaven was acquired by Cursor, Fandom was acquired by TPG, Teachable was acquired by Hotmart, Clarity Money was acquired by Goldman Sachs, Rupa Health was acquired by Fullscript [^1].

**Notable patterns:** Goldberg has a strong track record in AI companies, with recent investments in Perplexity and Fal.ai suggesting a deepening focus on AI infrastructure and applications. Her consumer investments (Discord, StubHub) tend to be community-driven platforms with strong network effects.

**Co-investor patterns:** As a Bessemer partner, she frequently co-invests with other Bessemer partners including Byron Deeter and Sameer Dholakia.
