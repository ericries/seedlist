## Background

Zachary Bogue is a Co-Founder and Managing Partner of DCVC (Data Collective), a deep tech venture capital firm he co-founded with Matt Ocko in 2010 [^1][^2]. DCVC has grown to manage approximately $4 billion across 13 funds [^2][^3].

Bogue has over 20 years of Silicon Valley experience as an entrepreneur, venture capitalist, adviser, and angel investor [^1]. Before founding DCVC, his early angel investments included Square, AngelList, and Uber [^1][^4].

He holds a bachelor's degree with honors in environmental science and public policy from Harvard University and a JD with honors from Georgetown University Law School, where he served as executive editor of *The Tax Lawyer* [^1][^5]. His legal and policy background is distinctive among deep tech venture investors.

Bogue has been designated a Young Global Leader by the World Economic Forum and hosts an annual "Deep Tech in Davos" event [^5][^6]. He also serves on the boards of the East Palo Alto Charter School and the Fine Arts Museums of San Francisco [^1].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Bogue says publicly about his investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Bogue has described DCVC's mission as backing "entrepreneurs solving some of the world's hardest problems using applied AI" [^3]. He defines deep tech as companies that use computational approaches like machine learning and deep learning to tackle real-world problems in a capital-efficient way [^3].

On climate investing, Bogue has stated: "We've been quietly and happily investing in the climate for over a dozen years" [^3], with DCVC making its first AI-enabled climate investment in 2012 [^7]. He has emphasized that DCVC Climate is "a for-profit fund with the same return expectations and profile" as their other funds [^3], distinguishing DCVC from impact-first investors.

On policy independence, Bogue has said: "Our companies need to succeed regardless of who's in the White House" [^3].

On impact criteria, he has stated: "We're only going to invest in companies that will have unequivocal large scale positive impact" [^3].

DCVC's typical investment range is $5M-$25M, with a sweet spot around $15M [^8].

## Inferred Thesis

The analysis below is based on 14 verified investments attributed to Bogue personally or to DCVC with Bogue's board involvement, from press coverage, the DCVC website, and regulatory filings [^1][^2][^4][^9][^10][^11].

**Sector concentration (based on 14 verified investments):**
- Climate / energy: 5 of 14 (36%) — Fervo Energy, Oklo, Twelve, CH4 Global, Radiant Nuclear
- Life sciences / computational biology: 2 of 14 (14%) — Recursion Pharmaceuticals, Relation Therapeutics
- Fintech / data: 3 of 14 (21%) — Square, Tala, Merlon Intelligence
- Geospatial / space: 1 of 14 (7%) — Planet Labs
- Platform / marketplace: 1 of 14 (7%) — AngelList
- Ride-hailing / logistics: 1 of 14 (7%) — Uber
- Other: 1 of 14 (7%) — Zymergen (synthetic biology / industrial)

**Key patterns:**

- **Climate and energy dominance**: More than a third (5 of 14) of Bogue's verified investments are in climate tech and energy, spanning nuclear (Oklo, Radiant Nuclear), geothermal (Fervo Energy), carbon transformation (Twelve), and methane reduction (CH4 Global). This represents a strong personal concentration within DCVC's broader portfolio.

- **Deep tech with real-world physical outputs**: Unlike many software-focused VCs, Bogue's portfolio companies "put steel in the ground, build electrons in the grid, and create wet labs" [^3]. These are companies with significant physical infrastructure and capital intensity once commercial.

- **Board-level engagement**: Bogue has served on boards at Recursion Pharmaceuticals (NASDAQ: RXRX), Twelve, CH4 Global, and Oklo, suggesting hands-on involvement beyond check-writing [^1][^9].

- **Early angel hits**: Bogue's pre-DCVC angel portfolio includes three companies that became multi-billion-dollar enterprises: Square (now Block), Uber, and AngelList [^1][^4]. This track record informed DCVC's focus on backing technical founders solving large-scale problems.

- **Profit-driven impact**: Bogue explicitly rejects the "impact investor" label, saying DCVC invests in climate "to make money" rather than for ethical reasons [^7]. This positions DCVC as a returns-first fund that happens to back climate companies because of their commercial potential.

- **Sample size caveat**: Only 14 investments could be independently verified as attributable to Bogue. DCVC as a firm has invested in 366+ companies [^12]. Percentages reflect Bogue's personal portfolio emphasis, not the full fund.
