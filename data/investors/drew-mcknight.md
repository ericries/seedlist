## Background

Drew McKnight is Co-Chief Executive Officer and Managing Partner at Fortress Investment Group, based in Dallas, Texas [^1][^2]. He serves on Fortress's Board of Directors, Leadership Committee, and multiple investment committees [^1]. McKnight is also Co-Chief Investment Officer of the Drawbridge Special Opportunities Fund, the Fortress Lending Funds, and the Fortress Credit Opportunities Funds [^3].

McKnight joined Fortress in February 2005 [^1][^2]. Prior to Fortress, he worked at Fir Tree Partners, where he analyzed and traded high-yield and convertible bonds, bank debt, derivatives, and equities for the value-based hedge fund [^1][^2]. Before Fir Tree, he worked at Goldman, Sachs & Co. in Leveraged Finance and the Distressed Bank Debt trading group [^1][^2].

He holds a B.A. in Economics from the University of Virginia [^1][^2][^4].

McKnight was named a "Hedge Fund Rising Star" by Institutional Investor, which noted his work on some of the Fortress credit group's most innovative and high-pressure transactions, including a negotiated purchase of $205 million in preferred shares of Harbinger Group in 2011 [^5]. He was approximately 34 years old at the time of that recognition in 2012 [^5].

In April 2023, McKnight was named Co-CEO of Fortress alongside Joshua Pack, following the completion of an acquisition by a consortium led by Mubadala Capital [^6]. Fortress management retained a 32% equity interest, while the Mubadala-led consortium holds 68% [^7].

McKnight serves on the Board of Trustees of the American Enterprise Institute, the Board of Overseers at the Hoover Institution, the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas Financial Sector Advisory Council, and is a member of the Council on Foreign Relations [^1][^2][^4]. He also sits on advisory boards at the University of Virginia's Center for Politics, SMU's Cox School of Business Alternative Asset Management Center, and the UT Southwestern Medical Center President's Advisory Board [^1][^4]. He formerly served on the Board of Advisors at the Center for a New American Security (CNAS) [^3].

McKnight also serves as Chairman of Fortress Value Acquisition Corp. V, a $287.5 million SPAC that began trading on Nasdaq in February 2026 [^8]. He previously served as CEO and Director of Fortress Value Acquisition Corps. I, II, and III [^6].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what McKnight says publicly about Fortress's investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

McKnight has described private credit as "a $6 trillion opportunity set" and positions Fortress as a leader in the space [^9]. On the attractiveness of the asset class, he has stated that private credit "is actually a place to hide — where you can earn those high single digits, low double digit returns on a net basis in a somewhat insulated world, versus the volatility in the stock market and the risk in the bond market" [^9].

McKnight sees the biggest current opportunity in asset-based finance, particularly as traditional banks reduce their lending exposure. He has described the capital migration from banking to private credit as immense: "The amount of capital that does need to move from the small and regional banking system into private credit is frankly immense" [^9].

On deal selection, McKnight has emphasized conviction-based investing: "When we write huge checks, they have to be our highest conviction ideas. There's a lot of those that seem OK, but they're not jump up and down ideas" [^10].

On market differentiation, McKnight has stated: "It's really hard to differentiate yourself when there's no defaults. When you start to have some failures in a typical economic cycle, you're going to start to see some variance in returns" [^10].

Regarding partnerships, McKnight has said: "In conversations with our partners, we increasingly hear that they want tailored and scalable investment solutions that can enhance returns across the credit spectrum. At the same time, we're seeking to expand borrowers' access to capital by securing larger and more diverse pools of capital from investors" [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

**Important context:** Drew McKnight operates as a credit and alternative investment executive at Fortress Investment Group, not a traditional venture capital investor. Fortress manages approximately $54 billion in assets under management as of September 2025 [^8] across credit, real estate, private equity, and permanent capital strategies. McKnight's investment activity is primarily in large-scale credit, lending, and special situations rather than early-stage startup investing. The analysis below reflects this different investment profile.

- SPAC mergers / public equity: 4 of 14 (29%) — MP Materials (FVAC I), ATI Physical Therapy (FVAC II), FVAC III (liquidated), FVAC IV (liquidated)

**Key patterns:**

- **Credit-first investor, not VC:** McKnight's investment approach is fundamentally credit-oriented — structured lending, senior secured facilities, and forward-flow agreements. This is distinct from venture capital and involves much larger check sizes ($50M-$500M+) with downside protection through collateral and seniority.

- **SPAC track record is mixed:** Of four completed Fortress Value Acquisition SPACs under McKnight's leadership, one was highly successful (FVAC I / MP Materials, +523% from $10 offer price), one underperformed (FVAC II / ATI Physical Therapy), and two liquidated without completing mergers (FVAC III, FVAC IV) [^8]. A fifth SPAC (FVAC V) launched in February 2026.

- **Scale of operations:** In 2025, Fortress's direct lending strategies originated $5.8 billion in new commitments across 59 transactions [^11]. Individual deals range from $112 million (Hollywood Feed) to $1.2 billion (Upstart forward-flow).

- **Co-investor and partner patterns:** Fortress frequently partners with other large alternative asset managers. The $1 billion Mubadala partnership and the Upstart deal (with Citi providing debt financing) reflect an institutional-scale operation [^7][^12].

- **Board involvement in select deals:** McKnight has taken board seats at companies where Fortress has meaningful equity exposure — MP Materials (since 2020) and AURA Network Systems (since 2022) [^4][^13]. He formerly sat on the boards of ATI Physical Therapy, HRG Group, Mosaic Acquisition Corp, and Fidelity & Guaranty Life [^6].
