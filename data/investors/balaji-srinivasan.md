## Background

Balaji S. Srinivasan (born May 24, 1980) is an American entrepreneur, angel investor, and author [^1]. The son of physician parents who immigrated from Tamil Nadu, India, he grew up in Plainview, Long Island, New York [^1]. He earned BS, MS, and PhD degrees in electrical engineering and an MS in chemical engineering, all from Stanford University [^2][^1].

At Stanford, Srinivasan conducted graduate research at the intersection of electrical engineering, computer science, and genomics during the Human Genome Project era [^3]. He taught courses in computer science and statistics at Stanford and created the open online course "Startup Engineering," which attracted 250,000 students globally [^3]. He has over 5,000 citations as a research scientist, with papers published in Nature Reviews Genetics, New England Journal of Medicine, Genome Research, and Nature Biotechnology [^2]. In 2013, MIT Technology Review named him to its "Innovators Under 35" (TR35) list for his work on clinical genomics [^4].

In 2007, Srinivasan co-founded Counsyl, a genetic testing company that screened prospective parents for over 100 Mendelian diseases [^1]. Counsyl won the Wall Street Journal Innovation Award for Medicine in 2010 and was named one of Scientific American's Top 10 World Changing Ideas [^2]. Counsyl was acquired by Myriad Genetics for $375 million in 2018 [^5].

In 2013, Srinivasan joined Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) as a general partner, focusing on crypto, biomedicine, and online education [^6][^3]. While at a16z, he made early investments in OpenGov, Omada Health, and Benchling, and recruited Vijay Pande to help build what became the a16z bio fund [^3][^6]. He served as General Partner from January 2013 to January 2015, then transitioned to Board Partner through January 2018 [^6].

Also in 2013, Srinivasan co-founded 21e6 (later 21 Inc), a Bitcoin mining startup that raised over $120 million [^1][^7]. The company pivoted to become Earn.com, a platform allowing senders to pay users in cryptocurrency to reply to emails [^7]. In April 2018, Coinbase acquired Earn.com for over $120 million, and Srinivasan became Coinbase's first CTO [^7]. He left Coinbase in May 2019 after approximately one year in the role [^8].

In April 2014, he co-founded Teleport, a job search engine, which was acquired by Topia in 2017 [^1]. He is also a co-founder and board member of Coin Center, a nonprofit focused on cryptocurrency policy advocacy [^2].

In July 2022, Srinivasan published "The Network State: How To Start a New Country," which became a Wall Street Journal bestseller [^9][^2]. In September 2024, he launched the Network School, a three-month residential program on a private island near Singapore focused on health, technology, and startup societies [^10]. In December 2023, he launched the Balaji Fund, a venture fund backed by Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong, AngelList co-founder Naval Ravikant, and venture capitalist Fred Wilson [^1][^11].

In 2013, Srinivasan gave a notable talk at Y Combinator's Startup School titled "Silicon Valley's Ultimate Exit," in which he urged the technology industry to explore digital alternatives to traditional governance, borrowing from Albert O. Hirschman's "Exit, Voice, and Loyalty" framework [^12].

In March 2023, Srinivasan made a public $1 million bet that Bitcoin would reach $1 million within 90 days, framing it as a warning about U.S. banking system fragility following the failures of Silicon Valley Bank, Signature Bank, and Silvergate Bank [^13]. He settled the bet early in May 2023, paying out $1.5 million in donations to Bitcoin Core development at Chaincode Labs, GiveDirectly, and the counterparty [^14].

## Stated Thesis

Srinivasan has publicly described his investment philosophy as centered on technologies that advance individual freedom and decentralization. He has stated that he would "only invest in those kinds of things that I believe further that aspect of freedom and control" [^15].

On his personal website, he lists his investment focus areas as crypto protocols (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Avalanche, Chainlink, NEAR, Polygon, XMTP, Zcash), alongside technology companies working on developer tools, biotech, AI, space, defense, and social infrastructure [^2].

Srinivasan has emphasized that prediction and investment are closely linked. In a conversation with Tyler Cowen, he stated that venture capitalists effectively make predictions through their returns [^16]. He has described the future as offering "more upside, more downside" as technology reshapes existing systems [^16].

The Balaji Fund, launched in December 2023 on AngelList, focuses on backing companies involved in startup societies, network states, and radical technological innovation [^11]. The fund is backed by prominent crypto and tech figures including Brian Armstrong, Naval Ravikant, and Fred Wilson [^11].

His broader thesis, articulated in "The Network State," is that the internet is replacing many traditional functions of the nation-state — mail (email), media (social media), mobility (ride-sharing), money (cryptocurrencies) — and that digitally-organized communities will eventually achieve physical territory and diplomatic recognition [^9].
