## Background

Girish Mathrubootham is the co-founder and managing partner of Together Fund, an operator-led venture capital firm he co-founded in 2021 [^1]. He is best known as the co-founder and former CEO of Freshworks, the SaaS company he built from a Chennai apartment in 2010 into a Nasdaq-listed company (FRSH) — the first Indian-founder-led, VC-backed SaaS unicorn to go public in the United States [^2].

Mathrubootham grew up in Trichy, Tamil Nadu. He holds a Bachelor's degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Shanmugha Arts Science Technology and Research Academy and an MBA in Marketing from the University of Madras [^3]. He joined Zoho (then AdventNet) in 2001, starting as a PreSales Engineer and rising over nearly a decade to VP of Product Management at the ManageEngine division, where he oversaw product strategy, customer support, and marketing [^4].

The founding of Freshworks traces to a personal experience in 2009: while relocating from Austin, Texas, to Chennai, Mathrubootham shipped household goods that included a Samsung LCD TV that arrived broken. When he posted about it publicly, the internet community rallied to resolve his complaint within a day. That experience, combined with his years building on-premise helpdesk systems at ManageEngine, crystallized his insight that customer support could be reimagined as a cloud-native, social-media-aware product [^5].

Freshworks (founded October 2010 as Freshdesk, with co-founder Shanmugam Krishnasamy) grew from that insight into a multi-product enterprise software platform, backed by Accel, Sequoia Capital, Tiger Global, and CapitalG [^2]. Freshworks listed on Nasdaq on September 22, 2021, raising $1.03 billion in its IPO; its market capitalization was approximately $13 billion on its first day of trading [^6]. More than 500 Indian employees became crorepatis as a result of the IPO [^7].

Mathrubootham stepped down as Freshworks executive chairman effective December 1, 2025, to devote full attention to Together Fund [^8]. He is a founding member of SaaSBOOMi, Asia's largest community of SaaS founders, and has invested personally in over 60 startups as an angel since the early years of building Freshworks [^1]. He published an autobiography, *All In: Memoirs of the Freshworks Founder* (HarperBusiness, April 2025), co-authored with journalist Pankaj Mishra [^9].

## Stated Thesis

Mathrubootham publicly describes Together Fund as an "operator-VC" firm whose competitive advantage is first-hand experience scaling enterprise SaaS from India to global markets in the current decade [^10]. The fund is explicitly positioned as India's first operator-led VC, oriented around the belief that the next wave of iconic AI companies can be built from India for the world [^1].

On founding motivation, Mathrubootham has said:

> "I didn't have a gilt-edged resume or family wealth but that never stopped me from dreaming big. I was lucky enough to find great investors and employees who saw the possibility of my ambitions. But I realize that not every founder is as fortunate, which is why I have backed more than sixty startups as an angel investor." [^1]

On Together Fund's differentiation:

> "We didn't initiate this venture to merely establish another VC fund... Our belief at Together Fund is that we can effect change through our current, first-hand experience, as operators who have scaled a company in this decade." [^10]

On the goal for portfolio founders:

> "Our goal is to share our playbooks, impart our knowledge, and genuinely assist other companies in their quest for success." [^10]

Together Fund publicly states it is "founder-first" — a philosophy defined as placing what is good for the founder above financial returns, on the belief that what is good for the founder is good for the company and ultimately good for investors [^1].

For Together Fund II ($150M), Mathrubootham announced a focus specifically on AI-native products and new software categories, targeting seed and Series A rounds with initial check sizes of $1–10M, at a pace of 8–10 investments per year [^11].

On his personal investment approach (from his earlier angel investing):

> "If I like the founder, I like the product — I want to invest." [^12]

> "You should have more mind in the game rather than skin in the game." [^12]

> "I always believe founders know the best about their business. Based on my learnings in Freshworks, if I can give them some advice... they own their decisions." [^12]

## Inferred Thesis

**Based on 32 verified Together Fund investments (the Together Fund portfolio page, accessed March 2026) plus 6 independently verifiable pre-Together Fund angel investments, totaling 38 data points.**

**Sector breakdown (Together Fund portfolio, 32 investments):**
- AI Infrastructure/Tools: 17 companies (53%) — e.g., Composio, Privado, Protecto, ScaleKit, Securden, Zipstack, Zipy, Bytebeam
- Applied AI (horizontal SaaS): 11 companies (34%) — e.g., Kula, Revenue Hero, Spendflo, Toplyne, Metaforms, Jhana, Presentations AI
- Healthcare/vertical SaaS: 4 companies (13%) — e.g., Confido, Rapid Claims, Spry Therapeutics

**Stage distribution:** Seed-dominant. 27 of 32 Together Fund portfolio entries are Seed (84%); 5 are Series A (16%) [^13]. Pre-Together Fund angel investments (Chargebee, Whatfix, Factors.AI, Fyle, Unacademy, Scripbox) were all early-stage angel/seed-era checks, consistent with a pre-seed/seed conviction. Check sizes have migrated from $5K–$50K angel checks to institutional $1M–$10M seed/Series A checks via the fund [^14].

**Geographic pattern:** India-US corridor exclusively. Together Fund's offices are in Bangalore and Silicon Valley [^11]. Portfolio companies are predominantly India-founded but building for US/global enterprise markets. This mirrors Mathrubootham's own Freshworks trajectory — Chennai-founded, US-targeted SaaS.

**Founder profile:** The portfolio skews heavily toward technical founders building B2B SaaS and AI tools. Most portfolio companies have two technical co-founders (e.g., Composio: Soham Ganatra and Karan Vaidya; Confido: Chetan Reddy and Vichar Shroff) [^15]. Repeat founders and former operators at scaled startups appear frequently (Coreworks founders Prashant Kumar and Pavan Sondur previously scaled enterprise software) [^16].

**Co-investor patterns:** Together Fund co-invests regularly with Blume Ventures (Zipy), Elevation Capital (Composio), Tiger Global (Securden), and Accel India. These are the same investors who backed Freshworks, suggesting Mathrubootham has maintained close relationships with this cohort [^17] [^18].

**Inferred priority vs. stated:** While Together Fund's stated thesis is now primarily "AI-native," the underlying pattern is B2B SaaS founders solving workflow problems for enterprise buyers — whether the label is SaaS, AI, or AI-infra. The shift from "SaaS fund" (Fund I, $85M) to "AI fund" (Fund II, $150M) reflects label evolution more than behavioral change; the portfolio companies remain SaaS-DNA companies using AI as a delivery mechanism [^19].

**Notable pattern:** Mathrubootham's personal angel investments (pre-Together Fund) are weighted toward Indian fintech (Scripbox, Chargebee, Fyle) and edtech (Unacademy), sectors absent from Together Fund's current portfolio. Together Fund has not invested in fintech or consumer at all. The fund reflects a conscious narrowing toward B2B enterprise software and AI infrastructure.

**Sample size caveat:** Together Fund's portfolio page lists 32 companies as of March 2026; the fund has stated it has invested in 34–35 AI startups [^1]. This analysis covers approximately 90% of known Together Fund investments. Pre-fund angel activity is estimated at 60+ startups total; only 6 pre-2021 angel investments could be independently verified (Chargebee, Whatfix, Factors.AI, Fyle, Unacademy, Scripbox). The personal angel portfolio breakdown is therefore not reliable enough to compute meaningful percentages.
