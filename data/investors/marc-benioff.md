## Background

Marc Russell Benioff (born September 25, 1964) is the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Salesforce, the enterprise cloud computing company, and the founder of TIME Ventures, a personal investment fund with over 200 investments [^1] [^2]. He is also the owner of *TIME* magazine, which he and his wife Lynne purchased in September 2018 for $190 million [^3].

Benioff was born in San Francisco. While in high school, he sold his first software application, "How to Juggle," for $75 [^3]. He graduated from the University of Southern California in 1986 with a B.S. in business administration [^3]. After college, he joined Oracle Corporation in a customer-service role and worked there for 13 years, becoming Oracle's Rookie of the Year at age 23 and the youngest vice president in the company's history at age 26, mentored by Oracle founder Larry Ellison [^3] [^4].

In 1999, Benioff co-founded Salesforce in a San Francisco apartment with the mission of "The End of Software," pioneering the software-as-a-service (SaaS) model [^3]. Salesforce has grown into one of the world's largest enterprise software companies.

In 2019, Benioff launched TIME Ventures as a dedicated investment fund focused on backing emerging technology companies [^2]. The firm has backed over 200 companies since its founding [^1]. In October 2021, Marc and Lynne Benioff announced $200 million in new climate investments, including a $100 million Benioff TIME Tree Fund and $100 million in TIME Ventures investments supporting "climate solutions-focused entrepreneurs" [^5].

## Stated Thesis

Benioff has publicly emphasized a "platform-first investment strategy" focused on companies that can become foundational infrastructure for entire ecosystems [^1]. He prioritizes companies with network effects, enterprise-grade execution, and defensible moats with high switching costs [^1].

On business purpose, Benioff has stated: "The business of business is improving the state of the world" [^6].

On hiring and talent, he has said: "Acquiring the right talent is the most important key to growth. Hiring was — and still is — the most important thing we do" [^6].

On customer focus: "Most of all, I discovered that in order to succeed with a product you must truly get to know your customers and build something for them" [^6].

TIME Ventures has a stated focus on sustainability and social impact, with Benioff investing through the lens of climate solutions and "ecopreneurs" — innovators targeting emissions reduction, carbon sequestration, and natural resource protection [^5].
