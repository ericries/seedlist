## Background

Martina Lauchengco is an Operating Partner at Costanoa Ventures, a seed and Series A venture firm based in Palo Alto with over $2 billion in assets under management [^1][^2]. At Costanoa, she sits on multiple portfolio company boards and coaches startups on go-to-market strategy as part of the firm's BuilderOps team [^1][^3].

Lauchengco holds a B.A. in Political Science and an M.A. in Organizational Behavior from Stanford University [^4]. She began her career at Microsoft as a product manager for Microsoft Word and Microsoft Office during the period when Word held less than 50% market share [^4][^5]. She left Microsoft to join Netscape in its early days, where she led product marketing for the Netscape browser, managed the product management team defining Netscape's client applications, and eventually led product marketing for the entire server product line [^4][^5].

After Netscape, Lauchengco held executive roles at AOL in corporate marketing and at Loudcloud/Opsware (later acquired by HP), where she led the marketing team and served as Chief of Staff to CEO Ben Horowitz [^4][^5]. She has over 25 years of experience in executive positions spanning product marketing, product management, and corporate marketing [^4].

In addition to her role at Costanoa, Lauchengco is a Partner at Silicon Valley Product Group (SVPG), where she has worked with companies including Google, Atlassian, EMC, Symantec, and Workiva [^5][^4]. She is also a Marketing Lecturer at UC Berkeley's Fung Institute for Engineering Leadership in the graduate engineering program [^6]. In 2022, she published *LOVED: How to Rethink Marketing for Tech Products*, which became a #1 Amazon bestseller in its category [^7].

In November 2020, Lauchengco was appointed to the advisory board of Bishop Fox, an offensive security firm, alongside Alex Stamos and Evan Wolf [^8].

## Stated Thesis

Lauchengco's publicly stated focus centers on go-to-market strategy for early-stage B2B startups. She has described her day-to-day work as coaching Costanoa portfolio companies on positioning, messaging, sales enablement, and marketing fundamentals [^3][^9].

She has publicly stated that go-to-market is as important as — or more important than — the product itself: "Building is now the easier part. It's much more how do you, whatever your idea happens to be, if you haven't figured out how you're going to bring it to market and penetrate your market" [^10]. She advocates for thinking about "market product fit" rather than "product-market fit," arguing that "it's the intersection of the market and its capacity to absorb whatever technology awesome you are building that actually determines its value" [^10].

As part of Costanoa's BuilderOps team, Lauchengco leads engagements focused on sales, marketing, and talent for portfolio companies, running hands-on sprints on positioning, messaging, funnel refinement, sales playbooks, and sales enablement [^3].

## Inferred Thesis

Lauchengco's role at Costanoa is primarily operational rather than deal-sourcing. As an Operating Partner, she works across the firm's portfolio rather than leading individual investments. Costanoa's firm-level investment data therefore provides the best lens into the types of companies she works with.

Costanoa has made approximately 94 first-time investments over 13 years, averaging 8 new investments annually, with 12 in 2024 and 17 in 2025 [^11]. The firm invests primarily at seed and Series A stages in B2B enterprise technology [^2].

Based on Costanoa's publicly disclosed portfolio companies and the firm's stated focus areas, the portfolio concentrates in:

- **Enterprise applications / AI-enabled SaaS**: The largest category, with companies like Alation (data catalog), Demandbase (ABM), AKASA (healthcare AI), Replicant (contact center AI), Vic.ai (accounting AI), and Focal Systems (retail AI) [^2][^12][^13]
- **Cybersecurity and national security**: AppOmni (SaaS security), Cyberhaven (data security), Kenna Security (vulnerability management, acquired by Cisco 2021), Bishop Fox (offensive security), Vannevar Labs (defense AI), Smack Technologies (national security AI), Cape (communications security) [^2][^8][^13][^14]
- **Fintech**: BillGo (bill management), Highnote (card issuing platform) [^12][^13]
- **Data infrastructure**: Bigeye (data observability), Earthmover (geospatial data), Noteable (notebooks) [^12][^13]
- **Space / defense tech**: Muon Space, Kepler Communications, Auterion [^2]

The firm targets check sizes of $2M-$5M with a sweet spot around $3.5M, taking concentrated 10-15%+ stakes in approximately 35 deals per fund [^15][^16].

Costanoa's portfolio has produced 5 unicorns (including Cyberhaven in 2025, Alation, and others) and 13 acquisitions including Quizlet, GameChanger Media (acquired by Dick's Sporting Goods, 2016), Kenna Security (acquired by Cisco, 2021), and Roadster (acquired by CDK Global for $360M, 2021) [^11][^13][^17].

Note: Because Lauchengco is an Operating Partner focused on go-to-market support rather than a deal-leading investor, individual investment attributions are not publicly tracked to her. Her influence is across the portfolio through hands-on coaching rather than through specific deal sourcing.
