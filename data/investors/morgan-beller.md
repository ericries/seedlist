## Background

Morgan Beller is a General Partner at NFX, a seed-stage venture firm headquartered in San Francisco, which she joined in September 2020 [^1][^2]. She is the fourth General Partner at the firm, joining co-founders James Currier, Pete Flint, and Gigi Levy-Weiss [^2].

Before NFX, Beller co-created Libra (later renamed Diem), Facebook's digital currency initiative, alongside David Marcus and Kevin Weil [^3][^4]. She joined Facebook's Corporate Development team in 2017 and was initially the only person working on the company's blockchain strategy [^5]. She became Head of Strategy for Novi, Facebook's digital wallet for the Libra payment system [^1][^6]. The Diem Association ultimately shut down in January 2022 [^4].

Prior to Facebook, Beller led Corporate Development at Medium, where she oversaw the Embedly acquisition and helped develop Medium's subscription strategy [^1][^2]. She began her career on the Deal Team at Andreessen Horowitz in 2013, when the firm had roughly 50 employees, and also scouted for Spark Capital [^2][^7].

Beller graduated from Cornell University with a bachelor's degree in Statistics and Information Science [^1]. She was named to the Forbes 30 Under 30 Venture Capital list and MIT Technology Review's 2020 Innovators Under 35 in the "Visionaries" category [^1][^5].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Beller says publicly about her investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Beller has described her core investment criteria as two constants: "one, a strong understanding of defensibility, specifically network effects and, two, a Founder who is already living in the future" [^2].

On the type of founders she seeks, Beller has stated: "They're contrarian. They really strongly believe in X where the world believes in Y. They have 'a thing'" [^2].

Beller has said she puts founder quality above all else: "I put people first" and has "continued to put people first over the course of the past 10 years" [^7]. She invests through NFX, which structures its thesis around network effects as the primary source of defensibility in digital businesses [^8].

Her stated focus areas include fintech, generative AI, crypto, marketplaces, and space [^1]. On crypto infrastructure, Beller has stated: "Web 3 developers should be building on open protocols" [^9]. She has also described stablecoins as "defense tech" [^10].

On navigating regulated markets, Beller has said: "We're trying to change the system, and there are a lot of people who are incentivized for the global financial system not to change" [^5].

## Inferred Thesis

The analysis below is based on 11 verified investments where Beller's involvement as the NFX lead or board member is confirmed through press coverage, NFX's website, or Crunchbase data [^1][^8][^9][^11][^12][^13][^14][^15][^16].

Beller's total portfolio is reported at 130+ investments [^8], but the vast majority are NFX fund-level investments where her individual role is not publicly distinguished from other GPs. The analysis below focuses on investments where her personal involvement is documented.

**Sector concentration (based on 11 verified investments):**
- Crypto / Web3: 5 of 11 (45%) — Ramp Network, Radicle, Celestia, Celo (board), Mysten Labs
- Space: 3 of 11 (27%) — Stoke Space, Starfish Space, Starcloud
- AI / Legal Tech: 1 of 11 (9%) — EvenUp
- Fintech: 1 of 11 (9%) — Ramp Network (also counted in crypto)
- SaaS: 1 of 11 (9%) — Radicle (also counted in crypto)

**Key patterns:**

- **Crypto-heavy early portfolio**: Beller's first investment at NFX was EvenUp (July 2020) [^11], followed quickly by Radicle (February 2021) [^9], her first crypto deal at NFX. Crypto/Web3 represents 5 of 11 verified personal investments (45%), consistent with her Libra background and stated Web3 focus.

- **Space as a distinct thesis**: 3 of 11 verified investments (27%) are in space technology — Stoke Space (seed, 2021) [^12], Starfish Space (seed, 2021) [^13], and Starcloud (seed, 2024) [^14]. This is an unusual concentration for a crypto-native investor and represents a distinctive personal thesis within NFX's broader portfolio.

- **Seed-stage concentration**: All 11 verified investments were at seed stage, with follow-on participation in later rounds (Celestia Series A/B, EvenUp Series B). This is consistent with NFX's fund-level focus on pre-seed and seed.

- **Co-investor patterns**: NFX under Beller frequently co-invests with Galaxy Digital (Ramp Network, Radicle) [^9][^15] and MaC Venture Capital (Stoke Space, Starfish Space) [^12][^13]. Other frequent co-investors include Y Combinator, Boost VC, and Liquid2 Ventures.

- **Board involvement signals conviction**: Beller holds board seats at Ramp Network, Starfish Space, Celo, Xternity, and Starcloud [^1], suggesting these represent her highest-conviction investments.

- **Technical founder preference**: Stoke Space (founded by Blue Origin engineers) [^12], Starfish Space (founded by Blue Origin veterans) [^13], and EvenUp (AI/data team) [^11] all have deeply technical founding teams, consistent with Beller's stated preference for technical founders.

- **Notable gap**: Despite stating generative AI as a focus area, only 1 of 11 verified personal investments (EvenUp) has a primary AI thesis, though the investment was made in 2020 before the generative AI wave. Her AI-specific deal flow at the individual level is not well documented.

**Sample size caveat**: 11 verified investments represent a small fraction of Beller's reported 130+ investments. The true sector and stage distribution may differ significantly from this sample.
