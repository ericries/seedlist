## Background

Gabe Greenbaum is a General Partner at B Capital Group, based in the firm's Los Angeles office, which he joined in December 2020 [^1][^2]. He focuses on early-stage technology investments and co-leads the firm's Enterprise Growth and Early Stage Investment Committee [^3].

Before joining B Capital, Greenbaum spent over eight years as a Partner at Pritzker Group Venture Capital, where he ran the firm's West Coast office and led investments across enterprise software, marketplaces, proptech, logistics/supply chain, machine learning, and IoT [^1][^2]. During his tenure at Pritzker Group, he backed companies including Openpath (acquired by Motorola), Scopely (acquired by Savvy Games for $4.9 billion), Augury, Pype (acquired by Autodesk), Project 44, Specright, HQO, VTS, Cue Health, and Catalytic [^3][^4].

Greenbaum is also a serial entrepreneur who co-founded two companies that were both acquired: StudentSpace, an early proptech platform based in Chicago (acquired by @Properties), and SwiftIQ [^1][^3]. He began his venture career at OCA Ventures [^5].

He holds a BA with honors from Washington University in St. Louis, an MBA from Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management, and a JD from Northwestern University School of Law [^1][^2]. Greenbaum has been named to LA's NextGen10, Chicago Crain's Tech50, TechWeek's Tech100, and BuiltWorlds Mavericks 50 [^3][^5]. He serves as a Foundation Board Trustee for the Children's Hospital of Los Angeles (CHLA) [^1].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Greenbaum says publicly about his investment focus. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Greenbaum describes his focus as B2B software and enterprise technology, with particular interest in companies at the intersection of AI and verticalized industries undergoing disruption — including industrial, supply chain, proptech, and construction tech [^3][^6].

At B Capital, his stated role is to help the firm "explore the unique needs of the next wave of global tech leaders, building a formal process to track and support the early-stage startup landscape," and to "connect B Capital into helping compelling B2B companies understand their addressable market and customer needs earlier in their growth cycle" [^2].

On his geographic focus, he has stated: "I do want to really spend a lot of time investing in the Southern California ecosystem, bringing additional awareness to the dynamic founders that are here" [^6].

B Capital as a firm publicly describes its strategy as focusing on B2B startups in enterprise technology, consumer enablement, fintech, insurtech, transportation, industrial, and healthcare [^6].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 18 verified investments across Greenbaum's career at B Capital and Pritzker Group (sample is not exhaustive — PitchBook lists additional investments not yet independently verified):

**Stage distribution:**
Greenbaum's verified investments span seed through Series B, with a concentration at seed and Series A stages at B Capital and growth-stage follow-on at Pritzker. At B Capital specifically, his named portfolio companies skew early (seed to Series A lead investments): Gameplanner.ai (seed), Precision Neuroscience (Series A lead), ORO Labs (Series A co-lead), Nektar.ai (seed), When Insurance (seed), CurbWaste (seed), GrayMatter Robotics (seed/Series A). This is notably earlier than B Capital's traditional Series B–D mandate, reflecting his specific role in expanding the firm's early-stage coverage.

**Sector breakdown (of 18 verified investments):**
- Enterprise SaaS / B2B software: 7 investments (39%) — ORO Labs, Nektar.ai, BuildOps, Specright, Catalytic, VTS, HQO
- Industrial / supply chain / logistics: 4 investments (22%) — GrayMatter Robotics, Project 44, Augury, CurbWaste
- AI / AI-native companies: 3 investments (17%) — Gameplanner.ai, ORO Labs (AI procurement), Nektar.ai
- PropTech / construction tech: 3 investments (17%) — BuildOps, HQO, StudentSpace (co-founded)
- Health IT / medtech: 2 investments (11%) — Precision Neuroscience, Cue Health
- Fintech / insurtech: 2 investments (11%) — Wildfire Systems, When Insurance

Note: sample size of 18 is insufficient for precise percentages across his full career. Several Pritzker Group-era investments could not be independently confirmed as Greenbaum-led deals. The percentages above reflect identified deals only and likely underweight proptech given his entrepreneurial background in the space.

**Vertical SaaS pattern:**
A strong pattern across Greenbaum's verified deals is vertical software for underserved industries — waste hauling (CurbWaste), commercial construction contracting (BuildOps), procurement (ORO Labs), and commercial real estate (HQO, VTS). These are markets that major SaaS players ignored and where Greenbaum appears to have developed a systematic thesis.

**AI and robotics overlay:**
At B Capital, Greenbaum has pursued AI investments across two vectors: pure AI platforms (Gameplanner.ai — acquired by Airbnb for ~$200M) and AI applied to physical-world industrial problems (GrayMatter Robotics, Precision Neuroscience). This suggests he views AI not purely as a software category but as an enabling layer for industrial transformation.

**Notable exits:**
Gameplanner.ai (Airbnb, ~$200M, 2023), Openpath (Motorola), Scopely ($4.9B acquisition by Savvy Games), Pype (Autodesk). Multiple exits in proptech and enterprise software suggest a durable conviction in those categories.

**Geographic concentration:**
Strong Southern California focus at B Capital (BuildOps, GrayMatter Robotics are LA-area companies) alongside continued Midwest roots (Chicago-area proptech networks from Pritzker Group years). Has noted explicit intent to invest more deeply in the SoCal ecosystem.

**Co-investor patterns:**
Frequently co-invests with Norwest Venture Partners (ORO Labs Series A), Bow Capital (GrayMatter Robotics), TTV Capital (Wildfire, CurbWaste), and Founders Fund (BuildOps). At Pritzker Group era, Fika Ventures and Mucker Capital appear repeatedly.
