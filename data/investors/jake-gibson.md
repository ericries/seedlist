## Background

Jake Gibson is a Founding Partner at Better Tomorrow Ventures (BTV), an early-stage fintech-focused venture capital firm he co-founded in 2019 with Sheel Mohnot [^1][^2]. Gibson co-founded NerdWallet, the personal finance comparison platform, where he served as COO and helped build the company over four years, leveraging SEO and content marketing to drive growth [^3][^4].

Before NerdWallet, Gibson spent six years on Wall Street as an interest rate derivatives trader [^1][^2]. After leaving NerdWallet, he spent five years as a full-time angel investor before co-founding BTV [^1][^2].

Gibson holds two bachelor's degrees from MIT in Math and Management Science, with a focus on Quantitative Finance [^1][^2]. He has also served as an Entrepreneur in Residence for 500 Fintech [^5].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Gibson says publicly about his investing approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Gibson has articulated a clear thesis centered on financial services as a lever for global impact: "If you want to create consumer surplus and improve economic outcomes around the world, I can't think of a bigger lever than financial services" [^1].

BTV leads pre-seed and seed rounds in fintech companies globally, with check sizes typically ranging from $500K to $3M [^2][^6]. The firm runs a program called The Mint to support fintech founders in the earliest stages of their journey, often even pre-incorporation [^6].

On portfolio support, Gibson has stated: "From startup to IPO, Ramp consistently delivers. It's part of the foundational tech stack that we encourage all of our founders to adopt" [^6]. BTV emphasizes hands-on operational support: "At The Mint, we focus on removing distractions for founders" [^6].

## Inferred Thesis

The analysis below is based on BTV's publicly listed portfolio of 150+ companies and Gibson's pre-BTV angel investments [^2][^6][^7].

**Sector concentration (based on BTV's portfolio of 150+ companies, of which 55 are classified as fintech):**
- Fintech (core): 55 of 150+ (approximately 37%) — spanning payments, lending, insurance, banking, infrastructure
- Enterprise applications: 43 of 150+ (approximately 29%) — including accounting, compliance, and B2B SaaS
- Payments: prominent sub-sector including Coast, Outpost, Butter (approximate)
- Cards & banking: including Relay, Mendel (approximate)
- Insurance: including Clearcover (approximate)
- Accounting & finance: including Basis, InScope, Layer (approximate)

*Note: BTV's portfolio exceeds 150 companies. The breakdown above uses BTV's own categorization from their portfolio page. Given the portfolio size, precise percentages across all companies are approximate [^7].*

**Key patterns:**

- **Pure fintech specialization**: BTV is among the most sector-focused seed funds in venture capital, with an almost exclusive focus on financial technology and adjacent enterprise applications [^2][^6].

- **Global geographic reach**: Unlike most US seed funds, BTV actively invests in fintech companies across Latin America, Africa, Europe, and Asia. Portfolio companies include ChipperCash (Africa), Mendel (Mexico), Outpost (London), and others [^7].

- **Pre-seed emphasis**: BTV's The Mint program and pre-seed focus allow Gibson and Mohnot to work with founders before they have even incorporated, capturing the earliest-stage deals [^6].

- **Operator-investor with fintech domain expertise**: Gibson's experience co-founding NerdWallet and trading derivatives on Wall Street gives him direct operational and domain knowledge in financial services [^3][^4].

- **Scale through fund progression**: BTV has grown from a small initial fund to $450M+ in AUM across three funds (Fund I, Fund II at $150M in 2021, Fund III at ~$140M), with 12 unicorns in the portfolio [^2][^6].

- **Pre-BTV angel portfolio includes breakout hits**: Before BTV, Gibson angel-invested in Albert, ChipperCash, Clearcover, Ethic, Ethos Insurance, Drip Capital, Flexport, Hippo, Human Interest, Nova Credit, Ramp, and Starship [^2]. Multiple of these became unicorns.
