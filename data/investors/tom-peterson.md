## Background

Tom Peterson is the co-founder and managing partner of Social Leverage, a seed-stage venture capital firm he founded with Howard Lindzon [^1]. Peterson also serves as the firm's chief compliance officer [^2]. He and Lindzon have been friends for over 25 years [^3].

Before entering venture capital, Peterson served as President and Chief Operating Officer of Neutral Posture Ergonomics (NASDAQ: NTRL), a publicly traded ergonomic chair manufacturer, from approximately 1994 to 2004 [^4][^5]. He led the company's privatization [^1][^4]. Earlier in his career, he worked as a Sales Manager at Imperial Oil Ltd from 1991 to 1994 [^4].

Peterson holds an MBA from the W.P. Carey School of Business at Arizona State University (1989-1991) and a B.A. from the University of Alberta (1984-1988) [^4].

Peterson began angel investing in 2005 alongside Lindzon, backing early-stage companies including LifeLock, GolfNow, Rent.com, and BuddyMedia [^4][^5]. In 2011, Peterson and Lindzon were recognized by Institutional Investor as "super angels" who were "reorganizing early-stage investing with disciplined capital, uniform investing rules and a sense of community" [^1][^6]. In 2013, they raised their first micro-VC fund, transitioning from angel investing to institutional fund management [^1][^3].

Peterson manages the day-to-day business of all Social Leverage entities and assists portfolio companies with operational and corporate development expertise [^1][^5]. He leads all financing, deal, and term sheet negotiations for the firm [^7]. He also serves on the boards of portfolio companies including SecFi, CommLoan, and Anyone Home, and as a board member and advisor at LineStar [^7].

## Stated Thesis

Peterson has stated that Social Leverage looks for founding teams with strong pedigrees and technical capability. In a 2013 interview, he described his investment criteria: "We look at the pedigree of the team and what they have done in the past. We like a minimum of two co-founders, and the founders to be developers" [^3].

On due diligence, Peterson has said the firm does not rely on social proof alone: "We don't solely rely on social proof. We do our homework on the companies — we have to" [^3].

Peterson has emphasized that startups need to demonstrate traction and product-market fit before approaching Social Leverage: "We don't get involved with a team until the company is product ready and proven the revenue" [^3]. He values market testing above all: "I continually go back to testing traction — and companies need to show me the traction. And, always be closing and selling" [^3].

On the pitch itself, Peterson looks for clarity: "Tell me the product, the pain points, why people will pay for this, the product market fit, value proposition, trending data, how to monetize and the economics behind it" [^3].

Social Leverage as a firm publicly describes its focus as "investing in exceptional founders building the future of finance and vertical AI" [^8].

**Notable Gaps:** The firm's stated emphasis on "vertical AI" is primarily visible in Fund V (2024-present) and Fund IV. The historical portfolio is overwhelmingly fintech and SaaS. Peterson's operational role as day-to-day manager and deal negotiator suggests he may be more focused on the business fundamentals and governance side of investments, while Lindzon drives deal sourcing through his media network and public profile.
