## Background

Ambar Bhattacharyya is a Managing Director at Maverick Ventures, the venture capital arm of Maverick Capital, based in San Francisco [^1]. He has spent nearly 20 years investing in early-stage healthcare and technology companies [^1]. Prior to joining Maverick in 2015, Bhattacharyya was an investor at Bessemer Venture Partners (2010-2015) and Bain Capital Ventures (2006-2008) [^2]. He began his career at Bain & Company as a Senior Associate Consultant (2003-2006) and also worked at The Bridgespan Group (2004-2005) [^2].

Before his venture career, Bhattacharyya served as assistant to the CEO at MinuteClinic, where he helped the company grow its national footprint prior to its acquisition by CVS [^3]. This operational experience in healthcare shaped his investing thesis; he has noted that at MinuteClinic he observed that "people were willing to pay all cash, out of pocket, for what they deemed to be a 'better' health-care experience" [^3].

Bhattacharyya holds an M.B.A. from Harvard Business School and a B.S. and B.A. from the University of Pennsylvania, where he studied economics and psychology [^3] [^1]. He is an advisor to the California Healthcare Foundation Innovation Fund and an Aspen Institute Health Innovators Fellow [^1].

He was named to Fortune's "40 Under 40" list in the healthcare category in 2020 [^3], Business Insider's "30 leaders under 40 transforming healthcare," recognized as a Global Shaper by the World Economic Forum [^4], and included on Forbes' Midas Brink List of the world's best venture capital investors in 2025 [^1].

During his venture career, Bhattacharyya has led or been actively involved in investments resulting in 16 IPOs and acquisitions, with six companies achieving valuations of over $1B in the public markets [^1]. He currently serves on the board of directors at Centivo, Cityblock Health, and Hims & Hers Health, and has held board seats at Artemis Health, BlueFlag Security, and Daymark Health [^5] [^6].

## Stated Thesis

Bhattacharyya and Maverick Ventures publicly describe their focus as investing at the intersection of healthcare and technology, with a particular emphasis on consumer-facing health applications and the transformative potential of AI [^7].

Bhattacharyya has stated that a core part of his thesis centers on the shift from "sick care" to preventive healthcare: "The root cause of the interest in these areas is the desire to do more preventive health care, turning the system from a 'sick care' system to a 'health system'" [^3]. He has emphasized the fundamental misalignment in traditional fee-for-service models, where "the financial incentives are aligned with treating people after they are sick, not necessarily spending time with a patient beforehand" [^3].

On consumer healthcare, he has identified a key thesis around healthcare segmentation: "There remains a major lack of segmentation in health care, and there are millions of patients who are willing to pay for their version of 'better'" [^3].

On AI in healthcare, Bhattacharyya has stated: "Artificial intelligence and machine learning in health care are not hypotheticals anymore" [^3]. He emphasizes a thematic approach to investing that identifies large megatrends growing exponentially, including AI and GLP-1 therapies [^4]. Maverick focuses on five key healthcare stakeholders — providers, payers, employers, pharma companies, and consumers — across approximately $4 trillion in annual healthcare spend [^4].

Maverick Ventures publicly states that it backs "a few teams per year so they can be true partners to each company" and provides a "no walls" approach between Maverick Capital's public and private investment teams [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 24 verified investments attributed to Bhattacharyya or made during his tenure at Maverick Ventures (2015-present), the following patterns emerge. Note: this represents a subset of Maverick Ventures' 124+ total investments [^8]; some investments are attributed to the firm broadly rather than to Bhattacharyya individually.

**Sector distribution** (based on 24 verified investments): Healthcare and healthtech dominate the portfolio — 18 of 24 investments (75%) are in healthcare or healthtech. Of the healthcare investments: 7 are in healthcare delivery/services (Cityblock, Centivo, Devoted Health, Oula, Daymark Health, Aligned Marketplace, Nourish), 4 are in health technology platforms (Hims & Hers, Garner Health, Notable Health, Claim Health), 3 are in life sciences/biotech (Caribou Biosciences, House Rx, Mandolin), 2 are in healthcare data/AI (ConcertAI, Collective Medical Technologies), and 2 are in consumer health (Chapter, Vetcove). The remaining 6 of 24 (25%) span enterprise software (Retool), AI/ML infrastructure (MosaicML), cybersecurity (BioCatch), quantum technology (Infleqtion), consumer tech (Coupang, LTK).

**Stage distribution** (based on 24 verified investments): 9 of 24 (38%) at seed stage (Hims & Hers, Cityblock, Chapter, Vetcove, House Rx, Notable Health, Daymark Health, Claim Health, Infleqtion); 9 of 24 (38%) at Series A (Centivo, Devoted Health, Garner Health, Collective Medical Technologies, Nourish, Oula, Mandolin, BioCatch, MosaicML); 6 of 24 (25%) at Series B or later (Caribou Biosciences, ConcertAI, Retool, Coupang, LTK, Aligned Marketplace follow-on). The roughly even split between seed and Series A is consistent with Maverick's stated focus on seed through Series B.

**Geographic concentration**: The majority of verified portfolio companies are headquartered in the United States, with notable concentration in New York City (Cityblock, Centivo, Garner Health, Chapter, Devoted Health) and San Francisco/Bay Area (Hims & Hers, Notable Health, MosaicML, Retool). The one notable international outlier is Coupang (South Korea).

**Co-investor patterns**: Frequent co-investors across Bhattacharyya's deals include Thrive Capital (Hims & Hers, Cityblock, Garner Health), Bain Capital Ventures (Centivo), F-Prime Capital (Centivo, Notable Health), 8VC (Oula, Notable Health), and Greylock Partners (Notable Health, Mandolin). There is a pattern of backing companies where other healthcare-focused funds also invest.

**Founder profile patterns**: Bhattacharyya shows a strong preference for founders with deep operational experience in healthcare. Cityblock's Iyah Romm came from Sidewalk Labs/Google; Devoted Health's founders include former CMS officials; House Rx's founders came from Flatiron Health; and Hims & Hers founder Andrew Dudum had consumer product experience. Several portfolio companies have founders with clinical backgrounds (Daymark Health's Dr. Justin Bekelman, Nourish's team of registered dietitians).

**Notable gap between stated and inferred thesis**: While Maverick publicly emphasizes consumer health, the portfolio leans more heavily toward B2B healthcare infrastructure (employer health plans, provider tools, care delivery platforms) than pure consumer applications. The consumer-facing investments (Hims & Hers, Chapter) are present but represent a minority of the overall portfolio.
