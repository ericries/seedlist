## Background

Sam Shank is a serial entrepreneur and angel investor based in San Francisco. He is best known as the founder and CEO of HotelTonight, the dominant last-minute hotel booking app, which he grew to generate billions of dollars in gross hotel bookings before it was acquired by Airbnb in March 2019 for approximately $400 million (roughly half cash, half pre-IPO stock) [^1][^2][^3].

Shank's entrepreneurial career began unconventionally. He started in Hollywood as a production assistant on Wes Craven's horror film *Scream* before pivoting to tech during the dot-com era [^1]. He survived the crash and went on to found DealBase.com, a travel deals search engine, and TravelPost.com, a hotel reviews site (acquired by SideStep, which was later acquired by Kayak, where Shank served as VP of Business Development) [^4][^5]. He also worked in comparison shopping at Excite, CNET, and NexTag [^5].

Shank holds an MBA in Entrepreneurship and Finance from Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management (2004) [^5][^6]. He also holds a degree from the University of Virginia [^6].

After Airbnb's acquisition of HotelTonight, Shank held various roles at Airbnb including Head of Guest Experience and Head of Commercialization, remaining for three years and eight months before departing in November 2022 [^1][^3].

As of 2025-2026, Shank serves as a Visiting Partner at a16z Speedrun, Andreessen Horowitz's accelerator program for AI-native startups [^7]. He is also building Stand Insurance, a wildfire insurance company utilizing science and AI to make high-risk homes insurable [^1][^7].

## Stated Thesis

Shank has not published a formal investment thesis. Based on his public statements and career, his investing is informed by his experience as a three-time founder in travel and consumer technology.

On LinkedIn, Shank has shared the advice: "Don't start with the product. Start with distribution" [^8], reflecting his operational philosophy that distribution strategy should precede product development.

At PhocusWire's Battleground event, Shank advised founders to "get a founder coach" for unbiased guidance, describing it as "an important investment" [^9]. He also emphasized creating products that are "crazy-different" rather than incrementally better [^9].

## In Their Own Words

"Don't start with the product. Start with distribution." — Sam Shank, LinkedIn post, 2026 [^8].

Shank has advised founders to do layoffs "only once" because "people can accept a single restructuring, but hinting at a second round causes them to fear additional rounds and disengage" [^9].

On leadership, Shank has suggested "giving five compliments for every critical comment, doing so visibly and authentically" [^9].

On HotelTonight's path to profitability, Shank's team adopted the mantra: "Screw what happened before. We're putting that behind us. It's time to build" [^15].
