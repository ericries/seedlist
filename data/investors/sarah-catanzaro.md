## Background

Sarah Catanzaro is a General Partner at Amplify Partners, the early-stage venture firm focused on technical founders [^1]. She joined Amplify in November 2017 as a Principal [^2][^3], was promoted to Partner in January 2020 [^4], and was elevated to General Partner in January 2022 [^5].

Catanzaro holds a B.S. in International Security Studies from Stanford University, where she conducted research at the Center for International Security and Cooperation (CISAC) under Professor Martha Crenshaw [^3][^6][^7]. She also studied at the Institut d'etudes politiques de Paris [^3].

Her career began in national security and intelligence. She served as Program Director at the Center for Advanced Defense Studies (C4ADS) from 2009 to 2011, where she directed projects on adversary behavioral modeling and Somali pirate network analysis, working with AI researchers from MIT and Carnegie Mellon on computational linguistics, threat evaluation, and network analysis [^1][^6][^7]. She then worked as a Cyber Intelligence Analyst at Cyveillance from 2011 to 2013 [^3][^6]. During this period, she was deployed to the US Secret Service to implement a threat intelligence platform, leveraging NLP to protect candidates including President Barack Obama during the 2012 presidential election [^1].

From 2013 to 2014, she served as an Embedded Analyst/Consultant at Palantir Technologies, implementing analytics solutions for government agencies [^1][^3][^6]. She then became Head of Data at Mattermark from 2014 to 2016, where she led a team of five to ten people using data mining and deep learning to build a private company intelligence platform covering over one million companies [^1][^6][^7]. Before joining Amplify, she spent approximately a year as a Data Partner at Canvas Ventures, where she sourced multiple investments [^2][^3].

When Amplify recruited Catanzaro, the firm described her as "impossibly smart with no discernable ego," possessing "an innate ability to authentically connect with founders, and deep domain experience" [^2]. She was the firm's first "practitioner turned investor" [^2]. Catanzaro also serves as an Associate at the Creative Destruction Lab (CDL), mentoring startups in the AI stream [^8].

## Stated Thesis

Catanzaro publicly describes her investment focus as: "I invest in the tools that I wish I had earlier in my career; tools that enable us to collect, store, manage, analyze, and model data more effectively" [^1][^9]. She identifies as "a data scientist first and an investor second" [^7].

She has articulated a critical view of the gap between ML prototyping and production deployment: "I think it's become a lot easier to develop and train ML models... But I actually don't think it's gotten any easier to build ML-driven applications" [^10]. On the challenges of ML tooling fragmentation, she has noted: "Many of those tools actually solve fairly narrow and specific problems. And those tools are not well integrated with each other" [^10].

Catanzaro emphasizes backing problem-solvers over technologists: "We don't invest in people who are just enamored with new technologies... we invest in people who want to solve problems and want to build products" [^9]. She has also stated: "AI is not a substitute for building great products... it's just an underlying technology" [^9].

On AI application startups specifically, she has written: "My AI investment thesis is that AI application startups will need to solve research and engineering problems that the labs are not currently focused on; thereby accumulating more technical defensibility" [^11].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 23 verified investments attributed to Catanzaro at Amplify Partners (from her Amplify bio page, Amplify blog posts, Evalyze profile, and press coverage), the following patterns emerge. This represents her personal deal attribution; as GP she may be involved in additional firm investments.

**Sector breakdown (23 verified investments):**
- Data infrastructure and analytics: 10 of 23 (43%) — Hex, dbt Labs (firm-wide), Hightouch (firm-wide), Datafold, Meroxa, Metaphor Data, MotherDuck, CedarDB, Spiral, Eppo
- ML/AI infrastructure and tools: 7 of 23 (30%) — OctoML, Einblick, DatologyAI, Modal Labs, Outerbounds, LangChain, PostgresML
- Creative AI and applied ML: 3 of 23 (13%) — RunwayML, David AI, Gantry
- Computational biology: 1 of 23 (4%) — InterVenn Biosciences
- Design and product tools: 1 of 23 (4%) — Maze
- Analytics / no-code: 1 of 23 (4%) — Bayes (acquired by Airtable)

**Stage distribution:** Predominantly seed-stage first checks, consistent with Amplify's firm-wide strategy. Some investments were made at Series A (RunwayML Series A, Outerbounds Series A). Catanzaro has also participated in follow-on rounds for portfolio companies that have scaled (DatologyAI Series A, Eppo Series B, RunwayML Series C, MotherDuck Series B).

**Geographic concentration:** Portfolio is concentrated in the San Francisco Bay Area and New York, with CedarDB in Munich representing an outlier international investment [^12].

**Check size:** $500K to $10M at initial check, with a sweet spot around $5M [^3][^13].

**Founder profile patterns:** Catanzaro has a pronounced preference for practitioner-turned-founders — engineers and data scientists who experienced pain points firsthand and then built solutions. Multiple portfolio founders came from major technology companies: Modal's Erik Bernhardsson from Spotify, Outerbounds' team from Netflix (Metaflow), WarpStream's founders from infrastructure backgrounds, and Spiral's founders from Palantir [^14][^15][^16]. She values founders who combine deep technical expertise with genuine product intuition.

**Co-investor patterns:** Based on downstream funding rounds, frequent co-investors include Lux Capital (RunwayML, Modal Labs), Sequoia Capital (Hex), Andreessen Horowitz (dbt Labs), and Benchmark (Hex Series B). She has co-invested with Mike Dauber on DatologyAI [^17].

**Exit patterns:** Several notable exits including WarpStream (acquired by Confluent for $220M, September 2024), Eppo (acquired by Datadog for $220M, May 2025), Einblick (acquired by Databricks), and Bayes (acquired by Airtable) [^18][^19][^1].

**Notable emphasis vs. stated thesis:** Catanzaro's stated thesis centers on data tools broadly, but her actual portfolio reveals a strong concentration in the "modern data stack" and its successors — companies building the infrastructure layer for data teams (dbt, Hex, Hightouch, Datafold, MotherDuck). More recently, her investments have shifted toward AI-native infrastructure (LangChain, DatologyAI, Spiral, Modal), reflecting the ecosystem's evolution from analytics-first to AI-first data platforms. Her portfolio has minimal consumer or fintech exposure, and digital biology (InterVenn) represents a small fraction despite Amplify's recent expansion into that sector.
