## Background

Jaimin Rangwalla is the Chief Investment Officer of Public Investments and Senior Managing Director at Coatue Management, one of the largest technology-focused investment firms with approximately $70 billion in assets under management [^1][^2]. In this role, he oversees the day-to-day operations of Coatue's public investing business — a portfolio valued at over $40 billion across roughly 52 positions as of Q4 2025 — as well as several other investing strategies [^1][^3].

Rangwalla graduated from the Leonard N. Stern School of Business at New York University with a B.S. in Finance and Accounting in 2004 [^4][^5]. He began his career as an Investment Banking Analyst at Merrill Lynch in the M&A division from 2004 to 2006 [^1][^4]. He then worked as a Private Equity Analyst at Fenway Partners, a middle-market private equity firm focused on branded consumer products and specialty distribution [^5][^6].

Rangwalla joined Coatue Management in 2007 as a Partner [^1][^4]. In 2012, he founded Shou Capital LLC, where he served as Director of Research [^5]. By 2014, he had been promoted to Senior Managing Director at Coatue [^4][^7]. In June 2024, following the departure of Senior Managing Partner Daniel Senft — described by journalist Eric Newcomer as "probably the most important person at Coatue not named Laffont" — Rangwalla was promoted to Chief Investment Officer of Public Investments [^8][^9].

Beyond his role managing public equities, Rangwalla has been actively involved in Coatue's private investments, particularly in the clean energy and autonomous systems sectors. He currently serves as a board member at Reliable Robotics Corporation, Form Energy, dcbel, and WeaveGrid, and as a board observer at Aurora Solar and Uplight [^4][^7][^10].

## Stated Thesis

Rangwalla has publicly articulated a strong conviction that AI represents a durable, fundamentals-driven investment opportunity rather than a speculative bubble. In Coatue's "C:\Take" commentary series, he stated: "We see today's AI-led rally as durable and supported by fundamentals, rather than a bubble" [^11]. He highlighted "the extraordinary pace of AI adoption, with ChatGPT reaching mass penetration years faster than the internet or PCs" as a key supporting indicator [^11].

In a Barron's interview, Rangwalla described the AI opportunity as "unlike anything before" and discussed where Coatue sees "trillion-dollar potential taking shape" [^12].

At the Morgan Stanley Investment Leadership Conference, Rangwalla participated in a panel alongside Point72's Harry Schwefel to discuss AI disruption, navigating market drawdowns, and upskilling future investment talent [^13].

On specific private investments, Rangwalla has expressed a consistent focus on companies at the intersection of technology and physical infrastructure. Regarding Reliable Robotics, he stated: "We believe Reliable Robotics is a leader in aircraft automation for commercial aviation. We were impressed by the team's clear vision, measured certification progress and track record of industry achievement" [^14]. On Form Energy, he said: "We believe Form Energy has one of the most promising technologies in the energy storage ecosystem" [^15]. On WeaveGrid, he noted: "WeaveGrid is bridging the tech, mobility, and energy worlds through a software platform that transforms EVs into energy assets. WeaveGrid's approach could revolutionize the role of EVs in the home" [^16].

## Inferred Thesis

Rangwalla's investment activity spans two distinct domains: managing Coatue's $40+ billion public equities portfolio and leading specific private investments where he takes board seats. The analysis below covers both.

### Public Equities (CIO of Publics)

As CIO of Publics, Rangwalla oversees Coatue's public equities portfolio. Based on the Q4 2025 13F filing (52 positions, ~$40 billion), the portfolio is heavily concentrated in large-cap technology [^3]:

- **Top holdings**: Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM), Microsoft (MSFT), Meta Platforms (META), Amazon (AMZN), GE Vernova (GEV) [^3]
- **Sector concentration**: Overwhelmingly technology — AI infrastructure, semiconductors, cloud computing, enterprise software, and consumer internet platforms
- **Recent activity (Q3 2025)**: Increased stakes in Alphabet, Microsoft, Broadcom, Oracle, Spotify, DoorDash, and Alibaba; reduced positions in Amazon, Nvidia, Constellation Energy, Reddit, and Tesla; initiated new positions in Synopsys, Applied Materials, and Snowflake [^3]

*Note: The public portfolio reflects Coatue's firm-wide public investing strategy under Rangwalla's leadership, not his individual stock picks. Philippe Laffont as founder and portfolio manager retains ultimate authority over the public book.*

### Private Investments (Board-Level Involvement)

Based on 7 verified private investments where Rangwalla has taken board seats or board observer roles:

**Sector allocation (7 companies):**
- **Clean energy / climate tech**: 5 companies (71%) — Form Energy, WeaveGrid, Aurora Solar, dcbel, Uplight
- **Autonomous systems**: 1 company (14%) — Reliable Robotics
- **AI / generative**: 1 company (14%) — Verse (clean energy AI)

**Stage distribution (7 companies):**
- **Series A**: 1 (WeaveGrid, $15M, 2021) [^16]
- **Series B**: 1 (dcbel, ~$40M led by Coatue) [^17]
- **Series C**: 2 (Form Energy, $76M led by Coatue, 2020; Reliable Robotics, $100M led by Coatue, 2021) [^14][^15]
- **Growth / follow-on**: 3 (Aurora Solar Series C/D led by Coatue, 2021-2022; Uplight, 2021; Form Energy Series D, 2021) [^18][^19][^20]

**Notable patterns:**
- **Strong clean energy / climate tech tilt** — 5 of 7 board seats are in clean energy companies, a sector that constitutes a small minority of Coatue's overall portfolio. This suggests Rangwalla has carved out a personal area of focus within the firm.
- **Deep-tech with hardware components** — Form Energy (iron-air batteries), dcbel (bidirectional EV chargers), Reliable Robotics (autonomous aircraft), and Aurora Solar (solar design software) all involve technology applied to physical infrastructure, not pure software.
- **Board-level engagement** — Unlike many hedge fund investors who take passive positions, Rangwalla actively sits on boards, suggesting a more hands-on approach to his private portfolio companies.
- **Coatue-led rounds** — Most of the companies where Rangwalla sits on the board had their key rounds led by Coatue, indicating he was likely the deal lead within the firm.

*Note: This analysis covers only the 7 companies where Rangwalla has verified board involvement. His broader influence on Coatue's 367-company private portfolio and $40B public portfolio is not captured here. Sample size is too small for reliable percentage-based conclusions about sector preferences.*
