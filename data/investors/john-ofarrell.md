## Background

John O'Farrell is an Irish venture capitalist who joined Andreessen Horowitz in June 2010 as the firm's third general partner and first non-founding partner [^1][^2]. In July 2013, he transitioned from investing to an advisory role, becoming an Advisory Partner [^3].

Before joining a16z, O'Farrell held executive vice president positions at three Silicon Valley technology companies [^4]. From 2001 to 2007, he was EVP of Business Development at Opsware (formerly Loudcloud), where he worked alongside CEO Ben Horowitz [^1]. Together they negotiated Loudcloud's 2002 exit from the services business and its emergence as server automation software company Opsware, including a $52 million initial contract from EDS [^1]. O'Farrell led the expansion of Opsware's product line through four acquisitions and orchestrated the sale of Opsware to Hewlett-Packard for $1.65 billion in June 2007 [^1].

From 1997 to 2001, O'Farrell was SVP of International at @Home Network (later Excite@Home), where he led the launch of broadband Internet services through joint ventures in the Benelux countries, Australia, and Japan [^1]. He joined Silver Spring Networks in January 2008 as EVP of Business Development, where he led the company's $90 million Series D fundraising led by Kleiner Perkins and Google [^1].

Earlier in his career, O'Farrell worked at Siemens and Digital Equipment in Germany and Luxembourg, at the European Commission, at Booz Allen Hamilton, at Eircom (formerly Telecom Eireann), and at US West [^2][^5]. He holds a Bachelor of Electronic Engineering from University College Dublin and an MBA from Stanford Graduate School of Business, where he graduated as an Arjay Miller Scholar [^1][^4]. He speaks English, French, German, and Portuguese [^4].

## Stated Thesis

O'Farrell has publicly described his investment focus as centered on technology founders building companies that can scale globally. In an interview with Silicon Republic, he stated: "Rather than being focused on mobile or social or local, we are much more focused on finding great companies" [^5]. He has emphasized the a16z model of supporting technology founders: "The entrepreneurs we invest in are technologists. We like technology founders, we bet that we can help them become great CEOs" [^2].

O'Farrell has described a16z's approach as delivering value beyond capital: "Capital is undifferentiated" and "The model we put together over the years is designed to deliver much more value than just capital" [^2]. He articulated the firm's founding philosophy: "When Ben and Marc set out to found the firm, they realised that venture capital is really a cartel. So we set out to build a firm that would be extremely attractive to entrepreneurs" [^2].

On investment decision-making, O'Farrell has stated: "The best investments are often controversial, and vigorous debate leads to better investment decisions" [^6].
