## Background

Brett Berson is a Partner at First Round Capital, where he has spent his entire career since joining as a college intern in 2008 [^1][^2]. He progressed from intern to associate (2010-2012), to Vice President of Platform, and was named Partner in September 2016 [^2][^3]. Berson graduated from New York University with degrees earned with highest distinction in economics and film [^1]. He was named to Forbes' 30 Under 30 list in venture capital in 2015 [^4].

Berson has carved out a distinctive dual role at First Round, working across both investing and firm operations [^1]. He leads First Round's Platform team, which focuses on transforming the firm's portfolio companies into a connected community of entrepreneurs [^1][^4]. He was the architect behind several of First Round's defining initiatives, including The Review (the firm's widely-read publication featuring tactical advice from founders), Angel Track (a program helping operators become angel investors), and the In Depth podcast, which he hosts [^1][^5].

## Stated Thesis

Berson publicly describes his interest in founders who "imagine if" — visionaries pursuing the most meaningful progress [^1]. He has stated that he believes the parallels between filmmaking and startup building are instructive, noting that successful companies are built on countless compounding micro-decisions [^1].

Berson has publicly emphasized his interest in systems of record, deep infrastructure, and businesses with strong network effects [^4]. First Round as a firm invests exclusively at the earliest stages, typically in a company's first formal round [^6].

Berson has articulated a framework distinguishing between "fast bake" companies (where momentum is visible early, like Uber and Square) and "slow bake" companies (where the journey to product-market fit takes longer, like Roblox and Clay) [^7]. This suggests patience with companies that take years to find their footing.

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 3 verified personal investments (Clay, Stedi, Persona) listed on the First Round website [^1], the sample size is too small for reliable percentage breakdowns. Qualitative observations:

**Sector focus:** All three verified investments are enterprise/B2B infrastructure companies — Clay (go-to-market tools), Stedi (healthcare claims processing APIs), and Persona (identity verification platform). This is consistent with his stated interest in systems of record and deep infrastructure [^1][^4].

**Stage pattern:** All three investments were at the seed stage, consistent with First Round's focus on the earliest institutional rounds [^6]. Clay's seed was $2.5M led by First Round in 2017 [^8]. Stedi's seed was $2.7M in 2017 [^9]. Persona's first round from First Round came at the Series A stage ($17.5M, January 2020) [^10].

**Founder profile:** His portfolio founders include technical builders with deep domain expertise — Kareem Amin (Clay) previously built products at multiple companies, Zack Kanter (Stedi) brought supply chain expertise, and Rick Song (Persona) spent 5 years as an engineer at Square working on identity and fraud [^11][^12].

**Patience with slow growth:** Clay spent roughly six years building before experiencing significant customer and revenue growth, reportedly near $0 in revenue as recently as spring 2022 [^8]. This aligns with his "slow bake" framework [^7].

**Follow-on conviction:** First Round has participated in follow-on rounds for all three companies — Clay through Series B [^13], Stedi through Series B [^14], and Persona through Series D [^10][^15].

Note: Only 3 investments could be independently verified as personally attributed to Berson. As a partner at First Round, he is likely involved in many more deals at the firm level, but individual attribution is limited in public sources.

## In Their Own Words

"It's a bit strange to only have one job for your entire career, but I've spent over 15 years at First Round because I believe the most meaningful progress throughout history comes from people who 'imagine if.'" — Brett Berson, First Round website [^1]

"Studying film taught me to pay attention to the thousands of micro-decisions — lighting choices, camera angles, editing cuts — that most never notice." — Brett Berson, First Round website [^1]

"Some companies are what we at @firstround like to call a 'fast bake,' where the ingredients coalesce from the start. (Uber and Square are examples — early on we saw strong momentum.) But others are more of a 'slow bake,' or an under-the-radar journey to land on the right product." — Brett Berson, X/Twitter post, June 2024 [^7]

"I'm a strong believer that working with outstanding people is one of the best things you can do." — Brett Berson, Mike Robbins podcast [^16]

"It's all about getting to a term sheet. Without one, you don't have leverage." — Brett Berson, First Round Review [^17]

"If you're in education, think about the start of the school year. September is probably a great time to raise because your performance in August is ticking up, and by September and October you're on a roll." — Brett Berson, First Round Review [^17]

"Keep in mind that you're having these fundraising conversations over a period of about 4 to 12 weeks. As you're fundraising, more data's coming in. The ideal is that data — whether it be user growth or new enterprise customers — is looking good and you're on the upswing." — Brett Berson, First Round Review [^17]
