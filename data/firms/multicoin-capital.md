## About

Multicoin Capital is a thesis-driven investment firm founded in May 2017 by Kyle Samani and Tushar Jain in Austin, Texas [^1]. The firm manages both a hedge fund and venture funds, investing in cryptocurrencies, tokens, and blockchain companies across public and private markets [^1]. The firm invests on behalf of institutional investors, family offices, foundations, and endowments [^2].

Multicoin has raised three venture funds: Venture Fund I (2018 vintage) [^3], Venture Fund II ($100M, closed November 2020, announced May 2021) [^4] [^5], and Venture Fund III ($430M, announced July 2022) [^6]. The firm's assets under management reached nearly $600M in 2024, representing 56% annual growth from 2023 [^7]. Venture Fund III invests $1M–$50M in early-stage companies [^2].

The hedge fund posted extraordinary returns from inception through 2021, returning 20,287% [^8]. However, the firm suffered a 91.4% loss in its hedge fund in 2022, driven by the FTX collapse and broad crypto market downturn [^9]. Multicoin had approximately 10% of hedge fund assets stuck on FTX and significant exposure to SOL, FTT, and SRM tokens [^9]. Despite this, the fund rebounded with a 100.9% gain in January 2023 alone [^9], and by 2024 AUM had recovered to nearly $600M [^7].

In February 2026, co-founder Kyle Samani stepped down from his managing director role to pursue interests in AI, longevity, and robotics [^10]. Day-to-day operations continue under Tushar Jain and Brian Smith [^10]. In March 2026, Spencer Applebaum and Shayon Sengupta were promoted to General Partner and Co-Heads of Venture [^11].

Both co-founders previously founded health tech startups before entering crypto. Samani co-founded Pristine, a Google Glass healthcare software company [^12]. Jain founded ePatientFinder, a health IT startup later acquired by Elligo Health Research [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Multicoin Capital publicly describes itself as a "thesis-driven investment firm that invests in cryptocurrencies, tokens, and blockchain companies reshaping trillion-dollar markets" [^2].

The firm organizes its investment focus around several published mega-theses [^1] [^4]:

- **Open Finance / DeFi**: Backing DeFi primitives, mainstreaming DeFi adoption, and DeFi applications particularly on Solana [^4].
- **Web3 Infrastructure**: Infrastructure for applications where users control the system rather than centralized platforms [^13].
- **DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks)**: Multicoin co-authored the widely cited "Proof of Physical Work" thesis, arguing that crypto-incentivized networks can build real-world infrastructure "10–100x faster" than traditional approaches [^14]. The firm claims to have been "the first major investor into this thesis" [^14].
- **Internet Capital Markets**: The firm argues Solana can power global financial infrastructure by outperforming traditional finance on latency while reducing fees by 90–99% [^15].
- **Stablecoins and Payments**: Payments infrastructure and real-world asset tokenization [^1].

The firm emphasizes concentrated, high-conviction positions. Kyle Samani stated that the firm sometimes puts "30–50%+ of a fund into a single asset like Solana" [^13]. Tushar Jain has described their approach to risk: "If your venture fund doesn't have any zeros in it, then you're not taking enough risk" [^16].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 152 verified investments tracked by CryptoRank [^17] and cross-referenced with DropsTab [^18]:

**Sector distribution** (from CryptoRank's breakdown of 152 investments) [^17]:
- Blockchain services/infrastructure: 39 investments (25.7%)
- DeFi: 35 investments (22.9%)
- Chain/Layer 1 infrastructure: 19 investments (12.5%)
- Social/consumer: 18 investments (11.8%)
- NFTs: 7 investments (4.9%)
- CeFi: 6 investments (4.2%)
- GameFi: 5 investments (3.5%)
- Stablecoins & currency: 3 investments (2.1%)
- Other categories: remaining ~13%

**Stage distribution** (from Tracxn data on 111 investments) [^19]:
- Seed: 56 investments (50.5%), average round size $6.46M
- Series A: 41 investments (36.9%), average round size $19.3M
- Series C+: 5 investments (4.5%), average round size $75.6M
- Remaining: strategic rounds and token sales

**Solana ecosystem concentration**: The most notable pattern is Multicoin's deep concentration in the Solana ecosystem. The firm led Solana's $20M Series A in 2019 [^20] after participating in the 2018 seed round [^21], and has since built a portfolio heavily tilted toward Solana-based projects including Drift, Jito, Hivemapper, Audius, Metaplex, and many others [^11] [^18].

**DePIN as a defining category**: Multicoin has emerged as the largest dedicated DePIN investor, backing Helium (decentralized wireless) [^22], Hivemapper (decentralized mapping) [^23], Render Network (decentralized GPU rendering) [^24], io.net (decentralized compute), Geodnet, and Gradient Network [^18].

**Geographic focus**: Primarily US-based companies, with secondary investments in Singapore and the United Kingdom [^19].

**Notable gaps vs. stated thesis**: Despite listing AI as a focus area on their website [^1], relatively few pure AI investments appear in the portfolio—most AI-adjacent investments are crypto-AI hybrids like Inference and CrunchDAO [^18]. The firm's actual portfolio is far more DeFi- and infrastructure-heavy than its stated thesis suggests, with social/consumer apps representing only ~12% of investments.

**Co-investor patterns**: Frequent co-investors include a16z Crypto, Solana Foundation, Alameda Research (pre-collapse), Coinbase Ventures, Pantera Capital, and Jump Crypto [^18] [^20] [^24].

## What Founders Say

Anatoly Yakovenko, co-founder and CEO of Solana Labs, acknowledged Multicoin as one of the few investors who remained committed through multiple fundraising rounds during the crypto winter: "Except for the few amazing folks, like Foundation, like Slow Ventures, like Multicoin, that actually backed us and stuck with us through every raise." — Acquired podcast, 2024 [^28]

No additional independently sourced founder testimonials found beyond the Solana founder's statement. The firm hosts an annual Multicoin Summit featuring portfolio founders [^29], but independently verifiable founder quotes about their experience with Multicoin as an investor were not located.
