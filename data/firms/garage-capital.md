## About

Garage Capital is a pre-seed and seed-stage venture capital firm based in Kitchener-Waterloo, Ontario, Canada [^1]. The firm was founded in 2012 by Michael Litt, Devon Galloway (co-founders of Vidyard, YC S11), and Mike McCauley (co-founder of BufferBox, YC S12, acquired by Google) [^2]. All three founders are University of Waterloo Engineering alumni who have built, scaled, and sold venture-backed companies [^1].

The firm is currently on its fifth fund (Garage Capital V) [^3] [^4]. BDC Capital is invested in Fund II, Fund III, and Garage Opportunities Fund I [^5]. In 2019, Garage Capital received $50 million from the federal Venture Capital Catalyst Initiative (VCCI), which required matching private capital on a 1:1 basis [^2]. Garage Capital is also part of Inovia Capital's Emerging Managers portfolio [^6].

As of early 2026, Garage Capital has invested in over 200 companies with a collective portfolio valuation exceeding $30 billion [^1]. The portfolio includes 12 companies valued at over $1 billion, including Groq, Astranis, Aalo Atomics, Substack, and Kepler Communications [^3]. The firm has seen 26 portfolio acquisitions, including DarwinAI (acquired by Apple in 2024), North Inc. (acquired by Google in 2020), SSIMWAVE (acquired by IMAX in 2022), Secoda (acquired by Atlassian in 2025), Clearpath Robotics (acquired by Rockwell Automation in 2023), and Kindred (acquired by Ocado in 2020) [^1] [^7] [^8] [^9] [^10] [^11].

## Stated Thesis

Garage Capital publicly describes itself as "built by founders, for founders," investing in founders at the earliest stages to help them build "category-defining businesses" through "experienced-based advice, operational empathy, and early stage capital" [^6].

The firm was created because its founders wanted to bring Silicon Valley-style seed funding to Canada's Waterloo Region. McCauley has stated: "One of the reasons we started Garage was because we didn't want every company to have to do that. We wanted to create the early-stage funding models that exist in Silicon Valley. A lot of those early-stage-focused VC funds are started by previous [company] founders, and there wasn't any of that in Waterloo [Region]" [^12].

McCauley has also said: "That funding aspect is an important ingredient for any early-stage company. You need that to have a complete, healthy ecosystem for startups" [^2].

The firm describes its two investment categories as "business software and moonshots" [^13]. It also describes itself as "an angel seed stage fund focused on supercluster companies that are looking to expand their networks into Silicon Valley" [^3].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 30 verified portfolio investments (a partial sample of 200+ total investments), the following patterns emerge. This sample is too small for reliable percentages; qualitative patterns are described instead.

**Stage focus:** Overwhelmingly pre-seed and seed, consistent with stated thesis. The firm occasionally participates in follow-on rounds at Series A and beyond for existing portfolio companies (e.g., ApplyBoard Series C, Groq through six rounds, Float Financial Series B) [^13] [^14] [^15].

**Sector distribution:** The portfolio spans a wide range, with significant clusters in enterprise software/SaaS (Fellow, OpenPhone, Relay, Secoda, Vendr, Humi), robotics and hardware (Gecko Robotics, Clearpath Robotics, Upside Robotics, Kindred, TRIC Robotics), deep tech and aerospace (Groq, Astranis, Kepler Communications, Aalo Atomics, DarwinAI), and consumer/marketplace platforms (Substack, ApplyBoard, BetterUp, Nurx). Per Tracxn data, the firm has invested in 118 tech companies, 93 enterprise (B2B) companies, and 79 software companies [^13].

**Geographic concentration:** Heavily weighted toward Canadian startups, particularly the Toronto-Waterloo corridor. Litt has stated: "We invest in Canada with the vast majority of companies in the Toronto-Waterloo corridor" [^12]. However, the portfolio also includes significant U.S.-based companies (Substack in San Francisco, Gecko Robotics in Pittsburgh, BetterUp in San Francisco, Groq in Mountain View), suggesting a strong secondary focus on YC-backed companies regardless of geography.

**Y Combinator connection:** The firm has deep YC ties — all three founders went through YC (S11 and S12), and YC-affiliated companies appear frequently in the portfolio (Substack, OpenPhone, Embark Trucks, Secoda). This represents a differentiated deal flow channel.

**Founder profile patterns:** The firm invests in technical founders, often from the University of Waterloo ecosystem. The "moonshots" category suggests appetite for capital-intensive deep tech (nuclear energy, satellites, AI chips) alongside capital-light SaaS.

**Notable gap:** Despite claiming focus on "business software and moonshots," the portfolio includes meaningful consumer and health investments (BetterUp, Nurx, Eli Health), which are not emphasized in public messaging.

**Co-investor patterns:** Frequently co-invests with Y Combinator, Inovia Capital, Craft Ventures, and other Canadian-focused funds like BDC Capital.
