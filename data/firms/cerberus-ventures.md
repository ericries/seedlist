## About

Cerberus Ventures is the venture capital arm of Cerberus Capital Management, launched in 2023 under the leadership of Chris Darby, former 17-year President and CEO of In-Q-Tel (the CIA's strategic investment firm) [^1][^2]. The fund raised approximately $75 million at its first close for an inaugural venture fund targeting hardware, defense, AI, quantum computing, and national security technologies [^3].

The team brings backgrounds in company-building, technology, investing, and national security [^4]. Several senior hires — Morgan Mahlock, Chenny Zhang — came directly from In-Q-Tel, bringing the intelligence community's technology evaluation framework to commercial venture investing [^5]. Amir Salek, a Senior Managing Director, was formerly Founder and Head of Silicon at Google and a Senior Director at Nvidia [^5]. Michael Rolnick, also a Senior Managing Director, is a Forbes Midas List honoree with decades of venture experience [^5].

The firm invests at Seed through Series B and occasional later rounds, leveraging Cerberus Capital Management's broader platform, networks, and capital to support portfolio companies [^4].

## Stated Thesis

Cerberus Ventures states it backs "pioneering founders looking beyond success toward significance — the disruptors, contrarians, and challengers that are shaping the future" [^4]. The fund focuses on five critical domains:

1. **Intelligence** — AI, machine learning, data analytics
2. **Energy and resources** — fusion, cooling infrastructure, clean energy
3. **Essential infrastructure** — cybersecurity, SaaS governance, supply chain
4. **Computing** — quantum computing, semiconductors, chip design
5. **Bio-science** — biosecurity, synthetic biology, oral therapeutics

Darby's stated mandate is to invest in technology that increases national security, both for the US and its allies [^1]. The strategy revolves around real-world hardware assets rather than pure software plays [^1].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 12 verified portfolio companies from the firm's portfolio page [^6]:

- **Bio-science**: 5 companies (42%) — Ansa Biotechnologies, Red Queen Bio, Roebling/Synonym, Syntis Bio, Virchow Medical
- **AI / Defense**: 2 companies (17%) — ChipStack (acquired by Cadence), Ghostdog Systems (acquired by Mission Essential)
- **Quantum Computing**: 2 companies (17%) — Lightsynq (acquired by IonQ), Maybell Quantum
- **Energy / Infrastructure**: 2 companies (17%) — DG Matrix, Ferveret
- **Cybersecurity**: 1 company (8%) — Nudge Security

**Notable patterns:**
- Remarkably high acquisition rate: 8 of 12 portfolio companies (67%) have been acquired, suggesting either strategic exits or acqui-hires
- Strong national security thread: Ghostdog acquired by Mission Essential (defense contractor), Lightsynq by IonQ (quantum leader)
- Bio-science is the largest sector despite the firm's defense branding — likely influenced by Chenny Zhang's In-Q-Tel biotech background
- Seed and Series A dominant — most entries are very early stage
- ChipStack (acquired by Cadence) and Lightsynq (acquired by IonQ) represent notable strategic exits to major industry players
