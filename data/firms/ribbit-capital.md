## About

Ribbit Capital is a venture capital firm founded in 2012 by Meyer "Micky" Malka, based in Palo Alto, California [^1][^2]. Malka founded the firm exclusively to invest in financial services and fintech globally, drawing on his own entrepreneurial experience building and running brokerages and banks across Venezuela, Brazil, and the United States [^2].

Before founding Ribbit, Malka co-founded Heptagon Group at age 19, a securities and investment broker dealer; developed Patagon, Latin America's first comprehensive internet-based financial services portal (acquired by Banco Santander for $750 million); and co-founded Banco Lemon in 2002, which became the largest private microfinance institution in Brazil [^2].

Ribbit's inaugural fund was approximately $100 million [^3]. The founding investment team consisted of Malka, Nick Shalek (founding team), and Nikolay Kostov (joined 2013) [^3]. Key fund milestones include:

| Fund | Vintage | Size |
|------|---------|------|
| Fund I | 2012 | ~$100M [^3] |
| Fund II | ~2014 | ~$125M [^3] |
| Fund III | ~2016 | ~$226M [^3] |
| Fund V | 2018 | $420M [^4] |
| Fund VII | 2022 | $1.15B [^5] |
| Fund X | 2023 | $800M [^6] |
| Fund Y (raising) | 2025 | $500M (target) [^7] |

As of 2024, Ribbit reported approximately $12 billion in regulatory assets under management [^4]. The firm has invested in 164 companies as of February 2026, with 24 new investments in the trailing 12 months [^6]. Ribbit's portfolio includes 38 unicorns, 16 IPOs, and 25 acquisitions [^6].

Fund I reportedly achieved approximately 70x returns, potentially making it one of the best-performing venture funds of its vintage [^3]. The firm's early LP base included BBVA, Silicon Valley Bank, and high-net-worth individuals, later expanding to include institutional investors such as Emory University Endowment, MIT Pension Fund, and Sequoia Heritage [^3].

The firm's mantra is: "The future belongs to the rebels" [^8].

## Stated Thesis

*(Self-reported: These represent what Ribbit says publicly about its approach. See Inferred Thesis for analysis of actual investment behavior.)*

Ribbit Capital publicly describes its focus as partnering with entrepreneurs who "challenge the status quo" in financial services [^8]. The firm states that "the companies that reshape industries don't just solve problems. They change how people think and feel about entire categories" [^8].

Ribbit positions itself across multiple sectors within finance: AI, global commerce, infrastructure, wealth management, money movement, identity, data, and energy [^8].

Malka has stated that Ribbit is "not a venture fund" but rather "a company, it's a startup. And we happen to be in the business of deploying capital" [^9]. He describes the firm's role as going beyond writing checks: "Our goal isn't just to write checks. It's to deposit ideas" [^9].

On the evolution of fintech, Malka has declared: "Fintech, as we've known it for the past decade, is dead. The last decade was about giving people access to money. The next decade is about contextual money" [^9][^10].

The firm emphasizes brand-building as central to its value proposition, stating that successful founders combine "vision, relentless execution, and the ability to build beloved brands" [^8].

## Inferred Thesis

*Based on 12 verified portfolio companies with known details. Sample size covers only ~7% of 164 total investments, so percentages should be treated as directional.*

**Stage distribution:** Ribbit invests across stages from seed to growth, with a stated primary focus on Series B rounds in US-based startups [^6]. The early funds (I-III) show seed and Series A entries (Coinbase Series A in 2013, Robinhood Series A in 2014, Revolut Series A in 2016) [^3], while later investments include growth-stage deals (Nubank Series D in 2016) [^3]. This suggests the firm has moved upmarket as fund sizes have grown from $100M to $1B+.

**Sector breakdown (based on 12 verified companies):** Digital banking/neobanks (Nubank, Revolut, Monzo — 3 of 12, 25%), trading/investing platforms (Robinhood, Coinbase — 2 of 12, 17%), corporate finance (Brex — 1 of 12, 8%), lending/BNPL (Affirm — 1 of 12, 8%), insurtech (Alan — 1 of 12, 8%), payments (Chipper Cash — 1 of 12, 8%), fintech infrastructure (Groww — 1 of 12, 8%), auto marketplace (Kavak — 1 of 12, 8%), fintech/banking (One/Walmart — 1 of 12, 8%) [^2][^3][^6][^11].

**Geographic patterns:** The portfolio is notably global: Brazil (Nubank), UK (Revolut, Monzo), France (Alan), India (Groww), Mexico (Kavak), Africa (Chipper Cash), alongside US companies (Robinhood, Coinbase, Brex, Affirm) [^6][^11]. Ribbit's stated mandate explicitly targets opportunities "not always traveling across borders" in any geography [^3].

**Crypto/digital assets:** Ribbit's Fund I mandate permitted direct cryptocurrency ownership — unusual for venture funds in 2012. The firm purchased Bitcoin directly in 2013, which generated substantial returns [^3]. Investments in Coinbase and Robinhood (which later added crypto trading) demonstrate an early and persistent conviction in crypto/digital assets.

**Check size:** $2M-$15M range [^11], though this has likely expanded with larger fund sizes.

**Co-investor patterns:** Ribbit frequently co-invests with Sequoia Capital (Nubank, Robinhood), DST Global (Robinhood), and other fintech-focused investors.

**Notable pattern:** Ribbit has backed 11 fintech unicorns, more than any other investor [^6]. The firm's operational DNA — Malka's personal experience running banks and brokerages — distinguishes it from generalist VCs entering fintech. In January 2021, Walmart announced a partnership with Ribbit to create fintech startup One, which reached a $2.5 billion valuation by December 2024 [^2].

## In Their Own Words

Malka on purpose: "Our goal isn't just to write checks. It's to deposit ideas" [^9].

Malka on conviction: "Be more concentrated and have more conviction" and "Having conviction is one of the most important traits" [^9].
