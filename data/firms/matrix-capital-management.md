## About

Matrix Capital Management Company, LP is an American hedge fund founded in 1999 by David E. Goel and Paul J. Ferri, headquartered in Waltham, Massachusetts [^1][^2]. The firm is structured as a Delaware limited partnership [^1]. Goel, the principal owner, has ultimate authority over all investment decisions and business affairs, while Ferri served as co-founder [^2][^3].

Goel was born circa 1971 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada [^2]. He graduated magna cum laude from Harvard University with a degree in government in 1993, having previously attended Phillips Exeter Academy [^2]. His career prior to Matrix included financial analyst at Morgan Stanley's technology investment banking division (1993-1995), associate at General Atlantic Partners (1995-1996), and technology research analyst at Tiger Management under Julian Robertson (1996-1999) [^2][^3]. Goel is classified as a "Tiger Cub" — a former Tiger Management employee who launched a successful hedge fund [^1][^3].

Matrix Capital Management grew to approximately $11 billion in assets under management by mid-2024 [^3][^4]. Its Q1 2024 13F filing reported $10.77 billion in managed securities across 23 positions [^5]. The firm delivered mostly double-digit annual returns since inception, including a 56% return in the first 10 months of 2013 and a three-year annualized return of approximately 38% through mid-2024 [^2]. In 2020, the fund's equal-weighted top-20 portfolio returned approximately 92% over 12 months [^6].

In August 2024, Goel announced the closure of Matrix Capital Management and the return of approximately $11 billion to investors, citing personal health concerns [^3][^4][^7]. The firm completed 25 years of operations. As of Q4 2025, the residual 13F portfolio contained only 2 positions worth $57.6 million, reflecting the ongoing wind-down [^5].

**Note:** Matrix Capital Management should not be confused with Matrix Partners (later rebranded as Matrix), the venture capital firm. Paul Ferri founded Matrix Partners separately in 1977; it is a distinct entity focused on early-stage venture investments [^1][^8].

The firm also operates AyurMaya Capital Management Fund, a $655 million private equity fund focused on life sciences companies [^9][^10]. Alan Colowick serves as Senior Managing Director on the Technology-Life Sciences team across both Matrix Capital and AyurMaya [^11].

## Stated Thesis

Matrix Capital Management publicly describes its investment focus as "a concentrated portfolio of high-conviction investments in U.S. companies focused on semiconductors, software, generative artificial intelligence, machine learning, neural networks, and the technology convergence in life sciences" [^1].

Goel has stated that when evaluating portfolio performance, "I think in 10-year terms," reflecting a commitment to long-term value creation rather than short-term trading [^2].

On cloud computing investments, Goel stated his thesis was predicated on "the disruptive power of cloud computing across the technology universe" and that each portfolio company would be "a harbinger for where all software companies will have to go if they want to remain relevant" [^2].

The firm emphasizes bottom-up fundamental analysis of individual companies, maintaining a concentrated portfolio of 10-20 core holdings [^2]. Goel advocates for patience during market cycles, believing true value emerges from staying committed to fundamentally sound investments during periods of uncertainty [^2].

**Note:** These stated principles apply to public equity hedge fund management — not to startup or venture capital investing.

## Inferred Thesis

**Matrix Capital Management is not a venture capital or startup investor.** Its investment activity falls into two categories:

### 1. Public Equity Hedge Fund (Primary Activity — ~95% of AUM)

The Matrix Capital Management Fund is a long/short equity hedge fund investing in publicly traded securities [^1][^5]. Based on 13F filings:

- **Peak portfolio:** 23 positions worth $10.77 billion (Q1 2024) [^5]
- **Top holdings concentration:** 92.39% in top 10 positions (Q1 2024) [^5]
- **Largest historical positions:** NVIDIA Corp. (2,658,512 shares as largest holding), Qualcomm Inc., TransDigm Group Inc. (Q2 2024) [^5][^12]
- **Notable long-term holds:** Netflix (held from Q3 2012 through 2024, acquired at ~$8 split-adjusted) [^6]
- **Sector concentration:** Semiconductors, software/cloud, and AI-related public equities [^1][^5]
- **Strategy:** Long/short equity, concentrated high-conviction positions [^1][^2]

This is classic Tiger Cub hedge fund investing — concentrated bets on large-cap public technology companies, not startup investing.

### 2. Private Equity / Life Sciences (Secondary Activity — ~$655M via AyurMaya)

The AyurMaya Capital Management Fund invests in private life-sciences companies [^9][^10]. Known investments include:

- Maze Therapeutics (led $190M Series C with Foresite Capital) [^8]
- Alumis (precision medicine, autoimmune disorders) [^8][^11]
- Rivos (RISC-V chip startup) [^8]
- Aura Biosciences (tumor-targeted therapeutics) [^8]
- ReCode Therapeutics (genetic medicines, Series B) [^13]
- Solve Therapeutics [^11]
- Adaptive Biotechnologies (board seat, later exited) [^14]

These are primarily late-stage private investments in biotech and life sciences — closer to growth equity than early-stage venture capital.

### Why This Is Not a VC Firm

- **$11B in public equities vs. $655M in private investments:** The overwhelming majority of capital is deployed in publicly traded stocks
- **13F filing history** shows a portfolio of large-cap tech stocks (NVIDIA, Netflix, Qualcomm), not startup equity [^5]
- **No seed, Series A, or early-stage investing** — private investments through AyurMaya are growth-stage biotech
- **Fund structure** is a hedge fund (long/short equity), not a venture fund
- **David Goel's background** is as a Tiger Cub hedge fund manager, not a venture capitalist [^3]
