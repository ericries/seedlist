## About

Koch Disruptive Technologies (KDT) is the venture capital and growth equity arm of Koch Industries (now Koch, Inc.), one of the largest privately held companies in the United States [^1]. Founded in 2017 by Chase Koch, son of Charles Koch, KDT launched in January 2018 with the mission of connecting emerging technology companies with Koch's industrial platform [^2][^3]. The firm is headquartered in Wichita, Kansas, with additional operations in Israel [^4].

KDT has deployed more than $4 billion across a portfolio of more than 70 direct investments [^2][^3]. Unlike traditional venture capital firms that raise third-party funds, KDT invests directly from Koch's balance sheet, giving it a flexible mandate to invest at any stage from seed to late-stage growth [^5]. The firm operates with a team of approximately 25 people, including three based in Israel [^3].

A distinguishing feature is Koch Labs, an internal team of five people with deep Koch domain expertise who facilitate collaboration between Koch's subsidiaries and portfolio companies [^3][^6]. Through Koch Labs, portfolio companies gain access to Koch's global capabilities in logistics, IT, manufacturing, regulatory affairs, and legal support [^6]. The firm also leverages its relationship with Molex, a Koch subsidiary specializing in electrical and interconnect solutions, to offer startups technical insight, go-to-market pathways, and commercialization opportunities [^1].

In April 2024, Chase Koch transitioned from his role as President of KDT to Executive Vice President of Origination and Partnerships at Koch Industries, with Byron Knight assuming the role of President of KDT [^3][^4].

## Stated Thesis

KDT publicly describes its mission as partnering with "principled entrepreneurs with transformative technologies" to "achieve their potential by leveraging mutually beneficial partnerships" [^1]. The firm states it looks for companies that can transform Koch's core capabilities, create new capabilities, or expand Koch into new platforms [^7].

Chase Koch has described the founding thesis as bringing "Koch Industries to the most disruptive and transformative founders in the world, and bring that platform as a laboratory for them to experiment in" [^7]. Koch has emphasized that KDT was created because Koch Industries "needed a better way to tap into the innovation in the startup community" [^3].

The firm states its investment approach is grounded in three core principles: capability-based thinking (rather than industry-bound), creative destruction, and mutual benefit [^7]. KDT publicly emphasizes its focus on eight sectors: supply chain and manufacturing, cybersecurity, fintech, semiconductors, connectivity, enterprise software, energy, and healthcare [^1][^3].

Byron Knight has described KDT's mission as investing "in transformative technology companies that disrupt the traditional paradigms and provide impactful solutions to make the world a better place" [^8].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 67 verified portfolio companies listed on KDT's website [^5]:

**Sector distribution:**
- Healthcare: 15 of 67 companies (22%) -- the largest single sector, spanning cell therapy (Cellares), drug discovery (Valo Health, Immunai), medical devices (Insightec), digital health (Rula, Oshi Health, NeuraHealth), and insurance (Sidecar Health)
- Supply chain and manufacturing: 13 of 67 (19%) -- heavily weighted toward autonomous logistics (Gatik, Outrider, Gideon Brothers) and supply chain software (Arkestro, Optimal Dynamics)
- Cybersecurity: 9 of 67 (13%) -- including industrial cybersecurity (Dragos), application security (Snyk), and identity (Beyond Identity)
- Semiconductors: 9 of 67 (13%) -- photonics and optical (Celestial AI, Polaris Electro-Optics, Teramount), AI chips (Rivos, Pliops), and imaging (Vayyar)
- Enterprise software: 7 of 67 (10%) -- including AI agents (Sema4.ai, Rhino.ai) and quantum computing (QC Ware)
- Connectivity: 6 of 67 (9%) -- satellite (Astranis), networking (Alkira, Nile), and wireless (Cohere)
- Fintech: 5 of 67 (7%) -- rewards (Ibotta), insurance comparison (The Zebra), consumer finance (Upgrade)
- Energy: 3 of 67 (4%) -- batteries (BlueCurrent), superconductors (MetOx), lithium extraction (Standard Lithium)

**Stage distribution:** KDT invests across stages, but the pattern strongly favors growth-stage companies. Most verified investments are Series B through Series D, with KDT frequently leading rounds of $50M or more. The firm's most recent verified investment -- Lucidean's $18M seed round co-led with Entrada Ventures in December 2025 -- shows willingness to invest at seed stage for strategically relevant semiconductor and connectivity companies [^9].

**Check sizes:** KDT invests at a wide range, from co-leading an $18M seed round (Lucidean) [^9] to leading a $165M Series D (Sidecar Health) [^10] and a $200M Series D (Dragos) [^11]. Investments of $50M-$150M appear most common based on publicly disclosed rounds.

**Geographic focus:** The portfolio is predominantly US-based, with a secondary cluster in Israel (Insightec, Vayyar, Immunai, proteanTecs, CYREBRO, One-Layer, Teramount, Percepto, Greeneye). KDT maintains a dedicated Israel office with a managing director and associate [^4].

**Notable pattern -- industrial and deep-tech bias:** Unlike most corporate venture arms that focus on software, KDT has substantial exposure to hardware, robotics, and physical-world technologies. 13 of 67 companies (19%) are in autonomous vehicles and robotics, and 9 of 67 (13%) are in semiconductors -- sectors that align directly with Koch Industries' manufacturing and industrial operations.

**Notable pattern -- Koch as customer:** Several portfolio companies (Dragos, Rhino.ai, Sidecar Health) have Koch Industries as a customer or deployment partner. Koch selected Sidecar Health coverage for a segment of its workforce [^10]. Byron Knight stated that the "Dragos Platform plays a key part in managing risk across our enterprise" [^11]. This dual investor-customer relationship is a distinctive KDT feature.

**Co-investor patterns:** KDT frequently co-invests with BlackRock (Dragos), NEA (Outrider), Innovation Endeavors, and Temasek's Xora Innovation (Celestial AI). The firm participates in growth rounds alongside institutional crossover investors rather than traditional early-stage VCs.

**Exits:** KDT reports 11 unicorns, 3 IPOs, and 9 acquisitions across its portfolio [^2]. The most notable exit is Ibotta, which went public on NASDAQ at a market cap of approximately $2.4 billion [^2].
