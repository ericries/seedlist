## About

Summit Partners is a growth equity investment firm founded in 1984 by Roe Stamps and Stephen Woodsum, who previously worked together at TA Associates, with Greg Avis joining as a co-founder shortly after [^1][^2]. The firm is considered one of the earliest private equity firms to focus on growth investing [^1]. Summit Partners is headquartered in Boston, with additional offices in Menlo Park, New York, London, and Luxembourg [^3].

Since its founding, Summit has invested in more than 550 companies across technology, healthcare, life sciences, and other growth industries [^3][^4]. The firm has achieved over 175 public offerings and 250+ strategic exits or mergers [^3][^4]. Summit now manages more than $46 billion in assets across its fund complex [^5]. The firm's most recent fund, Summit Partners Growth Equity Fund XII, closed in 2024 at $9.5 billion after approximately five months of fundraising, with the general partner committing as the single largest investor [^5]. Prior funds include Growth Equity Fund XI at $8.35 billion and Growth Equity Fund X at $4.9 billion [^6].

Peter Y. Chung has served as CEO since 2015, having joined the firm in 1994 [^7]. Previously, he worked in the Mergers & Acquisitions department at Goldman Sachs [^7]. Scott Collins serves as COO and Managing Director, having joined in 1996 and having founded the firm's London office in 2001 [^8]. The firm employs over 80 growth equity investment professionals across North America and Europe [^4].

## Stated Thesis

Summit Partners publicly states that its strategy "is grounded in our foundational belief that profitable growth is the most reliable source of superior risk-adjusted returns through market and economic cycles" [^5][^9]. The firm focuses on identifying "category-leading companies" and providing resources to drive growth [^3].

The firm typically invests between $75 million and $500 million, functioning as either minority or majority shareholders [^9]. Summit structures deals to support organic growth acceleration or acquisition-driven platform creation, tailoring approaches to each portfolio company's specific objectives [^9].

Summit concentrates expertise in three key sectors: technology, healthcare and life sciences, and growth products and services [^3][^9]. Beyond board participation and advisory roles, Summit operates the Peak Performance Group (PPG), a dedicated support team providing complimentary services in operations and revenue optimization, M&A support, human capital recruitment and strategy, technology and data science, and capital markets structuring [^9].

The firm has stated: "For over 40 years, Summit has partnered with profitable, category-leading companies and their leadership teams seeking to build best-in-class businesses by leveraging its deep industry expertise and its Peak Performance Group value enhancement platform" [^4].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on Summit's portfolio of 550+ companies and the 57 current portfolio companies identified from the firm's portfolio page [^10]:

**Sector distribution (from 57 current portfolio companies):** Technology represents the largest allocation with 32 companies (56%), healthcare and life sciences accounts for 15 companies (26%), and growth products and services covers 10 companies (18%) [^10]. Within technology, the portfolio skews heavily toward enterprise SaaS, cybersecurity, and marketing technology. Within healthcare, clinical research and healthcare services are prominent themes.

**Stage distribution:** Summit invests exclusively at the growth stage. The firm's $75M-$500M check size range [^9] positions it firmly in growth equity, targeting companies with proven revenue and profitability. This is not a venture capital firm in the traditional sense — it targets established businesses seeking capital to accelerate growth.

**Geographic concentration:** The portfolio spans North America and Europe, with European investments managed primarily from the London and Luxembourg offices. European technology investments are led by Antony Clavel and Han Sikkens [^11][^12].

**Investment approach patterns:** Summit frequently pursues "platform" strategies, making an initial growth equity investment and then supporting add-on acquisitions. Since 2021, the firm has facilitated 900+ portfolio company acquisitions and 200+ capital markets transactions [^9]. This M&A-intensive approach is a defining characteristic.

**Notable patterns:** The firm targets profitable, category-leading companies — a meaningful constraint that distinguishes it from venture capital firms willing to invest in pre-profit companies. The firm's emphasis on "profitable growth" [^5] suggests it avoids high-burn, growth-at-all-costs business models. The consumer sector (Dr. Squatch, Brooklinen, Hairstory) represents a smaller but notable allocation to direct-to-consumer brands [^10].
