## About

Prima Materia is a European investment and company-building firm founded in February 2021 by Spotify co-founder Daniel Ek and early Spotify investor Shakil Khan [^1][^2]. In September 2020, Ek pledged €1 billion of his personal wealth to back European deep-tech "moonshot" startups over the following decade [^3][^4]. The firm is headquartered in Stockholm, Sweden [^5].

Prima Materia explicitly distinguishes itself from traditional venture capital. The firm's FAQ states it is "not a venture capital firm" and does not pursue growth-stage financings, preferring instead to meet founders at the beginning of their journey [^6]. The firm describes itself as "not investors but builders and active owners to a small number of companies building great businesses for the long term" [^7]. The investment team consists of three people: Daniel Ek (Founder), Shakil Khan (Co-Founder), and Pia Michel (Head of New Projects) [^7][^8].

Prima Materia maintains an intentionally small and concentrated portfolio, stating it is "not looking for many investment opportunities to deploy capital" and has noted it "no longer make[s] new fund investments" [^6]. Ek serves as Chairman of both Helsing and Neko Health, two of the firm's core portfolio companies [^9][^10]. The firm's tagline is "Ideas too urgent to be rushed" [^5].

## Stated Thesis

Prima Materia publicly describes its mission as partnering "with exceptional people to build companies that leverage technology to help solve meaningful problems for society" [^6]. The firm states it works with "ambitious scientists, engineers, and entrepreneurs who develop technologies that change our shared trajectory" [^6].

The firm identifies its focus areas as "clean energy, AI, healthcare and natural resources" and broader moonshot technologies that could reshape society [^6]. Prima Materia emphasizes an extremely long time horizon — Ek has publicly described it as a "100-year project," not seeking to exit companies in 5-7 years [^11]. The firm says it commits its "most valuable resource, our own time, to helping them succeed from day one" [^7].

In announcing the Helsing Series D investment, Ek stated: "As Europe rapidly strengthens its defence capabilities in response to evolving geopolitical challenges, there is an urgent need for investments in advanced technologies that ensure its strategic autonomy and security readiness" [^10].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 5 verified direct investments (Helsing, Neko Health, Epiterna, Homa Games, Northvolt) plus 1 fund investment (20VC), Prima Materia's actual behavior reveals a distinctive pattern:

**Stage distribution:** Prima Materia predominantly invests at the earliest stages, often as the founding or sole investor. Of 5 direct company investments: 2 involved founding/incorporation-stage capital (Neko Health, Epiterna), 2 were Series A (Helsing, Homa Games), and 1 was Series B (Northvolt). The firm is essentially a company builder, not a traditional fund deployer.

**Sector breakdown (5 direct investments):** Defense/AI: 1 (20%), Healthtech: 1 (20%), Longevity/biotech: 1 (20%), Climate/energy: 1 (20%), Gaming: 1 (20%). The portfolio is extremely diversified by sector but unified by a "deep tech solving big problems" thesis.

**Geographic focus:** Strongly European. Helsing is Munich-based, Neko Health is Stockholm-based, Epiterna is Swiss, Homa Games is Paris-based, and Northvolt is Swedish [^3][^12][^13][^14][^15].

**Check size:** Extremely large for early stage. The Helsing Series A was €100M (out of €102.5M total round) [^3]. Epiterna received €10M as sole investor [^12]. Ek invested approximately €30M in Neko Health before external investors joined [^16]. This is consistent with deploying personal billionaire-scale capital rather than fund economics.

**Co-investor patterns:** Prima Materia frequently invests alone or as dominant lead, then co-invests with top-tier European and global VCs in follow-on rounds. Subsequent investors across portfolio companies include General Catalyst (Helsing, Neko Health), Lightspeed (Helsing, Neko Health), Accel (Helsing), Lakestar (Neko Health), and Atomico (Neko Health) [^10][^16].

**Founder profile patterns:** Prima Materia backs founders with deep technical or scientific expertise. Torsten Reil (Helsing) is a gaming/AI developer, Alejandro Ocampo (Epiterna) is a 15-year aging researcher, and Hjalmar Nilsonne (Neko Health) is an engineer [^2][^12][^17].

**Notable gaps from stated thesis:** Despite claiming focus on "clean energy" and "natural resources," only 1 of 5 direct investments (Northvolt) falls in climate tech. Defense tech (Helsing) has received by far the most capital — over €700M across rounds — but is not prominently featured in the firm's stated focus areas. The firm also invested in gaming (Homa Games), which is not mentioned in its stated thesis at all.
