## About

Chemistry is an early-stage venture capital firm founded in October 2024 by three veteran VCs: Ethan Kurzweil (formerly 16 years at Bessemer Venture Partners), Kristina Shen (formerly General Partner at Andreessen Horowitz and 7 years at Bessemer), and Mark Goldberg (formerly Partner at Index Ventures for nearly a decade) [^1][^2][^3].

The firm launched with a significantly oversubscribed $350 million debut fund focused on leading seed and Series A rounds [^1][^2]. Chemistry is headquartered in San Francisco [^4].

Prior to founding Chemistry, the three partners collectively led investments in over a dozen unicorns at the early stage, served on 50+ boards, and led nearly 100 investments [^1]. Notable investments from their prior roles include PagerDuty, Intercom, Twitch, Plaid, Persona, LaunchDarkly, and ServiceTitan [^3][^5][^6].

The founding team brings complementary backgrounds: Kurzweil's expertise spans developer platforms, data infrastructure, and gaming from his Bessemer tenure [^3]; Shen led B2B software investing at a16z [^5]; and Goldberg focused on early-stage software and fintech at Index, having previously been among Dropbox's first business hires [^6].

## Stated Thesis

Chemistry publicly states that the firm was built around the question: "What would a venture firm look like if its success was fully aligned with its founders' success?" [^1].

The firm describes its approach as "stripping away the excess" of large venture platforms and focusing on "going all in with our founders from the earliest stages" [^1]. Chemistry targets two to three investments per year per partner, emphasizing depth of involvement over breadth [^1].

Mark Goldberg has described Chemistry's philosophy as "conviction-based investing rather than consensus-driven decisions," optimizing for "outliers, not averages" [^7]. He has stated that the firm focuses on "non-obvious bets that appear contrarian initially" [^7].

Ethan Kurzweil has said that Chemistry writes $3-30M checks with an average of $10-12M for Series A investments [^8].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on a limited sample of verified Chemistry investments (the firm launched in late 2024), analysis is preliminary:

**Known Chemistry portfolio companies:** Decagon, Assort Health, Serval, Datacurve [^4][^9][^10]. With only 4 verified investments, the sample is too small for reliable sector percentages.

**Qualitative patterns from verified investments:**
- Enterprise software and AI applications appear dominant (Decagon in AI customer support, Assort Health in healthcare AI, Datacurve in AI training data) [^4][^9][^10]
- Serval reached unicorn status ($1B valuation) within months of its Series A, suggesting Chemistry targets high-growth trajectories [^4]
- The firm appears to lead rounds rather than participate, consistent with its stated approach [^1]

**Patterns from partners' prior portfolios (pre-Chemistry):** The partners' combined prior investments at Bessemer, a16z, and Index skew heavily toward fintech (Plaid, Bridge, Anrok, Lithic), developer tools (PagerDuty, LaunchDarkly, Intercom), and enterprise infrastructure (Persona, Pilot, ServiceTitan) [^3][^5][^6]. This track record suggests Chemistry will continue targeting these sectors.

**Check size:** $3-30M, averaging $10-12M at Series A [^8].
