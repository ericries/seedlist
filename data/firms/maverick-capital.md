## About

Maverick Capital is a long/short public equity hedge fund founded by Lee Ainslie in 1993 in Dallas, Texas [^1]. Ainslie is one of the original "Tiger Cubs," having served as a managing director and technology analyst at Julian Robertson's Tiger Management before launching Maverick with $38 million in seed capital from the family of Texas entrepreneur Sam Wyly [^1][^2].

The firm manages over $13.5 billion in gross assets and employs a bottom-up, fundamental analysis approach to public equity investing [^3]. Maverick holds both long and short positions, focusing exclusively on equities and avoiding bonds, commodities, currencies, and options [^1]. The 49-member investment team averages about five positions per investment professional, a ratio lower than the hedge fund industry average [^4].

From 1995 to 2014, the fund returned a compounded annual return of 13% [^1].

**Relationship to Maverick Ventures:** Maverick Capital began making private (venture) investments using hedge fund capital as early as 2004 under the leadership of David Singer [^3]. In 2014, the firm decided to separate its private investment activities and launched Maverick Ventures as a standalone venture capital entity on January 1, 2015, with its own LP base and fund structure [^3][^5]. Maverick Ventures operates independently with an evergreen fund structure, though the two entities maintain a "no wall" approach, allowing portfolio companies to access Maverick Capital's analysts and public company networks [^6]. Approximately 30% of Maverick Ventures' capital comes from Maverick Capital partners, including Ainslie [^3].

**For startup founders: All venture/startup investing is conducted through Maverick Ventures (see separate profile).**

## Stated Thesis

Maverick Capital's stated thesis pertains to public equity investing, not venture capital. The firm publicly describes its approach as fundamental, bottom-up stock selection with a focus on companies making "good capital-allocation decisions," examining how incremental returns on invested capital compare to cost of capital [^4].

Lee Ainslie has stated: "The odds of being a successful investor will be the degree to which they take advantage of data capabilities" [^7].

Maverick Capital does not publicly state a venture or startup investing thesis. That function is handled by Maverick Ventures.
