## About

Paradigm is a frontier technology investment firm co-founded in June 2018 by Matt Huang, a former partner at Sequoia Capital, and Fred Ehrsam, co-founder of Coinbase [^1]. The firm is headquartered in San Francisco, California, and manages approximately $12.7 billion in assets [^2].

Paradigm launched with a $750 million open-ended fund in October 2018, backed by institutional investors including Harvard, Yale, and Stanford endowments [^3]. In November 2021, the firm raised $2.5 billion for Paradigm One, its first traditional venture fund and at the time the largest cryptocurrency-related venture fund in history [^4]. In June 2024, Paradigm closed its third fund at $850 million, focused on early-stage crypto projects [^5].

In October 2023, Ehrsam stepped down from his managing partner role, with Huang remaining as managing partner [^2]. In August 2025, Huang was announced as CEO of Stripe's blockchain project Tempo while continuing in his role at Paradigm [^2].

The firm invested approximately $278 million in FTX across two rounds between 2021 and 2022, which was subsequently written down to zero following FTX's collapse [^6]. Huang testified at Sam Bankman-Fried's trial and publicly expressed regret over the investment [^6].

Beyond investing, Paradigm has developed open-source infrastructure tools including Foundry (an Ethereum development framework) and Reth (an execution node), positioning the firm as both an investor and builder in the crypto ecosystem [^5].

## Stated Thesis

Paradigm publicly describes itself as "a frontier technology investment firm that builds and invests in crypto, AI, robotics, and across new frontiers from the earliest stages" [^1]. The firm's stated philosophy is research-driven and technically oriented, with a team that includes multiple research partners and a CTO [^1].

In announcing its third fund, Paradigm stated: "crypto would be one of the most important technical and economic shifts of the coming decades" [^5]. The firm emphasizes being "the first outside investor in projects that have shaped the crypto landscape," and describes a commitment to "accelerate a positive future for crypto, not just as investors but as builders" [^5].

The firm invests across a wide range from as little as $1 million to over $100 million [^3]. Paradigm offers EIR and incubation programs for founders at the earliest stages [^5].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 100+ verified portfolio companies from Paradigm's portfolio page, the following patterns emerge [^1].

**Sector breakdown (100+ verified investments):** The portfolio is overwhelmingly concentrated in crypto and blockchain infrastructure. DeFi protocols represent approximately 25 of 100+ investments (25%), including Uniswap, Compound, dYdX, Euler, Synthetix, Ribbon Finance, and Opyn. Blockchain infrastructure accounts for approximately 30 of 100+ (30%), including Optimism, Starkware, Aztec, Monad, Flashbots, Lido, and Conduit. Crypto exchanges and wallets represent approximately 15 of 100+ (15%), including Coinbase, Phantom, Magic Eden, OpenSea, and Blur. Gaming and consumer crypto represent approximately 10 of 100+ (10%), including Sky Mavis, Limit Break, and AI Arena. Non-crypto investments represent a small but growing share, including Citadel Securities (market making), Kalshi (prediction markets), Andromeda (AI compute), and Nous Research (open-source AI) [^1].

**Stage distribution:** Paradigm invests across all stages, from pre-seed incubation to growth rounds exceeding $100 million. The $225 million lead investment in Monad Labs in April 2024 demonstrates willingness to write very large checks for conviction bets [^7]. The earliest-stage investments come through the EIR and incubation programs [^5].

**Geographic concentration:** Investments span globally, with significant representation in the United States and international crypto hubs. The firm's Israel-adjacent investments and Asian crypto exchanges suggest a global mandate [^1].

**Co-investor patterns:** Paradigm frequently co-invests with other crypto-native funds including Polychain, a16z crypto, and Dragonfly, as well as traditional venture firms like Sequoia on crossover deals [^7].

**Notable gaps:** Despite recent additions of AI investments (Nous Research, Andromeda), the portfolio remains overwhelmingly crypto-focused. The stated expansion into "AI, robotics, and across new frontiers" is not yet reflected in portfolio composition, which remains 90%+ crypto [^1].

**Recent strategy shift:** Post-2023 investments are almost exclusively infrastructure plays — MEV infrastructure (Flashbots), parallel execution (Monad), AI models (Nous Research) — suggesting a move away from consumer-facing crypto applications [^2].
