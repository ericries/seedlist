## About

Array Ventures is a pre-seed and seed-stage venture capital firm founded in 2016 by Shruti Gandhi in San Francisco, California [^1][^2]. Gandhi is an engineer-turned-founder-and-investor who has also served as a Columbia University computer science professor and Commissioner at the San Francisco Employees' Retirement System [^3].

The firm's first fund was $7 million, which Gandhi returned at a fivefold multiple [^4]. Array has since raised subsequent funds, though specific sizes for Fund II and beyond have not been publicly disclosed. The firm's first close on Fund 2 occurred in March 2018 [^5]. As of 2026, Array has made over 100 investments across its history, with 15 exits to companies including Apple, Amazon, PayPal, ServiceNow, Gartner, and JAMF [^1][^2].

Array operates with a lean team supplemented by a network of 500+ industry experts who serve as angel investors, advisors, and customers for portfolio companies [^1]. The firm also runs Array AI Labs, an initiative focused on AI research and development [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Array Ventures publicly describes itself as "Beyond The First Check. Your First BD Hire And Partner For 2nd Wave Of Customers," emphasizing its role in helping founders with business development and go-to-market, not just capital [^1].

Shruti Gandhi has described the firm's investment focus: "I invest in technology needed for the 'New World of Work.' That includes companies with deep tech that leverages a new go-to-market techniques to accelerate the enterprise sales cycle" [^5].

On the firm's approach to founder support, Gandhi has stated: "Array comes in and helps them figure out how to get from what we call the 0 to 1 to 10 journey. When we invest there at 0 — no revenue — we help them get to one million ARR. And then from there, we help them set up for a 10 million ARR journey" [^3].

The firm's stated thesis centers on backing technical founders building enterprise data, AI infrastructure, security, and developer tools [^1][^6].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on Array's publicly listed portfolio of approximately 52 active investments [^1] and verified exits, the firm's actual investment behavior reveals:

**Stage distribution:** Array invests overwhelmingly at pre-seed and seed stages, with check sizes of $250K to $3M [^1]. The firm positions itself as a "first institutional check" investor, targeting 8-15% ownership [^7]. Some follow-on investments extend to Series A.

**Sector breakdown:** Based on the available portfolio data and exit history, Array is heavily concentrated in enterprise software and AI. Key sub-sectors include: AI/ML and data infrastructure (Placer.ai, Rad AI, Wabi, Happyrobot, AnswersAI, BlazeAI); enterprise SaaS and developer tools (Blumira, CandorIQ, Modal, Cast, Capsule); and security (Blumira, Corelight). The firm also has investments in robotics and physical AI (Blendid, Sotto).

**Exit patterns:** 15 exits in approximately 8 years of investing, with acquirers including Apple, Amazon, PayPal (Simility), ServiceNow, Gartner, Samsung, GoDaddy, McGraw Hill, and Brocade [^1][^5]. This suggests a strong track record of building enterprise-relevant companies that attract strategic acquirers.

**Founder profile:** Array strongly favors technical founders, consistent with Gandhi's own engineering background. The firm emphasizes founder-market fit and prioritizes deep technical IP over application-layer companies [^6].

**Check size:** $250K to $3M, with a focus on the lower end for pre-seed investments [^1].

**Geographic focus:** Primarily San Francisco and Silicon Valley, with investments across North America [^1].

**Notable pattern:** Array is a solo-GP fund, which is relatively uncommon at this scale. Gandhi leverages her technical background and expert network of 500+ advisors to provide operational support that larger firms typically offer through dedicated platform teams.
