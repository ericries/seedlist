## About

Better Tomorrow Ventures (BTV) is a San Francisco-based venture capital firm founded in 2019 by Jake Gibson and Sheel Mohnot, focused exclusively on fintech investing at the pre-seed and seed stage [^1][^2]. Gibson co-founded NerdWallet (Nasdaq: NRDS), and Mohnot built and sold two fintech companies before founding the 500 Fintech accelerator [^3][^4].

BTV raised its debut fund (BTV I) at $75 million in 2019 [^2]. In February 2022, the firm closed BTV II at $225 million — triple the size of its first fund — during the fintech boom [^5]. In October 2025, BTV closed its third fund at $140 million, bringing total AUM to approximately $450 million [^2][^6].

The fund III is smaller than fund II by design, reflecting the post-2022 fintech correction. BTV III targets 30-35 companies with check sizes of $500K to $4 million [^2]. The firm always leads or co-leads initial rounds [^7].

The team includes Partner Nihar Bobba, who was recruited by Mohnot to drop out of Wharton's MBA program to join the firm, and Principal JC Bahr-de Stefano [^3][^8]. Yoni Lateiner serves as Head of Talent [^3].

BTV also operates The Mint, an incubation program for fintech founders, often engaging before a company even has a name [^4].

## Stated Thesis

BTV publicly describes itself as an operator-led fintech fund, emphasizing that every team member is a former founder [^1][^4]. Mohnot has articulated a core thesis that "everything is fintech" — vertical SaaS companies increasingly derive revenue from payments and financial services rather than software subscriptions [^7]. He cites Toast as an example: the restaurant software company now derives 83% of its revenue from financial services [^7].

Mohnot has stated: "I think it all comes down to a founder and their ability to iterate fast... Are they tenacious? Are they breaking through walls to make stuff happen?" [^7]

The firm publicly states it looks for companies at the intersection of finance and software, with a particular interest in AI-powered financial services and embedded finance [^2][^7]. Mohnot has noted that about 20% of global GDP is financial services and "it's still not digital enough," arguing that "the massive digitization opportunity is still ahead of us" [^7].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 12 featured and verified portfolio companies from BTV's website and press coverage [^1][^9]:

**Sector distribution:** Of 12 verified investments: 4 accounting/finance automation (33%), 3 cards/banking/payments (25%), 3 infrastructure/embedded finance (25%), 2 other/healthcare (17%). The firm is deeply specialized in fintech — Tracxn reports 55 fintech investments and 43 enterprise application investments across the full portfolio [^9].

**Stage distribution:** BTV exclusively leads at pre-seed and seed, with follow-on investment through Series B and occasionally Series C [^7]. Featured companies include Unit (Series B), Coast (Series B), and Relay (Series B), suggesting active follow-on participation [^1].

**Check size:** $500K to $3.5 million for initial investments, with BTV III checks ranging $500K to $4 million [^2][^7].

**Geographic focus:** Primarily US-based companies, with some presence in Latin America (Brazil, Mexico), Europe, and Israel (for US-focused operations) [^7].

**Founder profile patterns:** The firm strongly favors founders with prior operating experience in financial services or adjacent industries. The "operators backing operators" positioning suggests a preference for repeat founders or those from fintech incumbents.

**Co-investor patterns:** Portfolio includes companies also backed by Ribbit Capital, QED Investors, and a16z — suggesting BTV sits in the fintech-specialist ecosystem alongside these larger funds.

**Notable emphasis areas:** Accounting automation (Basis, InScope, Layer) appears to be a concentrated bet within the portfolio, likely reflecting a thesis about the accountant shortage and AI-driven transformation of financial back-office work [^7].

**Portfolio performance:** BTV reports Q1 2025 was its best quarter ever, following a difficult 2022-2023 period when generalist funds pulled back from fintech [^7]. The portfolio includes 12 unicorns according to Tracxn [^9].
