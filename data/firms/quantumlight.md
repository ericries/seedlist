## About

QuantumLight is a London-based quantitative venture capital firm founded in 2022 by Nik Storonsky, the CEO and co-founder of Revolut (valued at $45 billion with 60 million+ users across 39 countries) [^1][^2]. The firm positions itself as "the first truly systematic venture capital and growth equity firm," using a proprietary AI model called Aleph to identify high-potential technology companies [^2].

QuantumLight closed its inaugural $250 million Fund I at hard cap in May 2025, backed by a global group of LPs including billionaire tech founders and prominent institutions [^3][^4]. Storonsky personally contributed approximately $60 million to the fund [^1]. The firm made its first investment in July 2023 [^1].

The firm is led day-to-day by CEO Ilya Kondrashov, a Cambridge graduate, former Goldman Sachs analyst, and co-founder of Kriya, a B2B fintech platform that processed over 30 billion pounds in payments [^1][^2]. The team of approximately two dozen people includes data engineers, quant traders, AI scientists, and seasoned company founders [^1][^5].

As of early 2026, QuantumLight is reportedly in talks with LPs for a second fund targeting approximately $500 million [^6].

In addition to capital, the firm offers portfolio companies a "Scale-up Operating System" — a set of operational playbooks drawn from Storonsky's experience scaling Revolut, covering topics like driving high performance and hiring top talent [^2][^3].

## Stated Thesis

QuantumLight publicly describes its approach as "systematic investing" driven by its proprietary AI model, Aleph, which tracks over 700,000 venture-backed companies across more than 10 billion data points collected since the 1990s to identify "outlier growth-stage companies" [^2].

Storonsky has stated: "Our ambition is to build the world's best systematic venture capital and growth equity firm — and support the new generation of founders by sharing some of the operating principles that we developed at Revolut" [^3].

Kondrashov has stated: "Our goal is to make the invisible operating systems behind iconic companies like Revolut visible and replicable. Founders shouldn't have to reinvent the wheel when it comes to building high-performing teams" [^3].

The firm claims its Aleph system "beats the top quartile VC benchmark by 2X" [^2].

Note: Some companies span multiple sectors; percentages reflect primary classification and total exceeds 100%.

**Stage focus:** Growth-stage investing, typically allocating approximately $5 million per investment [^1]. The fund targets companies that have demonstrated initial traction rather than pre-seed or seed.

**Geographic patterns:** Investments span the US, Europe, and emerging markets, with no strict geographic concentration. The firm itself is London-based, and several portfolio companies are European (Monta in Denmark, Lovable originally from Europe, Sipay in Turkey) [^2][^7].

**Investment pace:** Approximately one deal per month, targeting consistent deployment over three years [^1].

**Unicorns:** 3 portfolio companies have reached unicorn status: Function Health, TogetherAI, and Lovable [^7].

**Notable patterns:**
- Heavy AI orientation across the portfolio, including both pure-play AI companies and AI-enhanced businesses in other sectors
- Broad geographic spread unusual for a London-based fund
- The data-driven model (Aleph) represents a differentiated sourcing approach compared to traditional relationship-driven VC
- Strong emphasis on operational support through playbooks, borrowing from Storonsky's Revolut scaling experience
