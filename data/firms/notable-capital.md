## About

Notable Capital is a U.S.-focused venture capital firm that emerged on March 29, 2024, when GGV Capital split into two successor entities: Notable Capital (U.S.) and Granite Asia (Asia) [^1][^2]. The firm is headquartered in San Francisco with offices in New York [^3].

GGV Capital was originally founded in 2000 as Granite Global Ventures in Silicon Valley and Singapore, with an initial focus on cross-border venture investments bridging the United States and Asia [^4][^5]. The split was driven by growing geopolitical tensions between the U.S. and China, though the firm never explicitly cited this as the reason [^1].

Notable Capital retained approximately $4.2 billion in assets under management from GGV Capital's total of approximately $9.2 billion at the time of the split [^1][^6]. Granite Asia, led by Jenny Lee and Jixun Foo from Singapore, manages approximately $5 billion [^1]. On its website, Notable Capital states it has "$5 billion assets under management" and has "partnered with ambitious founders for over two decades" [^3].

The firm's investment team includes Managing Partners Hans Tung, Jeff Richards, and Glenn Solomon, along with Partners Oren Yunger, Dan Cahana, and Eliya Elon, among others [^7]. The investment team has collaborated for an average of fifteen years [^3]. Jeff Richards explained the reasoning behind the new name: "We named the firm after the founders that we back, the ideas they pursue, and the causes that we work for" [^3].

Under the GGV banner, the firm backed more than 400 companies over two decades [^6]. In January 2021, GGV Capital raised $2.52 billion across new funds for "entrepreneurs around the world" [^6]. As Notable Capital, the firm continues to invest in the U.S., Israel, Europe, and Latin America [^1].

## Stated Thesis

Notable Capital describes itself as a multi-stage venture firm that backs "founders from the earliest stages of their idea to exit" [^3]. The firm focuses on enterprise/cloud infrastructure and consumer applications [^8].

Hans Tung has articulated a philosophy centered on globalization and consumer-facing mobile internet companies, believing in the intersection of AI and fintech [^8]. He has compared the current AI landscape to the early internet days [^8]. Tung has been named to the Forbes Midas list for nine consecutive years [^8].

Glenn Solomon focuses on enterprise/cloud and infrastructure investments and joined GGV Capital in 2006 [^9]. Jeff Richards concentrates on investments across North America, Latin America, Europe, and Israel, as well as cross-border U.S.-India deals [^9].

The firm invests across seed through growth stages, with initial investments historically ranging from $5 million to $25 million per deal [^5].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on Notable Capital's (and its predecessor GGV Capital's) portfolio, the firm's actual investment behavior reveals a multi-stage investor with significant consumer and enterprise breadth.

**Stage distribution:** The firm invests across all stages, with approximately 40% of activity in pre-seed and seed, 30% in Series A, and 30% in Series B and later [^10]. This is genuinely multi-stage, unlike many firms that claim multi-stage but cluster at one end.

**Sector patterns:** Based on the portfolio companies identified, the firm has significant exposure to consumer marketplaces (Airbnb, StockX, Poshmark, DraftKings), enterprise/developer tools (Slack, Zendesk, HashiCorp, Vercel), fintech (Affirm, Coinbase, Stori), and cybersecurity (Orca Security, Drata) [^3][^6].

**Geographic patterns:** While GGV Capital historically invested across the U.S. and Asia, Notable Capital focuses exclusively on the U.S., Israel, Europe, and Latin America [^1]. The firm has had notable success with cross-border companies that expand globally.

**IPO and exit track record:** The firm has seen 45 unicorns, 39 IPOs, and 94 acquisitions across the GGV/Notable platform [^10]. Recent exits include HashiCorp (acquired by IBM, February 2025) [^10]. This is a strong exit track record indicating the firm can pick companies that reach scale.

**Co-investor patterns:** Given the firm's multi-stage approach, Notable Capital frequently co-invests with top-tier firms at different stages — from seed specialists through growth investors.

**Notable bets:** Investments in Anthropic and ByteDance (via GGV) represent two of the most valuable private AI companies globally, demonstrating early recognition of the AI wave [^8].

## In Their Own Words

"We named the firm after the founders that we back, the ideas they pursue, and the causes that we work for." — Jeff Richards, Managing Partner, on the Notable Capital rebrand, 2024 [^3].

Hans Tung has stated that he believes in the power of globalization, especially for consumer-facing mobile internet companies, making it a cornerstone of his investment thesis [^8].

Glenn Solomon has articulated a perspective that investing in enterprise/cloud and infrastructure represents a "trillions ahead" opportunity in the software industry [^9].

## What Founders Say

Torq co-founder and CEO Ofer Smadari has shared the story of how Notable Capital backed his founding team as they went from a previous exit to building an ambitious cybersecurity platform [^3].
