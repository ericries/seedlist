## About

Creandum is a European early-stage venture capital firm founded in 2003 by Staffan Helgesson in Stockholm, Sweden [^1]. The firm was one of the earliest institutional believers that global category-defining companies could be built from Europe [^2]. Creandum started with a €40M first fund backed by two Swedish pension funds [^3].

The firm has grown to manage approximately $2.2 billion across seven funds, having raised roughly €1.6B in total [^4]. Creandum maintains offices in Stockholm, Berlin, London, and San Francisco [^5]. The London office at 36 Carnaby Street was opened in 2022 [^6], while the San Francisco hub has operated since 2016 under Carl Fritjofsson [^7].

Creandum's fund history spans two decades of steady growth [^8] [^9] [^10] [^11] [^12]:

| Fund | Year | Size |
|------|------|------|
| Fund I | 2003 | €40M |
| Fund II | 2007 | €80M |
| Fund III | 2013 | €135M |
| Fund IV | 2016 | €180M |
| Fund V | 2019 | €265M |
| Fund VI | 2022 | ~€448M (~$500M) |
| Fund VII | 2024 | €500M |

Fund II is regarded as one of Europe's strongest-performing institutional VC funds, returning approximately 13x [^3]. Across the past 15 years, fund valuations have ranged between 6x and 13x [^13]. All LPs from every previous fund since 2003 returned as investors in Fund VI [^14].

The firm has backed over 170 companies, with roughly 24 reaching unicorn status — approximately one in six early-stage investments [^4]. Notable exits include Spotify (IPO 2018 at $29.5B market cap), iZettle (acquired by PayPal for $2.2B in 2018), Small Giant Games (acquired by Zynga for ~$700M), and Depop (acquired by Etsy for $1.6B in 2021) [^3] [^15].

Creandum operates as an equal partnership: all partners share equal carry, equal votes, and equal responsibility [^16]. The firm has a strong promote-from-within culture, with six of its current partners having been promoted from analyst or associate roles [^3]. A structured mentorship system pairs each team member with a different partner at each career stage [^6].

## Stated Thesis

Creandum publicly states its mission as: "We back the companies of tomorrow before it's obvious" [^2].

General Partner Simon Schmincke has described the approach: "The early stage is where we have always been most comfortable. We commit to founders before the world catches on, before everyone knows, before it's obvious" [^14].

The firm organizes investments around six verticals: Health, Climate, Technical SaaS, Application SaaS, Fintech, and Consumer [^1]. Fund VII specifically targets AI, SaaS, and climate tech opportunities [^5].

Creandum describes itself as product-obsessed but not operationally interventionist. Ines Streimelweger, Investment Manager, stated: "The founding team is one of the key criteria we look at... there needs to be a strong personal connection" [^15]. The firm positions itself as "strategic thought partners who don't necessarily have all the answers, but who ask the right questions" [^15].

Partner Sabina Wizander has stated: "In our experience, founders who focus on building the best products still prevail. Those who dare to think in generational opportunities and solutions will succeed by virtue of their burning passion for product excellence" [^14].

The firm uses a three-part founder test [^3]:
1. Does the founder have genuine ambition matching the difficulty level?
2. Can they explain how their industry will be transformed?
3. Would you work for them for a decade?

Creandum publicly invests in seed and Series A rounds, targeting 35-40 investments per fund over 2-3 years [^5]. Check sizes range from $200K to $10M [^17]. Fund VI committed to ensuring at least 25% of portfolio companies have female CEOs or majority female co-founder teams [^18].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 40 verified investments below (a subset of 170+ total investments), the following patterns emerge. Sample size is limited; percentages are approximate.

**Stage distribution:** Creandum invests predominantly at seed and Series A. Based on Tracxn data covering broader portfolio activity, the firm has made 60 seed investments and 72 Series A investments, with 12 Series B investments — indicating roughly 42% seed, 50% Series A, and 8% follow-on at Series B [^19]. This is consistent with their stated early-stage focus.

**Sector breakdown (from 40 verified investments below):** Fintech is the strongest vertical — 9 of 40 verified investments (23%) are in fintech (Klarna, Trade Republic, Pleo, iZettle, Embat, Taxfix, N26-adjacent via Schmincke). SaaS/enterprise software accounts for roughly 10 of 40 (25%) including Better Stack, Codesphere, Factorial, Leapsome, cargo.one, Amie. Consumer represents about 7 of 40 (18%) including Spotify, Depop, Vivino, Kahoot!, Lovable. Health is 3 of 40 (8%) with Kry, Lindus Health, Virta Health. Gaming is 2 of 40 (5%) with Small Giant Games and one other. Climate/energy and AI are emerging verticals in more recent funds.

**Geographic patterns:** Despite pan-European positioning, the portfolio retains strong Nordic roots. The firm reports 44 investments still in Sweden alone [^4]. The portfolio spans the Nordics, DACH region (Germany/Austria/Switzerland), UK, Southern Europe (Spain with Factorial, Embat), and increasingly the US.

**Founder profile preferences:** Creandum emphasizes product-obsessed founders with global ambition. Staffan Helgesson has stated he invests based on "gut feeling" and looks for founders he would personally work for [^1]. The firm has a track record of investing in young, ambitious founders — Markus Villig of Bolt was notably young when backed [^3]. The firm is willing to do structural work (e.g., cap table restructuring) to invest in founders they believe in, as demonstrated with Trade Republic [^20].

**Co-investor patterns:** At the later stages, Creandum portfolio companies frequently attract Sequoia, Founders Fund, Accel, and other tier-1 US funds [^20]. At seed/Series A, European co-investors include Northzone (Spotify co-lead), Index Ventures, and various Nordic funds.

**Notable gaps:** While Creandum publicly emphasizes climate tech as a focus area since Fund VI, the number of verified climate investments in the portfolio remains small relative to fintech and SaaS. The metaverse/web3 focus mentioned during Fund VI fundraising has not materialized into prominent portfolio companies.

**Decision speed:** Staffan Helgesson has noted that "competition is brutal. We have to be able to be ready to pull the trigger in 5, 6, 7 days if we have to" [^21]. With Lovable, Fredrik Cassel reportedly showed up to lunch with a term sheet already prepared [^4].

"Gut feeling is real, trust it." — Staffan Helgesson, referencing investments in Spotify, Monta, and Bolt, EU.VC interview [^1]

"There is little to no luck in business life. It all favours the prepared mind and hard working individual." — Staffan Helgesson, EU.VC interview [^1]

"We like to keep small — we are kind of striving to be underdogs." — Johan Brenner, General Partner, Sifted interview [^16]

"Keeping it small and investing at early stages gives us better returns." — Johan Brenner, General Partner, Sifted interview [^16]

"Creandum is an equal partnership — all partners have equal voting rights. We also have equal pay, equal bonuses and equal carry. That's been really important in building our culture." — Johan Brenner, General Partner, Sifted interview [^16]

"It doesn't matter who brought the company in — if we decide to invest, it is the founder that gets to pick which one of us they want to work with." — Johan Brenner, General Partner, Sifted interview [^16]

No independently sourced founder testimonials found.
