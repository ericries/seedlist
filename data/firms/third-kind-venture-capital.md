## About

Third Kind Venture Capital is a New York-based early-stage venture capital firm founded in 2010 by Shana Fisher [^1][^2]. The firm is headquartered at 555 West 18th Street in New York City [^3]. The firm's name is a reference to "Close Encounters of the Third Kind," reflecting its philosophy of seeking extraordinary, transformational companies [^4].

Fisher previously served as a program manager at Microsoft, then as Vice President of Technology and Media M&A at Allen & Company, followed by Senior Vice President of Strategic Planning & M&A at IAC/InterActiveCorp from 2003 to 2010 [^5][^6]. She also holds a Master of Arts from NYU's Interactive Telecommunications Program and a Bachelor of Arts from Hampshire College [^7]. Prior to founding Third Kind, Fisher ran High Line Venture Partners [^5][^6]. She has served as a Board Partner at Andreessen Horowitz since 2013 [^5][^8].

Alex Binkley serves as Partner and COO. He attended Harvard University and Boston University School of Law, previously co-founded Funding Community, and served as an Associate and Senior Associate at WilmerHale providing legal counsel to startups [^9].

In January 2017, Third Kind Venture Capital closed its inaugural fund at $44.325 million [^10][^3]. In 2019, the firm announced plans to raise a second fund targeting $65 million [^3]. As of late 2025, the firm has made over 120 investments and seen 61 portfolio exits [^11].

## Stated Thesis

Third Kind Venture Capital publicly describes its focus as backing "extraordinary founders building transformational products" [^4]. The firm emphasizes founder quality and product vision over early metrics, seeking technical founders with deep market understanding across software-first solutions [^4].

Fisher has stated that her competitive advantage lies in evaluating entrepreneurs rather than markets or technology: "I understand the markets and products and technology from my background, but I don't think that I evaluate them better than others. That's not my competitive differentiation. When entrepreneurs come to me, they come with an idea and a product. No team, no traction. So, I evaluate the entrepreneur and the product. I'd like to think that I have a strong grasp of human psychology. I really try to understand what great entrepreneurs have in them, and I look for the ones who have it" [^5].

The firm publicly targets investments across software, financial services, digital media, information technology, life sciences, and education [^1][^11].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 25 verified investments below, Third Kind Venture Capital's actual portfolio reveals several patterns:

**Stage distribution:** The portfolio is heavily weighted toward seed and pre-seed investments. Of 25 verified investments: 12 seed (48%), 5 Series A (20%), 3 pre-seed (12%), 3 Series B (12%), 2 angel/early (8%). This aligns with the firm's stated pre-seed/seed focus.

**Sector breakdown:** The portfolio spans a notably wide range of sectors. Of 25 verified investments: 6 fintech/crypto (24%), 5 enterprise software/SaaS (20%), 4 consumer/marketplace (16%), 3 AI/ML (12%), 2 hardware/robotics (8%), 2 health/biotech (8%), 3 other (12%). The crypto/Web3 concentration is notable and not prominently featured in the firm's stated thesis.

**Geographic concentration:** The portfolio is overwhelmingly US-based, with a strong New York concentration consistent with the firm's location [^11].

**Check size:** Typical investments range from $500K to $5M, with a sweet spot around $1.5M [^12][^4]. The firm does not typically lead rounds [^12].

**Founder profile patterns:** Fisher's stated emphasis on evaluating entrepreneurs based on "human psychology" rather than metrics suggests a founder-first approach. Her early identification of Pinterest, MakerBot, Stripe, and Vine pre-traction confirms a pattern of betting on founders before market validation [^5].

**Co-investor patterns:** Third Kind frequently co-invests with other early-stage and seed funds. Notable co-investors include MaC Venture Capital, Coinbase Ventures, and ARK Invest in recent deals [^13][^14].

**Notable gaps:** The firm claims a life sciences and education focus, but verified portfolio data shows limited investment activity in those sectors. The crypto/Web3 portfolio is substantial but not prominently mentioned in public messaging.

*Note: This analysis is based on 25 verified investments out of 120+ total. The sample represents approximately 20% of known investments; percentages should be treated as directional rather than definitive.*
