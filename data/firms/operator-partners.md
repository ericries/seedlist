## About

Operator Partners is a New York-based venture capital firm founded in 2020 by Nat Turner and Zach Weinberg, the co-founders of Flatiron Health and Invite Media [^1]. The firm launched in May 2020 as a self-funded vehicle investing exclusively the personal capital of its partners, with no outside limited partners [^2]. Turner and Weinberg met as undergraduates at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania and went on to co-found Invite Media (acquired by Google in 2010 for approximately $81 million) and Flatiron Health (acquired by Roche in February 2018 for $1.9 billion) [^3][^4][^5].

The founding partners were later joined by Gil Shklarski, former CTO of Flatiron Health who scaled the engineering team from inception to over 350 professionals [^6], and Amit Avner, founder and CEO of Taykey (acquired by Innovid) and the first investor in fintech unicorn Plaid [^2]. Zach Goldstein, previously an investor at Union Square Ventures, and Olivia Benjamin, formerly of Bain Capital Ventures and Goldman Sachs, round out the team as partners [^7][^8].

The firm's Fund II closed in 2024 at $40M-$70M in committed capital, all sourced from the partners' personal wealth [^9]. Operator Partners writes checks of $250K-$2M across pre-seed, seed, and Series A stages, and does not lead rounds or take board seats [^9][^10]. As of January 2026, the firm has invested in 144 companies, with 10 unicorns and 19 acquisitions in its portfolio [^11].

Nat Turner also serves as CEO of Collectors, the parent company of PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator) and PCGS, which he acquired in February 2021 for $853 million [^12]. Zach Weinberg co-founded Curie.Bio in 2023, a biotech venture firm with $520 million focused on seed and Series A investments in therapeutics [^13].

## Stated Thesis

Operator Partners describes its thesis as "operators funding operators" [^2]. The firm positions itself as providing capital from the partners' own personal wealth alongside hands-on operational advisory, rather than board control [^9]. Amit Avner stated at launch: "We are operators funding operators. Our investment goes beyond money, we also bring with us our experience of building companies" [^2].

The firm publicly emphasizes speed and founder respect in their process: "We get to yes — or no — quickly. We understand what it's like to run a company and will respect your time" [^9]. They also state: "If there's not already a lead, we'll help find one" [^10].

Operator Partners describes their support model as on-demand: founders email, call, or text the partners individually or as a group, with some engaging weekly and others monthly or quarterly [^14]. Common areas of engagement include product evaluation, customer introductions, hiring support, culture building, and strategic guidance [^14].

The firm highlights that it deliberately avoids leading rounds or taking board seats, differentiating itself from traditional venture capital firms [^10]. Zach Weinberg described this philosophy: "Really what you're trying to do is find ways to help the founder in any way without distracting them" [^15].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 144 verified investments as reported by Tracxn [^11], with portfolio details cross-referenced across Nat Turner's personal portfolio page [^16], Massinvestor [^17], and Crunchbase [^18]:

**Stage distribution:** Operator Partners has made 40 investments at seed stage, 35 at Series A, and 5 at Series B, per Tracxn data [^11]. This indicates a roughly even split between seed (50%) and Series A (44%), with occasional Series B follow-ons (6%). The firm's check sizes range from $250K-$500K at pre-seed, $500K-$1M at seed, and $1M-$2M at Series A [^9].

**Sector breakdown:** Based on Tracxn's categorization of 144 investments: the portfolio skews heavily toward enterprise/B2B software (114 of 144 companies, or 79%, are classified as Enterprise B2B) [^11]. Within that, key verticals include digital health/healthcare (prominent holdings include Spring Health, Headway, Carbon Health, Ro, Clover Health, Oscar Health, Apella, and numerous health-focused startups — at least 29 of Nat Turner's 180+ personal investments are healthcare-related) [^16], fintech (Plaid, Cedar, Ramp, Cardless, Casa, Chainalysis) [^16], developer tools and infrastructure (Prefect, Flatfile, Immuta, Stytch, Composio) [^16][^17], and cybersecurity (TRM Labs, Anagram Security, Aim Security) [^17].

**Geographic concentration:** The firm is headquartered in New York and the majority of investments are US-based (66 of approximately 80 geo-tagged investments), with 5 investments in Israel [^11], reflecting Amit Avner's Israeli background and network [^2].

**Founder profile patterns:** The firm's name and thesis signal a strong preference for operator-founders — people who have built and run companies. The portfolio includes many technical founders and companies in complex regulated industries (healthcare, fintech) where operational experience matters.

**Co-investor patterns:** Operator Partners consistently invests as a non-lead co-investor alongside institutional leads. Notable co-investment relationships include rounds led by Tiger Global (Spring Health Series B) [^19], HighlandX (Apella Series B) [^20], and Redpoint (Serval Series A) [^9]. The firm's average round sizes — $6.26M at seed, $17.4M at Series A, $38.1M at Series B — reflect their role as a smaller participant in larger institutional rounds [^11].

**Notable gaps vs. stated thesis:** While the firm describes itself broadly as backing "tech companies," the actual portfolio is heavily concentrated in enterprise software and healthcare, with relatively limited consumer exposure. The firm does not appear to invest meaningfully in hardware, deep tech, or climate, despite no stated exclusions.
