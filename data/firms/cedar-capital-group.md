## About

Cedar Capital Group (also known as Cedar Investment Management) is a technology-focused venture capital firm founded in 2023 by John Curtius, a former partner at Tiger Global Management [^1][^2]. The firm is registered with the SEC as Cedar Capital Group LP [^3].

Curtius departed Tiger Global in October 2022 after serving as a partner from 2017 to 2022, where he led the firm's software investing practice [^1][^4]. At Tiger Global, Curtius oversaw more than 250 venture investments and reportedly generated approximately $4.5 billion in profits through more than 25 public software investments and over 45 private software deals [^5][^6]. Cedar was planned to launch in mid-2023, with Curtius agreeing to remain at Tiger Global through June 2023 during the transition [^7].

Cedar was reported to be raising $1 billion from institutional investors, which would have ranked among the largest debut funds raised by a first-time fund manager during the 2022-2023 venture capital slowdown [^2][^7]. No public confirmation of the final fund close amount has been found.

## Stated Thesis

Cedar Capital Group publicly describes itself as "a leading global technology investment firm with a mission to back the world's best founders in secular growth areas early on" [^8].

The firm focuses on Series A through Series C stage investments in enterprise software, B2B SaaS, fintech, infrastructure, AI, and machine learning [^1][^4][^5]. Curtius has stated that Cedar will not invest in crypto [^7].

Curtius has publicly articulated his investment philosophy around software economics: "Software is the best business model in the world, and can offer incredibly compelling risk-adjusted return opportunities" [^4].

The firm's stated geographic focus covers the United States and Europe [^2][^7].

## Inferred Thesis

Cedar Capital Group's public portfolio data under its own banner is extremely limited. Only one investment — participation in Pallet's $18M Series A in October 2024 — has been publicly confirmed as a Cedar-era deal [^9]. The Signal by NFX profile lists Curtius's investment range as $100K to $5M with a sweet spot of $1.5M [^5].

Given the insufficient Cedar-specific portfolio data (1 verified investment), meaningful percentage-based sector or stage analysis cannot be computed. The following observations are qualitative only:

**Stage focus:** The firm's stated Series A to Series C focus represents a more targeted approach than Tiger Global's broader multi-stage strategy [^1]. The one confirmed Cedar investment (Pallet) was a Series A round [^9].

**Sector orientation:** Curtius's track record at Tiger Global was heavily weighted toward enterprise software and B2B SaaS, with notable investments in Databricks, Snowflake, Snyk, SentinelOne, GitLab, Toast, and Kustomer [^1][^4]. His stated focus at Cedar is consistent with this background.

**Co-investor patterns:** In the Pallet deal, Cedar/Curtius invested alongside Bain Capital Ventures (lead), Bessemer Venture Partners, and Activant Capital [^9]. This is too small a sample to identify patterns.

**Notable gaps:** Despite the firm's $1B fundraising ambitions and mid-2023 planned launch, publicly verifiable Cedar-specific investments remain scarce as of March 2026. The firm may be operating with a lower public profile than its predecessor platform.
