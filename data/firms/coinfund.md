## About

CoinFund is a crypto-native investment firm and registered investment adviser founded in 2015 by Jake Brukhman and Alex Felix [^1][^2]. The firm is headquartered in Brooklyn, New York [^3]. CoinFund describes itself as "one of the world's most experienced cryptonative investment firms," investing across seed, venture, and liquid stages in the blockchain ecosystem [^1].

Brukhman, who holds degrees in mathematics and computer science, previously worked at Highbridge Capital and Amazon before founding CoinFund [^4]. Alex Felix spent 10 years across research, investing, and structuring, including arranging buyouts at American Capital, with prior roles at BofA Merrill Lynch, RBS, and Guggenheim Partners [^5]. Felix has personally and professionally invested in over 100 blockchain-focused companies since 2013, including seed rounds of Ethereum and Blockdaemon [^5].

The firm manages approximately $1 billion in assets under management across six investment vehicles with over 100 portfolio investments [^3][^6]. CoinFund's fund progression includes early liquid and venture funds, a $83 million Seed III fund closed in March 2021 [^7], a $300 million Ventures I fund announced in August 2022 [^8], and a $158 million Seed IV fund closed in July 2023 that exceeded its initial $125 million target [^9]. Institutional investors including pensions, endowments, and family offices have supplied significant portions of CoinFund's capital, with notable limited partners including Teacher Retirement System of Texas, Adams Street Partners, StepStone Group, Accolade Partners, and Theta Capital Management [^8][^9].

In September 2021, CoinFund brought on David Pakman, previously a partner at Venrock for 13 years, as Managing Partner and Head of Venture Investments [^10]. Pakman had led the Series A of Dollar Shave Club (sold to Unilever for $1 billion) and personally invested in Coinbase prior to its IPO [^10]. Chris Perkins, a former global co-head of futures, clearing, and FXPB at Citi and Iraq war veteran who served nine years in the U.S. Marine Corps, joined as Managing Partner and President [^11]. Seth Ginns, who invested in public growth equities over 18 years at Jennison Associates and was an early angel investor in Coinbase (2012) and participant in the Ethereum ICO (2014), leads liquid investments [^12]. The firm has grown to approximately 30 team members globally [^4][^6].

## Stated Thesis

CoinFund publicly states its mission as championing "the leaders of the new internet" through "pioneering investment strategies in blockchain" [^1]. The firm says it strategically invests in "the decentralized stack and key financial infrastructure" [^3].

For the Seed IV fund, CoinFund described its areas of expertise as including "ZK/ML/AI; DeFi; Layer 2s; consumer services, gaming, NFTs and DAOs; infrastructure including nodes, security, analytics, middleware, interoperability and scalability, and emerging markets" [^9]. The firm has publicly emphasized a technology-driven thesis for decentralized AI, stating it will "advance the field of AI research and enable the development of artificial intelligence as a public good on open, decentralized networks" [^13].

David Pakman has stated publicly: "The blockchain investment landscape is expanding: by geography, with more fruitful sub-sectors...leading to more users onchain" [^6]. Alex Felix has described the industry's maturation as representing "one of the most compelling mega-trends of our time" [^6].

CoinFund's stated investment criteria emphasize three factors: established track records, responsible token management practices, and known (non-anonymous) founders [^14]. Brukhman has noted that 90% of the crypto projects CoinFund backs include governance measures to protect investors and restrict token distribution over several years [^14].

## Inferred Thesis

Based on 30 verified investments with sourced data, CoinFund's actual portfolio reveals a firm that is heavily concentrated in blockchain infrastructure and DeFi, with a growing allocation to decentralized AI.

**Sector breakdown** (based on 30 verified investments): blockchain infrastructure and L1/L2 protocols account for approximately 10 of 30 investments (33%), including Solana, Polkadot, Flow, Monad, Moonbeam, and Blockdaemon. DeFi represents approximately 8 of 30 (27%), including Ondo Finance, ether.fi, Veda, Balancer, and LI.FI. NFTs and consumer applications account for approximately 5 of 30 (17%), including Rarible, Dapper Labs, Decentraland, and Gunzilla Games. Decentralized AI and compute represent approximately 4 of 30 (13%), including Prime Intellect, Pluralis, Recall Network, and Giza. Data and security represent approximately 3 of 30 (10%), including The Graph, Irys, and Cloudburst Technologies.

**Stage distribution:** CoinFund leads or co-leads primarily at seed and Series A stages. Check sizes range from $500K to $15M, with a sweet spot around $6-10M for Ventures I investments [^8][^3]. The firm also maintains significant liquid token positions through its liquid strategies led by Seth Ginns [^12].

**Geographic concentration:** The portfolio is heavily weighted toward US-based and globally distributed crypto-native teams, consistent with the firm's Brooklyn, New York base.

**Co-investor patterns:** CoinFund frequently co-invests with other crypto-native firms including Multicoin Capital, Coinbase Ventures, and Distributed Global. The Ventures I fund LPs include traditional institutional allocators rather than crypto-native funds [^8].

**Founder profile patterns:** The firm strongly favors founders with deep technical backgrounds in cryptography, distributed systems, and protocol design. CoinFund explicitly avoids anonymous founders [^14]. Several portfolio companies were founded by ex-employees of major institutions (Dakota founded by Coinbase/Square alumni, Ondo Finance by Goldman Sachs alumni).

**Notable observation:** CoinFund's stated thesis is broad, but its actual deployment shows concentrated bets on infrastructure layers (L1s, cross-chain, data) and DeFi primitives (restaking, stablecoins, vaults) over consumer-facing applications. The growing AI allocation (13% of verified portfolio) represents a deliberate expansion of thesis beyond pure crypto into the intersection of decentralized compute and AI, a trend accelerating in 2024-2025. The firm's liquid strategy, run separately from venture, provides a differentiated approach rare among crypto-native VCs.
