## About

Character.AI is a generative AI chatbot platform founded in November 2021 by Noam Shazeer and Daniel De Freitas, both former Google Brain engineers who co-authored key research on transformer models [^1][^2]. The platform lets users create and chat with AI characters, and reached millions of daily active users averaging two hours on the platform within months of its September 2022 beta launch [^3]. In August 2024, Google signed a $2.7 billion licensing deal for Character.AI's technology, and both founders returned to Google DeepMind; Dominic Perella (former general counsel) became interim CEO [^4][^5].

## Funding History

| Date | Round | Amount | Lead | Co-investors |

| Dec 2021 | Seed | $43M | — | Elad Gil, SV Angel, A Capital [^6][^7] |
| Mar 2023 | Series A | $150M | Andreessen Horowitz | Nat Friedman, Elad Gil, SV Angel, A Capital [^2][^3][^8] |
| Aug 2024 | Google licensing deal | $2.7B | Google (non-exclusive license) | — [^4][^5][^9] |

The Series A valued the company at $1 billion [^2][^8]. The August 2024 Google deal was structured as a non-exclusive technology license rather than an acquisition; Google took no ownership stake in the company [^4][^5]. The deal drew DOJ scrutiny over whether it functioned as a disguised acquisition [^9]. Prior to the licensing deal, Google had been negotiating a direct investment at a $5 billion valuation in November 2023, but that transaction never closed [^9].

## What Investors Say

**Sarah Wang, General Partner, Andreessen Horowitz (Series A lead):** "Character.AI is rapidly and dramatically advancing generative AI, with the potential to transform how humans connect not just with AI, but more broadly reinvent how we interact with technology as a whole in our everyday lives." She added: "Noam and Daniel have been trailblazers in AI for nearly two decades, and we're excited to support them in the next chapter of their journey" [^8][^10].

## What Founders Say

**Noam Shazeer, Co-Founder:** "The internet was the dawn of universally accessible information, but we're now entering the dawn of universally accessible intelligence" [^3][^10].

**Noam Shazeer on AI progress:** Shazeer compared current AI models to "the first airplane flyer of language models," noting "we're most likely in the infancy of the technology" [^1].
