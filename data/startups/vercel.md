## About

Vercel is a cloud platform for frontend developers, originally founded in 2015 as ZEIT by Guillermo Rauch, Tony Kovanen, and Naoyuki Kanezawa [^1]. The company rebranded to Vercel in April 2020 alongside its Series A announcement [^2]. Vercel is the creator and maintainer of Next.js, the most widely used React framework, which has over 1 million monthly active developers [^3]. The company provides a frontend cloud platform for building, deploying, and scaling web applications [^4]. Vercel is headquartered in San Francisco, CA [^1].

## What Investors Say

Dan Levine, Partner at Accel, described Vercel as "like AWS for the front end" and noted: "The Vercel team understood this nearly a decade ago, and is ahead of the curve on AI's role in the future of the web as well. Their vision is the reason why we first partnered with Vercel in the earliest days" [^2] [^3].

Salesforce Ventures stated: "When we first invested in Vercel's Series C in 2021, we saw a bold vision: simplify, accelerate, and unify how software is built" and added: "While Vercel is the leading framework and infrastructure provider for front-end developers today, we are most excited about Guillermo's vision for being the leading agentic infrastructure" [^4].

## What Founders Say

Guillermo Rauch stated at the Series F announcement: "The web is undergoing a massive evolution. We're moving from the world of pixels to the world of tokens" [^4].

Rauch also said: "Our bet on open source has become a powerful flywheel, and we're excited to continue democratizing agents and making them accessible to everyone" [^10].

At the Series E, Rauch stated: "Vercel's Frontend Cloud has been adopted by a growing number of enterprises globally to build more performant personalized web experiences" [^9].
