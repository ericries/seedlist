## About

Sierra is a conversational AI platform for enterprises, founded in 2023 by Bret Taylor and Clay Bavor [^1]. Taylor previously served as Co-CEO of Salesforce, co-created Google Maps, was CTO of Facebook, and founded Quip (acquired by Salesforce for $750M) [^1][^6]. Bavor spent 18 years at Google, most recently leading Google Labs, and previously led Google's AR/VR efforts and Google Lens [^6]. The two met while working together at Google in 2005 [^1].

Sierra builds AI agents that handle customer service interactions for enterprises, using a "constellation" of large language models from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Meta [^3]. The company launched in February 2024 and reached $100M ARR within seven quarters [^5]. Enterprise customers include WeightWatchers, SiriusXM, Sonos, ADT, Ramp, SoFi, Rivian, Deliveroo, Discord, and Wayfair [^3][^5]. Over 50% of Sierra's customers have revenue exceeding $1B, and 20% exceed $10B [^5]. The company has offices in San Francisco, New York, London, Atlanta, Singapore, Tokyo, Paris, Madrid, and Toronto [^6].

## What Investors Say

Sequoia's Ravi Gupta, a Sierra board member, said the $110M seed round gives the startup the chance "to always be on offense" [^1].

## What Founders Say

Bret Taylor on Sierra's mission: "The greatest opportunity we have is to enable every company, no matter how sophisticated or technical, to deploy [AI] successfully" [^1].

Clay Bavor on the market opportunity: "In 1995, if you wanted to exist digitally, you needed a website. Ten years ago, you needed a mobile app. Now, you need an AI agent" [^7].

Taylor and Bavor on founding Sierra: "Starting Sierra was an early bet that AI agents would transform the enterprise software industry" [^8].

On reaching $100M ARR: "Credit goes to our amazing team, who've worked their tails off to build the business, and to our growing band of customers — the real pioneers of applied AI" [^5].

Bret Taylor on the May 2026 Series E and competitive dynamics: "There's just a lot of competition. We are multiples larger than the next biggest and are trying to invest aggressively so that we can continue to expand our lead" [^10].

Taylor on a future IPO: an IPO is "definitely in our future," though the company plans to remain private for now [^10].
