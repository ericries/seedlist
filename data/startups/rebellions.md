## About

Rebellions is a South Korean fabless AI chip startup founded in September 2020 by Sunghyun Park, Jin-wook Oh, Hyo-eun Kim, and Sung-ho Shin [^1][^2]. The company designs AI inference accelerators — chips optimized for the compute required for AI models to respond to user queries — while outsourcing fabrication [^1]. Rebellions develops the Rebel100 NPU platform and recently launched RebelRack and RebelPOD, vertically integrated AI infrastructure systems [^3]. The company's software stack integrates with open-source tools including vLLM, PyTorch, and Hugging Face [^3]. Rebellions is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, with expanding U.S. operations [^3]. The company is a central part of the South Korean government's "K-Nvidia" initiative to boost the country's domestic AI semiconductor sector [^1].

Total raised: $850M, with $650M raised in the last six months [^1]. The Korea National Growth Fund contributed approximately 250 billion Korean won (~$165M), marking the first direct government investment under South Korea's "K-Nvidia" initiative [^1]. Mirae Asset has been an investor since the Series A [^3]. The company plans to IPO later in 2026 [^1].

Known investors across all rounds include: Mirae Asset Financial Group, Korea National Growth Fund, Arm, Samsung, SK Hynix, SK Telecom, KT (Korea Telecom), Aramco, Kindred Ventures, Top Tier Capital, Korea Development Bank, Korelya Capital, Lion X Ventures, and Pegatron VC [^1][^2][^3][^5].
