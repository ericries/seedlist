## About

CavilinQ is a University of Chicago spinout developing modular quantum interconnect hardware designed to link multiple quantum processors, addressing physical scaling limitations inherent in single-processor architectures [^1][^2]. The company's technical approach involves cavity-enhanced photonic links that create high-fidelity light-matter interfaces, pioneered by scientific co-founders Hannes Bernien (University of Chicago) and Mikhail Lukin (Harvard University) [^1]. CavilinQ plans to establish a specialized laboratory in Cambridge, MA [^1].
