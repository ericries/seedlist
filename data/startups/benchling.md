## About

Benchling is a cloud-based R&D platform for life sciences, founded in 2012 by Sajith Wickramasekara and Ashu Singhal, who met at MIT [^1][^2]. The company was part of Y Combinator's Spring 2012 batch and provides digital lab notebooks, molecular biology tools, and workflow management to over 200,000 scientists [^2][^3]. Benchling was valued at $6.1 billion as of its Series F in November 2021 [^4].

## What Investors Say

Eric Vishria, General Partner at Benchmark, on the Series B (June 2018):

> "Benchling is coming in at the optimal time to supercharge the incredible work scientists are doing." [^3]

Matt Murphy, Partner at Menlo Ventures, on the Series C (July 2019):

> "Life science R&D has become incredibly complex...Benchling's software replaces pen and paper workflows and becomes the system of record for a wide range of biotech and pharma R&D projects." [^6]

Will Reed, General Partner at Spark Capital, on the Series D (May 2020):

> "As the world increasingly runs on biotech, biotech will run on Benchling." [^7]

Ram Woo, Partner at Altimeter Capital, on the Series F (November 2021):

> "Now that scientists can read, write, and edit the code of life, biotechnology is becoming one of the most important platforms for innovation since the internet." [^4]
