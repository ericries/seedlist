## About

Smule is a social music platform founded in 2008 by Jeff Smith and Stanford professor Ge Wang that enables users to sing, create, and share music collaboratively [^1]. The company's flagship app, Sing! Karaoke, connects millions of users worldwide through collaborative music-making [^2]. Smule has raised approximately $211M across 13 funding rounds [^3].

## What Investors Say

"I've backed founders who are bipolar and pregnant and had all kinds of distractions and impairments. In general, if I can find an entrepreneur who is brilliant and passionate and honest, we'll work around the other stuff." — David Cowan, Bessemer Venture Partners, on backing Jeff Smith while he pursued a PhD at Stanford [^9]

"Smule is a deeply original, bold idea with a mission of changing the way the world experiences music. Our investment will advance Smule's music mission across the Indian subcontinent and unlock the creativity of many millions along the way." — Rishi Jaitly, CEO of Times Bridge [^10]

## What Founders Say

"Smule is about empowering our community to do more of what they love — from singing with their favourite celebrities to collaborating with friends to discovering new music." — Jeff Smith, CEO & Co-Founder [^10]
