## About

Affirm is a buy now, pay later (BNPL) financial technology company founded in 2012 by Max Levchin, Nathan Gettings, Jeffrey Kaditz, and Alex Rampell as part of the initial portfolio of startup studio HVF [^1][^2]. Levchin is a co-founder of PayPal. The company is based in San Francisco and went public on NASDAQ on January 13, 2021, closing its first day at $97.24 per share with a market value of $23.6 billion [^3]. Prior to IPO, Affirm had raised approximately $1.5 billion across 10 funding rounds [^4].

Affirm priced its IPO at $49.00 per share, offering 24.6 million shares of Class A common stock and raising approximately $1.2 billion. AFRM opened at $90.90 and closed its first trading day at $97.24, valuing the company at approximately $23.6 billion [^3][^7][^8]. The Series G Preferred was priced at $19.93 per share with an approximate $5 billion post-money valuation, per the S-1 filing [^9].
