## About

Bazaarvoice is a SaaS platform that enables brands and retailers to collect and display user-generated content such as product ratings, reviews, and Q&A on e-commerce sites [^1]. The company was co-founded in May 2005 by Brett Hurt and Brant Barton in Austin, Texas [^2]. Hurt, a former Dell executive, built the company around the insight that online word-of-mouth drives consumer purchasing decisions [^2]. Major early clients included Best Buy, Costco, Dell, Macy's, and Procter & Gamble [^1].

Bazaarvoice went public on the NASDAQ (ticker: BV) on February 23, 2012, offering 9.5 million shares at $12 per share and raising $114 million [^2]. The stock opened at $16 on its first day, a 33% gain [^2].

On February 1, 2018, Marlin Equity Partners completed a take-private acquisition of Bazaarvoice at $5.50 per share in cash [^3]. In March 2021, Thomas H. Lee Partners made a majority investment at a valuation exceeding $1.5 billion [^2].
