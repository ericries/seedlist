## About

Moment Energy is an EV battery repurposing company that takes retired electric vehicle battery packs and turns them into grid-scale energy storage systems [^1]. The company removes original automaker battery management systems and replaces them with proprietary software, allowing modules of different chemistries to be combined into larger storage solutions [^1]. Moment Energy says it is the first company to receive UL certification for repurposed EV battery storage [^1]. Headquartered in Port Coquitlam, British Columbia, the company is building a gigawatt-scale second-life battery factory in Austin, Texas, and has supply agreements with Mercedes-Benz and Nissan [^1][^2]. The four founders — Edward Chiang, Gabriel Soares, Sumreen Rattan, and Gurmesh Sidhu — met at Simon Fraser University, where they worked together on an electric race vehicle team [^3].
