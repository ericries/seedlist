## About

Axoft is a Cambridge, Massachusetts-based neurotechnology company developing bio-inspired, implantable brain-computer interfaces (iBCIs) [^1]. The company was founded in 2021 by Paul Le Floch (CEO), Tianyang Ye (CTO), and Jia Liu, an assistant professor at Harvard's School of Engineering and Applied Sciences [^2].

Axoft's iBCIs are built using its proprietary Fleuron material, which the company describes as up to 10,000x softer than the polyimide used in existing iBCIs, with 8x more region access, 32x more sensors/stimulators per thread than standard flexible probes, and over 60% less signal attenuation than polyimide [^1]. The company is pursuing applications in disorders of consciousness, with plans to expand clinical trials globally and pursue U.S. regulatory approval [^1].
