## About

Panda Surgical is a University College London (UCL) spinout developing handheld robotic systems for minimally invasive neurosurgery [^1]. The company was co-founded in 2022 by Manios Dimitrakakis (CEO), UCLH neurosurgeon Hani Marcus (CMO), and UCL Computer Science Professor Danail Stoyanov (Scientific Advisor), translating technology developed during Dimitrakakis's PhD at the Wellcome/EPSRC Centre for Interventional and Surgical Sciences (WEISS) [^2]. The platform features detachable wristed instruments as small as 3mm in diameter, combining robotic dexterity with AI-powered software to enable safer and more precise procedures in confined surgical spaces [^1][^3]. The company is headquartered at UCL Here East in London [^1].

PrimaNova Technologies led the initial seed round in May 2023 and entered a strategic partnership to bring the system into the clinic [^4]. In December 2024, Panda Surgical, in partnership with UCLH and UCL, secured an NIHR Invention for Innovation Product Development Award (i4i PDA) exceeding £1 million to fund a two-year project including the first-in-human clinical trial for brain tumor removal surgery [^5]. In September 2025, the company closed a £1 million strategic round led by Oxford Investment Consultants, with participation from New Wave Ventures, UCL Business, and US and European neurosurgeons [^3][^6].

## What Investors Say

Hector Edwards of Oxford Investment Consultants, who joined the board after leading the September 2025 round, described the company as having a "great team, exciting technology, and real passion to transform neurosurgery" [^6].

## What Founders Say

Manios Dimitrakakis, CEO: "Our mission is to enhance the neurosurgeon's capabilities to improve patient outcomes and allow hospitals to maximise their resources" [^5].

Hani Marcus, CMO and UCLH neurosurgeon: "Combining robotic instruments with artificial intelligence has the potential to transform neurosurgery" [^5].
