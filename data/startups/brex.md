## About

Brex is a fintech company founded on January 3, 2017, by Brazilian entrepreneurs Henrique Dubugras and Pedro Franceschi [^1][^2]. The founders, who previously co-founded payments company Pagar.me in Brazil, joined Y Combinator's Winter 2017 batch originally working on a VR startup but pivoted to fintech three weeks into the program after experiencing firsthand the difficulty of obtaining a corporate credit card as startup founders [^1][^2][^3].

Brex launched publicly in June 2018 with a corporate credit card designed specifically for startups, underwriting based on a company's funding and cash balance rather than personal credit history [^4]. The company expanded into expense management, business accounts, and AI-powered spend management software [^5]. Brex reached unicorn status in October 2018, just four months after its public launch [^1].

In January 2026, Capital One announced a definitive agreement to acquire Brex for $5.15 billion in a combination of cash and stock ($2.75 billion in cash and 10.6 million Capital One shares), a significant discount from Brex's peak $12.3 billion valuation in January 2022 [^6][^7]. The transaction is expected to close in mid-2026 [^7].

## What Investors Say

Scott Shleifer, Partner at Tiger Global, on leading Brex's Series D: "Brex builds finance for the next generation of businesses. We're excited to partner as they grow rapidly and lead an industry dominated by incumbents" [^5].

Mood Rowghani, General Partner at Kleiner Perkins Digital Growth Fund, on leading Brex's Series C-2: "We are investing behind Brex's fundamental vision -- that businesses want customized payment solutions tailored to their specific needs. It is this vision that will continue to propel Brex's extraordinary growth" [^10].

Richard D. Fairbank, Capital One Founder, Chairman, and CEO, on the acquisition: "Acquiring Brex accelerates this journey, especially in the business payments marketplace" [^7].

## What Founders Say

Pedro Franceschi, on the Capital One acquisition: "This isn't a story about cost synergies. It's a story about growth acceleration, about two founder-led companies coming together to bring a better way to manage money to millions of businesses" [^14].

Pedro Franceschi, on combining with Capital One: "Together, we'll maximize founder mode by combining Brex's payments expertise and spend management software with Capital One's massive scale" [^7].

Henrique Dubugras, on founding insight: "We could not get a credit card for our startup despite having $120,000 in funding from Y Combinator" [^3].

Henrique Dubugras, on product philosophy: "We believe you have to actually rebuild the financial product" when redefining customer experience, rather than simply layering an app on top of existing solutions [^15].

Henrique Dubugras, on ambition: "We never wanted to be serial entrepreneurs. We just wanted to build one company and work on it for, like, 30 years. That's our dream and it's what we want to do with Brex" [^2].

Henrique Dubugras, on the Series C: "With this fresh funding, we are able to enhance our growth efforts and continue to benefit founders and operators" [^9].

Henrique Dubugras, on the Series D: "Our investors believe in our team, business model, product vision, and customers. This round fuels growth of our financial solution" [^5].
