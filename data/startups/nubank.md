## About

Nubank is a Brazilian digital bank founded in 2013 by David Vélez, Cristina Junqueira, and Edward Wible in São Paulo, Brazil [^1]. It is the largest neobank in Latin America, serving over 90 million customers across Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia [^2]. The company launched its first product — a no-fee credit card managed entirely through a mobile app — in 2014 [^3]. Vélez, a Colombian-born Stanford MBA, previously worked as a partner at Sequoia Capital scouting Latin American investments before founding Nubank [^1]. Junqueira brought deep experience from traditional Brazilian banking, while Wible provided the technical foundation as CTO [^1]. The company went public on the NYSE on December 9, 2021, raising $2.6 billion at a $41.5 billion valuation — making it the most valuable financial institution in Latin America at the time of listing [^4][^5].
