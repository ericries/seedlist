## About

Warby Parker is a direct-to-consumer eyewear company founded in 2010 by Wharton MBA students Neil Blumenthal, Dave Gilboa, Andrew Hunt, and Jeffrey Raider [^1][^2]. The company disrupted the eyewear industry by selling prescription glasses online for $95, bypassing Luxottica's near-monopoly on the traditional retail market [^2][^3]. Warby Parker went public via direct listing on the NYSE on September 29, 2021, opening at $54.05 per share and closing its first day at a valuation of approximately $6 billion [^4].
