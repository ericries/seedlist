## About

Flatiron Health is an oncology-focused health technology company founded in 2012 in New York City by Nat Turner and Zach Weinberg, who previously co-founded Invite Media (acquired by Google in 2010) [^1][^2]. The company built the first cloud-based oncology data platform, integrating cancer center data systems to provide longitudinal patient population views and enable clinical and business analytics [^1]. Flatiron's OncologyCloud platform included OncoEMR (electronic health record), OncoAnalytics (advanced analytics), OncoBilling (practice management), and SeeYourChart (patient portal) [^3]. By 2016, Flatiron partnered with over 265 community cancer clinics and five major academic research centers [^3]. Roche acquired the company in April 2018 for approximately $1.9 billion, building on an existing 12.6% equity stake from its Series C lead investment [^4][^5]. The company raised a total of approximately $313 million across three rounds prior to the acquisition [^1][^2][^3].

## What Investors Say

Dr. Krishna Yeshwant, Google Ventures Partner, on the Series A: "Oncology is one of the fastest growing segments of the health care industry, and oncologists and researchers need new and advanced tools to keep up." [^1]

Dr. Krishna Yeshwant on the Series B: "They are working on one of the biggest problems in healthcare, and their progress has been nothing short of stunning to date." [^2]

Daniel O'Day, COO of Roche Pharmaceuticals, on the Series C: "This partnership will generate valuable insights to shape progress in both the development of new medicines and the way those medicines are made available to patients." [^3]

Daniel O'Day on the acquisition: "This is an important step in our personalised healthcare strategy for Roche, as we believe that regulatory-grade real-world evidence is a key ingredient to accelerate the development of, and access to, new cancer treatments." [^4]
