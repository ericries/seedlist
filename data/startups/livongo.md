## About

Livongo Health was a digital health company founded in 2014 by Glen Tullman, the former CEO of Allscripts, to help people with chronic conditions manage their health through connected devices and data science [^1]. Tullman was inspired to start the company after his son Sam was diagnosed with type 1 diabetes [^2]. The company originated from EosHealth, founded by Dr. Kimon Angelides in 2008, which was acquired by 7wire Ventures in October 2013 and rebranded as Livongo Health [^2]. Headquartered in Mountain View, California, Livongo's initial focus was diabetes management, delivering a connected blood glucose meter with real-time coaching and personalized health nudges [^1]. The company later expanded to address hypertension, weight management, pre-diabetes, and behavioral health [^3].

Livongo raised approximately $235 million across five private funding rounds before filing for its IPO on June 28, 2019 [^4]. The company went public on the Nasdaq on July 25, 2019 at $28 per share, raising approximately $355 million in gross proceeds [^5]. The IPO closed with full exercise of the underwriters' over-allotment option, bringing total gross proceeds to approximately $408.5 million from 14,590,050 shares [^6]. Livongo reported 2018 revenue of $68.4 million with a net loss of $33.4 million [^4].

On August 5, 2020, Teladoc Health announced a merger with Livongo in a deal valued at approximately $18.5 billion [^7]. The merger was completed on October 30, 2020, with Livongo shareholders receiving 0.5920 shares of Teladoc Health stock plus $11.33 in cash per Livongo share [^8].
