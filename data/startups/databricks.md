## About

Databricks was founded in 2013 by seven UC Berkeley researchers: Ali Ghodsi, Ion Stoica, Matei Zaharia, Patrick Wendell, Reynold Xin, Andy Konwinski, and Arsalan Tavakoli-Shiraji [^1]. The company was created to commercialize Apache Spark, an open-source distributed computing project developed at UC Berkeley [^1]. Ali Ghodsi, the CEO, is a Swedish-American computer scientist who joined UC Berkeley in 2009 as a visiting scholar, where he co-authored influential papers on Apache Spark and Apache Mesos [^2]. Databricks is headquartered in San Francisco, California [^1]. The company pioneered the "lakehouse" architecture combining data lakes and data warehouses, and has grown rapidly, surpassing $5.4 billion in revenue run-rate by 2025 [^3]. Ron Conway invested in Databricks through A.Capital, an early-stage venture firm he founded in 2014, and SV Angel's growth fund also includes Databricks in its portfolio through Ashvin Bachireddy's involvement via Geodesic Capital [^4] [^5].

## What Investors Say

> "If you want to build competitive advantage using Artificial Intelligence and you are not Google or Facebook, Databricks is essential. We've looked at every solution on the market and nothing else comes close."
> -- Ben Horowitz, co-founder and general partner at Andreessen Horowitz, Series D announcement, August 2017 [^9]

Ben Horowitz has also spoken about co-founder Matei Zaharia, calling him one of the "best distributed systems people out of academia in the last 10 years," and stating that between Zaharia's talent and the team's work on Apache Spark, he would have invested "should they have had no plan at all" [^18].
