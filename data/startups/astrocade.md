## About

Astrocade is a Los Altos, California-based interactive entertainment platform that uses generative AI to let non-technical users build playable games and experiences from natural language prompts [^1] [^2]. The company was founded in late 2022 by Stanford-trained AI researchers Amir Sadeghian (CEO), Ali Sadeghian (CTO), and Fei-Fei Li, co-director of Stanford's Human-Centered AI Institute [^3].

The platform is designed around the thesis that generative AI collapses the gap between imagining a game and building one, making game creation a mainstream consumer activity rather than a specialist skill [^2]. As of the May 2026 funding announcement, Astrocade reported more than 20 million engaged users across the eight months following launch, with hundreds of millions of plays per month [^1].

## What Investors Say

Sequoia Capital partner David Cahn, in a Fortune interview about the round, on Astrocade's user base:

> "Women between 20 and 40 are the best users. You'd think it's younger boys—10 to 15, that classic gaming segment—but that's not really who this is." [^2]

## What Founders Say

Co-founder Ali Sadeghian, on the gap between game players and game creators:

> "If you go into a classroom and ask 'how many people play games?' everyone says 'I play.' If you ask 'how many of you want to create games', 80% will say they want to create a game." [^2]

Co-founder Amir Sadeghian, on the platform's model:

> "We're actually most inspired by YouTube, the way they nurture their creators." [^2]
