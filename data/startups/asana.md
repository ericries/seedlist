## About

Asana is a work management platform founded on December 16, 2008, by Dustin Moskovitz and Justin Rosenstein, both former engineers at Facebook [^1]. Moskovitz was Facebook's co-founder and VP of Engineering; Rosenstein was an engineering lead who also worked at Google [^1] [^2]. The two built an internal productivity tool at Facebook called "Tasks" and left to turn the concept into a standalone product [^1]. Asana launched publicly in 2011 and went public via direct listing on the NYSE on September 30, 2020, at an initial valuation of approximately $5.5 billion [^3]. The company had raised approximately $213 million in private funding prior to going public [^4].
