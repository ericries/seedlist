## About

Seaport Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biotechnology company developing oral therapies for neuropsychiatric disorders, including depression, anxiety, and other central nervous system conditions [^1]. The company uses its proprietary Glyph platform — a lymphatic-targeting prodrug technology designed to improve oral bioavailability and reduce first-pass metabolism — to reformulate clinically validated mechanisms into differentiated product candidates [^1]. Seaport is headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts and was founded in 2024 by Daphne Zohar (formerly founding CEO of PureTech Health and co-founder of Karuna Therapeutics) and Steven M. Paul, M.D. (former CEO of Karuna Therapeutics and former President of Lilly Research Laboratories) [^1].

The company priced its upsized initial public offering of 14,160,000 shares at $18.00 per share on April 30, 2026, for gross proceeds of approximately $254.9 million [^2]. Common stock began trading on the Nasdaq Global Select Market on May 1, 2026 under the ticker symbol "SPTX" [^2][^3]. Underwriters were granted a 30-day option to purchase up to an additional 2,124,000 shares [^2]. Upon closing, PureTech Health holds 16,685,013 shares of Seaport common stock [^2].
