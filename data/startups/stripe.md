## About

Stripe is a financial infrastructure platform that provides payment processing software and APIs for internet businesses [^1]. The company was founded in 2010 by Irish brothers Patrick and John Collison in Palo Alto, California [^2][^3].

Both brothers had prior entrepreneurial success, having sold their auction management system Auctomatic for $5 million in 2008 while still teenagers [^3]. Observing how difficult it was for developers to accept payments online, John suggested they build a prototype for an "easy way to move money online" on the way back from a startup event in 2009 [^4]. The company was initially conceived as "/dev/payments" and aimed to let developers integrate payments with just seven lines of code [^3][^2].

Stripe participated in Y Combinator and secured seed funding of approximately $2 million in 2011 from prominent investors including Peter Thiel, Elon Musk, Sequoia Capital, Andreessen Horowitz, and SV Angel [^2][^5]. The company launched publicly in the United States in September 2011 after a private beta period [^3].

As of February 2026, Stripe's valuation reached $159 billion through an employee tender offer, with Thrive Capital, Coatue, a16z, and others participating [^6]. The company has raised a total of approximately $9.8 billion across 24 funding rounds from over 120 investors [^5]. Stripe remains a private company as of March 2026.

## What Investors Say

**Peter Thiel (Seed investor):** Thiel was among the first institutional backers of Stripe, investing as part of the $2 million seed round in 2011. Thiel, along with fellow PayPal alumni Elon Musk and Max Levchin, recognized the opportunity to simplify online payments -- the very problem PayPal had first tackled a decade earlier [^2][^5].

**Sequoia Capital:** Sequoia has been one of Stripe's most consistent backers, participating in nearly every major round from the seed stage through the Series H. Their continued investment reflects conviction in Stripe's position as core internet infrastructure [^5][^7].

## What Founders Say

**Patrick Collison on the founding insight:** The idea originated on the way back from a startup event in 2009, when John suggested they build a prototype for an "easy way to move money online." Patrick later reflected: "Finally we'll make payments not like really suck on the internet, and so I think that the reaction to Stripe was great, payments don't suck anymore" [^4][^11].

**Patrick Collison on early skepticism:** "People told us we were crazy to try to compete in this space. They said we didn't understand the complexities of the financial system, that we were too young, too inexperienced. But we saw an opportunity to make things better, and we were determined to see it through" [^4].

**Patrick Collison on the mission:** Collison emphasized that the goal was to "make it super simple for people to build a business on the web" [^12].
