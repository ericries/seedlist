## About

Latus Bio is a clinical-stage gene therapy company developing AAV-based therapies for central nervous system and rare diseases, with offices in Philadelphia, PA and Boston, MA (Seaport) [^1] [^2]. The company was founded by Beverly Davidson, Ph.D., a gene therapy researcher at Children's Hospital of Philadelphia who received the American Society of Gene & Cell Therapy's Outstanding Achievement Award in 2026 [^4]. P. Peter Ghoroghchian, M.D., Ph.D., serves as CEO [^1].

Latus's lead programs are LTS-201, an AAV gene therapy targeting MSH3 expression for Huntington's disease (IND expected Q3 2026), and LTS-101, an AAV gene therapy encoding the TPP1 gene for CLN2 (Batten) disease, which received FDA IND clearance in December 2025 along with Orphan Drug, Rare Pediatric Disease, and Fast Track designations [^4]. The company's strategy targets "large-rare and broader CNS indications" with potential applications also in kidney, eye, heart, and muscle diseases [^1].
