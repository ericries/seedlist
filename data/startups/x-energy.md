## About

X-energy is a Rockville, Maryland-based developer of small modular nuclear reactors (SMRs), founded in 2009 by Kam Ghaffarian [^1][^2]. The company's flagship Xe-100 is an 80-megawatt pebble-bed reactor designed for standardized manufacturing, using helium gas cooling and TRISO fuel pellets (uranium kernels wrapped in carbon and silicon) engineered to withstand higher temperatures and reduce meltdown risk [^1]. J. Clay Sell, former US Deputy Secretary of Energy, has served as CEO since 2019 [^2][^3].

The company has struck major commercial deals with Amazon's Climate Pledge Fund (which led its Series C-1 and committed to purchase up to 5 gigawatts of X-energy power by 2039) and with Dow Chemical, which is hosting an Xe-100 deployment at a Texas chemical plant for heat and power [^1].
