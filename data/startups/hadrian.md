## About

Hadrian is an American manufacturing company that designs and operates automated, software-driven factories to produce precision components for the aerospace and defense industries [^1]. The company was founded in November 2020 by Chris Power, an Australian entrepreneur who previously founded ADSC, a private equity firm focused on precision manufacturing rollups [^2]. Hadrian is headquartered in Los Angeles, California [^1].

The company uses proprietary software called "Opus" to coordinate factory operations and achieve production autonomy [^3]. Hadrian's factories operate at a 1:5-1:6 human-to-machine ratio compared to the industry standard of 2:1, and achieve 75-80% equipment uptime versus a 30% aerospace industry average [^2]. The company produces approximately 10,000 unique parts per month across drones, rockets, satellites, and naval systems, reducing lead times from the industry standard of 4-16 weeks to 1-3 weeks [^2].

Hadrian operates a 100,000-square-foot facility in Torrance, California ("Factory 2"), and is building a 270,000-square-foot manufacturing hub in Mesa, Arizona ("Factory 3"), which represents a $200 million capital investment and approximately 350 new jobs [^3]. The company has announced plans to build up to five additional factories over the course of 2026 [^4]. Defense contracts represent approximately 50% of current business, with 80% of revenue coming from 3-7 year production contracts [^5].
