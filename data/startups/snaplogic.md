## About

SnapLogic is an enterprise integration platform company founded in 2006 [^1]. The company was led by Gaurav Dhillon, founder and former CEO of Informatica, who was named CEO when SnapLogic closed its Series A financing [^2]. SnapLogic is headquartered in San Mateo, California [^1].

The company provides an Intelligent Integration Platform (iPaaS) that connects enterprise applications, data, and APIs across cloud and on-premise environments [^3]. SnapLogic's platform uses pre-built connectors called "Snaps" that can be assembled to define integration tasks without writing code [^4].

In August 2025, founder and CEO Gaurav Dhillon retired, and Brad Stewart was appointed Chief Executive Officer. Dhillon transitioned to Chairman [^5].

## What Investors Say

**Ben Horowitz (Andreessen Horowitz):** Horowitz joined SnapLogic's board of directors after leading the $10M Series B round in December 2010 [^7][^8]. Andreessen Horowitz participated in every round from Series A through Series F, making it the most consistent institutional backer [^2][^7][^9][^11][^13][^15].

## What Founders Say

**Gaurav Dhillon (Founder & Former CEO) on future plans (2016):** "An IPO is very much in our future. This is our mezzanine financing, and we don't anticipate the need for additional capital" [^15].

**Gaurav Dhillon on the platform:** "We've hit that stride just right with simple self-service for complicated tasks. We hide complexity from the business customer while maintaining security and scalability" [^15].
