## About

ngmoco ("Next Generation Mobile Company") was a mobile game publisher and developer for iOS and Android, founded in July 2008 by former Electronic Arts executive Neil Young [^1]. The company pioneered free-to-play mobile gaming with its Plus+ social network platform. It was acquired by Japan's DeNA in October 2010 for up to $400 million ($300M upfront plus $100M earnout) [^2].

Total raised: $40.6M across three rounds [^5]. Kleiner Perkins invested just over $10M total and held ~32.7% at exit [^6]. Co-founders Neil Young and Bob Stevenson each held 9.5% [^2].
