## About

Exergy3 is a University of Edinburgh spinout developing modular thermal-energy-storage units that convert surplus renewable electricity — such as curtailed wind generation — into high-temperature industrial heat at up to 1,200°C, targeting decarbonization of industrial processes like distilling, chemicals and food production [^1]. The company is led by CEO Dr Markus Rondé and CSO Dr Adam Robinson [^2].
