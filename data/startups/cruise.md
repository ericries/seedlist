## About

Cruise (originally Cruise Automation) was an autonomous vehicle company founded by Kyle Vogt in October 2013 [^1]. Dan Kan joined as co-founder and COO after the company participated in Y Combinator [^2]. The company developed self-driving car technology, initially demonstrated through modified Audi vehicles tested on Bay Area highways [^3]. General Motors acquired Cruise in March 2016 for over $1 billion in cash and stock, when the company had approximately 40 employees [^4]. Post-acquisition, Cruise continued operating as a subsidiary and raised billions in external funding from SoftBank, Honda, Microsoft, Walmart, and T. Rowe Price [^5][^6][^7][^8]. GM spent over $10 billion on Cruise in total [^9]. In December 2024, GM announced it would stop funding Cruise's robotaxi operations, citing the competitive landscape and capital requirements [^10].

## What Investors Say

"Honda chose to collaborate with Cruise and General Motors based on their leadership in autonomous and electric vehicle technology and our shared vision of a zero-emissions and zero-collision world." — Seiji Kuraishi, Executive Vice President and Representative Director COO of Honda, October 2018 [^6]

"This is the logical next step in General Motors and Honda's relationship, given our joint work on electric vehicles, and our close integration with Cruise." — Mary Barra, Chairman and CEO of GM, October 2018 [^6]

"Our increased investment position not only simplifies Cruise's shareholder structure, but also provides GM and Cruise maximum flexibility." — Mary Barra, on GM acquiring SoftBank's stake and investing an additional $1.35B, March 2022 [^12]
