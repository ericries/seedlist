## About

MOTHER.Tech is a Brooklyn-based AI creative software company founded in 2023 by Kelsey Falter, Raissa Chagas, and Miles Seiver [^1][^2]. The company is the maker of Degen, a one-tap AI creative app that replaces prompt engineering with artist-designed modular tools called "gens" — covering images, video, memes, carousels, and audio [^1][^3]. Degen launched publicly on May 5, 2026, on iOS and Android via an invite-code system following an invite-only beta [^3].

The platform structures its experience around taste-based channels, a remixable personal library, and a usage-based creator economy in which artists earn revenue each time someone runs their generator, decoupling creator compensation from reach [^1][^2].

## What Founders Say

> "We spent years inside the biggest platforms and saw how the systems actually work — quietly taking more from users than they give back. Degen is the app we wanted to exist."
> — Raissa Chagas, Co-Founder, MOTHER.Tech [^2][^3]

On the company's product philosophy, Kelsey Falter has asked: "What if we stopped optimizing for engagement and started building for play and creation?" [^1]

On user data control, Falter has stated that Degen users own their content and can delete it outright, "not just hide it from their profile, but actually remove it from the system. We don't retain copies for training models or serving ads against your creations." [^3]
