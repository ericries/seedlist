## About

Datadog is a cloud monitoring and observability platform founded in 2010 by Olivier Pomel and Alexis Le-Quoc in New York City [^1]. The founders met as undergraduates at Ecole Centrale Paris and later worked together for nine years at Wireless Generation [^1]. Datadog went public on the Nasdaq on September 19, 2019 under the ticker DDOG, pricing 24 million shares at $27 on September 18, 2019 to raise $648 million at an approximately $7.8 billion offering valuation (roughly $8.7 billion fully diluted) [^7] [^8].

## What Investors Say

Shardul Shah of Index Ventures said: "The combination of Datadog's beautifully crafted product, stellar team, and hyper growth, is why we're excited to continue to support them" [^4].

Dev Ittycheria of OpenView said: "Datadog has created a monitoring platform that has been explicitly designed to monitor highly elastic compute environments on public or private clouds" [^3].

Murat Bicer of RTP Ventures said: "We see Datadog setting the standard for infrastructure monitoring in the cloud age: they aggregate tens of billions of records a day from dozens of sources for their largest customers, yet present it in a simple interactive package" [^2].
