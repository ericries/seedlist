## About

Merge Labs is a brain-computer interface (BCI) research startup that emerged from stealth on January 15, 2026 [^1]. The company is developing noninvasive BCI technology that uses molecules instead of electrodes and transmits/receives information using ultrasound, in contrast to Neuralink's surgically implanted electrode approach [^1][^2]. It reportedly spun out of Forest Neurotech, a nonprofit focused on BCI research [^1]. Sam Altman co-founded the company "in a personal capacity" alongside Caltech researcher Mikhail Shapiro, Forest Neurotech co-founders Tyson Aflalo and Sumner Norman, and Alex Blania (CEO, also associated with Tools for Humanity/Worldcoin) [^1][^2]. The company describes itself as dedicated to "bridging biological and artificial intelligence to maximize human ability, agency, and experience" [^1]. Multiple outlets noted the governance concern of Altman's dual role as OpenAI CEO and Merge Labs co-founder [^1][^2][^3].
