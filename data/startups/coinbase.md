## About

Coinbase was founded in June 2012 by Brian Armstrong and Fred Ehrsam in San Francisco, California [^1]. Armstrong, a former Airbnb software engineer, and Ehrsam, a former Goldman Sachs trader, met through the Bitcoin subreddit on Reddit [^1]. The company was part of Y Combinator's Summer 2012 batch [^2]. Coinbase grew from a simple platform for buying and selling Bitcoin into one of the largest cryptocurrency exchanges in the United States [^1]. Fred Ehrsam served as the company's first president before departing to co-found the crypto investment firm Paradigm [^1]. Coinbase went public via a direct listing on the Nasdaq exchange on April 14, 2021, with a reference price of $250 per share, giving the company an estimated value of $47 billion [^3]. SV Angel was an early seed investor in Coinbase, and Topher Conway of SV Angel has worked closely with the company [^4].

## What Investors Say

> "Bitcoin adds an economic layer that has long been needed on the Internet... Bitcoin's low-level technical protocol creates a clear and universal measure of agreed-upon value between transacting parties, cutting charges from intermediaries like credit card companies."
> -- Chris Dixon, Andreessen Horowitz, on leading Coinbase's Series B, December 2013 [^7]

Andreessen Horowitz and Union Square Ventures were the two largest outside investors in Coinbase at the time of its IPO, with a16z's stake worth approximately $9.7 billion based on the listing price [^3].

## What Founders Say

> "From the earliest days I built Coinbase to harness the power of cryptocurrency to create more freedom in the world."
> -- Brian Armstrong, CEO and co-founder [^11]

> "Our mission is to increase economic freedom in the world."
> -- Brian Armstrong, Coinbase mission statement [^11]

Armstrong has spoken about recognizing early on that someone needed to build a cryptocurrency company that was not "sketchy," based in a legitimate area, willing to work with regulators, and prepared to obtain licenses [^12]. He and the Coinbase team were "essentially pushing the industry forward by saying, 'We think this should be a regulated, trusted industry'" [^12].
